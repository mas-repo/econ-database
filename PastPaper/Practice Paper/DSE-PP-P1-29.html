Money and Banking - Properties of Money & Asset Liquidity

難度 (Difficulty): ⭐☆☆☆☆ (簡單 Easy)

核心概念 (Key Concepts):

  • 流動性 (Liquidity)
  • 貨幣的優良特徵 (Properties of Good Money)
  • 資產轉換 (Asset Conversion)

📝 題目 (The Question)

若某一資產可以在短時間內輕易地轉為現金，並且不會招致損失，那麼它可被視為一種_______的資產。

A. 便攜
B. 耐用
C. 容易拆細
D. 流動

An asset which can be readily converted into cash at short notice without making a loss is regarded as a _______ asset.

A. portable
B. durable
C. divisible
D. liquid

💡 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

在經濟學中，我們經常討論資產的特質，尤其是作為「貨幣」應具備的條件。這道題目測試的是對流動性 (Liquidity) 的精確定義。

In economics, we frequently discuss the properties of assets, especially the conditions required to act as "money". This question tests the precise definition of Liquidity.

流動性 (Liquidity): 指一項資產能夠在不損失其價值的情況下，迅速轉換為交易媒介（即現金）的容易程度。現金本身就是流動性最高的資產。

Liquidity: Refers to the ease with which an asset can be quickly converted into a medium of exchange (i.e., cash) without a loss in value. Cash itself is the most liquid asset.

為了量度一個經濟體系中的流動性，經濟學家會根據資產的流動性高低來劃分貨幣供應 (Money Supply)。例如，包含最流動資產（公眾持有的法定貨幣 \(C\) 及 活期存款 \(D\)）的狹義貨幣供應 \(M1\) 可表示為：

To measure liquidity in an economy, economists categorize the money supply based on the degree of liquidity of various assets. For example, the narrowest money supply \(M1\), which consists of the most liquid assets (legal tender held by the public \(C\) and demand deposits \(D\)), is defined as:

$$ M1 = C + D $$

其他優良貨幣的物理特徵（但非財務流動性指標）包括：

Other physical properties of good money (but not financial indicators of liquidity) include:

  • 便攜性 (Portability): 體積小、重量輕，易於攜帶。 (Small, lightweight, easy to carry.)
  • 耐用性 (Durability): 不易損壞或腐爛。 (Not easily worn out or perishable.)
  • 可分性/容易拆細 (Divisibility): 能輕易分割成較小面額以進行不同金額的交易。 (Easily divided into smaller denominations for transactions of various sizes.)

✅ 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

錯誤 Wrong 選項 A: 便攜 (portable)

便攜性是指資產在物理上容易攜帶，例如紙幣比黃金更容易攜帶。這與「轉換為現金的難易度及價值損失」無關。

Portability means the asset is physically easy to carry, like paper notes compared to heavy gold. This has nothing to do with the "ease of conversion to cash and loss of value".

錯誤 Wrong 選項 B: 耐用 (durable)

耐用性是指資產在物理上不易隨時間耗損，例如硬幣比蘋果耐用。這描述的是資產的物理壽命，而非其市場轉換能力。

Durability means the asset does not easily deteriorate physically over time, like coins compared to apples. This describes the physical lifespan of the asset, not its market convertibility.

錯誤 Wrong 選項 C: 容易拆細 (divisible)

容易拆細是指資產可以分割成不同大小的部分而不失其按比例計算的價值，例如100元紙幣可換成十個10元硬幣。這與變現速度無直接關係。

Divisibility means the asset can be broken down into smaller units without losing its proportional value, like a $100 note into ten $10 coins. This has no direct relationship with the speed of cashing out.

正確 Correct 選項 D: 流動 (liquid)

這正是題目所給出的教科書式定義。「短時間內」（時間成本低）與「不招致損失」（價值保留）是流動性資產的兩大核心特徵。

This is the exact textbook definition provided in the question. "At short notice" (low time cost) and "without making a loss" (value retention) are the two core characteristics of a liquid asset.

🔍 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point): 留意題目中的關鍵字眼 「轉為現金」 (converted into cash) 及 「不招致損失」 (without making a loss)。

The Pivot Point: Pay attention to the keywords "converted into cash" and "without making a loss" in the prompt.

這題屬於「直接定義題」(Direct Definitional Question)。遇到這類題目，你必須清楚區分資產的「物理特徵」(A, B, C) 與「金融/市場特徵」(D)。「轉為現金」是一個金融交易過程，因此必定指向流動性。

This is a "Direct Definitional Question". When encountering such questions, you must clearly distinguish between an asset's "physical properties" (A, B, C) and its "financial/market properties" (D). "Converting to cash" is a financial transaction process, thus it inevitably points to Liquidity.

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

概念混淆 (Conceptual Confusion): 很多同學會將「便攜」與「流動性」混淆，以為「容易帶去銀行」就代表「流動性高」。

Many students confuse "portability" with "liquidity," thinking that "easy to carry to the bank" implies "high liquidity."

糾正： 鑽石非常便攜（體積小價值高），但其流動性其實很低。因為要將二手鑽石短時間內變現，往往需要大幅降價賣給當鋪或珠寶商（招致巨大損失）。因此，便攜 \(\neq\) 流動。

Correction: Diamonds are highly portable (small size, high value), but their liquidity is actually very low. To convert a second-hand diamond into cash at short notice, you usually have to drop the price significantly to sell it to a pawn shop or jeweler (incurring a huge loss). Therefore, Portable \(\neq\) Liquid.

🙋 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

問：房地產（物業）的流動性高嗎？

Q: Is real estate (property) highly liquid?

答： 非常低。賣樓通常需要幾個月時間（未能短時間內變現）。如果急需用錢必須在一週內賣出，賣家通常需要以低於市價的「跳樓價」出售（招致損失）。因此，房地產是典型的低流動性資產。

A: Very low. Selling a property usually takes months (cannot be done at short notice). If you urgently need cash and must sell within a week, you typically have to sell at a "fire-sale" price below market value (making a loss). Hence, real estate is a typical low-liquidity asset.

問：股票是高流動性資產嗎？

Q: Are stocks highly liquid assets?

答： 相對較高，但不完美。雖然在交易時段內可以瞬間賣出股票（短時間），但股票價格波動大，賣出時可能低於你的買入價（可能招致損失）。相比之下，銀行活期存款的流動性比股票更高。

A: Relatively high, but not perfect. Although you can sell stocks instantly during trading hours (short notice), stock prices fluctuate wildly, and you might sell them below your purchase price (potentially making a loss). In contrast, demand deposits in banks have higher liquidity than stocks.

⚡ 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

秒殺法： 當你在 DSE 卷上看到 "cash" (現金) 與 "asset" (資產) 的轉換關係時，立刻在腦海中鎖定 "Liquidity" (流動性)。其他選項 (Portable, Durable, Divisible, Acceptable) 全都是描述物件本身的「物理或社會屬性」，只有 Liquidity 是描述資產在市場上的「變現能力」。

Instant Kill Method: Whenever you see the conversion relationship between "cash" and "asset" on a DSE paper, immediately lock onto "Liquidity". The other options (Portable, Durable, Divisible, Acceptable) describe the physical or social attributes of the object itself, whereas Liquidity specifically describes an asset's "cash-convertibility" in the market.
