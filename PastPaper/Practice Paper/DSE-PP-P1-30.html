HKDSE Economics Tutorial

Topic: Money and Inflation (貨幣與通脹) Difficulty: ⭐⭐ (2/5) Concepts: Real Value of Money, Price Level, Purchasing Power

1. The Question (題目)

當_______正在上升，貨幣的實質價值下降。

A. 通縮率
B. 一般物價水平
C. 貨幣需求
D. 名義利率

The real value of money decreases when the _______ is rising.

A. deflation rate
B. general price level
C. money demand
D. nominal interest rate

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

貨幣的實質價值 (Real Value of Money) / 購買力 (Purchasing Power)
貨幣的實質價值是指一單位貨幣能夠購買到的商品和服務的數量。它與經濟體系中的「一般物價水平」成反比關係。當物價上升（通脹）時，同一張紙幣能買到的東西減少了，這意味著貨幣的實質價值下降。
Real Value of Money / Purchasing Power
The real value of money refers to the quantity of goods and services that one unit of currency can buy. It has an inversely proportional relationship with the "general price level" in the economy. When prices rise (inflation), the same banknote buys fewer items, meaning the real value of money has decreased.
$$\text{Purchasing Power of Money} = \frac{1}{\text{General Price Level} \; (P)}$$
由公式可見，一般物價水平 \(P\) 位於分母。當 \(P\) 上升，分數的數值就會下降，即購買力下跌。
As seen from the formula, the general price level \(P\) is in the denominator. When \(P\) increases, the value of the fraction decreases, meaning purchasing power falls.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: B

B. 一般物價水平 (general price level)
正確。根據定義，當一般物價水平上升（即發生通貨膨脹），每單位貨幣所能購買的商品和服務數量就會減少。因此，貨幣的實質價值會直接因為物價水平的上升而下降。
B. general price level
Correct. By definition, when the general price level rises (i.e., inflation occurs), the quantity of goods and services that each unit of money can purchase decreases. Therefore, the real value of money drops directly because of a rising price level.
A. 通縮率 (deflation rate)
錯誤。通縮（Deflation）代表一般物價水平正在「下跌」。如果通縮率「正在上升」（例如從 2% 升至 5%），這意味著物價下跌的速度加快了。由於物價變得越來越便宜，一單位貨幣能買到的東西反而更多，因此貨幣的實質價值是上升的，而非下降。
A. deflation rate
Incorrect. Deflation means the general price level is "falling". If the deflation rate is "rising" (e.g., from 2% to 5%), it means prices are dropping at an accelerating pace. Because goods are getting cheaper, one unit of currency can buy more, so the real value of money actually increases, not decreases.
C. 貨幣需求 (money demand)
錯誤。貨幣需求是指人們願意以流動現金形式持有財富的傾向。貨幣需求上升可能會導致名義利率上升（在貨幣供應不變的情況下），但它並不直接決定或等同於貨幣的實質購買力下降。
C. money demand
Incorrect. Money demand refers to people's desire to hold their wealth in the form of liquid cash. An increase in money demand might lead to a higher nominal interest rate (assuming money supply is constant), but it does not directly determine or equate to a drop in the real purchasing power of money.
D. 名義利率 (nominal interest rate)
錯誤。名義利率是持有貨幣的「機會成本」（Opportunity cost of holding money），因為把錢拿在手上就等於放棄了存入銀行賺取利息的機會。名義利率上升代表持有現金的代價變大，但這不等於現金在市場上能買到的漢堡包變少了（購買力是由物價決定的）。
D. nominal interest rate
Incorrect. The nominal interest rate is the "opportunity cost of holding money" because holding cash means giving up the chance to earn interest in a bank. A rising nominal interest rate means the cost of holding cash is higher, but it doesn't mean your cash buys fewer hamburgers in the market (purchasing power is strictly determined by the price level).

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point)：
這道題目的核心在於明白「貨幣的實質價值 (Real value of money)」是一個相對概念。錢本身沒有固定價值，它的價值取決於它能換到多少商品。因此，只要題目問到「貨幣實質價值」或「購買力」，你的大腦應該立刻聯想到「物價 (Price Level)」。
The Pivot Point:
The core of this question lies in understanding that the "real value of money" is a relative concept. Money itself has no fixed value; its value depends on how many goods it can be exchanged for. Therefore, whenever a question asks about the "real value of money" or "purchasing power," your brain should immediately link it to the "Price Level."

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

  • 概念混淆： 學生常將「持有貨幣的成本 (Cost of holding money)」與「貨幣的價值 (Value of money)」混淆。名義利率是成本，物價水平才是決定價值的因素。
  • 字眼陷阱： 看到選項 A 的「上升」，很多學生會覺得「通縮率上升 = 數字變大」，卻忽略了通縮本身代表「物價下跌」，雙重邏輯下其實代表購買力上升。
  • Concept Confusion: Students often confuse the "cost of holding money" with the "value of money". Nominal interest rate is the cost, while the general price level determines the value.
  • Wording Trap: Seeing the word "rising" in Option A, students might think "rising deflation rate = bigger numbers," forgetting that deflation inherently means "falling prices." By double logic, a rising deflation rate actually means purchasing power is going up.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 老師，如果發生通貨膨脹（物價上升），通常名義利率也會跟著上升來對抗通脹呀。那為什麼不能選 D 呢？
A: 這是個好問題！在現實中，物價上升和名義利率上升經常同時發生（費雪效應 Fisher Effect）。但在經濟學的定義上，直接導致「貨幣實質價值」改變的數學分母只有「一般物價水平」。名義利率上升只是一個可能伴隨發生的現象或政策結果，它不是導致貨幣貶值的直接定義。做 MC 題時，必須選擇最直接、符合定義的答案。
Q: Teacher, if inflation happens (prices rise), nominal interest rates usually rise too to combat inflation. So why can't we choose D?
A: That's a great question! In reality, rising prices and rising nominal interest rates often happen together (Fisher Effect). However, by economic definition, the only mathematical denominator that directly changes the "real value of money" is the "general price level." A rising nominal interest rate is a correlated phenomenon or a policy response, not the direct definitional cause of money losing its value. For MC questions, you must choose the most direct, definitional answer.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧：
這是一條「純定義題」(Definitional Question)。
在 DSE 考試中，只要看到 Real value of money 或 Purchasing power，請立即尋找帶有 Price level 或 Inflation / Deflation 的選項。由於題目說價值「下降」，你需要找一個代表物價「上升」的選項。B 完全吻合。
Speed-kill Trick:
This is a "pure definition question."
In the DSE exam, whenever you see Real value of money or Purchasing power, immediately scan the options for Price level or Inflation / Deflation. Since the question states the value is "decreasing", you need an option that signifies prices are "rising". Option B is a perfect match.
