Topic / 課題: Basic Economic Concepts (Scarcity & Choices) / 基本經濟學概念 (稀少性與選擇)

Difficulty / 難度: ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ (3/5)

Key Concepts / 核心概念: Scarcity (稀少性), Free Goods (免費物品), Competition (競爭), Discrimination (歧視)

1. The Question (題目)

沒有稀少性，

(1) 歧視不會存在。
(2) 市價不會存在。
(3) 物品不會存在。

A. 只有(1)、(2)
B. 只有(1)、(3)
C. 只有(2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2)及(3)

In the absence of scarcity,

(1) there will be no discrimination.
(2) there will be no market price.
(3) there will be no goods.

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

Definition of Scarcity (稀少性的定義)

稀少性是指人類的慾望大於可用以滿足慾望的資源。由於資源不足以滿足所有人的所有慾望，我們必須作出選擇。

Scarcity occurs when human wants exceed the available resources to satisfy them. Because resources are insufficient to satisfy all human wants, choices must be made.

$$ \text{Human Wants (人類慾望)} > \text{Available Resources (可用資源)} $$

Consequences of Scarcity (稀少性的後果)

因為資源稀少，我們需要分配資源，這必然會引發競爭 (Competition)。而在競爭中，我們必須決定「誰勝誰負」，這就需要一套分配準則，經濟學稱之為歧視 (Discrimination)（例如：價高者得就是一種以價格為準則的歧視）。

Because resources are scarce, they must be allocated, which inevitably leads to competition. In any competition, there must be criteria to determine the winners and losers. Economics refers to these criteria as discrimination (e.g., "highest bidder gets the good" is a form of price discrimination).

Goods vs. Economic Goods (物品與經濟物品)

物品 (Good): 任何能夠滿足人類慾望的東西。
經濟物品 (Economic Good): 數量不足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品（涉及稀少性及代價）。
免費物品 (Free Good): 數量足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品（沒有稀少性，無須付出代價，如空氣）。

Good: Anything that satisfies human wants.
Economic Good: A good whose quantity is insufficient to satisfy all human wants (involves scarcity and cost).
Free Good: A good whose quantity is sufficient to satisfy all human wants (no scarcity, zero opportunity cost, e.g., air).

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): A. 只有(1)、(2) / (1) and (2) only

Statement (1): 歧視不會存在 / There will be no discrimination.

正確。歧視是分配稀少資源的準則。如果沒有稀少性，每個人都能隨心所欲地得到他們想要的一切，根本不需要分配，也沒有競爭，因此歧視就不會存在。

TRUE. Discrimination serves as a criterion to allocate scarce resources. Without scarcity, everyone gets exactly what they want. There is no need for allocation, no competition, and therefore, no discrimination.

Statement (2): 市價不會存在 / There will be no market price.

正確。市價 \(P\) 其實就是其中一種用來分配資源的「歧視準則」（價高者得）。如果沒有稀少性，物品的供應是無限的，消費者不需要放棄任何東西來獲取它，價格就會跌至零（\(P = 0\)），即市價不存在。

TRUE. Market price \(P\) is simply one type of "discrimination criterion" used for allocation (the highest bidder gets it). Without scarcity, the supply of goods is infinite relative to wants, consumers do not need to give up anything to obtain them, and the price falls to zero (\(P = 0\)). Hence, market price ceases to exist.

Statement (3): 物品不會存在 / There will be no goods.

錯誤。這是DSE最常見的陷阱！物品（Goods）的定義是「能滿足人類慾望的東西」。即使沒有稀少性，我們仍然有慾望（例如呼吸），而空氣能滿足這個慾望，所以空氣是一件「物品」。沒有稀少性，物品只是從「經濟物品」變成了「免費物品」，但物品本身依然存在。

FALSE. This is the most common DSE trap! The definition of a "Good" is anything that satisfies human wants. Even without scarcity, we still have wants (e.g., breathing), and air satisfies this want, making it a "good." Without scarcity, economic goods simply turn into free goods, but goods themselves still exist.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題關鍵 (Pivot Point)：這道題目的核心在於區分「物品 (Goods)」與「經濟物品 (Economic Goods)」。當題目假設「沒有稀少性 (absence of scarcity)」時，它實際上是在描繪一個烏托邦：所有經濟物品都變成了免費物品。

Pivot Point: The core of this question lies in distinguishing between "Goods" and "Economic Goods." When the question assumes the "absence of scarcity," it is portraying a utopia where all economic goods have simply become free goods.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

⚠️ The "Discrimination" Trap (對「歧視」的誤解)

日常生活中，「歧視」通常指種族或性別歧視等負面詞語。但在經濟學中，任何分配準則都是歧視。例如：考試按分數錄取學生是「成績歧視」，超級市場賣蘋果是「價格歧視」。同學容易因為日常用語的干擾而選錯。

In daily life, "discrimination" usually refers to racial or gender prejudice. But in economics, any allocation criterion is a form of discrimination. For example, admitting students based on grades is "grade discrimination," and selling apples in a supermarket is "price discrimination." Students often get confused by the colloquial meaning of the word.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: Why is price exactly $0 when there is no scarcity? (為什麼沒有稀少性時，價格必定是零？)

A: 價格是你為了獲得某物品而必須放棄的其他選項的價值（即代價）。當沒有稀少性時，資源無限，你拿走一部分並不會剝奪其他人的份額，因此沒有人願意為此付費。在供需圖中，這意味著供給曲線在需求曲線與數量軸相交處的右方，均衡價格為 $0。

A: Price represents the value of other options you must give up to obtain a good. When there is no scarcity, resources are virtually infinite; taking some doesn't deprive anyone else, so no one is willing to pay for it. In a supply and demand graph, this means the supply curve lies to the right of where the demand curve intersects the quantity axis, making the equilibrium price $0.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

🚀 The Elimination Method (刪除法速解)

在DSE考試中，時間就是分數！一看到這題，聰明的考生會立即留意到選項 (3) 說「物品不會存在」。因為空氣、陽光等「免費物品」顯然是存在的，你馬上就知道 (3) 是絕對錯誤的。

看看選項：
A. 只有(1)、(2)
B. 只有(1)、(3)
C. 只有(2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2)及(3)

只要確定 (3) 錯，B、C、D 可以秒殺！根本不需要深入思考 (1) 和 (2) 的複雜概念，直接選 A 即可。節省下來的2分鐘可以留給後面困難的微觀計算題。

In the DSE exam, time is marks! A smart candidate will immediately spot statement (3) claiming "no goods will exist." Since "free goods" like air and sunlight obviously exist, you instantly know (3) is completely false.

Look at the options. Once you confirm (3) is false, you can immediately eliminate B, C, and D! You don't even need to heavily analyze the complex concepts in (1) and (2). Just pick A and save 2 minutes for harder microeconomics calculation questions later!
