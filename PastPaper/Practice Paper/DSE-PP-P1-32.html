📝 HKDSE Economics Masterclass

Topic (主題): 宏觀經濟政策與產出缺口 / Macroeconomic Policies & Output Gap
Difficulty (難度): ⭐⭐.5 / 5
Key Concepts (核心概念): 通縮缺口 Deflationary Gap, 總需求 AD, 擴張性政策 Expansionary Policy

1. 題目 / The Question

下列哪項有助收窄通縮（產出）缺口？

A. 法定儲備比率增加
B. 中央銀行在市場售賣債券
C. 政府支出增加
D. 撤銷進口關稅

Which of the following can help narrow a deflationary (output) gap?

A. An increase in the required reserve ratio
B. A sale of bonds by the central bank in the market
C. An increase in government expenditure
D. The abolishment of a tariff on imports

2. 概念重溫 / Concept Review

通縮缺口 (Deflationary Gap)
當經濟體系的均衡產出（Real GDP, \( Y \)）低於充分就業產出（Full-employment level of output, \( Y_f \)）時，便會出現通縮缺口。要收窄此缺口，政府或央行需要推行擴張性政策 (Expansionary Policies) 來刺激總需求 (Aggregate Demand, \( AD \))，使其曲線向右移。

A deflationary gap occurs when the equilibrium level of real GDP (\( Y \)) is less than the full-employment level of output (\( Y_f \)). To narrow this gap, the government or central bank needs to implement expansionary policies to stimulate Aggregate Demand (\( AD \)) and shift the curve to the right.

總需求方程式如下：

The Aggregate Demand equation is:

$$ AD = C + I + G + (X - M) $$

當中 \( C \) = 私人消費 (Consumption)，\( I \) = 投資 (Investment)，\( G \) = 政府支出 (Government Expenditure)，\( X \) = 出口 (Exports)，\( M \) = 進口 (Imports)。

Where \( C \) = Consumption, \( I \) = Investment, \( G \) = Government Expenditure, \( X \) = Exports, and \( M \) = Imports.

3. 答案與詳細分析 / Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案 / Correct Option: C

A. 法定儲備比率增加 (Increase in required reserve ratio) - ❌ 錯誤 (Wrong)

這是一項緊縮性貨幣政策。法定儲備比率增加會導致銀行可借出的資金減少，貨幣供應量下降，利率上升。這會壓抑投資 \( I \) 和消費 \( C \)，導致總需求 \( AD \) 下降，進一步擴大通縮缺口。

This is a contractionary monetary policy. An increase in the required reserve ratio reduces banks' ability to lend, leading to a decrease in money supply and an increase in interest rates. This suppresses investment \( I \) and consumption \( C \), causing a decrease in aggregate demand \( AD \), which widens the deflationary gap.

B. 中央銀行在市場售賣債券 (Sale of bonds by the central bank) - ❌ 錯誤 (Wrong)

這同樣是一項緊縮性貨幣政策（公開市場操作）。央行賣出債券會從市場抽走資金，導致貨幣供應減少，利率上升。如同選項A，這會令 \( AD \) 下降，令缺口惡化。

This is also a contractionary monetary policy (Open Market Operations). When the central bank sells bonds, it drains liquidity from the market, reducing money supply and raising interest rates. Similar to Option A, this decreases \( AD \) and worsens the gap.

C. 政府支出增加 (Increase in government expenditure) - ✅ 正確 (Correct)

這是一項擴張性財政政策。政府支出 \( G \) 是總需求 \( AD \) 的直接組成部分。當 \( G \) 增加時，根據公式 \( AD = C + I + G + (X - M) \)，總需求 \( AD \) 會直接上升，帶動整體物價和產出增加，有效收窄通縮缺口。

This is an expansionary fiscal policy. Government expenditure \( G \) is a direct component of aggregate demand \( AD \). When \( G \) increases, according to the formula \( AD = C + I + G + (X - M) \), \( AD \) increases directly. This drives up the price level and real output, effectively narrowing the deflationary gap.

D. 撤銷進口關稅 (Abolishment of a tariff on imports) - ❌ 錯誤 (Wrong)

撤銷進口關稅會令外國進口貨品變得更便宜，本地市民會增加購買進口貨，導致進口 \( M \) 增加。由於淨出口為 \( (X - M) \)，進口增加會令淨出口下降，從而拉低總需求 \( AD \)，這會擴大通縮缺口。

Abolishing an import tariff makes imported goods cheaper, leading local citizens to buy more imports, thus increasing \( M \). Since net exports are \( (X - M) \), an increase in imports reduces net exports, pulling down aggregate demand \( AD \). This would widen the deflationary gap.

4. 題目拆解 / Problem Decomposition

解決這類題目的「關鍵樞紐」(Pivot Point) 是：翻譯題目要求。
「收窄通縮缺口」 = 「需要刺激經濟」 = 「需要找出能讓 AD 升 或 SRAS 升 的選項」。
只要你判斷出每個選項是屬於「擴張性 (Expansionary)」還是「緊縮性 (Contractionary)」政策，就能輕易剔除錯誤答案。

The "Pivot Point" for solving this type of question is translating the question's requirement.
"Narrow a deflationary gap" = "Need to stimulate the economy" = "Need to find the option that increases AD or increases SRAS."
As long as you classify each option as either an "expansionary" or "contractionary" policy, you can easily eliminate the wrong answers.

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 / Common Mistakes & Traps

  • 混淆買賣債券的效果 (Confusing buying vs. selling bonds): 很多學生會記錯公開市場操作。記住口訣：央行「買」債券 = 放「水」(資金) 出來 = 擴張經濟；央行「賣」債券 = 抽乾市場的「水」 = 緊縮經濟。
  • Confusing Open Market Operations: Remember the rule of thumb: When the central bank "buys" bonds, it injects "water" (liquidity) into the market = Expansionary. When it "sells" bonds, it drains "water" from the market = Contractionary.
  • 忽略進口對淨出口的負面影響 (Ignoring the negative impact of imports on NX): 學生常以為撤銷關稅是「好事」，所以對經濟有幫助。但在 AD-AS 模型中，進口 \( M \) 是 AD 的減項。進口增加會令 AD 下降！
  • Misjudging tariffs: Students often think removing tariffs is "good" and thus helps the economy. However, in the AD-AS model, imports \( M \) are a leakage. Increasing imports decreases AD!

6. 學生常見疑問 / Student Q&A

🙋‍♂️ 學生問 (Student): "撤銷進口關稅（選項D）不是會令入口原材料變平，從而降低生產成本，令短期總供給 (SRAS) 增加嗎？SRAS增加也能收窄通縮缺口啊！"

"Doesn't abolishing tariffs (Option D) make imported raw materials cheaper, reducing production costs and increasing Short-Run Aggregate Supply (SRAS)? Increasing SRAS also narrows a deflationary gap!"

👨‍🏫 導師答 (Tutor): 這是一個很好的進階思考！但在 DSE 經濟科的標準框架下，進口關稅的直接且主要影響是針對消費品進口，這會立即導致進口 \( M \) 飆升，淨出口大幅下降，強烈拖垮 AD。相比之下，選項 C（增加政府支出）是一個 100% 確定、直接的擴張性需求管理政策，沒有任何反向作用。在多項選擇題中，我們必須選擇最直接及最肯定的答案。

Answer: That is excellent high-level thinking! However, in the standard HKDSE economics framework, the primary and direct effect of removing import tariffs is applied to imported consumer goods, immediately causing imports \( M \) to surge and net exports to drop heavily, dragging down AD. In contrast, Option C (increasing government expenditure) is a 100% guaranteed, direct expansionary demand-side policy with no opposing effects. In MCQs, always choose the most direct and definitive answer.

7. 考試技巧與速解 / Exam Tricks & Shortcuts

💡 邏輯排除法 (The Logic Elimination Method)

在 MC 題目中，如果兩個選項的經濟效果完全相同，那麼這兩個選項都必然是錯的（因為 MC 只有一個答案）。
看選項 A 和選項 B：兩者都是「減少貨幣供應」的緊縮性貨幣政策。既然它們的最終效果一樣，就不可能是唯一正確答案，你可以瞬間同時剔除 A 和 B！

In MCQs, if two options have the exact same economic effect, both must be wrong (since there is only one correct answer).
Look at Options A and B: Both are contractionary monetary policies that "reduce money supply". Since their ultimate effects are identical, neither can be the unique correct answer. You can instantly eliminate both A and B!
