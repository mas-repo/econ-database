Topic: National Income Accounting (國民收入會計) Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5) Key Concepts: Per-capita Real GDP, Living Standard, Non-marketed goods

1. The Question (題目)

某國的人均實質GDP會低估一般生活水平，因為

The per-capita real GDP of a country would understate the general living standard because

A. 它包括了非消費品的價值。
it includes the value of non-consumption goods.
B. 它沒有考慮人口的多寡。
it does not consider the size of population.
C. 它沒有反映社會中罪案率上升。
it does not reflect the increasing crime rate in society.
D. 它沒有包括全部非市場物品。
it does not include all non-marketed goods.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

要準確量度一個國家的生活水平，我們通常使用人均實質本地生產總值 (Per-capita Real GDP)。然而，GDP 作為量度生活水平的指標有其局限性。

To accurately measure a country's living standard, we usually use Per-capita Real GDP. However, GDP has limitations as an indicator of living standards.

$$ \text{Per-capita Real GDP} = \frac{\text{Real GDP}}{\text{Population}} $$
  • Understate (低估): 實際生活水平 高於 GDP 數字所顯示的水平。通常是因為 GDP 漏算了一些「好東西」（如閒暇、非市場物品）。
    Actual living standard is higher than what the GDP figure suggests. This usually happens because GDP omits "good things" (e.g., leisure, non-marketed goods).
  • Overstate (高估): 實際生活水平 低於 GDP 數字所顯示的水平。通常是因為 GDP 漏算了一些「壞東西」（如污染、罪案）。
    Actual living standard is lower than what the GDP figure suggests. This usually happens because GDP omits "bad things" (e.g., pollution, crime).

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): D

Option A (選項 A) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

非消費品（如國防武器或資本物品）會計算在 GDP 內，但它們未必能直接提升市民當前的生活水平。因此，這會導致 GDP 高估 (overstate) 生活水平，而不是低估。

Non-consumption goods (like national defense weapons or capital goods) are included in GDP, but they may not directly improve the current living standard of citizens. Therefore, this would cause GDP to overstate the living standard, not understate it.

Option B (選項 B) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

題目已經指明是「人均 (per-capita)」實質 GDP，這意味著它已經將 GDP 除以人口，因此已經考慮了人口的多寡。

The question specifically mentions "per-capita" real GDP, which means it has already divided GDP by the population. Therefore, it has considered the size of the population.

Option C (選項 C) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

罪案率上升會降低市民的生活質素，但 GDP 並不會扣除這些負面影響。由於 GDP 數字沒有下降，但實際生活變差了，這會導致 GDP 高估 (overstate) 生活水平。

An increasing crime rate lowers the quality of life, but GDP does not deduct these negative impacts. Since the GDP figure doesn't drop but actual life gets worse, this causes GDP to overstate the living standard.

Option D (選項 D) - 正確 (Correct)

非市場物品（如義務工作、自己做家務）能提升生活水平，但因為沒有市場交易，所以不計入 GDP。由於實際生活水平因這些活動而提升，但 GDP 數字沒有反映出來，因此 GDP 低估 (understate) 了一般生活水平。

Non-marketed goods (like volunteer work, DIY chores) improve living standards, but because there are no market transactions, they are excluded from GDP. Since the actual living standard is improved by these activities but the GDP figure doesn't reflect it, GDP understates the general living standard.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (關鍵字眼): 題目的核心在於「低估 (understate)」一詞。

Pivot Point: The core of the question lies in the word "understate".

解題邏輯：
1. 找出 GDP 沒有包含的項目。
2. 判斷該項目對生活是「好」還是「壞」。
3. 漏算「好東西」= 數字太小 = 低估 (Understate)。
4. 漏算「壞東西」= 數字太大 = 高估 (Overstate)。

Solving Logic:
1. Identify items excluded from GDP.
2. Determine if the item is "good" or "bad" for living.
3. Omitting "good things" = Number is too small = Understate.
4. Omitting "bad things" = Number is too large = Overstate.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：混淆高估與低估 (Confusing Overstate and Understate)
學生經常死背例子，而忘記背後的邏輯。當遇到罪案率 (Crime rate) 或污染 (Pollution) 時，直覺認為是負面，就選了低估。記住：污染令生活變差，但 GDP 數字沒變小，所以 GDP 顯得比實際生活「好」，這就是高估！

Trap 1: Confusing Overstate and Understate
Students often memorize examples without understanding the logic. When seeing crime rate or pollution, they intuitively think it's negative and choose "understate". Remember: Pollution makes life worse, but the GDP figure doesn't shrink, so GDP looks "better" than actual life. That is an overstatement!

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 甚麼是非市場物品 (Non-marketed goods)？可以給些例子嗎？
Q: What are non-marketed goods? Can you give examples?

A: 這些是指沒有經過市場交易（沒有市價）但有價值的物品或服務。例如：媽媽在家煮飯（如果出外用膳會計入 GDP，但在家煮則不會）、義工服務、自己修理水喉等。這些都令生活更好，但 GDP 算不到。

A: These are valuable goods or services not traded in the market (no market price). Examples: A mother cooking at home (eating out counts in GDP, but home cooking doesn't), volunteer services, DIY plumbing. They make life better, but GDP can't count them.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

口訣 (Mnemonic):
- 漏算好事 ➔ 低估 (Missed Good ➔ Understate)
- 漏算壞事 ➔ 高估 (Missed Bad ➔ Overstate)

Mnemonic:
- Missed Good ➔ Understate
- Missed Bad ➔ Overstate

在考場上，看到 "understate"，立刻尋找選項中「沒有計算在內的好事」。選項 D 的非市場物品（如義工）是好事，秒殺！

In the exam, when you see "understate", immediately look for a "good thing that is excluded" in the options. Option D's non-marketed goods (like volunteering) is a good thing. Instant solve!
