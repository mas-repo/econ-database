HKDSE Economics Topic Analysis

Topic (課題): Inflation and Interest Rates / 通脹與利率

Difficulty (難度): ★★★☆☆

Key Concepts (關鍵概念): Nominal Interest Rate (名義利率), Real Interest Rate (實質利率), Cost of Holding Cash (持有現金的成本)

1. The Question (題目)

假設實際通脹率和名義利率分別是-1%和3%。下列哪些陳述是正確的？

(1) 持有現金的成本是3%。
(2) 預期實質利率高於名義利率。
(3) 持有現金的實際實質回報率是1%。

A. 只有 (1)、(2)
B. 只有 (1)、(3)
C. 只有 (2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)

Suppose the actual inflation rate and the nominal interest rate are -1% and 3% respectively. Which of the following statements are correct?

(1) The cost of holding cash is 3%.
(2) The expected real interest rate is higher than the nominal interest rate.
(3) The actual real rate of return on holding cash is 1%.

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這條問題，我們需要清楚理解名義利率、實質利率和通脹率之間的關係（費沙方程式 Fisher Equation），以及持有現金的經濟含義。

To solve this question, we need a clear understanding of the relationship between the nominal interest rate, real interest rate, and inflation rate (Fisher Equation), as well as the economic implications of holding cash.

Fisher Equation (費沙方程式)

$$\text{Real Interest Rate } (r) = \text{Nominal Interest Rate } (R) - \text{Inflation Rate } (\pi)$$

Key Definitions (關鍵定義)

  • Cost of Holding Cash (持有現金的成本):
    現金的名義回報率是 \( 0\% \)。如果將錢存入銀行，你可以賺取名義利率 \( R \)。因此，持有現金的機會成本就是放棄了的名義利率。
    The nominal return on cash is \( 0\% \). If you put money in the bank, you earn the nominal interest rate \( R \). Therefore, the opportunity cost of holding cash is the forgone nominal interest rate.
  • Real Rate of Return on Cash (持有現金的實質回報率):
    由於現金的名義回報率是 0%，根據費沙方程式，現金的實質回報率就是 \( 0\% - \text{Inflation Rate} \)。
    Since the nominal return on cash is 0%, according to the Fisher equation, the real return on cash is \( 0\% - \text{Inflation Rate} \).

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer (正確答案): Option B

分析 (1)：持有現金的成本是3%。 (The cost of holding cash is 3%.)

正確。持有現金的機會成本等於名義利率。題目給出名義利率為 3%，因此持有現金的成本就是 3%。

Correct. The opportunity cost of holding cash is equal to the nominal interest rate. The question states the nominal interest rate is 3%, so the cost of holding cash is indeed 3%.

分析 (2)：預期實質利率高於名義利率。 (The expected real interest rate is higher than the nominal interest rate.)

錯誤。根據公式，預期實質利率 = 名義利率 - 預期通脹率 \( (r_e = R - \pi_e) \)。題目只提供了實際通脹率 (-1%)，並沒有提供預期通脹率。在沒有預期通脹率數據的情況下，我們無法計算預期實質利率，因此不能斷定這句陳述正確。

Incorrect. According to the formula, Expected Real Interest Rate = Nominal Interest Rate - Expected Inflation Rate \( (r_e = R - \pi_e) \). The question only provides the actual inflation rate (-1%) and does not provide the expected inflation rate. Without data on expected inflation, we cannot calculate the expected real interest rate, so this statement cannot be determined as correct.

分析 (3)：持有現金的實際實質回報率是1%。 (The actual real rate of return on holding cash is 1%.)

正確。持有現金的名義回報率永遠是 0%。實際實質回報率 = 名義回報率 - 實際通脹率。代入數字：\( 0\% - (-1\%) = +1\% \)。在通縮期間，物價下跌，手持現金的購買力會上升。

Correct. The nominal return on holding cash is always 0%. Actual real rate of return = Nominal return - Actual inflation rate. Plugging in the numbers: \( 0\% - (-1\%) = +1\% \). During deflation, prices fall, meaning the purchasing power of cash on hand increases.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的「關鍵突破點」(Pivot Point) 在於區分「實際」(Actual) 與「預期」(Expected) 的數據，以及對「現金」(Cash) 屬性的隱含理解。

The "Pivot Point" of this type of question lies in distinguishing between "Actual" and "Expected" data, and the implicit understanding of the properties of "Cash".

  • 隱含資訊 (Implicit Info): 題目沒有明言，但考生必須知道「現金的名義回報率是 \( 0\% \)」。
    The question doesn't explicitly state it, but candidates must know that "the nominal return on cash is \( 0\% \)".
  • 擾亂視線 (Distractors): 題目給了「實際通脹率」(Actual Inflation)，卻在陳述 (2) 中問「預期實質利率」(Expected Real Interest Rate)。這是一個經典的考評局陷阱，測試考生有沒有看清楚字眼。
    The question gives the "Actual Inflation" but asks about the "Expected Real Interest Rate" in statement (2). This is a classic HKEAA trap testing if candidates read the wording carefully.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：混淆「預期」與「實際」 (Confusing "Expected" with "Actual")

很多學生會直接將實際通脹率 (-1%) 當作預期通脹率來計算陳述 (2)，得出預期實質利率為 \( 3\% - (-1\%) = 4\% \)，大於名義利率的 3%，從而誤以為 (2) 是正確的。記住：在經濟學中，除非題目特別說明「預期等於實際」，否則兩者不能混為一談！

Many students will directly use the actual inflation rate (-1%) as the expected inflation rate to calculate statement (2), finding an expected real interest rate of \( 3\% - (-1\%) = 4\% \), which is greater than 3%, thus mistakenly thinking (2) is correct. Remember: In economics, unless the question explicitly states "expected equals actual", the two cannot be conflated!

陷阱 2：負負得正的數學錯誤 (Mathematical error with negative signs)

計算實質回報時，公式是減去通脹率。當通脹率是負數（即通縮）時，必須記得「負負得正」：\( 0 - (-1\%) = +1\% \)。有些學生會不小心算成 -1%。

When calculating the real return, the formula is to subtract the inflation rate. When inflation is negative (deflation), you must remember "negative and negative make positive": \( 0 - (-1\%) = +1\% \). Some students carelessly calculate it as -1%.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼持有現金會有「回報」？現金不是不會生息嗎？
Q: Why does holding cash have a "return"? Doesn't cash generate no interest?

A: 現金確實沒有「名義」上的利息（名義回報 = 0%）。但「實質」回報看的是購買力。在通縮（-1%）的情況下，物價下跌了 1%。這意味著你手上的同一張 100 元鈔票，相比去年能買到多 1% 的貨物。這增加的購買力，就是持有現金的「實質回報」。
A: Cash indeed has no "nominal" interest (nominal return = 0%). But "real" return looks at purchasing power. In the case of deflation (-1%), prices have dropped by 1%. This means the same $100 bill in your hand can buy 1% more goods compared to last year. This increased purchasing power is the "real return" of holding cash.

Q: 考試時，如果題目沒有提「預期通脹率」，我是不是永遠要假設它未知？
Q: In exams, if the question doesn't mention the "expected inflation rate", should I always assume it's unknown?

A: 是的，在 HKDSE 考試中，這是一個非常嚴格的規則。「實際 (Actual/Ex-post)」和「預期 (Expected/Ex-ante)」是兩個獨立的概念。除非題目說「屬於非預期通脹 (unanticipated inflation is zero)」或「大眾準確預測通脹 (perfect foresight)」，否則絕對不能把實際數字直接當成預期數字。
A: Yes, in the HKDSE exam, this is a very strict rule. "Actual (Ex-post)" and "Expected (Ex-ante)" are two independent concepts. Unless the question says "unanticipated inflation is zero" or "perfect foresight", you absolutely cannot treat the actual figure as the expected figure.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

剔除法 (Method of Elimination) 是最強武器！

在 MC 卷中，時間就是分數。當你分析完陳述 (1) 和 (2) 後，其實你根本不需要計算 (3) 就能得出答案：

  1. 你知道 (1) 必定正確。
  2. 你看穿了 (2) 是考評局的「預期 vs 實際」陷阱，知道 (2) 必定錯誤（因為無法證明）。
  3. 看看選項：
    • A. 有 (2) ❌
    • C. 有 (2) ❌
    • D. 有 (2) ❌
  4. 唯一沒有 (2) 的選項就只有 B！ 這樣你可以節省最少 30 秒的時間。

Method of Elimination is your strongest weapon!

In the MC paper, time is marks. After analyzing statements (1) and (2), you don't even need to calculate (3) to get the answer:

  1. You know (1) is definitely correct.
  2. You see through HKEAA's "Expected vs Actual" trap in (2) and know (2) is incorrect (as it cannot be proven).
  3. Look at the options:
    • A. Contains (2) ❌
    • C. Contains (2) ❌
    • D. Contains (2) ❌
  4. The only option without (2) is B! This saves you at least 30 seconds.
