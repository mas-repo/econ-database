HKDSE Economics Tutorial 經濟學補習解析

  • Topic (課題): 市場干預與彈性 (Market Intervention and Elasticity) - 稅務負擔 (Tax Burden)
  • Difficulty (難度): ★★★★☆ (4/5 Stars)
  • Key Concepts (核心概念): Price Elasticity of Demand (需求價格彈性) Price Elasticity of Supply (供應價格彈性) Tax Incidence (稅項負擔)

1. The Question (題目)

當對某物品徵收從量銷售稅，下列哪項／些組合可能令消費者稅項負擔大於生產者稅項負擔？

When a per-unit sales tax is imposed on a good, which of the following combinations may result in the consumers’ tax burden exceeding the producers’ tax burden?

(1) 高彈性需求；高彈性供應
(2) 高彈性需求；低彈性供應
(3) 低彈性需求；高彈性供應
(4) 低彈性需求；低彈性供應

(1) elastic demand; elastic supply
(2) elastic demand; inelastic supply
(3) inelastic demand; elastic supply
(4) inelastic demand; inelastic supply

A. 只有 (2)
B. 只有 (1)、(3)
C. 只有 (2)、(4)
D. 只有 (1)、(3) 及 (4)

A. (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (4) only
D. (1), (3) and (4) only

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

稅項負擔的黃金法則 (The Golden Rule of Tax Burden)

在自由市場中，當政府徵收銷售稅時，稅項負擔會由消費者和生產者共同分擔。分擔的比例完全取決於需求價格彈性 \(E_d\) 與 供應價格彈性 \(E_s\) 的相對大小。

In a free market, when the government imposes a sales tax, the tax burden is shared between consumers and producers. The proportion of the burden depends entirely on the relative magnitude of the Price Elasticity of Demand \(E_d\) and the Price Elasticity of Supply \(E_s\).

口訣：「誰越缺乏彈性（越難走），誰就要承擔越多稅款！」

Rule of thumb: "The side that is more inelastic (less able to escape the market) bears a larger share of the tax burden!"

$$ \frac{\text{消費者稅項負擔 (Consumer Burden)}}{\text{生產者稅項負擔 (Producer Burden)}} = \frac{E_s}{E_d} $$
  • 若 \( E_d < E_s \) ：消費者負擔 > 生產者負擔
  • 若 \( E_d > E_s \) ：消費者負擔 < 生產者負擔
  • 若 \( E_d = E_s \) ：消費者負擔 = 生產者負擔
  • If \( E_d < E_s \): Consumer Burden > Producer Burden
  • If \( E_d > E_s \): Consumer Burden < Producer Burden
  • If \( E_d = E_s \): Consumer Burden = Producer Burden

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是：D

The correct option is: D

題目要求找出「可能令消費者稅項負擔大於生產者稅項負擔」的組合。根據剛才的概念，我們必須找出有可能出現 \( E_d < E_s \) 的情況。

The question asks for combinations that "may result in the consumers' tax burden exceeding the producers' tax burden". Based on our concept, we must find situations where it is possible that \( E_d < E_s \).

(1) 高彈性需求；高彈性供應 (elastic demand; elastic supply) - 可能 (Possible)

「高彈性」代表彈性值大於 1。假設需求彈性 \( E_d = 1.5 \)，而供應彈性 \( E_s = 4.0 \)。在這種情況下，兩者都是高彈性，但 \( E_d < E_s \)。因此，這是有可能的。

"Elastic" means the elasticity value is greater than 1. Suppose \( E_d = 1.5 \) and \( E_s = 4.0 \). Both are elastic, but \( E_d < E_s \). Therefore, this is possible.

(2) 高彈性需求；低彈性供應 (elastic demand; inelastic supply) - 絕對不可能 (Impossible)

「高彈性需求」即 \( E_d > 1 \)；「低彈性供應」即 \( E_s < 1 \)。這意味著數學上必然出現 \( E_d > E_s \)。因此，消費者負擔必定小於生產者負擔。此情況絕對不可能符合題目要求。

"Elastic demand" means \( E_d > 1 \); "inelastic supply" means \( E_s < 1 \). This mathematically guarantees that \( E_d > E_s \). Thus, the consumer burden will strictly be less than the producer burden. This is impossible.

(3) 低彈性需求；高彈性供應 (inelastic demand; elastic supply) - 必定發生 (Definite)

「低彈性需求」即 \( E_d < 1 \)；「高彈性供應」即 \( E_s > 1 \)。這意味著數學上必然出現 \( E_d < E_s \)。因此，消費者負擔必定大於生產者負擔。這絕對符合題目要求（既然「必定」，自然也包含在「可能」之內）。

"Inelastic demand" means \( E_d < 1 \); "elastic supply" means \( E_s > 1 \). This mathematically guarantees that \( E_d < E_s \). Thus, the consumer burden will strictly be greater than the producer burden. This definitely fits the requirement.

(4) 低彈性需求；低彈性供應 (inelastic demand; inelastic supply) - 可能 (Possible)

「低彈性」代表彈性值小於 1。假設需求彈性 \( E_d = 0.2 \)，而供應彈性 \( E_s = 0.8 \)。在這種情況下，兩者都是低彈性，但 \( E_d < E_s \)。因此，這也是有可能的。

"Inelastic" means the elasticity value is less than 1. Suppose \( E_d = 0.2 \) and \( E_s = 0.8 \). Both are inelastic, but \( E_d < E_s \). Therefore, this is also possible.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point): 題目的關鍵字是「可能」(may)。很多同學以為「高彈性」就代表一個固定的數值，忘記了即使兩者同屬「高彈性」（即大於1），它們的實際數值仍然可以有大小之分。

The Pivot Point: The keyword here is "may" (可能). Many students mistakenly treat "elastic" as a fixed value, forgetting that even if both are "elastic" (i.e., > 1), their actual numerical values can still differ in magnitude.

5. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

DSE 選擇題「剔除法」 (Elimination Method)

在考場上，你可以用 10 秒鐘解出這題：

In the exam room, you can solve this in 10 seconds:

  1. 先找「絕對錯誤」的選項。我們知道要消費者負擔較大，需求必須比供應更缺乏彈性。
  2. First, find the "absolutely wrong" option. We know that for the consumer to bear a larger burden, demand must be more inelastic than supply.
  3. 看 (2)：需求高彈性 (\(>1\))，供應低彈性 (\(<1\))。這裡需求彈性絕對大於供應彈性，消費者負擔一定較小。所以 (2) 必定錯！
  4. Look at (2): Elastic demand (\(>1\)) and inelastic supply (\(<1\)). Demand elasticity is definitely greater here, so consumer burden is strictly smaller. Option (2) is definitely wrong!
  5. 看看選項：A 和 C 都包含 (2)，直接刪去！剩下 B 和 D。
  6. Look at the choices: Options A and C contain (2). Cross them out! We are left with B and D.
  7. 比較 B 和 D，差異在於 (4)。問自己：兩個都是「低彈性」時，有可能 \( E_d < E_s \) 嗎？當然可以（例如 0.1 和 0.9）。既然 (4) 有可能，答案就是 D。秒殺！
  8. Compare B and D. The difference is (4). Ask yourself: When both are "inelastic", is it possible that \( E_d < E_s \)? Of course (e.g., 0.1 and 0.9). Since (4) is possible, the answer must be D. Done!

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：將「分類」等同於「數值」 (Equating "Categories" with "Values")
學生常犯的錯誤是以為「高彈性 = 高彈性」。在經濟學中，(1) 和 (4) 的情況雖然兩者屬於同一分類（同屬高彈性或同屬低彈性），但它們的相對數值 (Relative value) 可以不同。切記：彈性是一個連續的光譜 (Spectrum)，不是單一的點。

Trap: Equating "Categories" with "Values"
A common careless error is assuming "Elastic = Elastic". In economics, although both demand and supply in cases (1) and (4) fall into the same category, their relative values can differ. Remember: Elasticity is a continuous spectrum, not a single point.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

🙋‍♂️ 學生問： 老師，如果題目將「可能 (may)」改成「必定 (must)」，答案會是甚麼？

🙋‍♂️ Student: Teacher, what if the question changed the word "may" to "must"? What would the answer be?

👨‍🏫 導師答： 非常好的問題！如果題目問的是「必定」，那麼 (1) 和 (4) 就不成立了。因為兩個都是高彈性時，也有可能 \( E_d > E_s \)（例如 \( E_d=5, E_s=2 \)），這樣消費者負擔就會較小。唯一能「數學上保證」消費者負擔較大（即必定 \( E_d < E_s \)）的情況只有 (3)。所以如果改為「必定」，答案就只會包含 (3)。

👨‍🏫 Tutor: Excellent question! If the question asked for "must", then (1) and (4) would be incorrect. When both are elastic, it is also possible that \( E_d > E_s \) (e.g., \( E_d=5, E_s=2 \)), in which case the consumer burden is smaller. The only situation that "mathematically guarantees" a larger consumer burden (i.e., strictly \( E_d < E_s \)) is (3). So, if the word was "must", the answer would only include (3).
