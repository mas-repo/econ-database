HKDSE Economics Question Analysis

Topic: Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
Key Concepts: Three Basic Economic Questions (三個基本經濟問題), Positive Statement (實證性陳述), Normative Statement (規範性陳述)

The Question (題目)

一位政府官員表示：「政府已決定降低某隧道的收費，我們預期會吸引更多私家車使用該隧道，從而紓緩鄰近高速公路的擠塞情況。」

上述政府的決定與經濟學中的 ______ 問題相關，而上述政府官員的陳述屬 ______ 陳述。

A. 「怎樣生產」 …… 規範性
B. 「為誰生產」 …… 規範性
C. 「怎樣生產」 …… 實證性
D. 「為誰生產」 …… 實證性

A government official said, 'The government has decided to reduce the toll of a tunnel and we expect to attract more private vehicles to use the tunnel and relieve the congestion on a nearby highway.'

The above decision of the government is related to the _____ question in Economics and the above statement by the government official is a _____ statement.

A. 'how to produce' ...... normative
B. 'for whom to produce' ...... normative
C. 'how to produce' ...... positive
D. 'for whom to produce' ...... positive

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. Three Basic Economic Questions (三個基本經濟問題)

資源稀少性迫使社會必須回答三個基本問題：

Scarcity forces society to answer three basic questions:

  • 生產甚麼 (What to produce): 決定生產哪種物品及數量。 (Deciding which goods to produce and in what quantities.)
  • 怎樣生產 (How to produce): 決定生產的方法，即勞力密集還是資本密集。 (Deciding the method of production, e.g., labor-intensive vs. capital-intensive.)
  • 為誰生產 (For whom to produce): 決定物品如何分配給社會上不同的人。在市場經濟中，這通常由價格機制（Price Mechanism）決定。 (Deciding how goods are distributed among members of society. In a market economy, this is usually determined by the price mechanism.)

2. Positive vs. Normative Statements (實證性與規範性陳述)

  • 實證性陳述 (Positive Statement): 客觀、沒有價值判斷、可以利用事實來驗證對錯的陳述。通常涉及「是甚麼 (what is)」或「將會發生甚麼 (what will be)」。
  • Positive Statement: Objective, contains no value judgments, and can be tested by facts to be true or false. Usually deals with "what is" or "what will be".
  • 規範性陳述 (Normative Statement): 主觀、包含價值判斷、無法利用事實來驗證對錯的陳述。通常涉及「應該怎樣 (what ought to be)」、「好與壞」。
  • Normative Statement: Subjective, contains value judgments, and cannot be tested by facts. Usually deals with "what ought to be" or "good/bad".
$$ \text{Price Mechanism Allocation: If } P \downarrow \implies Q_d \uparrow $$

(當價格 \(P\) 下降，願意且能夠支付該價格的需求量 \(Q_d\) 會增加，這改變了資源的分配對象。)

(When price \(P\) decreases, the quantity demanded \(Q_d\) by those willing and able to pay increases, changing who gets the resource.)

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是 (Correct Option): Option D

第一部分：政府的決定 (Part 1: The Government's Decision)

政府決定「降低某隧道的收費」。改變價格會影響誰願意且能夠使用該隧道（即分配給私家車司機）。這涉及資源分配的機制，因此屬於「為誰生產」的問題。這並不涉及隧道的建造或營運方式（如使用人手收費還是自動收費），所以不是「怎樣生產」。

The government decided to "reduce the toll of a tunnel". Changing the price affects who is willing and able to use the tunnel (i.e., allocating it to private vehicle drivers). This involves the mechanism of resource distribution, thus it relates to the "for whom to produce" question. It does not involve how the tunnel is built or operated (e.g., manual vs. auto-toll), so it is not "how to produce".

第二部分：官員的陳述 (Part 2: The Official's Statement)

官員表示「我們預期會吸引更多私家車...從而紓緩...擠塞」。這是一個對未來的預測，指出了一個因果關係（降低收費 \(\rightarrow\) 吸引私家車 \(\rightarrow\) 紓緩擠塞）。這個預測是可以透過日後收集交通數據來驗證其對錯的。陳述中沒有包含「我們應該這樣做」或「這是公平的」等價值判斷，因此這是一句實證性陳述。

The official stated "we expect to attract more private vehicles... and relieve congestion". This is a prediction about the future, pointing out a cause-and-effect relationship (lower toll \(\rightarrow\) attract cars \(\rightarrow\) relieve congestion). This prediction can be tested for accuracy by collecting traffic data later. The statement does not contain value judgments like "we should do this" or "this is fair", therefore it is a positive statement.

選項排除 (Elimination):

  • A & C 錯誤: 降低收費改變了分配對象，而非生產方法，所以不是「怎樣生產」。 (Lowering the toll changes who gets the service, not the production method, so it's not "how to produce".)
  • B 錯誤: 該陳述是一個可驗證的預測，沒有價值判斷，所以不是規範性陳述。 (The statement is a testable prediction without value judgments, so it is not normative.)

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答這類雙重概念題，我們應該將題目拆開兩部分獨立分析：

To solve this type of dual-concept question, we should break it down into two independent parts:

  • Pivot Point 1 (關鍵點 1): 尋找與「價格」或「分配」相關的字眼。「降低收費」直接改變了使用者的門檻，決定了「誰」可以使用隧道。這立刻將範圍縮窄至「為誰生產」。
  • Pivot Point 1: Look for keywords related to "price" or "allocation". "Reduce the toll" directly changes the threshold for users, deciding "who" gets to use the tunnel. This immediately narrows it down to "for whom to produce".
  • Pivot Point 2 (關鍵點 2): 判斷句子是否可被數據驗證。「預期會吸引...」是一個客觀的因果推論。只要有因果推論且沒有「應該 (should)」、「最好 (best)」等主觀字眼，就是實證性 (Positive)。
  • Pivot Point 2: Determine if the sentence can be verified by data. "Expect to attract..." is an objective causal inference. As long as there is a causal inference without subjective words like "should" or "best", it is positive.

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

  • 陷阱一：誤將「政策目標」當作「規範性陳述」。 很多同學認為政府官員發言或推行政策，背後一定有「為市民好」的價值觀，所以選了規範性。但請注意，題目問的是「該陳述」的性質。該陳述只是一個客觀預測，並沒有表達主觀願望。
  • Trap 1: Confusing "policy goals" with "normative statements". Many students think that when a government official speaks or implements a policy, it must be driven by a value judgment of "doing good for citizens", leading them to choose normative. However, the question asks about the nature of "the statement" itself. The statement is merely an objective prediction and does not express a subjective desire.
  • 陷阱二：混淆「怎樣生產」與「為誰生產」。 同學可能誤以為「使用私家車」是一種生產交通服務的方法。實際上，私家車司機是這項隧道服務的「消費者」，改變收費是改變了消費者的組合。
  • Trap 2: Confusing "how" and "for whom" to produce. Students might mistakenly think "using private vehicles" is a method of producing transport services. In reality, private vehicle drivers are the "consumers" of the tunnel service; changing the toll changes the composition of consumers.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 如果官員說「政府應該降低收費，因為這對市民最公平」，這會是甚麼陳述？

Q: What if the official said, "The government should reduce the toll because it is fairest to the citizens"? What kind of statement would that be?

A: 這樣的話，它就會變成規範性陳述 (Normative Statement)。因為句子中包含了「應該 (should)」和「公平 (fairest)」這些基於個人價值觀的主觀判斷，無法用客觀數據去證明甚麼才是絕對的「公平」。

A: In that case, it would become a Normative Statement. The sentence contains subjective judgments based on personal values, such as "should" and "fairest". You cannot use objective data to prove what is absolutely "fair".

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

在DSE考試中，遇到這類題目可以使用關鍵字秒殺法 (Keyword Elimination)：

In the DSE exam, you can use the Keyword Elimination method for these questions:

  • 看到「價格 (Price)」、「收費 (Toll)」、「配額 (Quota)」 \(\rightarrow\) 90% 機率是 為誰生產 (For whom to produce)。
  • 看到「預期 (Expect)」、「將會導致 (Will lead to)」、「如果...則 (If... then)」 \(\rightarrow\) 必定是 實證性 (Positive)。
  • 看到「應該 (Should / Ought to)」、「太高/太低 (Too high / Too low)」、「公平 (Fair)」 \(\rightarrow\) 必定是 規範性 (Normative)。

根據上述技巧，題目有「收費」和「預期」，直接鎖定「為誰生產」和「實證性」，5秒內即可選出答案 D！

Based on the tricks above, the question has "toll" and "expect", directly locking in "for whom to produce" and "positive". You can choose D in 5 seconds!
