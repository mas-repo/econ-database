Topic: Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念)

Difficulty / 難度 ★★☆☆☆
Economic Goods (經濟物品) Scarcity (稀少性) Allocation (分配)

The Question / 題目

一件損壞的機器
A broken machine
A. 沒有使用價值。
has no use value.
B. 不是經濟物品。
is not an economic good.
C. 可按照價格來分配。
can be allocated by means of price.
D. 不能作進一步生產之用。
cannot be used for further production.

Concept Review / 概念重溫

1. 經濟物品 (Economic Good)

只要某物品的數量不足以滿足所有人的慾望，即存在稀少性 (Scarcity)，它就是經濟物品。
As long as the quantity of a good is insufficient to satisfy everyone's wants, scarcity exists, and it is an economic good.

定義 Definition:
在價格為零時，需求量大於供應量。
\( Q_d > Q_s \text{ at } P=0 \)

2. 分配與價格 (Allocation & Price)

因為存在稀少性，社會必須透過某種機制來分配物品。在市場經濟中，價格 (Price) 是最主要的分配準則。人們願意付出代價（價格）來獲取經濟物品。
Because scarcity exists, society must use a mechanism to allocate goods. In a market economy, price is the primary criterion for allocation. People are willing to pay a cost (price) to obtain economic goods.

Answer & Detailed Analysis / 答案與詳細分析

Correct Answer / 正確答案:
Option C
Option A - 錯誤 Incorrect

分析：「沒有使用價值」是不正確的。即使機器壞了，它可能仍有零件價值或廢鐵價值（Scrap value）。只要有人想要它（例如回收商），它就有使用價值。 Analysis: "Has no use value" is incorrect. Even if the machine is broken, it may still have value in parts or scrap value. As long as someone wants it (e.g., a recycler), it has use value.

Option B - 錯誤 Incorrect

分析：損壞的機器通常仍是「經濟物品」。為什麼？因為你不能在街上隨意免費拿到無限量的廢鐵。回收商願意付錢買廢鐵，這證明了它有市價，有稀少性。 Analysis: A broken machine is usually still an "economic good." Why? Because you cannot get unlimited scrap metal for free on the street. Recyclers are willing to pay for scrap, proving it has a market price and scarcity.

Option C - 正確 Correct

分析：既然損壞的機器（廢鐵）是經濟物品，它就具有稀少性。在市場中，我們透過「價格機制」來決定誰獲得這堆廢鐵（通常是出價最高的回收商）。因此，它可按價格分配。 Analysis: Since the broken machine (scrap metal) is an economic good, it possesses scarcity. In a market, we use the "price mechanism" to decide who gets this scrap (usually the highest-bidding recycler). Therefore, it can be allocated by price.

Option D - 錯誤 Incorrect

分析：「不能作進一步生產」是錯誤的。損壞的機器可以被當作「原材料」（廢鐵）投入生產過程，熔煉成新的鋼鐵產品。這屬於生產過程的一部分。 Analysis: "Cannot be used for further production" is incorrect. The broken machine can be used as "raw material" (scrap) in the production process, melted down into new steel products. This is part of the production process.

Problem Decomposition / 題目拆解

這類題目的核心在於測試你對「物品定義」的理解，而不是對機器功能的理解。
The core of this question tests your understanding of the "definition of goods", not the functionality of the machine.

  • 關鍵字 (Pivot Point): 「損壞 (Broken)」是一個干擾詞。在經濟學中，物品的狀態（好或壞）不決定它是否為經濟物品，稀少性才是關鍵。
    "Broken" is a distractor. In economics, the condition of the good does not determine if it's an economic good; scarcity is the key.
  • 邏輯鏈 (Logic Chain):
    機器壞了 \(\rightarrow\) 仍有廢鐵價值 \(\rightarrow\) 人們想要它且供應有限 \(\rightarrow\) 它是經濟物品 \(\rightarrow\) 有市價 \(\rightarrow\) 可按價格分配。

Student Q&A / 學生常見疑問

Q: 如果這台機器爛到完全沒人要（價格 = 0），答案還是 C 嗎？

If the machine is so damaged that absolutely no one wants it (Price = 0), is the answer still C?

A: 如果完全沒人要（甚至沒人願意免費拿走），它可能變成「免費物品」(Free Good) 甚至是「壞品」(Bad)。那時它就不是經濟物品，也不能透過正價格分配。但在考試中，除非題目特別說明「無人問津」，否則「機器」通常隱含金屬價值，即視為經濟物品。
If absolutely no one wants it (not even for free), it might become a "Free Good" or even a "Bad". In that case, it's not an economic good and can't be allocated via a positive price. However, in exams, unless specified as "unwanted", a "machine" implies metal value, treating it as an economic good.

Exam Tricks / 考試技巧

代入法 (Substitution Method):
看到「損壞的機器」，腦中直接替換成「一堆廢鐵 (A pile of scrap metal)」。
When you see "A broken machine", mentally substitute it with "A pile of scrap metal".

  • 廢鐵有沒有用？有 (Option A 錯)。
  • 廢鐵是不是經濟物品？是，賣廢鐵能賺錢 (Option B 錯)。
  • 廢鐵能不能生產？能，回收再造 (Option D 錯)。
  • 廢鐵有沒有價格？有，所以選 C。
