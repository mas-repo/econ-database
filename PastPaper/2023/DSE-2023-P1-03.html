Topic / 課題： Basic Economic Concepts: Property Rights & Resource Allocation (基本經濟概念：產權與資源分配)

Difficulty / 難度： ★★★☆☆

Key Concepts / 關鍵概念： Private Property Rights (私有產權), Basic Economic Problems (基本經濟問題), Non-price Competition (非價格競爭)

The Question (題目)

一支香港樂隊採用了實名登記制發售其演唱會門票。這些門票不可轉讓，所以炒賣的機會減少。

以下哪項／哪些有關上述實名登記制的陳述是正確的？

(1) 這是解決「生產什麼」的問題的一個機制。
(2) 在該機制下，門票的私有產權會被削弱。
(3) 在該機制下，門票的分配並不涉及非價格競爭。

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (2)
C. 只有 (3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)

A band in Hong Kong adopted a real-name registration system for the sale of its concert tickets. The tickets were not transferrable so that the chance of ticket scalping (炒賣) was reduced.

Which of the following statements about the above real-name registration system is/are correct?

(1) It is a mechanism to tackle the problem of ‘what to produce’.
(2) The private property rights over the tickets would be weakened under the system.
(3) Non-price competition would not be involved in the allocation of the tickets under the system.

A. (1) only
B. (2) only
C. (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 基本經濟問題 (Basic Economic Problems)

社會必須解決三個基本經濟問題：
- 生產什麼 (What to produce)：決定生產哪種物品及數量。
- 怎樣生產 (How to produce)：決定生產的方法（例如勞力密集或資本密集）。
- 為誰生產 (For whom to produce)：決定物品如何分配給消費者。

Society must solve three basic economic problems:
- What to produce: Deciding the types and quantities of goods to produce.
- How to produce: Deciding the method of production (e.g., labor-intensive vs. capital-intensive).
- For whom to produce: Deciding how goods are allocated among consumers.

2. 私有產權 (Private Property Rights)

完整的私有產權包括三項專有權利：使用權 (Right to use)、獲取收入權 (Right to derive income) 及 轉讓權 (Right to transfer)。若其中任何一項受到限制，私有產權便會被削弱。

Complete private property rights include three exclusive rights: the right to use, the right to derive income, and the right to transfer. If any of these is restricted, private property rights are weakened.

3. 非價格競爭 (Non-price Competition)

當物品的價格 \(P\) 低於均衡價格時，會出現短缺。除了以金錢競價外，人們會透過排隊（時間）、抽籤（運氣）等非價格競爭方式來獲取物品。

When the price \(P\) of a good is below the equilibrium price, a shortage occurs. Besides bidding with money, people will use non-price competition methods such as queuing (time) or lotteries (luck) to obtain the good.

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option / 正確答案： B

陳述 (1) - 錯誤 (False)

實名登記制決定了門票「分配給誰」（即願意實名登記及不打算轉售的人），這是解決「為誰生產 (For whom to produce)」的問題，而不是「生產什麼 (What to produce)」。

The real-name registration system determines "who gets the tickets" (those willing to register and not resell), which tackles the problem of "for whom to produce", not "what to produce".

陳述 (2) - 正確 (True)

題目指出門票「不可轉讓」。這直接剝奪了持有人的「轉讓權 (Right to transfer)」。因此，門票的私有產權被削弱了。

The question states the tickets are "not transferrable". This directly deprives the holder of the "right to transfer". Therefore, the private property rights over the tickets are weakened.

陳述 (3) - 錯誤 (False)

即使採用實名制並減少了炒賣，演唱會門票通常仍供不應求。買家仍需要透過「鬥快上網搶飛」或「排隊」等方式競爭，這些都屬於非價格競爭 (Non-price competition)。

Even with a real-name system and reduced scalping, concert tickets are usually still in short supply. Buyers still need to compete by "rushing to buy online fast" or "queuing", which are forms of non-price competition.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point)： 留意題目中的關鍵字「不可轉讓 (not transferrable)」。在經濟學中，看到「轉讓」一詞，必須立刻聯想到「私有產權 (Private Property Rights)」的三大元素。這直接確認了陳述(2)是正確的。

Pivot Point: Pay attention to the keyword "not transferrable". In economics, when you see the word "transfer", you must immediately link it to the three elements of "Private Property Rights". This directly confirms statement (2) is correct.

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：誤以為沒有炒賣 = 沒有競爭。
很多學生認為實名制消除了黃牛黨（價格競爭的一種體現），就等於沒有競爭。事實上，只要物品是稀少 (scarce) 的，競爭就必然存在。不能用錢買，人們就會用時間（排隊）去爭奪。

Trap: Mistaking "no scalping" for "no competition".
Many students think that eliminating scalpers means there is no competition. In fact, as long as a good is scarce, competition must exist. If people cannot compete with money, they will compete with time (queuing).

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

問：如果門票可以轉讓，但政府規定不能以高於原價售出，這會削弱私有產權嗎？
答：會的。這限制了持有人「獲取收入權 (Right to derive income)」，因為你無法透過資產獲取最大的市場回報，所以私有產權同樣被削弱。

Q: If tickets were transferrable, but the government ruled they cannot be sold above the original price, would this weaken private property rights?
A: Yes. This restricts the "right to derive income" because you cannot maximize the market return from your asset, so private property rights are still weakened.

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

消去法 (Elimination Method)：
一看到「實名制」決定誰能入場，就知道這是「為誰生產」，所以 (1) 錯。選項 A 和 D 立刻被淘汰。剩下 B (只有2) 和 C (只有3)。再看「不可轉讓」直接對應產權削弱，確認 (2) 正確，即可秒選 B，甚至不需要深究 (3)。

Elimination Method:
Seeing that the "real-name system" decides who gets in tells you it's "for whom to produce", so (1) is wrong. Options A and D are eliminated. You are left with B (2 only) and C (3 only). Seeing "not transferrable" directly corresponds to weakened property rights, confirming (2) is correct. You can immediately choose B without even overthinking (3).
