2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

Unanticipated Inflation (非預期通脹)

當實際通脹率高於預期通脹率時，就會出現非預期通脹。在這種情況下，貨幣的購買力下降幅度超出預期。

  • 得益者 (Gainers): 需要支付固定名義金額的人（如固定利率借款人、支付固定租金的租客）。因為他們還款時，貨幣的實質購買力已經下降。
  • 受害者 (Losers): 收取固定名義金額的人（如固定利率貸款人、收取固定退休金的長者）。因為他們收到的錢，實質購買力減少了。

Unanticipated inflation occurs when the actual inflation rate is higher than the expected inflation rate. In this case, the purchasing power of money falls more than expected.

  • Gainers: People who pay a fixed nominal amount (e.g., fixed-rate borrowers, tenants paying fixed rent). They pay back with money that has lower real purchasing power.
  • Losers: People who receive a fixed nominal amount (e.g., fixed-rate lenders, pensioners receiving fixed pensions). The money they receive has lower real purchasing power.
$$ \text{Real Interest Rate (實質利率)} = \text{Nominal Interest Rate (名義利率)} - \text{Inflation Rate (通脹率)} $$

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option C

A. 錯誤。 貸款人（Lender）收取固定利息。由於出現非預期通脹，他收回的本金和利息的實質購買力下降了，因此他是受害者。
A. Incorrect. A lender receives fixed interest. Due to unanticipated inflation, the real purchasing power of the principal and interest he receives falls, making him a loser.
B. 錯誤。 僱員的薪金按通脹率調整（Indexation），這意味著他的名義收入會隨物價上升而增加，其實質收入保持不變。因此他沒有得益也沒有受損。
B. Incorrect. The employee's salary is adjusted according to the inflation rate (indexation). This means his nominal income increases with the price level, keeping his real income unchanged. He neither gains nor loses.
C. 正確。 學生支付固定的名義學費。由於出現非預期通脹，貨幣貶值，他支付的學費的實質價值（購買力）下降了，因此他得益。
C. Correct. The student pays a fixed nominal tuition fee. Due to unanticipated inflation, the value of money falls, so the real value (purchasing power) of the tuition fee he pays decreases, making him a gainer.
D. 錯誤。 持有定期存款的長者相當於貸款人（借錢給銀行），收取固定利息。非預期通脹導致其存款及利息的實質購買力下降，因此他是受害者。
D. Incorrect. An elderly holding time deposits is essentially a lender (lending money to the bank) receiving fixed interest. Unanticipated inflation reduces the real purchasing power of his deposits and interest, making him a loser.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

第一步：判斷通脹情況。
CPI 由 99.9 升至 101.4，實際通脹率為正數。預期物價不變（預期通脹率 = 0%）。因此，實際通脹 > 預期通脹，出現非預期通脹。

第二步：尋找「支付固定金額」的人。
在非預期通脹下，支付固定金額的人會得益，因為他們付出的錢「不值錢」了。選項 C 的學生就是支付固定金額的人。

Step 1: Determine the inflation scenario.
CPI rose from 99.9 to 101.4, so actual inflation is positive. Expected inflation is 0%. Thus, Actual Inflation > Expected Inflation, resulting in unanticipated inflation.

Step 2: Look for the person "paying a fixed amount".
Under unanticipated inflation, those who pay a fixed amount gain because the money they pay is "worth less". The student in Option C fits this description.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱： 忘記區分「貸款人 (Lender)」和「借款人 (Borrower)」。
很多學生看到「固定利率」就以為會得益。記住：借款人 (Borrower) 得益，貸款人 (Lender) 受損。

Trap: Confusing "Lender" and "Borrower".
Many students see "fixed interest rate" and assume a gain. Remember: Borrowers gain, Lenders lose.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 如果題目說「人們完全預期到物價的轉變」，答案會不同嗎？ (What if the question said "people fully expected the change in price level"?)
A: 如果完全預期到，名義利率和合約通常已經包含了通脹溢價（Inflation Premium）。在這種情況下，沒有人會因為通脹而在財富重分配中得益或受損（除非合約無法更改）。
A: If fully expected, nominal interest rates and contracts would typically have already included an inflation premium. In that case, no one would gain or lose in terms of wealth redistribution due to inflation (unless contracts are rigid).
