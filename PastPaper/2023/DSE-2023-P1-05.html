HKDSE Economics: Classification of Goods (物品分類)

Topic: Microeconomics (微觀經濟學) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Economic Goods, Public Goods, Producer Goods

The Question (題目)

下列哪項有關物品分類的陳述是正確的？

Which of the following statements about the classification of goods is correct?

  1. 街頭藝人所用的樂器是共用品，因為用這些樂器演奏的音樂可被多人同時享用。
    Musical instruments used by street performers are public goods because the music performed with these instruments can be enjoyed concurrently by many people.
  2. 課本是私用品，因為生產多一個單位的課本的邊際成本是正數。
    Textbooks are private goods because the marginal cost of producing an additional unit of textbooks is positive.
  3. 香港故宮文化博物館展出的藝術品是消費者物品，因為它們的生產涉及成本。
    Artworks exhibited in the Hong Kong Palace Museum are consumer goods because costs are involved in their production.
  4. 受歡迎的免費網上英語學習影片是經濟物品，因為它們的數量不足夠滿足人們的慾望。
    Popular free online videos for learning English are economic goods because their quantities are not enough to satisfy human wants.

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 經濟物品 vs 免費物品 (Economic Goods vs Free Goods)

經濟物品 (Economic Goods): 其數量不足以滿足人類所有的慾望。換言之，在價格為零時，需求量大於供應量 \( (Q_d > Q_s) \)。生產或獲取這類物品涉及機會成本。
Their quantity is insufficient to satisfy all human wants. In other words, at zero price, quantity demanded exceeds quantity supplied. Production or acquisition involves opportunity cost.

2. 私用品 vs 共用品 (Private Goods vs Public Goods)

私用品 (Private Goods): 具有「競爭性」(Rivalry)，即一個人消費會減少其他人可消費的數量。
共用品 (Public Goods): 具有「非競爭性」(Non-rivalry)，即多人可同時消費而不會減少其他人的消費量。
Private goods are rivalrous in consumption (one person's consumption reduces availability for others). Public goods are non-rivalrous (can be consumed concurrently without reducing availability).

3. 消費者物品 vs 生產者物品 (Consumer Goods vs Producer Goods)

消費者物品 (Consumer Goods): 直接滿足消費者慾望的物品。
生產者物品 / 資本物品 (Producer / Capital Goods): 用作生產其他物品或服務的物品。
Consumer goods satisfy human wants directly. Producer goods are used to produce other goods and services.

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

Option A - Incorrect

分析： 雖然音樂本身可能具有非競爭性（多人可同時聆聽），但「樂器」本身是私用品。一件樂器在同一時間只能由一個人（或一組特定的人）彈奏，具有競爭性。
Analysis: While the *music* might be non-rivalrous (many can listen concurrently), the *musical instrument* itself is a private good. An instrument can only be played by one person at a time, making it rivalrous in consumption.

Option B - Incorrect

分析： 生產的邊際成本為正數 \( (MC > 0) \) 是「經濟物品」(Economic Goods) 的特徵，而不是「私用品」(Private Goods) 的定義。私用品的定義是基於消費時的競爭性。
Analysis: Having a positive marginal cost \( (MC > 0) \) is a characteristic of an *economic good*, not the definition of a *private good*. Private goods are defined by rivalry in consumption.

Option C - Incorrect

分析： 第一，生產涉及成本是「經濟物品」的特徵，不能用來證明它是消費者物品。第二，博物館內的藝術品是被館方用來提供「展覽服務」的，因此它們屬於「生產者物品」(Producer Goods) 或資本物品。
Analysis: First, involving production costs proves it is an *economic good*, not a consumer good. Second, artworks in a museum are used by the museum to provide "exhibition services", making them *producer goods* (or capital goods).

Option D - Correct

分析： 這是經濟物品的標準定義。即使影片在金錢上是「免費」的，但只要它們的數量不足以滿足所有人的慾望（即具有稀少性 Scarcity），它們就是經濟物品。
Analysis: This is the standard definition of an economic good. Even if the videos are "free" in monetary terms, as long as their quantity is insufficient to satisfy all human wants (i.e., they are scarce), they are economic goods.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的關鍵樞紐 (Pivot Point) 在於「因為 (because)」後面的解釋是否與前面的物品分類定義精準匹配。
The Pivot Point of this type of question is whether the explanation after "because" exactly matches the definition of the classification mentioned.

  • 選項 B 和 C 的前半部分可能看似合理，但後半部的解釋（MC為正數、涉及成本）全部都是指向「經濟物品」，造成邏輯錯配 (Logical mismatch)。
  • 選項 D 加入了「免費 (free)」這個干擾詞 (Distractor)，試圖誘導學生以為免費的東西就是免費物品 (Free goods)。但只要看清後面的定義（數量不足以滿足慾望），就能確認它是經濟物品。

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：將「零金錢價格」與「免費物品」混淆
學生常誤以為不用付錢 (Price = $0) 的就是 Free Good。記住，只要生產該物品需要放棄其他選項（有機會成本），或者大家想要更多，它就是 Economic Good。網上影片需要時間、電力和伺服器空間來製作和儲存，因此絕對是經濟物品。
Trap: Confusing "Zero Monetary Price" with "Free Goods". Students often think that if something costs $0, it's a Free Good. Remember, if producing it involves giving up alternatives (opportunity cost), it's an Economic Good. Online videos require time, electricity, and server space, so they are definitely economic goods.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼博物館的藝術品不是消費者物品？遊客看藝術品不是直接獲得滿足嗎？
Why aren't the museum's artworks consumer goods? Don't visitors get direct satisfaction from looking at them?

A: 遊客直接消費的是博物館提供的「展覽服務」(Exhibition service)。藝術品是博物館用來「生產」這項服務的工具或資本，因此對博物館而言，藝術品是生產者物品 (Producer goods)。
Visitors directly consume the "exhibition service" provided by the museum. The artworks are the tools or capital used by the museum to "produce" this service. Therefore, to the museum, the artworks are producer goods.

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

「配對法」速解 (Matching Shortcut):
做這類題目時，直接看「因為 (because)」後面的字眼，然後在腦海中浮現對應的術語：

  • 看到 "concurrently enjoyed" \(\rightarrow\) 聯想 Public Good
  • 看到 "marginal cost is positive" \(\rightarrow\) 聯想 Economic Good
  • 看到 "costs are involved" \(\rightarrow\) 聯想 Economic Good
  • 看到 "not enough to satisfy wants" \(\rightarrow\) 聯想 Economic Good

然後檢查前半部是否與你的聯想一致。選項 B 和 C 的前半部都不是 Economic Good，直接淘汰！選項 D 前後完美吻合，一秒選出答案。
Check if the first half matches your association. Options B and C do not match "Economic Good" in their first half, eliminate them immediately! Option D matches perfectly.
