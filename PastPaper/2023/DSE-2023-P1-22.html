HKDSE Economics: Question Analysis

Topic: 市場失效與界外影響 (Market Failure & Externalities) Difficulty: ★★★★☆ Key Concepts: 負面界外影響, 定額稅, 替代品

1. 題目 (The Question)

塑膠飲管是塑膠廢料的主要源頭。大部分塑膠飲管會分解成較細小的粒子，釋出危害環境的化學物質。根據以上資料，下列哪項陳述是正確的？
Plastic straws are a major source of plastic waste. Most plastic straws would break into smaller particles releasing chemicals which threaten the environment. Based on the above information, which of the following statements is correct?
  • A. 塑膠飲管的使用引致負面界外影響，而塑膠飲管的使用量低於效率水平。
    The use of plastic straws results in negative externality, and the quantity of plastic straws used is below the efficient level.
  • B. 為達至總社會盈餘極大化，各地政府應全面禁止使用塑膠飲管。
    To maximise total social surplus, governments around the world should completely ban the use of plastic straws.
  • C. 對提供塑膠飲管予顧客的業務徵收一項定額稅，必然可以回復塑膠飲管市場的效率。
    Charging a lump-sum tax on the businesses that provide plastic straws to customers must restore efficiency in the market of plastic straws.
  • D. 向環保紙飲管生產者提供從量補貼，傾向降低塑膠飲管市場的效率損失。
    Granting a per-unit subsidy to the producers of environmentally-friendly paper straws tends to reduce the deadweight loss in the market of plastic straws.

2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

負面界外影響 (Negative Externality): 當生產或消費活動對第三方造成未被補償的成本時，就會出現負面界外影響。此時，邊際社會成本大於邊際私人成本。
When a production or consumption activity imposes uncompensated costs on third parties, a negative externality exists. In this case, Marginal Social Cost is greater than Marginal Private Cost.
$$ MSC = MPC + MEC $$
效率水平 (Efficient Level): 資源分配達到最優的狀態，即邊際社會利益等於邊際社會成本 \(MSB = MSC\)。在負面界外影響下，市場的均衡產量會高於效率水平（過度生產/消費）。
The optimal allocation of resources where Marginal Social Benefit equals Marginal Social Cost \(MSB = MSC\). Under negative externalities, the market equilibrium quantity is above the efficient level (overproduction/overconsumption).

3. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

選項 A (錯誤): 塑膠飲管確實引致負面界外影響，但這會導致市場價格未能反映全部社會成本，結果是市場使用量高於（而非低於）效率水平。
Option A (Incorrect): Plastic straws do cause a negative externality, but this leads to a market price that fails to reflect the full social cost. As a result, the market quantity used is above (not below) the efficient level.
選項 B (錯誤): 總社會盈餘極大化的條件是 \(MSB = MSC\)。全面禁止意味著產量為零，但最初幾單位的塑膠飲管可能帶來的邊際利益極大，大於其邊際社會成本。因此，最佳污染量通常不是零。
Option B (Incorrect): Total social surplus is maximized when \(MSB = MSC\). A complete ban means quantity is zero, but the marginal benefit of the first few units of straws might be very high, exceeding their marginal social cost. Thus, the optimal level of pollution is rarely zero.
選項 C (錯誤): 定額稅 (Lump-sum tax) 屬於固定成本，不會改變邊際成本 (Marginal Cost)。由於企業的利潤極大化條件是 \(MR = MC\)，定額稅不會改變企業的產量決定，因此無法糾正資源錯配。
Option C (Incorrect): A lump-sum tax is a fixed cost and does not change Marginal Cost (MC). Since a firm maximizes profit where \(MR = MC\), a lump-sum tax will not change the firm's output decision and thus cannot correct the misallocation of resources.
選項 D (正確): 環保紙飲管是塑膠飲管的替代品 (Substitute)。向紙飲管提供從量補貼會降低其價格，導致消費者轉用較便宜的紙飲管，從而減少對塑膠飲管的需求。塑膠飲管的產量下降，會拉近市場產量與效率水平的距離，從而降低無效率損失 (Deadweight Loss)。
Option D (Correct): Eco-friendly paper straws are substitutes for plastic straws. Granting a per-unit subsidy to paper straws lowers their price, causing consumers to switch to them, which decreases the demand for plastic straws. The drop in plastic straw quantity moves it closer to the efficient level, thereby reducing the deadweight loss.

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解題關鍵 (Pivot Point): 本題測試考生對政府干預手段的理解。關鍵在於分辨「定額稅」與「從量稅/補貼」的分別，以及明白跨市場（替代品）的影響。

This question tests students' understanding of government interventions. The pivot point is distinguishing between a "lump-sum tax" and a "per-unit tax/subsidy", and understanding cross-market (substitute) effects.

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

  • 誤解定額稅的作用: 很多學生看到「稅」就以為能減少產量。記住：只有從量稅 (Per-unit tax) 才會改變邊際成本 \(MC\) 並減少產量；定額稅只影響利潤，不影響產量。
  • Misunderstanding lump-sum taxes: Many students see "tax" and assume it reduces output. Remember: only a per-unit tax changes Marginal Cost \(MC\) and reduces output; a lump-sum tax only affects profit, not output.
  • 極端字眼陷阱: 選項 B 的「全面禁止 (completely ban)」通常是陷阱。經濟學強調邊際分析，極少情況下最優解是完全沒有該物品。
  • Extreme wording trap: "Completely ban" in Option B is usually a trap. Economics emphasizes marginal analysis; rarely is the optimal solution zero quantity.

6. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 為什麼補貼紙飲管不是直接解決塑膠飲管的界外影響？

Q: Why isn't subsidizing paper straws a direct solution to the externality of plastic straws?

A: 它確實是間接的方法。直接的方法是對塑膠飲管徵收從量稅（庇古稅）。但補貼替代品（紙飲管）能有效減少塑膠飲管的需求，這同樣能將塑膠飲管的產量推向社會最優水平，因此選項 D 說「傾向降低 (tends to reduce)」是正確且嚴謹的表達。

A: It is indeed an indirect method. The direct method is a per-unit tax (Pigouvian tax) on plastic straws. However, subsidizing a substitute (paper straws) effectively reduces the demand for plastic straws, which also pushes the quantity of plastic straws towards the social optimum. Hence, Option D's phrasing "tends to reduce" is accurate and rigorous.

7. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

刪除法 (Elimination Method):

  • 看到「負面界外影響」，立刻聯想「產量過多 (Overproduction)」，秒殺 A。
  • 看到「全面禁止 (completely ban)」，通常違背邊際等值原則 \(MSB=MSC\)，秒殺 B。
  • 看到「定額稅 (Lump-sum tax)」配「回復效率」，立刻判斷無效，秒殺 C。
  • 只剩下 D，檢查邏輯：補貼替代品 -> 減少原物品需求 -> 減少過度消費 -> 減少無效率損失。邏輯正確！

Shortcut: Negative externality means overproduction (eliminates A). "Completely ban" violates marginal principles (eliminates B). "Lump-sum tax" doesn't change output or efficiency (eliminates C). D is the only logical choice left.
