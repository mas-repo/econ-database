Topic (課題): Methodology in Economics / Positive vs. Normative Statements (經濟學方法論 / 實證與規範性陳述)

Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

Key Concepts (核心概念): Normative Statement (規範性陳述) Positive Statement (實證性陳述) Value Judgment (價值觀判斷)

2. The Question (題目)

以下哪項／哪些是規範性陳述？

(1) 推行消費券計劃會導致通脹。
(2) 宣傳旅遊業是刺激經濟增長的最適切的途徑。
(3) 政府應繼續吸引內地企業來港投資。

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (3)
C. 只有 (1)、(2)
D. 只有 (2)、(3)

Which of the following is a normative statement/ are normative statements?

(1) Launching consumption voucher schemes will lead to inflation.
(2) Promoting tourism is the most appropriate way to stimulate economic growth.
(3) The government should continue to attract Mainland enterprises to invest in Hong Kong.

A. (1) only
B. (3) only
C. (1) and (2) only
D. (2) and (3) only

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

實證性陳述 (Positive Statements)

實證性陳述是關於「是什麼 (what is)」的陳述。它們描述經濟變量之間的因果關係，缺乏價值觀判斷。最重要的是，實證性陳述可以被事實或數據推翻 (refutable by facts)。即使一個陳述是錯誤的，只要它能被測試，它仍然是實證性陳述。

Positive statements are claims about "what is". They describe causal relationships between economic variables and are free from value judgments. Most importantly, positive statements are refutable by facts. Even if a statement is factually incorrect, as long as it can be tested, it remains a positive statement.

例如，我們可以建立一個實證的市場需求模型：

For example, we can build a positive market demand model:

$$Q_d = \alpha - \beta P$$

這表示當價格 $P$ 上升時，需求量 $Q_d$ 會下降。這是一個可以被現實數據測試的陳述。

This indicates that when price $P$ increases, quantity demanded $Q_d$ decreases. This is a statement that can be tested using real-world data.

規範性陳述 (Normative Statements)

規範性陳述是關於「應該是什麼 (what ought to be)」的陳述。它們涉及個人的價值觀判斷 (value judgments)，例如公平、好壞或適當與否。由於它們基於主觀意見，因此不能被事實證明或推翻 (cannot be proved or refuted by facts)。

Normative statements are claims about "what ought to be". They involve personal value judgments, such as fairness, goodness, or appropriateness. Because they are based on subjective opinions, they cannot be proved or refuted by facts.

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 / Correct Option: D

選項分析 / Step-by-Step Analysis:

陳述 (1): 推行消費券計劃會導致通脹。 / Launching consumption voucher schemes will lead to inflation.
這是一個實證性陳述。它提出了一個可測試的因果關係（消費券 $\rightarrow$ 通脹）。我們可以收集經濟數據來驗證這個說法是否正確，當中不涉及個人喜好。因此，這不是規範性陳述。

This is a positive statement. It proposes a testable causal relationship (vouchers $\rightarrow$ inflation). We can collect economic data to verify whether this claim is true or false, and it does not involve personal preferences. Therefore, it is not a normative statement.

陳述 (2): 宣傳旅遊業是刺激經濟增長的最適切的途徑。 / Promoting tourism is the most appropriate way to stimulate economic growth.
這是一個規範性陳述。句子中的「最適切 (most appropriate)」涉及強烈的價值觀判斷。對於什麼才是「最適切」，不同人有不同的標準（有人可能認為發展科技更適切），這無法純粹用數據來證明對錯。

This is a normative statement. The phrase "most appropriate" involves a strong value judgment. Different people have different standards for what is "appropriate" (some might think developing technology is more appropriate), and this cannot be proven right or wrong purely by data.

陳述 (3): 政府應繼續吸引內地企業來港投資。 / The government should continue to attract Mainland enterprises to invest in Hong Kong.
這是一個規範性陳述。句子中的「應 (should)」表明了說話者的主觀立場或建議。無論這建議有多合理，它始終建基於價值判斷，無法被客觀事實推翻。

This is a normative statement. The word "should" indicates the speaker's subjective stance or recommendation. No matter how reasonable the suggestion is, it is ultimately based on a value judgment and cannot be refuted by objective facts.

總結： 由於 (2) 和 (3) 都是規範性陳述，正確答案為 D。

Conclusion: Since both (2) and (3) are normative statements, the correct answer is D.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point):
這類題目的核心在於尋找「價值觀字眼」。解題時，你不需理會陳述中的經濟學理論是否正確，只需問自己一個問題：「我能用數據證明這個句子錯嗎？」如果不能，那就是規範性陳述。

The core of this type of question lies in looking for "value-laden words". When solving, you don't need to care whether the economic theory in the statement is correct. Just ask yourself one question: "Can I use data to prove this sentence wrong?" If no, it is a normative statement.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：將「預測未來」誤當作規範性陳述
很多同學看到陳述 (1) 有「會導致 (will lead to)」這類預測未來的字眼，就以為這不是既定事實，所以是規範性。這是錯誤的！未來的預測依然是可被驗證的（只要時間到了，我們就能測量是否有通脹），所以它是實證性陳述。

Trap 1: Mistaking "future predictions" for normative statements.
Many students see future tense words like "will lead to" in statement (1) and assume it's normative because it's not a settled fact yet. This is incorrect! Future predictions are still testable (once the time comes, we can measure if inflation occurred), so they are positive statements.


陷阱 2：以為「錯誤」的句子就是規範性陳述
例如：「香港的失業率目前是 100%」。這句話顯然是錯的，但它仍然是實證性陳述，因為我們可以拿真實的勞動人口數據來推翻它。

Trap 2: Believing that "false" sentences are normative statements.
For example: "The unemployment rate in Hong Kong is currently 100%." This statement is obviously wrong, but it is still a positive statement because we can use actual labor force data to refute it.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼陳述 (2) 中提到了「刺激經濟增長」這個客觀的經濟結果，它依然是規範性陳述？

A: 雖然「經濟增長」可以被量度（實證），但句子的重點在於「最適切 (most appropriate)」。只要句子中包含了一個無法量度的主觀評價，整個陳述就會變成規範性。如果句子改為「宣傳旅遊業『會』刺激經濟增長」，那它就會變成實證性陳述。

Q: Why is statement (2) still normative when it mentions an objective economic outcome like "stimulate economic growth"?

A: Although "economic growth" can be measured (positive), the focus of the sentence is on the phrase "most appropriate". As long as a sentence contains an immeasurable subjective evaluation, the entire statement becomes normative. If the sentence were changed to "Promoting tourism 'will' stimulate economic growth", it would become a positive statement.

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

關鍵字掃描法 (Keyword Scanning):
在 DSE 考試中，如果你看到以下字眼，該陳述有 99% 的機率是規範性陳述 (Normative)：

  • 應該 (should / ought to)
  • 最好 / 最適切 (best / most appropriate)
  • 公平 / 不公平 (fair / unfair)
  • 太高 / 太低 (too high / too low) - 例如：「樓價太高」，多少才算「太高」是主觀的。
相反，如果看到「會 (will)」、「導致 (cause)」、「上升/下降 (increase/decrease)」，通常是實證性陳述。

Keyword Scanning:
In the DSE exam, if you spot the following words, there is a 99% chance the statement is Normative:

  • 應該 (should / ought to)
  • 最好 / 最適切 (best / most appropriate)
  • 公平 / 不公平 (fair / unfair)
  • 太高 / 太低 (too high / too low) - e.g., "Property prices are too high". What constitutes "too high" is subjective.
Conversely, words like "will", "cause", or "increase/decrease" usually indicate positive statements.
