HKDSE Economics Tutor Guide

Topic (課題): Opportunity Cost & Sunk Cost (機會成本與沉沒成本)
Difficulty (難度): ★★★☆☆
Key Concepts (關鍵概念): Opportunity Cost, Sunk Cost, Explicit Cost, Implicit Cost

2. The Question (題目)

中文：盧先生三年前用 \(\$60,000\) 買入一輛二手車。假設他能以 \(\$2,000\) 將該車轉售。或者，他需要花 \(\$5,000\) 維修該車，才可以繼續使用它。他繼續使用該車的成本是多少？

A. \(\$2,000\)
B. \(\$5,000\)
C. \(\$7,000\)
D. \(\$60,000\)

English: Mr Lo spent \(\$60,000\) on buying a second hand car three years ago. Suppose he can resell the car at \(\$2,000\). Alternatively, he needs to spend \(\$5,000\) on repairing the car before he can continue to use it. What is his cost of continuing to use the car?

A. \(\$2,000\)
B. \(\$5,000\)
C. \(\$7,000\)
D. \(\$60,000\)

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

機會成本 (Opportunity Cost): 放棄了的最高價值選擇。在計算成本時，我們必須考慮所有因為作出某個選擇而放棄的代價。

The highest-valued option forgone. When calculating cost, we must consider all sacrifices made due to making a choice.

沉沒成本 (Sunk Cost): 已經付出且無法收回的歷史成本。沉沒成本不屬於機會成本。

Past costs that have already been incurred and cannot be recovered. Sunk costs are NOT part of opportunity costs.

$$ \text{Total Cost} = \text{Explicit Cost (顯性成本)} + \text{Implicit Cost (隱性成本)} $$

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option C

Option A (\(\$2,000\)): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

這只是隱性成本（放棄了的轉售收入）。它忽略了為了繼續使用汽車而必須支付的維修費用。

This is only the implicit cost (the forgone resale revenue). It ignores the repair fee that must be paid to continue using the car.

Option B (\(\$5,000\)): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

這只是顯性成本（實際支付的維修費）。它忽略了因為沒有賣掉汽車而損失的轉售收入。

This is only the explicit cost (the actual repair fee paid). It ignores the forgone resale revenue from not selling the car.

Option C (\(\$7,000\)): 正確 (Correct)

繼續使用汽車的總成本 = 放棄的轉售價值（隱性成本 \(\$2,000\)）+ 必須支付的維修費（顯性成本 \(\$5,000\)）= \(\$7,000\)。

Total cost of continuing to use the car = Forgone resale value (Implicit cost \(\$2,000\)) + Required repair fee (Explicit cost \(\$5,000\)) = \(\$7,000\).

Option D (\(\$60,000\)): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

這是三年前的買入價，屬於無法收回的沉沒成本（Sunk Cost），不應計算在未來的決策成本中。

This is the purchase price from three years ago. It is an unrecoverable sunk cost and should not be included in future decision-making costs.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

尋找「樞紐點」(Pivot Point): 題目的核心動作是「繼續使用該車」。我們需要問自己：「為了達成這個動作，盧先生付出了什麼代價？」

Finding the Pivot Point: The core action is "continuing to use the car". We need to ask: "What sacrifices must Mr. Lo make to achieve this action?"

  • 代價 1: 他不能賣掉車子，所以放棄了 \(\$2,000\)。(He can't sell it, so he gives up \(\$2,000\).)
  • 代價 2: 他必須從口袋裡拿出 \(\$5,000\) 去維修。(He must take \(\$5,000\) out of his pocket to repair it.)

干擾資訊 (Distractor): 「三年前用 \(\$60,000\) 買入」是陷阱。歷史價格對現在的決策毫無影響。

Distractor: "Spent \(\$60,000\) three years ago" is a trap. Historical prices have no impact on current decisions.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

學生最常犯的錯誤是只計算金錢支出（顯性成本）而忽略了放棄的收入（隱性成本）。在經濟學中，成本必須包含兩者。另一個常見錯誤是不捨得過去的付出，錯誤地將沉沒成本（\(\$60,000\)）加入計算。

The most common mistake students make is only calculating monetary expenses (explicit costs) while ignoring forgone income (implicit costs). In Economics, cost must include both. Another common error is being emotionally attached to past expenses, incorrectly adding the sunk cost (\(\$60,000\)) into the calculation.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼 \(\$60,000\) 完全不計算在內？ (Why is the \(\$60,000\) completely excluded?)

A: 因為無論盧先生選擇「繼續使用」還是「賣掉」汽車，這 \(\$60,000\) 都已經花掉了，無法改變。經濟學的成本只看「因為這個選擇而額外增加的代價」。

A: Because whether Mr. Lo chooses to "continue using" or "sell" the car, this \(\$60,000\) has already been spent and cannot be changed. Economic cost only looks at the "additional sacrifices made because of this specific choice."

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

速解法 (Shortcut): 在DSE考試中，當你看到「X年前買入」、「歷史價格」或「不可退還的按金」等字眼，立刻用鉛筆將該數字劃掉！這能瞬間幫你排除包含該數字的選項（如 Option D）。然後，將剩下的「放棄收入」和「額外支出」相加即可。

Shortcut: In the DSE exam, when you see phrases like "bought X years ago", "historical price", or "non-refundable deposit", immediately cross out that number with your pencil! This instantly helps you eliminate options containing that number (like Option D). Then, simply add up the remaining "forgone income" and "extra expenses".
