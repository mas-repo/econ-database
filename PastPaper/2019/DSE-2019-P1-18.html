Topic (課題): Market Structure 市場結構 Perfect Competition 完全競爭

Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

Key Concepts (核心概念): Price Taker 受價者 Profit Maximization 利潤極大化 Free Entry 自由進出

1. The Question (題目)

下列哪些有關完全競爭市場的陳述是正確的？
(1) 若廠商收取的價格高於市場價格，便不能生存。
(2) 進入市場並沒有限制。
(3) 廠商利潤極大化的條件為價格等於邊際成本。
Which of the following statements about a perfectly competitive market are correct?
(1) Firms cannot survive if they ask a price higher than the market price.
(2) There is no entry barrier to the market.
(3) The profit-maximising condition of the firms is that price equals marginal cost.
A. 只有 (1)、(2) / (1) and (2) only
B. 只有 (1)、(3) / (1) and (3) only
C. 只有 (2)、(3) / (2) and (3) only
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3) / (1), (2) and (3)

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

完全競爭市場的特徵 (Features of Perfect Competition):

  • 同質產品 (Homogeneous products): 所有廠商生產的產品完全相同。消費者不介意向誰購買。
    All firms produce identical products. Consumers have no preference for any specific firm.
  • 完全資訊 (Perfect information): 買賣雙方對市場價格和產品質素瞭如指掌。
    Buyers and sellers have full knowledge of market prices and product quality.
  • 自由進出 (Free entry and exit): 市場沒有進入壁壘，廠商可以自由加入或退出。
    There are no barriers to entry or exit. Firms can freely join or leave the market.
  • 受價者 (Price takers): 由於有大量買家和賣家，沒有單一廠商能影響價格。廠商面對的需曲線是完全彈性的 (水平線)。
    With many buyers and sellers, no single firm can influence the price. The demand curve facing a firm is perfectly elastic (horizontal).

利潤極大化條件 (Profit Maximization Condition):

所有廠商（無論任何市場結構）的利潤極大化普遍條件是邊際收益等於邊際成本：

The universal condition for profit maximization for ANY firm is Marginal Revenue equals Marginal Cost:

$$MR = MC$$

但在完全競爭市場中，由於廠商是受價者，價格 \(P\) 是一個常數，等於平均收益 \(AR\) 和邊際收益 \(MR\)。因此，條件可以簡化為：

However, in a perfectly competitive market, since firms are price takers, the price \(P\) is constant and equals Average Revenue \(AR\) and Marginal Revenue \(MR\). Thus, the condition simplifies to:

$$P = MR = AR \implies P = MC$$

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

陳述 (1): 正確 (Correct)

由於市場存在完全資訊且產品同質，如果一家廠商收取的價格 \(P\) 高於市場價格，所有顧客都會轉向其他收取市場價格的廠商購買。該廠商的銷售量將跌至零，無法生存。

Because there is perfect information and products are homogeneous, if a firm charges a price \(P\) higher than the market price, all customers will buy from other firms charging the market price. The firm's sales will drop to zero, and it cannot survive.

陳述 (2): 正確 (Correct)

「自由進出市場」是完全競爭市場的定義特徵之一。市場上沒有專利、牌照限制或龐大的初始資本要求等進入壁壘。

"Free entry and exit" is a defining characteristic of a perfectly competitive market. There are no entry barriers such as patents, licensing restrictions, or massive initial capital requirements.

陳述 (3): 正確 (Correct)

如概念重溫所述，一般利潤極大化條件為 \(MR = MC\)。在完全競爭下，廠商是受價者，價格等於邊際收益 (\(P = MR\))。因此，利潤極大化條件可寫成 \(P = MC\)。

As explained in the concept review, the general profit-maximizing condition is \(MR = MC\). Under perfect competition, firms are price takers, meaning price equals marginal revenue (\(P = MR\)). Therefore, the condition is \(P = MC\).

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：混淆 \(P = MC\) 與 \(MR = MC\)

很多學生誤以為 \(P = MC\) 適用於所有市場結構。這是不正確的！\(MR = MC\) 才是所有市場的通用法則。只有在完全競爭市場中，因為 \(P = MR\)，我們才可以說 \(P = MC\) 是利潤極大化的條件。在壟斷市場 (Monopoly) 中，\(P > MR\)，所以利潤極大化時 \(P > MC\)。

Trap: Confusing \(P = MC\) with \(MR = MC\)
Many students mistakenly believe \(P = MC\) applies to all market structures. This is incorrect! \(MR = MC\) is the universal rule. Only in perfect competition, because \(P = MR\), can we say \(P = MC\) is the profit-maximizing condition. In a monopoly, \(P > MR\), so at profit maximization, \(P > MC\).

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

問 (Q)：既然廠商不能加價，那麼它可以減價來搶佔所有市場份額嗎？

Q: Since a firm cannot raise its price, can it lower its price to capture the entire market share?

答 (A)： 理論上可以，但沒有必要。因為在完全競爭市場中，個別廠商的產量相對於整個市場來說微不足道。廠商在現有的「市場價格」下，已經可以售出它想售出的所有數量（需求曲線是完全彈性的）。減價只會無謂地減少它的利潤。

A: Theoretically yes, but it is unnecessary. In a perfectly competitive market, an individual firm's output is insignificant compared to the whole market. The firm can already sell all the output it wants at the prevailing market price (the demand curve is perfectly elastic). Lowering the price would only unnecessarily reduce its profit.
