📊 Topic: Market Intervention & Elasticity (市場干預與彈性)

Difficulty (難度): ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Key Concepts (核心概念): Tariff (關稅), Tax Burden (稅務負擔), Price Elasticity of Demand (需求價格彈性), Total Revenue (總收入)

📝 The Question (題目)

參閱以下有關甲國對進口大豆的市場需求。

Refer to the following market demand for imported soybeans in Country A.

價格 Price ($) 10 11 12 13 14
需求量 Quantity demanded (units) 700 600 500 400 300

當甲國政府對進口大豆徵收$3從量關稅，進口大豆的均衡價格由$11上升至$13。
下列哪些陳述是正確的？

When the government of Country A imposes a per-unit tariff of $3 on imported soybeans, the equilibrium price of the imported soybeans increases from $11 to $13.
Which of the following statements are correct?

(1) 進口大豆的總銷售收入（扣除關稅後）會是$4 000。
(2) 進口大豆的供應比起其需求更具彈性。
(3) 在原初和新的均衡價格範圍內，進口大豆的需求彈性小於一。

(1) The total sales revenue (net-of-tariff) of the imported soybeans is $4 000.
(2) The supply of the imported soybeans is more elastic than the demand for them.
(3) Within the range of the original and the new equilibrium prices, the elasticity of demand of the imported soybeans is smaller than one.

A. 只有 (1)、(2) / (1) and (2) only
B. 只有 (1)、(3) / (1) and (3) only
C. 只有 (2)、(3) / (2) and (3) only
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3) / (1), (2) and (3)

🧠 Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 稅務負擔 (Tax Burden): 當政府徵收從量稅 \(t\) 時，稅款由買賣雙方共同承擔。買家承擔的稅額是價格的升幅 \(\Delta P\)，賣家承擔的稅額是 \(t - \Delta P\)。

1. Tax Burden: When a per-unit tax \(t\) is imposed, the burden is shared. Consumers bear the price increase \(\Delta P\), and sellers bear the rest \(t - \Delta P\).

2. 彈性與稅務負擔的關係 (Elasticity and Tax Burden): 彈性較低（較缺乏彈性）的一方，需要承擔較大比例的稅務負擔。「越無彈性，負擔越大」。

2. Elasticity and Tax Burden: The side of the market that is more inelastic bears a larger share of the tax burden. "Less elastic = More burden".

3. 總收入與需求彈性 (Total Revenue and Demand Elasticity): 總收入 \(TR = P \times Q\)。如果價格上升導致總收入下降，這意味著需求量下降的百分比大於價格上升的百分比，即需求屬富有彈性 (\(E_d > 1\))。

3. Total Revenue and Demand Elasticity: \(TR = P \times Q\). If an increase in price leads to a decrease in total revenue, demand is elastic (\(E_d > 1\)).

✅ The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: A

Statement (1) - Correct (正確)

新的均衡價格是 \(P = \$13\)，對應的需求量是 \(Q = 400\)。扣除關稅後的總銷售收入，是指賣家實際收到的錢。賣家每單位收到 \(\$13 - \$3 = \$10\)。因此，淨收入 = \(\$10 \times 400 = \$4000\)。

The new equilibrium price is \(P = \$13\), and the new quantity is \(Q = 400\). Net-of-tariff revenue is what sellers actually keep. Sellers receive \(\$13 - \$3 = \$10\) per unit. Net revenue = \(\$10 \times 400 = \$4000\).

Statement (2) - Correct (正確)

關稅為 \(\$3\)。價格由 \(\$11\) 升至 \(\$13\)，買家承擔了 \(\$2\) 的稅款。賣家則承擔了剩下的 \(\$1\) (\(\$3 - \$2\))。因為買家承擔的稅款大於賣家，這表示需求比供應更缺乏彈性（即供應比需求更具彈性）。

The tariff is \(\$3\). Price rose from \(\$11\) to \(\$13\), so consumers bear \(\$2\) of the tax. Sellers bear the remaining \(\$1\) (\(\$3 - \$2\)). Since consumers bear a larger burden, demand is more inelastic than supply (meaning supply is more elastic than demand).

Statement (3) - Incorrect (錯誤)

原初總收入 = \(\$11 \times 600 = \$6600\)。新總收入（買家總支出）= \(\$13 \times 400 = \$5200\)。由於價格上升導致總支出下降，這證明了在該價格範圍內，需求是富有彈性的（彈性大於一）。

Original Total Revenue = \(\$11 \times 600 = \$6600\). New Total Revenue (Consumer Expenditure) = \(\$13 \times 400 = \$5200\). Since an increase in price led to a decrease in total expenditure, demand is elastic (elasticity is greater than one) in this range.

🚀 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧： 在多項選擇題中，你可以直接測試陳述 (3)。計算 \(P=11\) 和 \(P=13\) 時的總支出（$6600 變 $5200）。支出下跌代表彈性大於一。陳述 (3) 必定錯誤！立刻淘汰 B、C、D，答案直接鎖定為 A，完全不需要計算 (1) 和 (2)！

Shortcut: Test statement (3) first. Calculate Total Expenditure at \(P=11\) and \(P=13\) ($6600 vs $5200). A drop in expenditure when price rises means elasticity > 1. Statement (3) is false! Eliminate B, C, and D immediately. The answer must be A. You don't even need to calculate (1) and (2)!

🙋 Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼計算淨收入時，要用 $13 減去 $3？

Q: Why do we subtract $3 from $13 when calculating net revenue?

A: 因為 $13 是消費者支付的市場價格（包含關稅）。政府會從這 $13 中抽走 $3 作為稅收，所以賣家（出口商）真正落袋的只有 $10。淨收入就是指賣家真正收到的錢。

A: Because $13 is the market price paid by consumers (which includes the tariff). The government takes $3 out of this $13 as tax, leaving only $10 for the sellers. Net revenue refers to the money the sellers actually keep.
