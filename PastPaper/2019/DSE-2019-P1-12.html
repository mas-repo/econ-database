Yes. The core of the Law of Demand is the inverse relationship between price and quantity demanded. Since the relative price of high-quality fruit has fallen due to the fixed cost, rational consumers should switch to buying more high-quality fruit (substitution effect). If the ratio remains exactly the same, it implies consumers have zero response to the price change, which is not considered "consistent with the Law of Demand" in this context.

Great question! We must distinguish between "Nominal Price" and "Relative Price".
1. Absolute quantity drop: Since transport costs made both fruits more expensive in total, it's normal for total sales to drop.
2. Relative ratio rise: The question compares two fruits. Even though people are buying fewer fruits overall, among those who do buy, the proportion choosing high-quality fruit has increased (from 25% to 26.7%). This is the result of the fall in relative price.
