HKDSE Economics Tutorial (經濟學輔導)

Topic: Types of Business Ownership (企業擁有權類型) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
Key Concepts: Public Limited Company (公眾有限公司), Private Limited Company (私人有限公司), Privatization (私有化), Takeover (收購)

1. The Question (題目)

新世界中國地產有限公司（股票編號：0917）宣布把公司私有化，並取消其在香港交易所的上市地位。下列哪些是上述改變的可能好處？

  1. 該公司可以選擇不向公眾披露其財政帳目。
  2. 該公司可以減低被收購的風險。
  3. 該公司可享有較低的利得稅率。

A. 只有 (1)、(2)
B. 只有 (1)、(3)
C. 只有 (2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)

New World China Land Limited (Stock Code: 0917) announced privatisation of the company and cancelled its listing status at the Hong Kong Stock Exchange. Which of the following are the possible advantages of the above change?

  1. The company can choose not to disclose its financial accounts to the public.
  2. The company can reduce the risk of being taken over.
  3. The company can enjoy a lower profits tax rate.

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

在處理這道題目時，我們需要比較公眾有限公司 (Public Limited Company)與私人有限公司 (Private Limited Company)的特徵。「私有化」(Privatization) 在上市公司的語境中，指的是將一間公眾有限公司轉為私人有限公司，並從股票交易所退市。

To tackle this question, we must compare the features of a Public Limited Company and a Private Limited Company. "Privatisation" in the context of listed companies refers to converting a public limited company into a private limited company and delisting it from the stock exchange.

Key Features (主要特徵)

  • 財務披露 (Financial Disclosure): 公眾有限公司必須向公眾公開財務報告；私人有限公司只需向股東及政府披露。 Financial Disclosure: Public limited companies must publish their financial reports to the general public; private limited companies only need to disclose them to shareholders and the government.
  • 股份轉讓 (Share Transfer): 公眾有限公司的股份可以在自由市場（如港交所）自由買賣；私人有限公司的股份轉讓通常需要得到其他股東的同意。 Share Transfer: Shares of public limited companies can be freely traded on the open market (e.g., HKEX); transferring shares of a private limited company usually requires the consent of other shareholders.

Relevant Formula (相關公式)

雖然這是一條概念題，但我們可以重溫一下利得稅的計算方式：

Although this is a conceptual question, let's review how Profits Tax is calculated:

$$ \text{Tax Payable} = \text{Assessable Profits} \times \text{Profits Tax Rate} $$

在香港，法團（無論是公眾還是私人有限公司）的利得稅率 \( r \) 是標準化的（目前實行兩級制），並不會因為公司從公眾轉為私人而自動降低。

In Hong Kong, the profits tax rate \( r \) for corporations (whether public or private) is standardized (currently a two-tiered system), and it does NOT automatically decrease simply because a company changes from public to private.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): A. 只有 (1)、(2) / (1) and (2) only

Statement (1): 披露帳目 / Disclosing Accounts ✓

正確。 作為上市公司（公眾有限公司），法例規定必須定期對外公佈財務報表。退市並轉為私人有限公司後，公司可免除這項義務，從而保護商業機密並節省合規成本。

Correct. As a listed company (public limited company), it is legally required to publish financial statements to the public regularly. After delisting and becoming a private limited company, the company is exempted from this obligation, thereby protecting commercial secrets and saving compliance costs.

Statement (2): 收購風險 / Risk of being taken over ✓

正確。 上市公司的股份在公開市場自由買賣，任何人只要購入足夠比例的股份（例如超過 50%），即可引發「惡意收購」(Hostile Takeover)。私有化後，股份不能自由轉讓，大大降低了被外人強行收購的風險。

Correct. Shares of listed companies are traded freely on the open market. Anyone who acquires a sufficient percentage of shares (e.g., over 50%) can trigger a "Hostile Takeover". After privatisation, shares cannot be freely transferred, greatly reducing the risk of being forcibly taken over by outsiders.

Statement (3): 利得稅率 / Profits tax rate ✗

錯誤。 公司的利得稅率是基於其作為「法團」(Corporation) 的法律實體地位，而非其上市狀態。無論是公眾還是私人有限公司，在香港均適用相同的法團利得稅率。

Incorrect. The profits tax rate of a company is based on its legal entity status as a "Corporation", not its listing status. Both public and private limited companies are subject to the same corporate profits tax rate in Hong Kong.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：混淆「私有化」的兩種含義。
在經濟學中，Privatisation 有兩個意思：
1. 將政府公營機構賣給私人市場（例如：領展）。
2. 將上市公司除牌，轉為少數股東持有的私人公司（本題情況）。
同學若誤以為是第一種情況，可能會選錯答案。

Trap 1: Confusing the two meanings of "Privatisation".
In economics, Privatisation has two meanings:
1. Selling a government-owned public enterprise to the private sector (e.g., Link REIT).
2. Delisting a public company and turning it into a private company held by a few shareholders (the context of this question).
Students might choose the wrong answer if they mistake it for the first scenario.

陷阱 2：誤以為「私人 = 小型 = 稅率低」。
許多學生憑直覺認為私人公司規模較小，政府會給予稅務優惠。事實上，稅率只與「無限公司 (Unincorporated)」與「有限公司 (Incorporated)」的分類有關，公眾和私人有限公司均屬於 Incorporated，稅率相同。

Trap 2: Assuming "Private = Small = Lower Tax Rate".
Many students intuitively think private companies are smaller and thus receive government tax breaks. In reality, tax rates only differ between "Unincorporated" and "Incorporated" businesses. Both public and private limited companies are Incorporated and face the same tax rates.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 既然私有化有這麼多好處（保密、防收購），為什麼當初公司要上市呢？
A: 上市（成為公眾有限公司）最大的好處是集資能力 (Ability to raise capital)。公司可以向大眾發行股票來籌集大量資金進行擴張。當公司已經發展成熟，現金流充足，不再需要透過股市集資，大股東就可能覺得上市的「維護成本」（如審計費、披露要求）超過了其帶來的好處，從而選擇私有化。

Q: If privatisation has so many advantages (secrecy, anti-takeover), why did the company go public in the first place?
A: The biggest advantage of going public is the ability to raise capital. The company can issue shares to the general public to raise massive funds for expansion. When a company matures, has strong cash flows, and no longer needs to raise funds from the stock market, major shareholders might feel that the "maintenance costs" of being listed (e.g., audit fees, disclosure rules) outweigh the benefits, prompting them to privatise.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

The Elimination Method (消去法):

在 DSE 選擇題中，尋找「絕對錯誤」的選項通常比尋找正確選項更快。只要你清楚記得「企業所有權的轉換不會改變法定稅率」，你就可以立即判定 (3) 是錯的。
包含 (3) 的選項有 B、C、D。將它們全部劃掉！
剩下的唯一選項就是 A。你甚至不需要花時間去仔細驗證 (1) 和 (2)。這能為你節省寶貴的考試時間！

The Elimination Method:

In DSE MCQs, finding the "absolutely wrong" statement is often faster than proving the right ones. If you clearly remember that "changing business ownership type does not change the statutory tax rate", you instantly know (3) is false.
Options containing (3) are B, C, and D. Cross them all out!
The only remaining option is A. You don't even need to spend time carefully verifying (1) and (2). This saves you precious exam time!
