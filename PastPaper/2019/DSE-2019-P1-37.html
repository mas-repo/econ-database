HKDSE Economics: 題目解析 (Question Analysis)

📚 Topic: 宏觀經濟學 - 通脹與通縮 (Macroeconomics - Inflation & Deflation) ⭐ Difficulty: 3 / 5 🔑 Key Concepts: Unexpected Deflation, Wealth Redistribution, Nominal vs Real

1. 題目 (The Question)

在非預期通縮下，下列哪（幾）位人士會有所損失？

(1) 莫女士在一家接受存款公司持有一百萬元定期存款。
(2) 盧先生持有銀色債券收取利息，債券的回報率和通脹掛勾，最低回報為3%。
(3) 陳先生與一家網絡電視公司簽約兩年，每月以固定費用訂購其服務。

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (2)
C. 只有 (3)
D. 只有 (2)、(3)

Under unexpected deflation, which of the following persons will lose?

(1) Ms Mok holds a time deposit of one million dollars in a deposit taking company.
(2) Mr Lo receives interest from Silver Bond of which the rate of return is linked to the inflation, subject to a minimum return of 3%.
(3) Mr Chan signs a two-year contract with an online TV company to subscribe to its service by a fixed monthly payment.

A. (1) only
B. (2) only
C. (3) only
D. (2) and (3) only

2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

非預期通縮 (Unexpected Deflation) 發生時，實際物價水平的下降幅度大於人們的預期。這會導致貨幣的實質購買力 (Real Purchasing Power) 出乎意料地增加。

Unexpected deflation occurs when the actual price level falls more than people expected. This causes the real purchasing power of money to increase unexpectedly.

費雪方程式 (Fisher Equation):
$$ \text{實質利率 (Real Interest Rate, } r \text{)} = \text{名義利率 (Nominal Interest Rate, } R \text{)} - \text{通脹率 (Inflation Rate, } \pi \text{)} $$

財富重分配法則 (Wealth Redistribution Rule):
在非預期通縮下（\(\pi\) 為負數且低於預期），收取固定名義金額的人會獲益 (Gain)，因為他們收到的錢能買到更多東西；相反，支付固定名義金額的人會損失 (Lose)，因為他們付出的錢的實質價值變高了。

Under unexpected deflation (\(\pi\) is negative and lower than expected), receivers of fixed nominal amounts gain because the money they receive can buy more goods. Conversely, payers of fixed nominal amounts lose because the real value of the money they pay has increased.

3. 答案與詳細分析 (Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案是 (Correct Option): C. 只有 (3) / (3) only

分析 (1) 莫女士 (Ms Mok):
莫女士持有定期存款，她將收取固定名義利息和本金。在通縮下，物價下跌，她收回的貨幣的實質購買力上升了。因此，她會獲益。
Ms Mok holds a time deposit, meaning she receives fixed nominal interest and principal. Under deflation, the price level falls, so the real purchasing power of the money she receives increases. Therefore, she gains.

分析 (2) 盧先生 (Mr Lo):
銀色債券回報與通脹掛勾，但設有 3% 的最低回報保證。在通縮期間（通脹率為負數），回報率會觸及並維持在 3% 的下限。這意味著盧先生實質上變成了收取固定名義回報 (3%) 的人。由於物價下跌，這 3% 回報的實質購買力上升，因此他會獲益。
The Silver Bond is linked to inflation but has a minimum return guarantee of 3%. During deflation (negative inflation), the return rate hits the floor and stays at 3%. This effectively makes Mr Lo a receiver of a fixed nominal return (3%). Since prices are falling, the real purchasing power of this 3% return increases, so he gains.

分析 (3) 陳先生 (Mr Chan):
陳先生簽訂了合約，需要每月支付固定名義費用。在通縮下，貨幣升值（購買力上升），這意味著他每月支付的固定金額，其實質價值比預期中更高。他付出了更多實質財富，因此他會損失。
Mr Chan signed a contract requiring him to pay a fixed nominal fee monthly. Under deflation, the value of money increases (purchasing power rises), meaning the fixed amount he pays has a higher real value than expected. He is giving up more real wealth, so he loses.

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解答這類題目的關鍵點 (Pivot Point) 在於釐清兩個問題：

  1. 該人士是「收款人 (Receiver)」還是「付款人 (Payer)」？
  2. 金額是「固定 (Fixed)」還是「浮動 (Variable)」的？

陷阱位 (Distractor): 題目 (2) 中提到了「與通脹掛勾 (linked to inflation)」。很多學生看到這句就會認為回報會隨通縮下降而受損。但關鍵在於後面的「最低回報為3% (minimum return of 3%)」。這個保證將浮動回報在通縮時轉化為了固定回報！

The pivot point to solving this type of question is clarifying two things: (1) Is the person a Receiver or a Payer? (2) Is the amount Fixed or Variable?
Distractor: Statement (2) mentions "linked to inflation". Many students assume the return will drop during deflation, causing a loss. However, the crucial part is "minimum return of 3%". This guarantee converts the variable return into a fixed return during deflation!

5. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 為什麼陳先生 (3) 會損失？他每個月付的錢明明沒有變多啊？
Q: Why does Mr Chan (3) lose? The amount of money he pays every month hasn't increased!

A: 雖然他支付的「名義金額（例如每月 $500）」沒有變，但在通縮下，這 $500 可以買到比以前更多的商品。他原本可以用這 $500 買 10 杯咖啡，現在可以買 12 杯。他把這越來越值錢的 $500 交給了電視公司，代表他放棄的「實質購買力」增加了，所以他是損失的一方。
A: Although the "nominal amount (e.g., $500/month)" he pays remains unchanged, under deflation, this $500 can buy more goods than before. He could originally buy 10 coffees with it, but now he can buy 12. By giving this increasingly valuable $500 to the TV company, the "real purchasing power" he gives up has increased. Thus, he is the loser.

6. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

秒殺技巧 (One-Second Kill Trick):
在非預期通縮下，「收錢的人笑，給錢的人哭」（前提是固定金額）。
快速掃描題目：
(1) 莫女士：收利息 (Receiver) -> 笑 (Gain)
(2) 盧先生：收利息 (Receiver) -> 笑 (Gain)
(3) 陳先生：付月費 (Payer) -> 哭 (Lose)
題目問誰會損失 (Lose)，很明顯只有付款的陳先生，直接選 C！

Shortcut: Under unexpected deflation, "Receivers smile, Payers cry" (assuming fixed amounts).
Quick scan:
(1) Ms Mok: Receives interest -> Smiles (Gains)
(2) Mr Lo: Receives interest -> Smiles (Gains)
(3) Mr Chan: Pays monthly fee -> Cries (Loses)
The question asks who will lose. Obviously, it's only the payer, Mr Chan. Choose C directly!
