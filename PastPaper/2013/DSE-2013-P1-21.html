Topic: National Income Accounting (國民收入會計)
Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Keywords: Net Factor Income from Abroad, Resident vs Non-resident

HKDSE Economics Analysis

題目 (Question):

下列哪項並不包括在香港該年度的海外要素淨收入的計算之中？

Which of the following items is NOT included in the calculation of Hong Kong’s net factor income from abroad for the current year?

A. 一位日本教師在香港某語文學校工作一年所得的收入
The income received by a Japanese teacher who worked in a language school in Hong Kong for one year

B. 一位香港居民在加拿大擁有地產物業所賺取的租金收入
Rental income earned from real estate holdings in Canada owned by a Hong Kong resident

C. 一位印度顧問為香港機場管理局工作三個月所得的薪金
Salary received by an Indian consultant who worked for the Hong Kong Airport Authority for three months

D. 一位美國居民持有香港股票所賺取的股息
Dividends earned from shares in Hong Kong held by a US resident

概念重溫 (Concept Review)

1. 海外要素淨收入 (Net Factor Income from Abroad, NIA)

$$ \text{NIA} = \text{要素收入流入 (Inflow)} - \text{要素收入流出 (Outflow)} $$

它反映了本地居民 (Residents) 與 非本地居民 (Non-residents) 之間的收入流動。 It reflects the income flows between Residents and Non-residents.

2. 居民與非居民的定義 (Resident vs. Non-resident)

在國民收入會計中，區分兩者的關鍵通常是逗留時間 (Duration of Stay)，而不是國籍。 In National Income Accounting, the key to distinguishing the two is usually the Duration of Stay, not citizenship.

  • 居民 (Resident): 通常指在該經濟體系內居住或從事經濟活動一年或以上。
    Usually refers to living or engaging in economic activities in the economy for one year or more.
  • 非居民 (Non-resident): 逗留少於一年。
    Stays for less than one year.
Correct Answer: A

答案與詳細分析 (Answer & Detailed Analysis)

選項 A (Option A): 正確答案 (Correct)

分析：該日本教師在香港工作了一年。根據國民收入會計的定義，他被視為香港的常住居民 (Resident)。
既然他是香港居民，他在香港賺取的收入屬於「本地居民在本地賺取的收入」。這不涉及跨境資金流動，因此不計算在海外要素淨收入 (NIA) 之中（它直接計入 GDP 和 GNI 的內部部分）。

Analysis: The Japanese teacher worked in Hong Kong for one year. According to National Income Accounting, he is considered a Resident of Hong Kong. Since he is a HK resident earning income in HK, this is purely domestic income. It does not involve cross-border flows, so it is NOT included in NIA (it is part of domestic GDP and GNI directly).

選項 B (Option B): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析：香港居民（本地人）從加拿大（海外）賺取租金。這是要素收入流入 (Factor Income Inflow)。這會增加 NIA，所以包括在計算中。

Analysis: A HK resident earns rent from Canada (abroad). This is Factor Income Inflow. It increases NIA, so it IS included in the calculation.

選項 C (Option C): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析：印度顧問只工作了三個月（少於一年）。他被視為非本地居民 (Non-resident)。非本地居民在香港賺取薪金，屬於要素收入流出 (Factor Income Outflow)。這會減少 NIA，所以包括在計算中。

Analysis: The Indian consultant worked for only three months (< 1 year). He is considered a Non-resident. A non-resident earning salary in HK constitutes Factor Income Outflow. It decreases NIA, so it IS included in the calculation.

選項 D (Option D): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析：美國居民（非本地居民）持有香港股票賺取股息。這是非本地居民在香港賺取的投資回報，屬於要素收入流出 (Factor Income Outflow)。包括在計算中。

Analysis: A US resident (Non-resident) earns dividends from HK shares. This is investment income earned by a non-resident in HK, constituting Factor Income Outflow. It IS included in the calculation.

題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

這類題目的破題關鍵 (Pivot Point) 在於區分「居民」與「非居民」。

The Pivot Point of such questions lies in distinguishing between "Residents" and "Non-residents".

  1. 尋找時間關鍵詞 (Look for time keywords):
    看到 "一年 (one year)" vs "三個月 (three months)"。
    Spot "one year" vs "three months".
  2. 應用規則 (Apply the rule):
    \( \ge 1 \text{ Year} \rightarrow \text{Resident} \)
    \( < 1 \text{ Year} \rightarrow \text{Non-resident} \)
  3. 判斷流向 (Determine the flow):
    NIA 只計算 「居民」與「非居民」之間的交易。
    如果是「居民」與「居民」之間的交易（如選項 A），則不屬於 NIA。
    NIA only calculates transactions between "Residents" and "Non-residents". If it's a transaction between "Resident" and "Resident" (like Option A), it's not NIA.

常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

  • 誤解國籍 (Confusing Citizenship):
    學生常以為「日本教師」一定是外國人，所以他的收入一定是流出。
    糾正：經濟學看的是「常住地 (Center of Economic Interest)」，而不是護照。只要住滿一年，他就是香港的經濟居民。
    Students often assume a "Japanese teacher" must be a foreigner, so the income must be an outflow. Correction: Economics looks at "Center of Economic Interest", not passports. If they stay for a year, they are an economic resident of HK.
  • 忽略題目中的「不」字 (Ignoring "NOT"):
    題目問的是「並不包括」。這是一個典型的否定題。
    The question asks what is "NOT" included. This is a typical negative question.

學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 如果那位日本教師只工作了 11 個月，答案會改變嗎？

A: 會！如果他只工作 11 個月，他會被視為非本地居民 (Non-resident)。那麼他在香港賺取的收入就會變成要素收入流出 (Factor Income Outflow)，就會被計入 NIA 之中。那樣的話，選項 A 就不是正確答案了。

Q: If the Japanese teacher only worked for 11 months, would the answer change?
A: Yes! If he worked for only 11 months, he would be considered a Non-resident. His income earned in HK would then become Factor Income Outflow and WOULD be included in NIA. In that case, Option A would not be the answer.

Q: 為什麼選項 D 的股息是 NIA？股票不是買賣嗎？

A: 這裡指的是「股息 (Dividends)」，是持有資產的回報，屬於要素收入（資本的回報）。如果是「買賣股票賺取的差價（資本增值）」，那就不屬於 GDP 或 NIA 的計算範圍，但在這裡我們談論的是股息。

Q: Why are dividends in Option D considered NIA? Isn't stock trading just buying and selling?
A: This refers to "Dividends", which is the return on holding assets, classified as factor income (return on capital). If it were "capital gains from trading stocks", that would not be in GDP or NIA, but here we are talking about dividends.
