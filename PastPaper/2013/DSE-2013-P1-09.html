HKDSE Economics Topic Analysis

Topic (課題): Forms of Business Ownership (企業擁有權形式)

Difficulty (難度): ★★★☆☆ (3/5)

Key Concepts (關鍵概念): Partnership (合夥), Private Limited Company (私人有限公司), Legal Entity (獨立法人), Profits Tax (利得稅)

1. The Question (題目)

繁體中文

若一家合夥變成了一家私人有限公司，

(1) 該公司會成為一獨立法人。
(2) 該公司被收購的風險會較高。
(3) 該公司須付較高的利得稅率。
(4) 東主的數目會上升。

A. 只有(1)、(3)
B. 只有(1)、(4)
C. 只有(2)、(3)
D. 只有(2)、(4)

English

If a partnership is turned into a private limited company,

(1) the company will become an independent legal entity.
(2) the company will be at a higher risk of being taken over.
(3) the company will pay a higher profits tax rate.
(4) the number of owners will increase.

A. (1) and (3) only
B. (1) and (4) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (2) and (4) only

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

核心經濟理論 (Core Economic Theories)

合夥 (Partnership): 屬於非法團企業 (Unincorporated business)，東主與企業在法律上視為同一體，沒有獨立法人地位。利得稅率較低 (香港現行標準為 15%)。
Partnership: An unincorporated business where owners and the business are considered the same legal entity. It lacks independent legal status. The profits tax rate is lower (currently standard 15% in HK).

私人有限公司 (Private Limited Company): 屬於法團企業 (Incorporated business/Corporation)，擁有獨立法人地位 (Independent legal entity)。股份不能在公開市場自由轉讓，因此被惡意收購的風險極低。利得稅率較高 (香港現行標準為 16.5%)。
Private Limited Company: An incorporated business with independent legal entity status. Shares cannot be freely transferred in the public market, making the risk of hostile takeover extremely low. The profits tax rate is higher (currently standard 16.5% in HK).

稅款計算公式 (Tax Calculation Formula):

$$ \text{Tax Payable} = \text{Assessable Profits} \times \text{Profits Tax Rate} $$

在香港稅制下，$R_{corporate} > R_{unincorporated}$，其中 $R$ 代表利得稅率。
Under the HK tax system, $R_{corporate} > R_{unincorporated}$, where $R$ represents the profits tax rate.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option A

(1) 該公司會成為一獨立法人 / the company will become an independent legal entity. [正確 / True]

合夥企業沒有獨立法人地位，而有限公司（無論私人或公眾）在法律上是一個獨立法人，可獨立簽署合約及擁有資產。
A partnership does not have independent legal status. A limited company (both private and public) is an independent legal entity in the eyes of the law, meaning it can sign contracts and own assets independently.

(2) 該公司被收購的風險會較高 / the company will be at a higher risk of being taken over. [錯誤 / False]

私人有限公司的股份轉讓受到嚴格限制，必須獲得現有股東同意，且不能向公眾發行股份。因此，它面臨惡意收購的風險極低，與合夥企業相若。只有「公眾有限公司」才有較高被收購的風險。
The transfer of shares in a private limited company is strictly restricted and requires the consent of existing shareholders. It cannot issue shares to the public. Therefore, its risk of facing a hostile takeover is extremely low, similar to a partnership. Only "public limited companies" face a higher risk of takeover.

(3) 該公司須付較高的利得稅率 / the company will pay a higher profits tax rate. [正確 / True]

根據香港稅制，法團業務（如有限公司）的利得稅率高於非法團業務（如獨資經營和合夥）。
Under the Hong Kong tax system, the profits tax rate for incorporated businesses (like limited companies) is higher than that for unincorporated businesses (like sole proprietorships and partnerships).

(4) 東主的數目會上升 / the number of owners will increase. [錯誤 / False]

合夥的法定人數上限通常為50人，私人有限公司的股東上限同樣為50人。轉換擁有權形式並不代表東主數目「必然」會上升，原有合夥人可以直接成為新公司的股東，人數維持不變。
The legal maximum number of partners is generally 50, and the maximum number of shareholders in a private limited company is also 50. Changing the form of ownership does not mean the number of owners will "necessarily" increase. The original partners can simply become the shareholders of the new company, keeping the number constant.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (解題關鍵): 區分「私人」與「公眾」有限公司的特徵。
Differentiating the characteristics of "Private" versus "Public" limited companies.

  • 這題的陷阱在於第 (2) 項。很多學生看到「有限公司」就聯想到股票市場和惡意收購。必須看清楚題目寫的是「私人有限公司」(Private Limited Company)，其股份缺乏流動性。
  • The trap lies in statement (2). Many students see "limited company" and immediately think of the stock market and hostile takeovers. You must clearly note that the question specifies a "Private Limited Company", whose shares lack liquidity.
  • 第 (4) 項測試你的邏輯。選項說「會」上升 (will increase)，這表示一種必然性。既然兩者的法定人數上限都是50，人數沒有必然增加的理由。
  • Statement (4) tests your logic. The option states the number "will" increase, implying absolute certainty. Since both forms have a legal maximum of 50 owners, there is no necessary reason for the number to increase.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

混淆私人與公眾有限公司 (Confusing Private and Public Limited Companies)

學生常誤以為所有「有限公司」都可以上市集資及容易被收購。記住：只有公眾有限公司 (Public Limited Company) 的股份才可以在交易所自由買賣，從而產生被外人惡意收購的風險。
Students often mistakenly believe that all "limited companies" can list on the stock exchange to raise capital and are vulnerable to takeovers. Remember: Only the shares of a Public Limited Company can be freely traded on an exchange, thereby creating the risk of a hostile takeover by outsiders.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼政府要向有限公司徵收較高的利得稅率？
Why does the government charge a higher profits tax rate for limited companies?

A: 有限公司享有「有限債務責任」(Limited Liability) 的保護，股東的個人財產受到保障，不會因公司破產而遭到清盤。因為公司享有這項極大的法律優勢和獨立法人地位，政府因此對其徵收稍微高一點的稅率作為平衡。
Limited companies enjoy the protection of "Limited Liability," meaning shareholders' personal assets are protected and won't be liquidated if the company goes bankrupt. Because the company enjoys this significant legal advantage and independent corporate status, the government levies a slightly higher tax rate to balance it out.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

消去法 (Elimination Method)

在多項選擇題中，一旦你非常確定某個敘述的對錯，就可以快速刪除選項。
In multiple-choice questions, once you are absolutely certain about a statement, you can quickly eliminate options.

  1. Step 1: 你知道 (1) 絕對正確（成為法團必然有獨立法人地位）。因此，你可以立刻刪除選項 C 和 D。
  2. You know (1) is definitely correct (incorporation necessarily grants independent legal entity status). Therefore, you can immediately eliminate options C and D.
  3. Step 2: 現在只剩下 A (1, 3) 和 B (1, 4)。你只需要評估 (3) 和 (4)。
  4. Now you are left with A (1, 3) and B (1, 4). You only need to evaluate (3) and (4).
  5. Step 3: 閱讀 (4)「東主數目會上升」。常理推斷，公司重組並不強制增加老闆人數。既然 (4) 錯，答案必然是 A。你甚至不需要完全確定稅率的細節！
  6. Read (4) "the number of owners will increase". Logically, corporate restructuring does not forcibly increase the number of bosses. Since (4) is wrong, the answer must be A. You don't even need to be 100% sure about the tax rate details!
