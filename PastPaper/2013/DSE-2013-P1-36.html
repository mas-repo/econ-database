📊 HKDSE Economics Topic Breakdown

主題 (Topic): 稅制與收入不均 (Taxation and Income Inequality)

難度 (Difficulty): ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)

關鍵概念 (Key Concepts): 薪俸稅 (Salaries Tax), 個人免稅額 (Personal Allowance), 標準稅率 (Standard Tax Rate), 稅基 (Tax Base)

📝 題目 (The Question)

下圖顯示某經濟薪俸稅制的轉變。

The following diagram shows the change in the salaries tax structure of an economy.

與舊薪俸稅制相比，新稅制：

Compared to the old salaries tax structure, the new tax structure:

A. 的個人免稅額較少。
has a smaller personal allowance.

B. 的標準稅率較低。
has a lower standard tax rate.

C. 會收窄貧富之間的收入差距。
will narrow the income gap between the rich and the poor.

D. 會擴闊稅基。
will widen the tax base.

🧠 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

要拆解這類圖表題，我們必須將圖表上的幾何特徵與經濟學概念連結起來：

To analyze this type of graphical question, we must link the geometric features on the graph to economic concepts:

  • 個人免稅額 (Personal Allowance): 即不用繳稅的最高收入水平。在圖表上，這顯示為斜線與 \( x \) 軸的交點 (\( x \)-intercept)。交點越向右，代表免稅額越大。
    The maximum income level that is not subject to tax. On the graph, this is represented by the \( x \)-intercept. A shift to the right means a larger allowance.
  • 標準稅率 (Standard Tax Rate): 薪俸稅的最高稅率限制。在圖表上，這顯示為水平線的 \( y \) 軸數值 (plateau)。水平線越高，代表標準稅率越高。
    The maximum tax rate limit for salaries tax. On the graph, this is represented by the \( y \)-value of the horizontal plateau. A higher horizontal line means a higher standard tax rate.
  • 稅基 (Tax Base): 需要繳納該稅項的人數或經濟活動範圍。如果個人免稅額增加，部分原本需要交稅的低收入人士將跌出稅網，導致稅基收窄。
    The number of people or economic activities subject to the tax. If the personal allowance increases, some low-income earners who previously paid taxes will fall out of the tax net, narrowing the tax base.

✅ 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): C

首先，我們需要非常仔細地追蹤圖中的標籤 (Labels) 與線條 (Lines)：

First, we need to trace the labels and lines in the diagram very carefully:

  • 新稅制 (New tax structure): 指向頂部的水平線，順著線條往下看，它的斜線部分位於較右方。
  • Points to the top horizontal line. Tracing it down, its slanted section is on the right.
  • 舊稅制 (Old tax structure): 指向底部的水平線，順著線條往下看，它的斜線部分位於較左方。
  • Points to the bottom horizontal line. Tracing it down, its slanted section is on the left.
選項 A (Option A): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

由於「新稅制」的 \( x \) 軸交點比「舊稅制」更偏向右方，這表示免稅的收入門檻提高了。因此，新稅制的個人免稅額是較大，而非較少。

Since the \( x \)-intercept of the "New tax structure" is further to the right than the "Old tax structure", the tax-free income threshold has increased. Therefore, the new tax structure has a larger personal allowance, not smaller.

選項 B (Option B): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

圖中顯示「新稅制」的水平線高於「舊稅制」的水平線。由於 \( y \) 軸代表稅率，這表示新稅制的標準稅率是較高，而非較低。

The graph shows the horizontal line of the "New tax structure" is higher than that of the "Old tax structure". Since the \( y \)-axis represents the tax rate, this means the standard tax rate is higher, not lower.

選項 C (Option C): 正確 (Correct)

新稅制帶來了兩個改變：第一，個人免稅額增加，令低收入人士免於繳交薪俸稅，減輕了他們的稅務負擔；第二，標準稅率提高，令高收入人士需要按更高的稅率繳納更多的稅款。這種「劫富濟貧」的稅制改動會令稅制更具累進性 (progressive)，從而收窄貧富之間的收入差距。

The new tax structure brings two changes: First, the increased personal allowance frees low-income earners from salaries tax, reducing their burden. Second, the higher standard tax rate means high-income earners pay more taxes at a higher rate. This change makes the tax system more progressive, thereby narrowing the income gap between the rich and the poor.

選項 D (Option D): 錯誤 (Incorrect)

正如選項 A 的分析，新稅制增加了個人免稅額。那些收入介乎「舊免稅額」與「新免稅額」之間的市民，現在不再需要繳交薪俸稅（跌出稅網）。納稅人數減少，這表示稅基被收窄，而非擴闊。

As analyzed in Option A, the new tax structure increased the personal allowance. Citizens whose incomes fall between the "old allowance" and the "new allowance" no longer need to pay salaries tax (falling out of the tax net). The number of taxpayers decreases, meaning the tax base is narrowed, not widened.

🔍 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

破題關鍵 (Pivot Point): 視覺追蹤 (Visual Tracing)。這題的陷阱完全在於視覺上的誤導。學生很容易下意識地認為「左邊的線是新，右邊的線是舊」，或者沒有將標籤的水平線與底部的斜線正確連繫起來。

Pivot Point: Visual Tracing. The trap in this question is entirely visual misdirection. Students can easily unconsciously assume "the left line is new, the right line is old," or fail to correctly link the labeled horizontal lines with their respective slanted lines at the bottom.

只要能冷靜地將「新稅制」的線條由頭到尾畫一次，確認它是「較高的頂部 + 較右的底部」，這題的難度就會瞬間大幅下降。

As long as you calmly trace the "New tax structure" line from end to end, confirming it represents "higher plateau + further right base", the difficulty of this question drops significantly.

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

1. 混淆稅基與稅收 (Confusing Tax Base with Tax Revenue):
很多學生以為稅率上升就會「擴闊稅基」。記住，稅基 (Tax Base) 是看「有幾多人/項目要交稅」，而稅收 (Tax Revenue) 才是看「收到幾多錢」。免稅額上升必定導致稅基收窄，即使標準稅率同時上升也無法改變這一點。

Many students think an increased tax rate "widens the tax base". Remember, Tax Base is about "how many people/items are taxed", while Tax Revenue is about "how much money is collected". An increase in allowance strictly narrows the tax base, even if the standard rate increases simultaneously.

🙋 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

學生問： 點解選項 C 係啱？提高高收入人士嘅稅率，唔一定會收窄收入差距架？如果高收入人士逃稅呢？

Student: Why is Option C correct? Raising taxes on the rich doesn't *always* narrow the income gap, right? What if high-income earners evade taxes?

導師答： 這是個很好的思考！在現實世界中，過高的稅率的確可能引發逃稅或抑制工作意慾（即拉弗曲線效應 Laffer Curve effect）。但在 DSE 選擇題的框架下，我們通常假設「其他因素不變 (ceteris paribus)」，系統的累進性 (progressivity) 增加，其直接的會計結果 (accounting effect) 就是除稅後收入差距 (after-tax income gap) 的收窄。這題同時提高了免稅額（直接幫到窮人），所以收窄貧富懸殊的結論是非常穩固的。

Tutor: That's excellent critical thinking! In the real world, extremely high tax rates might induce tax evasion or disincentivize work (the Laffer Curve effect). However, in the framework of DSE multiple-choice questions, we usually assume *ceteris paribus*. An increase in the progressivity of the system directly results in a narrower after-tax income gap mathematically. Since this question also involves a higher allowance (directly helping the poor), the conclusion that it narrows the wealth gap is very robust.

💡 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks)

在考試中遇到這類圖表題，不要單憑眼睛看，必須動筆！ 拿著原珠筆，沿著「新稅制」的字眼，將整條線描粗一次。這樣你的大腦就能立刻分辨出兩條線的相對位置，避免因緊張而產生的視覺錯覺。

When facing graphical questions in the exam, do not just rely on your eyes; use your pen! Take your ballpoint pen and trace over the entire line labeled "New tax structure" to make it thicker. This helps your brain immediately distinguish the relative positions of the two lines, avoiding visual illusions caused by exam nerves.
