HKDSE Economics Tutor: Question Analysis

Topic (課題):
Market Intervention (市場干預)
Difficulty (難度):
⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5 - Classic Trap)
Key Concepts (核心概念):
Price Floor 價格下限 Ineffective Intervention 無效干預 Total Revenue 總收益

1. The Question (題目)

[繁體中文]

參閱以下供需圖：

若政府將價格下限由 \( P_2 \) 提高至 \( P_1 \)，賣方收取的總收益

  • A. 會上升。
  • B. 會下降。
  • C. 會維持不變。
  • D. 可能上升或下降，視乎需求的價格彈性。

[English]

Refer to the following demand-supply diagram:

If the government raises the price floor from \( P_2 \) to \( P_1 \), the total revenue received by the sellers

  • A. will rise.
  • B. will fall.
  • C. will remain unchanged.
  • D. may rise or fall, depending on the price elasticity of demand.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

價格下限 (Price Floor) 是一項由政府設定的法定最低價格，旨在保護賣方（如勞工或農產品生產者）。交易價格不能低於此下限。

A Price Floor is a legal minimum price set by the government, designed to protect sellers (like laborers or agricultural producers). The trading price cannot fall below this limit.

有效與無效干預 (Effective vs. Ineffective Intervention):
要令價格下限產生作用（有效），它必須設定在均衡價格之上。如果價格下限設定在均衡價格之下，它便是無效的 (Ineffective)，市場價格會自然維持在原本的均衡水平。

Effective vs. Ineffective Intervention: For a price floor to be effective (binding), it must be set above the equilibrium price. If it is set below the equilibrium price, it is ineffective (non-binding), and the market price will naturally remain at the original equilibrium level.

總收益 (Total Revenue):

$$ TR = P \times Q $$

Total Revenue is calculated by multiplying the market price (\(P\)) by the actual transacted quantity (\(Q\)).

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): C

Why Option C is correct (為何 C 正確):
在圖中，市場的均衡價格是 \( P_1 \)，均衡交易量是 \( Q_1 \)。
當政府將價格下限設定在 \( P_2 \) 時，由於 \( P_2 \) 低於均衡價格 \( P_1 \)，這是一個無效的價格下限。因此，市場會自行調節，實際交易價格依然是均衡價格 \( P_1 \)，交易量是 \( Q_1 \)。總收益 = \( P_1 \times Q_1 \)。
當政府將價格下限提高至 \( P_1 \)，這剛好等於均衡價格，依然沒有迫使市場偏離原本的均衡點。實際市場價格仍然是 \( P_1 \)，交易量仍然是 \( Q_1 \)。
因為價格和交易量在兩次情況下都沒有改變，所以總收益維持不變。

In the diagram, the market equilibrium price is \( P_1 \), and the equilibrium quantity is \( Q_1 \).
When the government sets a price floor at \( P_2 \), because \( P_2 \) is lower than the equilibrium price \( P_1 \), it is an ineffective (non-binding) price floor. Thus, the market adjusts itself, and the actual transacted price remains at the equilibrium price \( P_1 \), with quantity \( Q_1 \). Total Revenue = \( P_1 \times Q_1 \).
When the government raises the price floor to \( P_1 \), it perfectly aligns with the equilibrium price, still not forcing the market to deviate from its original equilibrium. The actual market price remains \( P_1 \) and the quantity remains \( Q_1 \).
Since both the price and transacted quantity remain unchanged in both scenarios, the total revenue remains unchanged.

Why A & B are incorrect (為何 A 與 B 錯誤):
這兩個選項假設了市場價格會跟隨價格下限由 \( P_2 \) 上升至 \( P_1 \)。但由於 \( P_2 \) 是一個無效的下限，市場價格從一開始就已經是 \( P_1 \)，並未發生過上升或下跌。

These options assume that the market price would follow the price floor and rise from \( P_2 \) to \( P_1 \). However, since \( P_2 \) is an ineffective floor, the market price was already at \( P_1 \) from the very beginning. No price rise or fall actually occurred.

Why D is incorrect (為何 D 錯誤):
彈性（Elasticity）只會在價格或交易量發生實際變動時，才需要用來判斷總收益的升跌。既然價格和交易量都沒有改變，總收益自然不變，無須考慮需求的價格彈性。

Elasticity is only needed to determine the change in total revenue when there is an actual change in price or quantity. Since neither price nor quantity changed, total revenue remains unchanged, making the price elasticity of demand irrelevant here.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答此題的樞紐點 (Pivot Point) 在於辨認出「價格下限 (Price Floor)」與「市場均衡點 (Market Equilibrium)」的相對位置。

The Pivot Point to solving this question lies in identifying the relative position of the "Price Floor" compared to the "Market Equilibrium".

  • 干擾資訊 (Distractor): 垂直的供應曲線 (S) 是一個視覺干擾。學生可能會花費精力去思考「完全缺乏彈性的供應」會如何影響結果，但其實只要看破 \( P_2 \) 是無效下限，供應曲線的形狀根本不影響這題的結論。
  • Distractor: The vertical supply curve (S) is a visual distractor. Students might waste effort thinking about how "perfectly inelastic supply" affects the outcome. However, once you realize \( P_2 \) is an ineffective floor, the shape of the supply curve doesn't impact the conclusion at all.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：誤將「價格下限」當作「實際市場價格」
很多同學一看到題目寫「價格由 \( P_2 \) 升至 \( P_1 \)」，就反射性地認為一開始的實際收入是 \( P_2 \times Q_1 \)，後來的收入是 \( P_1 \times Q_1 \)，因此誤選 A (上升)。緊記：下限不是定價！ 只要下限低於均衡點，市場就會無視它，直接以均衡價格成交。

Trap: Mistaking the "Price Floor" for the "Actual Market Price"
Many students see "price raised from \( P_2 \) to \( P_1 \)" and reflexively assume the initial actual revenue was \( P_2 \times Q_1 \) and the later is \( P_1 \times Q_1 \), mistakenly choosing A (will rise). Remember: A floor is not a fixed price! As long as the floor is below the equilibrium, the market ignores it and trades at the equilibrium price.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 如果題目將「價格下限」改為「價格上限 (Price Ceiling)」，答案會不同嗎？

解答: 會的！如果設定的是價格上限（最高價格），那麼設定在 \( P_2 \)（低於均衡點）就會是有效的 (Effective)。當時的實際價格會被壓制在 \( P_2 \)。若之後將上限放寬提高至 \( P_1 \)，實際市場價格就會由 \( P_2 \) 升回均衡點 \( P_1 \)。在交易量 \( Q_1 \) 不變的情況下，價格上升會令總收益上升（答案就會變成 A）。考評局經常利用「上限」與「下限」的字眼互換來設陷阱！

Answer: Yes! If it were a Price Ceiling (maximum price), setting it at \( P_2 \) (below equilibrium) would be Effective. The actual price would be suppressed at \( P_2 \). If the ceiling were then relaxed and raised to \( P_1 \), the actual market price would rise from \( P_2 \) back to the equilibrium \( P_1 \). With quantity \( Q_1 \) remaining constant, a price increase would cause total revenue to rise (the answer would become A). The HKEAA loves swapping "Ceiling" and "Floor" to set traps!

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

「箭嘴測試法」 (The Arrow Test):
在考試中，一秒判定價格管制是否有效：

  • 下限 (Floor) = 地板： 從設定的價格畫一個向上的箭嘴 (↑)。如果箭嘴能指中均衡點，代表無效 (Ineffective)。
  • 上限 (Ceiling) = 天花板： 從設定的價格畫一個向下的箭嘴 (↓)。如果箭嘴能指中均衡點，代表無效 (Ineffective)。

這題中，從 \( P_2 \) (Floor) 向上畫箭嘴，會指中均衡點 \( P_1 \)，因此一眼就能判定這干預是無效的，實際價格不受影響！

The Arrow Test:
In an exam, determine if a price control is effective in one second:

  • Floor: Draw an UPWARD arrow (↑) from the set price. If the arrow hits the equilibrium point, it is Ineffective.
  • Ceiling: Draw a DOWNWARD arrow (↓) from the set price. If the arrow hits the equilibrium point, it is Ineffective.

In this question, drawing an upward arrow from \( P_2 \) (Floor) hits the equilibrium \( P_1 \). Thus, you can instantly tell this intervention is ineffective and actual prices are unaffected!
