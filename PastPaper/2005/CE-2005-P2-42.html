📊 HKDSE Economics Topic Analysis

Topic (課題): 總需求與總供應 (AD-AS Model) / 財政政策與間接稅 (Fiscal Policy & Indirect Tax)

Difficulty (難度): ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5 Stars)

Key Concepts (核心概念): SRAS General Price Level Inflation

📝 The Question (題目)

開徵普及銷售稅會導致

A. 通貨膨脹。
B. 產出下降。
C. 消費支出增加。
D. 本地生產總值增加。

The introduction of a general sales tax would lead to

A. inflation.
B. lower output.
C. higher consumption expenditure.
D. higher GDP.

💡 Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 銷售稅與總供應 (Sales Tax and Aggregate Supply):
普及銷售稅是一種間接稅。對生產者而言，這會增加生產成本。在 AD-AS 模型中，生產成本上升會導致短期總供應 (SRAS) 曲線向左移動。

1. Sales Tax and Aggregate Supply:
A general sales tax is an indirect tax. For producers, this increases the cost of production. In the AD-AS model, an increase in production costs causes the Short-Run Aggregate Supply (SRAS) curve to shift to the left.

$$ \text{Cost of Production} \uparrow \implies \text{SRAS} \downarrow \text{ (Shifts Left)} $$ $$ \implies \text{Price Level (P)} \uparrow \text{ and Real Output (Y)} \downarrow $$

2. 通貨膨脹的定義 (Definition of Inflation):
通貨膨脹是指一般物價水平持續上升。一次性的政策（如開徵新稅）只會導致物價水平一次性上升，並不等於通貨膨脹。

2. Definition of Inflation:
Inflation is defined as a continuous increase in the general price level. A one-off policy (like introducing a new tax) only causes a one-off increase in the price level, which is not the same as inflation.

✅ The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option B

❌ Option A (A選項): 錯誤。開徵銷售稅會令物價水平 \(P\) 一次性上升，但不會導致物價持續上升。因此，它不符合通貨膨脹的定義。

❌ Option A: Incorrect. Introducing a sales tax causes a one-off increase in the price level \(P\), but it does not cause a continuous rise in prices. Therefore, it does not meet the definition of inflation.

✅ Option B (B選項): 正確。銷售稅增加了企業的生產成本，導致短期總供應 (SRAS) 減少。在圖表上，SRAS 向左移，新的均衡點會處於較高的物價水平和較低的實質產出 \(Y\)。

✅ Option B: Correct. A sales tax increases production costs for firms, leading to a decrease in Short-Run Aggregate Supply (SRAS). Graphically, SRAS shifts left, and the new equilibrium will be at a higher price level and a lower real output \(Y\).

❌ Option C (C選項): 錯誤。物價水平上升會降低市民的實質購買力（財富效應），通常會導致消費支出減少而非增加。

❌ Option C: Incorrect. A higher price level reduces the real purchasing power of citizens (wealth effect), which typically leads to lower, not higher, consumption expenditure.

❌ Option D (D選項): 錯誤。如 B 選項所述，實質產出（實質本地生產總值）會下降。除非題目特別指明「名義本地生產總值」(Nominal GDP) 且需求極度缺乏彈性，否則一般指實質 GDP 下降。

❌ Option D: Incorrect. As explained in Option B, real output (Real GDP) will fall. Unless the question specifically mentions "Nominal GDP" and demand is highly inelastic, GDP generally refers to real GDP, which decreases.

🔍 Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point): 辨清「一次性物價上升」與「通貨膨脹」的分別。這是考評局 (HKEAA) 最常設的陷阱！

解題步驟:
1. 辨認政策影響：銷售稅 = 生產成本上升。
2. 應用模型：生產成本上升 = SRAS 向左移。
3. 找出結果：物價 \(P\) 上升，產出 \(Y\) 下降。

Pivot Point: Distinguishing between a "one-off price increase" and "inflation". This is the most common trap set by the HKEAA!

Steps:
1. Identify policy impact: Sales tax = Higher production costs.
2. Apply model: Higher costs = SRAS shifts left.
3. Find result: Price level \(P\) increases, Output \(Y\) decreases.

⚠️ Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱： 很多學生看到「物價上升」就立刻選擇「通貨膨脹」(選項A)。
真相： 通貨膨脹 (Inflation) 必須是「持續的」(continuous/sustained)。稅率由 0% 加至 5%，物價只會跳升一次，之後便會穩定在新的水平，這不是通貨膨脹。

Trap: Many students see "prices rise" and immediately choose "inflation" (Option A).
Truth: Inflation must be "continuous" or "sustained". If a tax rate goes from 0% to 5%, prices will jump once and then stabilize at the new level. This is not inflation.

🙋 Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 銷售稅會影響總需求 (AD) 嗎？

A: 直接影響的是總供應 (SRAS)。雖然物價上升會沿著 AD 曲線移動（導致需求量減少），但銷售稅本身不會令 AD 曲線整條移動。消費稅主要被視為生產/銷售成本的增加。

Q: Does a sales tax affect Aggregate Demand (AD)?

A: The direct impact is on Aggregate Supply (SRAS). While the higher price level causes a movement along the AD curve (reducing the quantity of output demanded), the sales tax itself does not shift the entire AD curve. It is primarily treated as an increase in the cost of production/selling.

⚡ Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧： 在 DSE 經濟科中，只要題目提到「一次性的政策改變」（如增加某項稅收、提高最低工資），永遠不要選擇「通貨膨脹」或「通貨緊縮」。直接剔除選項 A，然後畫一個簡單的 AD-AS 交叉圖，SRAS 左移，馬上就能看出產出下降，秒選 B！

Shortcut: In DSE Economics, whenever a question mentions a "one-off policy change" (like introducing a tax or raising the minimum wage), never choose "inflation" or "deflation". Eliminate Option A immediately. Then, draw a quick AD-AS cross: shift SRAS left, and you instantly see output falls. Choose B in seconds!
