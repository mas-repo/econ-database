HKDSE Economics Micro-Lesson

課題 Topic: 稀少性、經濟物品與免費物品 (Scarcity, Economic Goods and Free Goods)
難度 Difficulty: ★☆☆☆☆ (Level 1: Basic Concept)
關鍵字 Key Concepts: 稀少性 Scarcity 經濟物品 Economic Goods 免費物品 Free Goods 競爭 Competition

題目 The Question

下列哪項有關經濟物品的陳述是正確的？ Which of the following statements about economic goods is correct?
  • A. 經濟物品可能不是稀少的。 A. Economic goods may not be scarce.
  • B. 人們會對經濟物品進行競爭。 B. People compete for economic goods.
  • C. 人們對經濟物品的偏好大於免費物品。 C. People prefer economic goods to free goods.
  • D. 人們對經濟物品和免費物品均會進行競爭。 D. People compete for both economic goods and free goods.

概念重溫 Concept Review

經濟物品 (Economic Good) 是一種數量不足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品。因此，要獲取經濟物品，必須付出代價（機會成本大於零）。因為資源不足，人們必須透過競爭 (Competition) 來決定誰能獲得該物品。

An Economic Good is a good whose quantity is insufficient to satisfy all human wants. Therefore, acquiring it involves an opportunity cost greater than zero. Because the resource is limited, people must engage in competition to determine who gets the good.

免費物品 (Free Good) 則是即使在價格為零的情況下，其數量已足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品（例如：陽光、空氣）。因此，獲取免費物品的機會成本為零，且不需要競爭。

A Free Good is a good whose quantity is sufficient to satisfy all human wants even at a zero price (e.g., sunlight, air). The opportunity cost of obtaining it is zero, and there is no competition involved.

決定一件物品是否為「經濟物品」的核心條件是稀少性 (Scarcity)。我們可以用以下數學條件來表示：

The core condition that determines whether a good is an "economic good" is scarcity. We can represent this with the following condition:

$$ \text{Quantity Demanded (需求量)} > \text{Quantity Supplied (供應量)} \quad \text{at } P = 0 $$

答案與詳細分析 The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案是：選項 B

The correct answer is: Option B

A. 經濟物品可能不是稀少的。 錯誤 Incorrect A. Economic goods may not be scarce.

根據定義，所有經濟物品必定是稀少的。如果一件物品不稀少，它就會成為免費物品。因此，「可能不是稀少的」這句話在邏輯上是矛盾的。

By definition, ALL economic goods are scarce. If a good is not scarce, it becomes a free good. Therefore, the phrase "may not be scarce" is a logical contradiction.

B. 人們會對經濟物品進行競爭。 正確 Correct B. People compete for economic goods.

因為經濟物品具有稀少性（即慾望大於現有資源），並不是每個人都能得到他們想要的數量。為了解決這個分配問題，人們必須參與競爭（例如透過出價、排隊或考試），這是經濟學中的基本定理。

Because economic goods are scarce (wants > available resources), not everyone can have as much as they want. To resolve this allocation problem, people must compete (e.g., by offering higher prices, queuing, or taking exams). This is a fundamental theorem in economics.

C. 人們對經濟物品的偏好大於免費物品。 錯誤 Incorrect C. People prefer economic goods to free goods.

物品的「偏好 (Preference)」與它是否屬於經濟物品無關。舉例來說，空氣是免費物品，而鑽石是經濟物品。從生存的角度來看，人們對空氣的偏好絕對遠大於鑽石，只是因為空氣供應無限，我們才不需要為它付費。

"Preference" has nothing to do with whether a good is economic or free. For example, air is a free good and diamonds are economic goods. In terms of survival, humans vastly prefer air over diamonds; we just don't pay for air because its supply is overwhelmingly abundant.

D. 人們對經濟物品和免費物品均會進行競爭。 錯誤 Incorrect D. People compete for both economic goods and free goods.

人們絕對不會對免費物品進行競爭。當價格為 \( P = 0 \) 時，免費物品的數量已經超過了所有人的需求，任何人都可以隨意拿取所需份量，競爭根本沒有必要存在。

People never compete for free goods. Since the quantity supplied exceeds quantity demanded at \( P = 0 \), anyone can take as much as they want. There is absolutely no need for competition.

題目拆解 Problem Decomposition

這是一條標準的定義題 (Definitional Question)。解題的「樞紐 (Pivot Point)」在於理解稀少性 (Scarcity)、經濟物品 (Economic Goods)與競爭 (Competition)三者是不可分割的鐵三角關係。

This is a standard definitional question. The "Pivot Point" for solving it lies in understanding that Scarcity, Economic Goods, and Competition form an inseparable triangle.

  • 有稀少性 ➔ 就是經濟物品 ➔ 必定產生競爭Has Scarcity ➔ Is an Economic Good ➔ Must generate Competition
  • 題目中的「偏好 (Preference)」是常見的干擾項 (Distractor)，用來誤導概念不清的同學。"Preference" in the options is a classic distractor used to mislead students with shaky concepts.

常犯錯誤與陷阱 Common Mistakes & Traps

⚠️ 概念混淆：以為「付費」才算是競爭

⚠️ Misconception: Thinking "paying money" is the only form of competition.

很多學生誤以為只有在市場上用金錢購買才算競爭。實際上，排隊花費的時間 (Time)、用武力搶奪 (Violence)、甚至用考試成績爭取大學學位 (Academic merits)，全部都是競爭經濟物品的形式！

Many students mistakenly believe that competition only happens in the market via monetary prices. In reality, spending time queuing, using physical force, or using exam results to fight for a university spot are all valid forms of competing for economic goods!

學生常見疑問 Student Q&A

🙋‍♂️ 學生問：「如果一件物品沒有人想要（例如垃圾），那它是不是免費物品？因為沒人跟別人競爭啊！」

🙋‍♂️ Student Q: "If no one wants a good (like garbage), does that make it a free good? Since no one is competing for it!"

👨‍🏫 導師答：這是一個很好的問題！如果一件東西完全沒有人想要，它在經濟學上根本不算是「物品 (Good)」，而是「缺憾 (Bad)」。物品的定義是「能滿足人類慾望的東西」。免費物品是指「大家想要，而且大自然給得非常多，多到拿不完」的東西（如呼吸的空氣）。

👨‍🏫 Tutor A: Great question! If something is desired by absolutely no one, in economics, it is not considered a "Good" at all; it's a "Bad". The definition of a good is "something that satisfies human wants". A free good is something people DO want, but nature provides it in such immense abundance that there's more than enough for everyone (like the air we breathe).

⚡ The Elimination Trick:

In HKDSE Economics MC papers, whenever you see an option that places "Free Goods" in the same sentence as "Cost / Scarce / Compete", you can immediately cross it out. This rule helps you eliminate Option D in less than 3 seconds!
