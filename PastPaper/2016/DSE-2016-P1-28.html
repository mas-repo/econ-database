HKDSE Economics Tutor Guide

Topic: 總需求與總供應 (AD-AS Model) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Aggregate Demand, Exports of Services, Long-run Equilibrium

1. The Question (題目)

深圳永久居民往香港的「一簽多行」被「一週一行」所取代。這會令香港的 _________ 和 __________下跌。

The ‘multiple entry period’（一簽多行）for the permanent Shenzhen residents to visit Hong Kong has been replaced by a ‘one trip per week cap’（一週一行）. This would reduce Hong Kong’s ____________ and ____________.

A. 總需求 …… 長期產出水平
aggregate demand …… output level in the long run
B. 總需求 …… 長期物價水平
aggregate demand …… price level in the long run
C. 短期總供應 …… 短期產出水平
short-run aggregate supply …… output level in the short run
D. 長期總供應 …… 長期產出水平
long-run aggregate supply …… output level in the long run

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

總需求 (Aggregate Demand, AD)

總需求是指在不同物價水平下，經濟體系內各部門對本地實質產出的計劃總開支。旅客在香港的消費屬於「服務輸出」(Exports of Services)。

Aggregate Demand is the total planned expenditure on domestic real output at different price levels. Tourists' spending in Hong Kong is classified as "Exports of Services".

$$ AD = C + I + G + (X - M) $$

長期均衡調整 (Long-run Equilibrium Adjustment)

當總需求下跌，短期內物價水平 \(P\) 和產出水平 \(Y\) 皆會下降，出現通貨緊縮缺口 (Deflationary gap)。在長期，要素價格（如工資）會向下調整，令短期總供應 (SRAS) 增加（向右移），直到產出水平回復至充分就業水平 (Full-employment output, \(Y_f\))。因此，長期產出不變，但長期物價水平會進一步下跌。

When AD falls, both price level \(P\) and output level \(Y\) drop in the short run, creating a deflationary gap. In the long run, factor prices (like wages) adjust downwards, causing Short-Run Aggregate Supply (SRAS) to increase (shift right) until output returns to the full-employment level \(Y_f\). Thus, long-run output remains unchanged, but the long-run price level falls further.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: B

Option A: Incorrect. 雖然總需求 (AD) 會下跌，但在長期，經濟會自我調整，產出水平會回復至原來的充分就業水平，因此長期產出水平不會下跌。
Although AD falls, in the long run, the economy self-adjusts and the output level returns to the original full-employment level. Therefore, the long-run output level does not fall.

Option B: Correct. 旅客減少導致服務輸出 \(X\) 減少，總需求 \(AD\) 下跌。在長期調整後，短期總供應向右移，導致長期物價水平較原來下跌。
Fewer tourists lead to a decrease in exports of services \(X\), causing AD to fall. After long-run adjustment, SRAS shifts to the right, leading to a lower long-run price level compared to the initial state.

Option C: Incorrect. 旅客減少直接影響的是需求面（總需求），而不是生產成本或生產力，因此不會直接令短期總供應 (SRAS) 下跌。
A decrease in tourists directly affects the demand side (AD), not production costs or productivity, so it does not directly reduce SRAS.

Option D: Incorrect. 旅客減少不會影響香港的生產力、資源數量或技術水平，因此長期總供應 (LRAS) 保持不變。
Fewer tourists do not affect Hong Kong's productivity, quantity of resources, or technology level, so LRAS remains unchanged.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (破題關鍵): 明白「旅客減少 = 出口減少 = 總需求下跌」。
Understand that "fewer tourists = decrease in exports = decrease in AD".

Distractors (干擾資訊): 題目刻意在選項中混入「短期」與「長期」的結果。學生必須清楚區分 AD 下跌在短期和長期的不同影響。
The question deliberately mixes "short-run" and "long-run" outcomes in the options. Students must clearly distinguish the different impacts of an AD decrease in the short run vs. the long run.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

Trap 1: 誤以為旅客是「勞動力」而選擇影響 LRAS。
Mistakenly thinking tourists are "labor force" and choosing options affecting LRAS. Tourists are consumers, not workers.

Trap 2: 忘記了長期的「古典學派」假設（產出必定回復至 \(Y_f\)）。
Forgetting the "Classical" assumption in the long run (output must return to \(Y_f\)).

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼旅客減少不是影響本地消費 (C) 而是出口 (X)？
Why does a decrease in tourists affect exports (X) instead of domestic consumption (C)?
A: 在國民收入會計中，消費 (C) 僅指「本地居民」的開支。非本地居民（如深圳旅客）在香港的開支，屬於將服務賣給外地人，因此計入「服務輸出」(Exports of Services)。
In national income accounting, Consumption (C) only refers to expenditure by "local residents". Spending by non-residents (like Shenzhen tourists) in HK is considered selling services to foreigners, thus counted as "Exports of Services".

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

速解法 (Shortcut): 看到「長期產出水平 (long-run output level) 下跌」，立即回想：什麼會改變長期產出？只有資源、技術、制度。旅客減少不在此列，所以長期產出不變。直接剔除 A 和 D！剩下 B 和 C，旅客是需求面，不會令 SRAS 下跌，剔除 C。答案秒選 B。
When you see "long-run output level falls", immediately recall: what changes long-run output? Only resources, technology, and institutions. Fewer tourists don't fit here, so long-run output is unchanged. Eliminate A and D instantly! Between B and C, tourists represent the demand side and won't decrease SRAS, eliminate C. The answer is B.
