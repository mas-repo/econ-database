1. Features of Monopoly:
A monopolist is the single seller in the market. Facing the entire downward-sloping market demand curve, a monopolist is a Price Searcher and can set either the price or the quantity (but not both simultaneously).

2. Natural Monopoly:
This does NOT refer to "legal prohibition of entry." A natural monopoly occurs when a single firm can supply the entire market demand at a lower average cost than two or more firms could. This usually involves huge fixed costs, leading to significant Economies of Scale.

Correct. While the definition of monopoly involves "no close substitutes," this does not mean "absolutely no substitutes."
