Topic: 宏觀經濟學 - 非預期通脹的影響 (Macroeconomics - Effects of Unexpected Inflation) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Key Concepts: Purchasing Power, Wealth Redistribution, Real vs Nominal

2. The Question (題目)

當非預期通脹出現，下列哪(幾)位人士會受損？

A. 一位向工人支付固定工資的廠家
B. 一位根據價格水平的轉變而調整租金的業主
C. 一位領取每月定額長俸的退休公務員
D. 以上三位人士均會受損，因為貨幣的購買力會下降。
When there is unexpected inflation, which of the following individual(s) will lose?

A. A firm owner paying his workers a fixed wage
B. A flat owner charging a rental with adjustment according to the change in price level
C. A retired civil servant receiving a fixed monthly pension
D. All of the above individuals will lose because the purchasing power of money will be lower.

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

在經濟學中，非預期通脹 (Unexpected Inflation) 會導致財富重分配。關鍵在於區分「名義價值 (Nominal Value)」與「實質價值 (Real Value)」。當一般物價水平 $P$ 上升時，貨幣的購買力會下降。

核心法則：
  • 收取固定名義金額的人（如債權人、領取固定薪金/退休金的人）會受損 (Lose)，因為他們收到的錢能買到的商品減少了。
  • 支付固定名義金額的人（如債務人、支付固定薪金的僱主）會獲益 (Gain)，因為他們付出的錢的實質購買力下降了。
In economics, unexpected inflation leads to the redistribution of wealth. The key is to distinguish between "Nominal Value" and "Real Value". When the general price level $P$ increases, the purchasing power of money decreases.

Core Rule:
  • Those who receive fixed nominal amounts (e.g., creditors, fixed-income earners, pensioners) lose because the money they receive buys fewer goods.
  • Those who pay fixed nominal amounts (e.g., debtors, employers paying fixed wages) gain because the real purchasing power of the money they give away has fallen.
Real Value Formula (實質價值公式):
$$ \text{Real Income} = \frac{\text{Nominal Income}}{\text{Price Index}} \times 100 $$

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): Option C

A. 一位向工人支付固定工資的廠家 (錯誤)
廠家是「支付方」。由於工資是固定的，通脹意味著廠家銷售產品的價格上升了，但勞工成本（名義工資）維持不變。因此，廠家支付的「實質工資」下降了，廠家實際上從中獲益。
A. A firm owner paying his workers a fixed wage (Incorrect)
The firm owner is the "payer". Since the wage is fixed, inflation means the prices of the goods they sell increase, but their labor cost (nominal wage) remains unchanged. Therefore, the "real wage" paid falls, and the firm owner actually gains.
B. 一位根據價格水平的轉變而調整租金的業主 (錯誤)
由於租金會「根據價格水平調整」，這意味著租金名義價值會隨通脹上升。這種指數化 (indexation) 保護了業主的實質購買力，因此他不會受損也不會獲益。
B. A flat owner charging a rental with adjustment according to the change in price level (Incorrect)
Because the rent "adjusts according to the change in price level", the nominal rent will rise with inflation. This indexation protects the flat owner's real purchasing power, so they neither gain nor lose.
C. 一位領取每月定額長俸的退休公務員 (正確)
退休公務員是「收取方」。他每月收到的退休金金額是「定額」(固定) 的。在通脹下，相同的金額能購買的商品和服務減少了，即實質購買力下降。因此，他必定會受損。
C. A retired civil servant receiving a fixed monthly pension (Correct)
The retired civil servant is the "receiver". The monthly pension they receive is "fixed". Under inflation, the same amount of money can buy fewer goods and services, meaning real purchasing power drops. Therefore, they definitely lose.
D. 以上三位人士均會受損... (錯誤)
雖然整體貨幣購買力下降是事實，但正如 A 和 B 的分析所示，並非所有人都在通脹中受損（有人獲益，有人不受影響）。
D. All of the above individuals will lose... (Incorrect)
While it is true that the overall purchasing power of money falls, as analyzed in A and B, not everyone loses during inflation (some gain, some are unaffected).

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

如何拆解這類題目？
1. 找關鍵字 (Pivot Point)： 尋找「固定 (Fixed)」或「調整 (Adjusted)」。只有牽涉「固定名義價值」的合約才會出現財富重分配。
2. 辨別身分： 確定該人士是「付出金錢 (Payer)」還是「收取金錢 (Receiver)」。
3. 邏輯推論： 通脹 = 錢變得不值錢。
- 收不值錢的錢 = 受損 (Loser)。
- 還不值錢的錢 = 獲益 (Gainer)。
How to approach this type of question?
1. Find the Pivot Point: Look for the words "Fixed" or "Adjusted". Wealth redistribution only occurs with contracts involving "fixed nominal values".
2. Identify the Role: Determine if the person is a "Payer" or a "Receiver" of money.
3. Logical Deduction: Inflation = money is worth less.
- Receiving less valuable money = Loser.
- Paying with less valuable money = Gainer.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱： 學生常選 D，誤以為「通脹對所有人都是壞事」。
真相： 在宏觀經濟學中，通脹是一場「零和遊戲」(Zero-sum game)。債權人損失的購買力，正是債務人節省下來的實質還款成本。此外，忽略題目中的「調整 (Adjustment)」字眼也是常見的粗心錯誤。
Trap: Students often choose D, mistakenly believing that "inflation is bad for everyone."
The Truth: In macroeconomics, unexpected inflation is largely a zero-sum game. The purchasing power lost by the creditor is exactly the real repayment cost saved by the debtor. Additionally, ignoring the word "adjustment" in the question is a common careless error.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 如果通脹是「預期之內 (Expected)」的呢？結果會有什麼不同？
A: 如果通脹是完全預期的，人們就會在合約中加入「通脹溢價 (Inflation Premium)」。例如，工人會要求每年加薪以抵銷通脹，銀行會提高名義利率。在這種情況下，就不會有財富重分配（沒有人會因此意外受損或獲益）。本題明確指出是「非預期 (Unexpected)」。
Q: What if the inflation was "Expected"? How would the result change?
A: If inflation is fully expected, people will include an "Inflation Premium" in their contracts. For example, workers would demand annual wage increases to offset inflation, and banks would charge higher nominal interest rates. In this case, there would be no wealth redistribution (no one unexpectedly loses or gains). This question explicitly states it is "Unexpected".

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

三秒速解法 (3-Second Shortcut)：
考試時看到「誰在通脹中受損？」，立刻在選項中尋找：
「收取 (Receive)」 + 「固定 (Fixed)」。
- A是「支付+固定」= 獲利。
- B是「調整」= 無影響。
- C是「收取+定額」= 受損。直接選 C！
3-Second Shortcut:
In the exam, when you see "Who loses in inflation?", immediately scan the options for:
"Receive" + "Fixed".
- A is "Pay + Fixed" = Gains.
- B is "Adjusts" = Unaffected.
- C is "Receive + Fixed" = Loses. Choose C immediately!
