課題 (Topic): 市場干預與價格的功能 (Market Intervention & Functions of Price)
Topic: Market Intervention & Functions of Price
難度 (Difficulty): ★★★☆☆ (中等 Moderate)
關鍵概念 (Key Concepts): 配給功能 (Rationing Function)、分配功能 (Allocative Function)、固定供應 (Fixed Supply)、需求量與需求的區別 (Demand vs. Quantity Demanded)

題目 The Question

著名藝人子華舉辦了一場表演，很多人抱怨未能購得門票。門票黃牛黨在互聯網上轉售這些門票，有買家付出高於港幣$10 000購入一張門票。上述個案說明了價格的 ___________ 功能，因為 ___________。
A famous artist, Tsz-Wah, held a show. Many people complained that they could not buy a ticket. Ticket touts resold the tickets on the Internet and some buyers paid a price higher than HK$10 000 for a ticket. The above case illustrates the ___________ function of price because ___________.
  • A. 配給 …… 這些門票配給予最高價值的買家
    A. rationing …… the tickets are rationed to the highest-valued buyers
  • B. 配給 …… 價格愈高，需求便會下降
    B. rationing …… demand will be reduced by a higher price
  • C. 分配 …… 價格愈高，利潤便會增加
    C. allocative …… more profits can be earned with a higher price
  • D. 分配 …… 更多的資源應該分配予生產商以增加供應
    D. allocative …… more resources should be allocated to the producer to raise the supply

概念重溫 Concept Review

在自由市場中，價格系統 (Price System) 扮演著兩個非常重要的角色：配給功能與分配功能。要解開這道題，我們必須清楚區分兩者。
In a free market, the price system plays two crucial roles: the rationing function and the allocative function. To solve this problem, we must distinguish between the two clearly.

1. 配給功能 Rationing Function

定義： 當資源或物品的數量已定（固定供應），價格決定了「誰可以獲得」這些物品。價格上升會淘汰那些支付意願較低的消費者，將有限的物品配給予願意且能夠支付最高價格的人。
Definition: When the quantity of a good is fixed, price determines "who gets" the goods. An increase in price eliminates consumers with a lower willingness to pay, rationing the scarce goods to those who value them most and are willing to pay the highest price.
Market Clearing Condition with Fixed Supply: $$ Q_d(P) = Q_s $$
由於短期內演唱會座位數量固定（即供應完全缺乏彈性，$E_s = 0$），價格 $P$ 會上升直至需求量 $Q_d$ 等於固定的供應量 $Q_s$。
Since the number of concert seats is fixed in the short run (perfectly inelastic supply, $E_s = 0$), price $P$ rises until quantity demanded $Q_d$ equals the fixed quantity supplied $Q_s$.

2. 分配功能 Allocative Function

定義： 價格引導資源的流向，決定了「生產什麼」及「生產多少」。當某物品價格上升，生產該物品的利潤會增加，吸引生產商將資源轉移到該行業，從而增加產量。這與生產者的行為有關。
Definition: Price acts as a signal guiding resource allocation, determining "what and how much to produce". When the price of a good rises, profitability increases, attracting producers to shift resources into this industry, thereby increasing output. This relates to producer behavior.

答案與詳細分析 The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案是：選項 A
The correct answer is: Option A

選項逐一擊破 Step-by-Step Breakdown:

A. 正確 Correct

題目描述的是「黃牛黨轉售門票」。在這個情況下，該場表演的門票總數已經固定（不可能憑空變出更多座位）。當價格被炒高至 $10,000，只有覺得這場表演價值高於或等於 $10,000 的買家才會購買。因此，價格發揮了配給功能，將現有固定數量的門票分配給最高價值的買家。
The question describes "touts reselling tickets". In this scenario, the total number of tickets for this specific show is fixed (you can't magically create more seats). When the price is bid up to $10,000, only buyers who value the show at $\ge \$10,000$ will buy them. Therefore, price serves the rationing function, distributing the existing, fixed quantity of tickets to the highest-valued buyers.

B. 錯誤 Incorrect

這是一個經典陷阱！根據需求定律，價格上升會導致「需求量」(Quantity Demanded) 下降，而不是「需求」(Demand) 下降。「需求」下降意味著整條需求曲線向左移，這是不正確的。此外，配給功能的核心意義在於「分配現有資源給最高價值者」，而非單純描述需求量下跌。
This is a classic trap! According to the law of demand, a higher price reduces "quantity demanded", NOT "demand". A decrease in "demand" means the entire demand curve shifts to the left, which is factually incorrect here. Furthermore, the core meaning of rationing is distributing existing resources to those who value them most, not merely describing a fall in quantity demanded.

C & D. 錯誤 Incorrect

這兩個選項都在描述分配功能 (Allocative function)。雖然高價格確實會吸引生產者投入更多資源（如選項D所言），但在「黃牛市場（二手市場）」這個特定情境中，高達 $10,000 的票價直接落入了黃牛黨的口袋，而不是主辦單位。更重要的是，這場已確定的表演座位數量是固定的，高昂的二手價格無法立刻令主辦方「增加該場表演的供應」。因此，這反映的是配給而非分配功能。
Both options describe the allocative function. While it's true that high prices generally attract producers to allocate more resources (as option D states), in the specific context of a "secondary/tout market", the $10,000 goes into the pockets of touts, not the producers/promoters. More importantly, the number of seats for this specific, scheduled show is strictly fixed. The high secondary price cannot cause the producer to instantly "raise the supply" for that exact same show. Thus, it illustrates rationing, not allocation.

題目拆解 Problem Decomposition

1. 尋找「轉捩點」(The Pivot Point):
題目的關鍵字是「未能購得」(Shortage / 短缺) 以及「轉售」(Resold / 二手市場)。當物品在二手市場以高價成交，意味著物品的供應量已經固定（Inelastic supply）。這必定是指向配給功能 (Rationing)。
1. Finding the Pivot Point:
The keywords are "could not buy" (indicating a shortage) and "resold" (secondary market). When a good is traded at a high price in a secondary market, it implies the supply is currently fixed (perfectly inelastic). This immediately points toward the rationing function.
2. 辨識干擾資訊 (Distractors):
「港幣$10 000」只是一個為了強調價格高昂的干擾數字。在經濟學邏輯中，無論黃牛價是 $2,000 還是 $10,000，其背後發揮的經濟學功能是完全一樣的。
2. Identifying Distractors:
"HK$10,000" is merely a distractor to emphasize the high price. Economically speaking, whether the tout price is $2,000 or $10,000, the underlying economic function taking place is exactly the same.

常犯錯誤與陷阱 Common Mistakes & Traps

陷阱一：混淆「需求」與「需求量」
DSE 考評局極度喜歡在選擇題中測試考生對 Demand (需求) 和 Quantity Demanded (需求量) 的字眼敏感度。記住：價格 $P$ 改變，只會導致「需求量」改變（沿著曲線移動 Movement along the curve）。看到「價格上升導致需求下降」，可立刻將該選項刪除！
Trap 1: Confusing "Demand" with "Quantity Demanded"
The HKEAA loves testing candidates' sensitivity to these terms. Remember: A change in price $P$ only causes a change in "Quantity Demanded" (a movement along the curve). If you see an option stating "a higher price reduces demand", eliminate it immediately!

學生常見疑問 Student Q&A

🙋‍♂️ 學生問：「如果子華看到黃牛票賣這麼貴，決定加開 10 場演唱會，那不就是『分配功能』(Allocative function) 了嗎？為什麼不能選 C 或 D？」
🙋‍♂️ Student Asks: "If Tsz-Wah sees tout tickets selling at such a high price and decides to hold 10 more shows, wouldn't that be the 'allocative function'? Why can't we choose C or D?"
👨‍🏫 導師答：「問得非常好！如果題目是說『主辦方看到市場反應熱烈，決定將原本用作舉辦話劇的場地，改為加開子華的演唱會』，那麼這確實是分配功能（資源重新分配以增加供應）。

但是，請仔細看題目：『上述個案說明了...』。個案中發生的是買家向黃牛黨買票這個行為。在這個特定的交易瞬間，資源（座位）數量是寫死的，高價格只是決定了『誰能進場』，所以答案必須是配給功能。」
👨‍🏫 Tutor Answers: "Excellent question! If the prompt said, 'Seeing the overwhelming market response, the promoter decided to reallocate venues originally meant for dramas to hold more Tsz-Wah shows,' then that WOULD be the allocative function (reallocating resources to increase supply).

However, read the question carefully: 'The above case illustrates...'. The case specifically describes buyers purchasing tickets from touts. In this specific transaction moment, the number of resources (seats) is rigidly fixed. The high price merely dictates 'who gets to enter'. Therefore, the answer must be the rationing function."

考試技巧與速解 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts

速解秒殺法 (The Elimination Shortcut):
The Elimination Shortcut:
  • 第一步：看到「黃牛 / 轉售 / 現有物品」👉 直接聯想 配給功能 (Rationing)。刪除 C 和 D。
    Step 1: See "Touts / Resale / Existing goods" 👉 Immediately link to Rationing. Eliminate C and D.
  • 第二步：檢查剩下的 A 和 B。選項 B 寫了「需求下降」(demand will be reduced)。這是死罪（應為需求量）。刪除 B。
    Step 2: Check the remaining A and B. Option B says "demand will be reduced". This is a cardinal sin in Economics (it should be quantity demanded). Eliminate B.
  • 結論：5秒內得出答案 A。
    Conclusion: Arrive at Answer A within 5 seconds.
