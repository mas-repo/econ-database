Topic: Financial Assets (金融資產) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Key Concepts: Risk & Return, Bonds vs Shares

2. The Question (題目)

中文版：
政府為65歲或以上的香港居民推出銀色債券。債券持有人會獲發利息，利率和本港通脹掛鉤，但年利率不會少於2%。債券持有人可在到期前將其債券以原價賣給政府。

下列哪(幾)項有關銀色債券的陳述是正確的？
(1) 持有銀色債券的風險較持有上市公司的股票的風險低。
(2) 在通脹時銀色債券的回報會上升，而上市公司股票的回報則不會。
(3) 銀色債券持有人收取的利息較上市公司股東收取的股息為高。

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (2)
C. 只有 (3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)

English Version:
The government launched the Silver Bond for Hong Kong residents aged 65 or above. Bondholders will be paid interest at a rate linked to inflation in Hong Kong, subject to a minimum rate of 2% per year. Bondholders may sell their bonds at original price to the government before maturity.

Which of the following statements about the Silver Bond is/are correct?
(1) The risk of holding the Silver Bond is lower than that of the shares of a listed company.
(2) The return of the Silver Bond would increase during inflation while the return of the shares of a listed company would not.
(3) The interest received by Silver Bond holders is higher than the dividend received by the shareholders of a listed company.

A. (1) only
B. (2) only
C. (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這條題目，我們需要理解債券（Bonds）與股票（Shares）在風險和回報上的根本分別。

To answer this question, we need to understand the fundamental differences between Bonds and Shares regarding risk and return.

  • Risk (風險): 政府債券通常被視為極低風險（甚至無風險），因為政府違約的機會極微。相反，股票價格波動大，且公司有倒閉風險。
  • Return (回報): 債券提供固定或掛鉤的利息（Interest），而股票提供浮動的股息（Dividend）及資本增值（Capital Gain）。

Fisher Equation (費沙方程式): 雖然題目沒有直接要求計算，但理解名義利率 \( i \)、實質利率 \( r \) 與通脹率 \( \pi \) 的關係有助理解通脹掛鉤債券的原理：

$$ r \approx i - \pi $$

銀色債券確保了當 \( \pi \) 上升時，\( i \) 也會跟隨上升，從而保障實質回報 \( r \)。

The Silver Bond ensures that when inflation \( \pi \) rises, the nominal interest rate \( i \) also rises, thereby protecting the real return \( r \).

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): A. 只有 (1) / (1) only

分析 (1): 正確 (Correct)
銀色債券由政府發行，違約風險極低。加上題目指明「債券持有人可在到期前將其債券以原價賣給政府」，這消除了資本虧損的風險。相反，上市公司股票價格可升可跌，風險顯著較高。

Silver Bonds are issued by the government, meaning default risk is extremely low. Furthermore, the question states bondholders can "sell their bonds at original price", eliminating capital loss risk. Conversely, share prices fluctuate, making them significantly riskier.

分析 (2): 錯誤 (Incorrect)
雖然銀色債券的回報會隨通脹上升，但說「上市公司股票的回報則不會」是錯誤的。在通脹期間，公司可能會提高產品價格，從而增加名義利潤，並可能派發更高的股息。股票通常被視為抗通脹的資產之一。

While the return of Silver Bonds increases with inflation, it is wrong to say the return of shares "would not". During inflation, companies can raise product prices, increasing nominal profits and potentially paying higher dividends. Stocks are often considered an inflation hedge.

分析 (3): 錯誤 (Incorrect)
這是一個絕對的陳述。上市公司的股息是浮動的，盈利好的公司派發的股息率絕對有可能高於銀色債券的利息（例如高於2%或當時的通脹率）。我們不能斷言債券利息必定高於股票股息。

This is an absolute statement. Dividends from listed companies are variable. A highly profitable company can easily pay a dividend yield higher than the bond's interest rate. We cannot definitively claim bond interest is always higher.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (關鍵點): 辨識選項中的「絕對字眼」（Absolute terms）。

Pivot Point: Identify absolute terms in the options.

  • 選項 (2) 中的「則不會 (would not)」過於武斷。
  • 選項 (3) 中的「較...為高 (is higher than)」沒有前設條件，忽略了股息的浮動性。
  • 題目中「以原價賣給政府」是確認風險極低的關鍵資訊（Distractor/Hint: 這是為了強化選項1的正確性）。

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼股票可以抗通脹？ (Why can stocks hedge against inflation?)

A: 通脹代表物價上升。對於企業而言，它們賣出的商品價格同樣上升，這會帶動名義收入（Nominal Revenue）增加。如果成本控制得宜，名義利潤也會上升，從而有能力派發更多股息。
Inflation means general prices are rising. For businesses, the prices of goods they sell also rise, increasing nominal revenue. If costs are managed, nominal profits rise, allowing for higher dividends.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

Elimination Method (排除法):
在經濟學多項選擇題中，帶有強烈絕對語氣的選項（如「一定」、「不會」、「總是」）通常是錯誤的。當你看到 (2) 的「則不會」和 (3) 的「較...高」（沒有給定具體數字比較），你應該立刻抱有懷疑態度。只要確定 (2) 或 (3) 其中一個是錯的，就可以大幅縮窄答案範圍。

In Economics MCQs, options with strong absolute words (like "would not", "always", "must") are frequently incorrect. Spotting these allows you to quickly eliminate distractors.
