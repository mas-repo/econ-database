HKDSE Economics Tutor - Market Intervention

課題 (Topic): Microeconomics - Market Intervention & Consumer Surplus (微觀經濟學 - 市場干預與消費者盈餘)

難度 (Difficulty): ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)

核心概念 (Key Concepts):
- Price Floor (價格下限)
- Substitute (替代品)
- Consumer Surplus (消費者盈餘)

📝 題目 (The Question)

若政府提高對物品X的有效價格下限，同時其替代品的價格上升了，在物品X市場的消費者盈餘______________。

If the government raises the effective price floor on Good X and at the same time the price of its substitute increases, the consumer surplus in the market of Good X ______________.

  • A. 會增加 / will increase
  • B. 會減少 / will decrease
  • C. 會維持不變 / will remain unchanged
  • D. 可能增加或減少 / may increase or decrease

🧠 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

1. 有效價格下限 (Effective Price Floor)

政府設定在均衡價格之上的最低合法價格。在有效價格下限下，市場價格被迫提高至下限水平，導致需求量 \(Q_d\) 小於供應量 \(Q_s\)。實際交易量取決於需求量（即 \(Q = Q_d\)）。

A legally established minimum price set above the equilibrium price. At this price, quantity demanded \(Q_d\) is less than quantity supplied \(Q_s\). The actual transacted quantity is determined by the quantity demanded (\(Q = Q_d\)).

2. 替代品 (Substitute)

兩種可以互相替代的物品。當替代品的價格上升，消費者會轉向購買物品X，導致物品X的需求增加（需求曲線向右移）。

Two goods that can be used in place of each other. When the price of a substitute increases, consumers switch to Good X, increasing its demand (demand curve shifts right).

3. 消費者盈餘 (Consumer Surplus, CS)

消費者願意支付的最高價格與實際支付價格之間的差額。在圖表上，它是需求曲線以下、價格線以上，直至交易量的面積。

The difference between the maximum amount consumers are willing to pay and the actual price they pay. Graphically, it is the area below the demand curve and above the price line, up to the transacted quantity.

$$ CS = \text{Total Willingness to Pay} - \text{Total Expenditure} $$

✅ 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option D

逐步分析 (Step-by-Step Analysis):

事件一：提高有效價格下限 (Event 1: Raising effective price floor)

提高價格下限會令物品X的價格 \(P\) 上升，同時由於價格上升，沿著需求曲線的需求量 \(Q_d\) 會減少。因為價格上升且交易量減少，消費者盈餘（CS）必然減少。

Raising the price floor increases the price \(P\) of Good X, and consequently, the quantity demanded \(Q_d\) decreases along the demand curve. Because price is higher and transacted quantity is lower, Consumer Surplus (CS) definitely decreases.

事件二：替代品價格上升 (Event 2: Price of substitute increases)

替代品價格上升會令物品X的需求增加，需求曲線向右移。這表示消費者對物品X的最高願意支付價格上升了。在任何給定的價格下，這會傾向令消費者盈餘增加。

An increase in the substitute's price increases the demand for Good X, shifting the demand curve to the right. This means consumers' maximum willingness to pay has increased. At any given price, this tends to increase Consumer Surplus.

綜合影響 (Combined Effect):

由於事件一令CS減少，而事件二令CS增加，兩股力量的方向相反。最終消費者盈餘是增加還是減少，取決於需求增加的幅度與價格下限提高的幅度之間的相對大小。因此，消費者盈餘可能增加或減少。

Since Event 1 decreases CS and Event 2 increases CS, the two forces act in opposite directions. The final effect on Consumer Surplus depends on the relative magnitude of the increase in demand versus the increase in the price floor. Therefore, CS may increase or decrease.

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

陷阱：忘記需求曲線移動
很多學生只看到「價格上升」，就立刻斷定消費者盈餘一定減少（選B）。他們忽略了替代品價格上升導致了整條需求曲線向右移動，這會創造出新的、更大的消費者盈餘面積。

Trap: Forgetting the demand curve shift
Many students only see "price increases" and immediately conclude that consumer surplus must decrease (choosing B). They ignore that the increase in the substitute's price shifts the entire demand curve to the right, which creates a new, potentially larger area of consumer surplus.

🙋 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 為什麼在價格下限時，交易量是由需求決定的？

Q: Why is the transacted quantity determined by demand when there is a price floor?

A: 因為有效價格下限高於均衡價格，導致供應量大於需求量（出現過剩）。在自由市場交易中，賣家不能強迫買家購買他們不想要的數量，所以實際成交量只能等於買家願意購買的數量（即需求量 \(Q_d\)）。

A: Because an effective price floor is above the equilibrium price, quantity supplied exceeds quantity demanded (creating a surplus). In a free market exchange, sellers cannot force buyers to buy more than they want to, so the actual transacted quantity can only equal what buyers are willing to buy (i.e., quantity demanded \(Q_d\)).

⚡ 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

「相反力量法則」 (The Opposing Forces Rule):
在DSE經濟科的MC題中，如果題目描述了兩個事件，而你發現這兩個事件對某個變數（如價格、交易量、盈餘）的推動方向是相反的（一個令其上升，一個令其下降），且題目沒有提供具體的數據或幅度，答案幾乎肯定是「不確定」 / 「可能增加或減少」。

The Opposing Forces Rule:
In DSE Economics MC questions, if the question describes two events and you realize they push a variable (like price, quantity, or surplus) in opposite directions (one increases it, one decreases it), and no specific data or magnitudes are provided, the answer is almost always "uncertain" / "may increase or decrease".
