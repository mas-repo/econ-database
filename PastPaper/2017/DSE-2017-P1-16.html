參看下列物品X的供需表。若政府對物品X開徵$2從量稅：

Refer to the following supply and demand schedules for Good X. If the government imposed a $2 per unit tax on Good X:

單位價格 / Unit price ($) 678910111213
需求量 / Quantity demanded 140130120110100908070
供應量 / Quantity supplied 60708090100110120130

A. 賣家的除稅後收入會是$990。
B. 賣家承擔的稅項負擔會是$90。
C. 總稅項收入會是$200。
D. 買家對物品X的總支出會下降$40。

B. tax burden borne by the sellers would be $90.
C. the total tax revenue would be $200.

3. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

當政府徵收從量稅 (Per-unit tax)時，供應曲線會向上平移，平移的垂直距離等同於稅額（此題為$2）。這意味著買家支付的價格 \(P_d\) 和賣家實際收取的價格 \(P_s\) 之間會產生一個差距。

When the government imposes a per-unit tax, the supply curve shifts upwards by a vertical distance equal to the tax amount (here, $2). This creates a wedge between the price buyers pay \(P_d\) and the net price sellers receive \(P_s\).

尋找新均衡點的核心條件如下：

The core conditions for finding the new equilibrium are:

$$ P_d - P_s = t $$ $$ Q_d(P_d) = Q_s(P_s) = Q_1 $$
  • \(t\): 從量稅 (Per-unit tax) = $2
  • \(P_d\): 買家支付的新價格 (New price paid by buyers)
  • \(P_s\): 賣家除稅後淨收價格 (Sellers' net price after tax)
  • \(Q_1\): 徵稅後的新交易量 (New equilibrium quantity after tax)
  • \(P_0\): 徵稅前的原有均衡價格 (Original equilibrium price)

關鍵公式 (Key Formulas):

$$ \text{Total Tax Revenue (總稅收)} = t \times Q_1 $$ $$ \text{Sellers' Tax Burden (賣家稅務負擔)} = (P_0 - P_s) \times Q_1 $$ $$ \text{Buyers' Tax Burden (買家稅務負擔)} = (P_d - P_0) \times Q_1 $$ $$ \text{Sellers' Net Revenue (賣家除稅後收入)} = P_s \times Q_1 $$

4. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): B

步驟 1：找出原本的均衡點 (Step 1: Find the original equilibrium)
在徵稅前，均衡點位於需求量等於供應量（\(Q_d = Q_s\)）的位置。從表中可見，當價格為$10時，\(Q_d = 100\)，\(Q_s = 100\)。
因此，原均衡價格 \(P_0 = \$10\)，原均衡數量 \(Q_0 = 100\)。

Before the tax, equilibrium is where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied (\(Q_d = Q_s\)). From the table, at a price of $10, \(Q_d = 100\) and \(Q_s = 100\).
Thus, Original Price \(P_0 = \$10\), Original Quantity \(Q_0 = 100\).

步驟 2：找出徵稅後的新均衡點 (Step 2: Find the new equilibrium after tax)
徵收$2從量稅後，買家價格 \(P_d\) 減去賣家價格 \(P_s\) 必須等於$2。同時，在該兩個不同價格下，需求量必須等於供應量。我們在表中尋找差距為$2的價格組合，使其對應的數量相等：
當 \(P_d = \$11\) 時，\(Q_d = 90\)。
當 \(P_s = \$9\) 時，\(Q_s = 90\)。
因為 \(\$11 - \$9 = \$2\) (等於稅額)，且兩者的數量皆為 90。所以，新均衡數量 \(Q_1 = 90\)。

After a $2 tax, the buyers' price \(P_d\) minus sellers' net price \(P_s\) must equal $2. Simultaneously, \(Q_d\) at \(P_d\) must equal \(Q_s\) at \(P_s\). We look for a price gap of $2 in the table where quantities match:
At \(P_d = \$11\), \(Q_d = 90\).
At \(P_s = \$9\), \(Q_s = 90\).
Since \(\$11 - \$9 = \$2\) (the tax), and both quantities are 90. So, New Quantity \(Q_1 = 90\).

A. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

賣家的除稅後收入 = \(P_s \times Q_1 = \$9 \times 90 = \$810\)。選項說是$990，這是買家總支出（\(\$11 \times 90\)），而非賣家淨收入。

Sellers' revenue net of tax = \(P_s \times Q_1 = \$9 \times 90 = \$810\). The option says $990, which is actually the buyers' total expenditure (\(\$11 \times 90\)), not sellers' net revenue.

B. 正確 (Correct)

賣家承擔的稅務負擔 = \((P_0 - P_s) \times Q_1 = (\$10 - \$9) \times 90 = \$1 \times 90 = \$90\)。計算完全正確。

Tax burden borne by sellers = \((P_0 - P_s) \times Q_1 = (\$10 - \$9) \times 90 = \$1 \times 90 = \$90\). This calculation is perfectly correct.

C. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

總稅項收入 = 稅額 \(\times\) 新數量 = \(\$2 \times 90 = \$180\)。選項說是$200，這是錯誤地用原數量（100）來計算。

Total tax revenue = Tax \(\times\) New Quantity = \(\$2 \times 90 = \$180\). The option says $200, which mistakenly uses the old quantity (100) for calculation.

D. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

原本買家總支出 = \(\$10 \times 100 = \$1000\)。新買家總支出 = \(\$11 \times 90 = \$990\)。支出下降了 \(\$1000 - \$990 = \$10\)，而不是$40。

Original buyers' expenditure = \(\$10 \times 100 = \$1000\). New buyers' expenditure = \(\$11 \times 90 = \$990\). The expenditure decreased by \(\$1000 - \$990 = \$10\), not $40.

5. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point):
解答這類題目的關鍵在於「不可假設交易量不變」。徵稅後，交易成本增加，必定導致市場交易量下降（無謂損失的產生）。你必須在表格中找到新的數量 \(Q_1\)，且滿足 \(P_d - P_s = \text{Tax}\)。

The Pivot Point:
The absolute key to these questions is understanding that "quantity does not remain constant". A tax increases transaction costs, inevitably reducing market quantity (creating a deadweight loss). You must find the new quantity \(Q_1\) in the table that satisfies \(P_d - P_s = \text{Tax}\).

6. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

⚠️ 陷阱一：用舊數量計算稅收 (Using old quantity for tax revenue)
很多同學一看到$2稅，就直接用原本的數量100去乘（\(2 \times 100 = 200\)），誤選C。記住，稅收是建基於新的交易量上！

⚠️ Trap 1: Using old quantity for tax revenue
Many students see the $2 tax and multiply it by the original quantity 100 (\(2 \times 100 = 200\)), mistakenly choosing C. Remember, tax revenue is based on the new, lower quantity!

⚠️ 陷阱二：混淆總支出與淨收入 (Confusing total expenditure with net revenue)
買家支付 \(\$11 \times 90 = \$990\)，這叫「買家總支出 (Total Expenditure)」。但這$990中，有$180交給了政府，賣家真正落袋的只有$810。選項A正是利用了這個盲點。

⚠️ Trap 2: Confusing total expenditure with net revenue
Buyers pay \(\$11 \times 90 = \$990\), which is the "Total Expenditure". However, out of this $990, $180 goes to the government, leaving sellers with only $810. Option A preys on this blind spot.

7. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 點解稅項負擔剛好是一人一半（買家加$1，賣家減$1）？是不是每次都會平分？

Q: Why is the tax burden shared exactly equally here (buyers pay $1 more, sellers get $1 less)? Is it always split 50/50?

A: 不是每次都平分！稅務負擔的比例取決於需求和供應的價格彈性 (Price Elasticity)。但在這條題目中，你觀察一下表格：價格每改變$1，需求量減少10，供應量也增加10。因為這兩條直線的斜率（反應程度）完全對稱，所以稅務負擔剛好是 50/50平分。如果需求較缺乏彈性 (inelastic)，買家就會承擔更多稅款。

A: No, it's not always equal! The tax burden ratio depends on the price elasticity of demand and supply. But look at the table for this specific question: for every $1 change in price, \(Q_d\) drops by 10, and \(Q_s\) rises by 10. Because the slopes (responsiveness) are perfectly symmetrical, the tax burden is split exactly 50/50. If demand were more inelastic, buyers would bear a larger share of the tax.

8. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

⚡ 10秒神速解法 (10-Second Shortcut):
剛才提到，表格的數據展現了完美的對稱性（每$1差額對應10個單位的變化）。
既然彈性對稱，稅務負擔必定平分！
總稅是$2 \(\rightarrow\) 買賣雙方各承擔$1。
原本均衡價是$10，所以新買家價 \(P_d = 10 + 1 = 11\)，新賣家價 \(P_s = 10 - 1 = 9\)。
對應數量直接看表格 \(P_d = 11\) 時 \(Q = 90\)。
連「試誤法 (Trial and Error)」都不需要，一眼看穿新均衡點！

⚡ 10-Second Shortcut:
As mentioned, the table data shows perfect symmetry (a $1 difference always corresponds to a 10-unit change).
Since elasticities are symmetrical, the tax burden MUST be split equally!
Total tax is $2 \(\rightarrow\) Buyers and sellers each bear $1.
Original price is $10, so new buyer price \(P_d = 10 + 1 = 11\), new seller price \(P_s = 10 - 1 = 9\).
Look at the table: at \(P_d = 11\), \(Q = 90\).
You don't even need Trial and Error to find the new equilibrium; you can see it instantly!
