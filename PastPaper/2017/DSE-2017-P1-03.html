Topic: Public Goods (共用品) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
Key Concepts: Public Good, Non-rivalry, Non-excludability

1. The Question (題目)

共用品

A. 是一種邊際生產成本為零的物品。
B. 只可以由政府提供。
C. 不能以私有產權保護。
D. 容許多人同時使用。

A public good

A. is a good with zero marginal cost of production.
B. can only be provided by the government.
C. cannot be protected by private property rights.
D. allows concurrent consumption by many individuals.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

What is a Public Good? (甚麼是共用品？)

在經濟學中，共用品 (Public Good) 的嚴格定義是基於其消費性質，而非由誰提供。它必須具備以下核心特徵：

In economics, the strict definition of a Public Good is based on its nature of consumption, not who provides it. It must possess the following core characteristic:

  • 非競爭性 (Non-rivalry / Concurrent consumption): 一個人的消費不會減少其他人可使用的數量。這意味著容許多人同時使用。增加一名額外使用者的邊際成本為零。
    One person's consumption does not reduce the quantity available to others. This means it allows concurrent consumption by many individuals. The marginal cost of serving an additional user is zero.
$$ MC_{\text{additional user}} = 0 $$

注意：這與邊際生產成本 \( MC_{\text{production}} \) 不同！
Note: This is different from the marginal cost of production \( MC_{\text{production}} \)!

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): D

Option A (錯誤 / Incorrect)

共用品的「邊際生產成本」通常是大於零的 \( (MC > 0) \)。例如，建造多一座燈塔是需要成本的。為零的是「增加一名使用者的邊際成本」。

The "marginal cost of production" for a public good is usually greater than zero \( (MC > 0) \). For example, building an additional lighthouse requires resources. What equals zero is the "marginal cost of serving an additional user".

Option B (錯誤 / Incorrect)

共用品不一定由政府提供。私營機構也可以提供共用品，例如免費電視廣播（透過廣告獲得利潤）。

Public goods are not necessarily provided by the government. Private firms can also provide public goods, such as free-to-air TV broadcasts (funded by advertising).

Option C (錯誤 / Incorrect)

共用品可以有私有產權保護（例如電視節目的版權），只是要排除不付費者使用的成本可能很高（非排他性）。

Public goods can be protected by private property rights (e.g., copyrights for TV programs), but the cost of excluding non-payers might be very high (non-excludability).

Option D (正確 / Correct)

這正是「非競爭性」(Non-rivalry) 的定義：容許多人同時使用，且不會互相影響。

This is exactly the definition of "Non-rivalry": it allows concurrent consumption by many individuals without affecting one another.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的關鍵點 (Pivot Point) 在於釐清「共用品」的學術定義與日常用語的分別。日常生活中，我們常以為「政府提供的就是共用品」，但在經濟學中，我們只看物品是否具備「非競爭性」。

The Pivot Point of this type of question is distinguishing the academic definition of "public goods" from everyday language. In daily life, we often assume "goods provided by the government are public goods," but in economics, we only look at whether the good possesses "non-rivalry".

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

Trap 1: Confusing Production Cost with Usage Cost (混淆生產成本與使用成本)

學生常誤選 A。請記住，生產一件共用品（如編寫一個軟件、拍攝一個電視節目）是需要龐大成本的 \( (MC_{\text{production}} > 0) \)。只有當物品已經存在時，多一個人去「看」或「用」的成本才是零 \( (MC_{\text{user}} = 0) \)。

Students often mistakenly choose A. Remember, producing a public good (like coding software or filming a TV show) requires huge costs \( (MC_{\text{production}} > 0) \). It is only when the good already exists that the cost of one more person "watching" or "using" it is zero \( (MC_{\text{user}} = 0) \).

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 既然私營機構可以提供共用品，那政府為什麼還要提供？
Q: Since private firms can provide public goods, why does the government still provide them?

A: 因為「搭便車問題」(Free-rider problem)。由於共用品通常具備非排他性（很難阻止不付費者使用），私營機構可能無法收到足夠的錢來彌補生產成本，導致市場提供的數量過少（市場失效）。因此，政府通常會介入，用稅收來提供共用品（如國防、街燈）。
A: Because of the "Free-rider problem". Since public goods are often non-excludable (hard to prevent non-payers from using), private firms may not collect enough revenue to cover production costs, leading to under-provision by the market (market failure). Therefore, the government often steps in to provide them using tax revenue (e.g., national defense, streetlights).

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

Keyword Matching (關鍵字配對):
在 DSE 經濟科中，看到「共用品 (Public Good)」，大腦應立刻反射出兩個詞：「非競爭性 (Non-rivalry)」 和 「同時使用 (Concurrent consumption)」。只要選項中出現這兩個概念的同義詞，99% 就是正確答案。直接略過那些關於「政府提供」的干擾選項！

In DSE Economics, when you see "Public Good", your brain should immediately reflect two terms: "Non-rivalry" and "Concurrent consumption". If a synonym for these concepts appears in the options, it is 99% the correct answer. Skip the distractor options about "government provision" directly!
