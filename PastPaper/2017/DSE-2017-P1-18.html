Topic (課題):
Market Intervention - Price Ceiling
市場干預 - 價格上限
Difficulty (難度):
★★☆☆☆ (Moderate / 中等)
Key Concepts (關鍵概念):
Price Ceiling 價格上限 Shortage 短缺 Non-price Competition 非價格競爭

📝The Question (題目)

假設政府對住宅單位實施有效的租金管制。下列哪項出現的可能性最低？

A. 住宅單位會出現短缺。
B. 黑市會出現。
C. 窮人能夠較容易租得住宅單位。
D. 業主會有較低意欲翻新其住宅單位作出租之用。

Suppose the government imposed an effective rent control on living units. Which of the following would LEAST likely occur?

A. There would be a shortage of living units.
B. Black market would emerge.
C. Poor people would be able to rent a living unit more easily.
D. Landlords would have a lower incentive to renovate their living units for rent.

🧠Concept Review (概念重溫)

有效價格上限 (Effective Price Ceiling / Rent Control)

政府為了保障消費者（租客），規定市場價格不能高於某一水平。要使政策「有效」，法定的最高價格 \( P_c \) 必須低於市場均衡價格 \( P_e \)。

To protect consumers (tenants), the government sets a maximum legal price. For the policy to be "effective", the price ceiling \( P_c \) must be set below the equilibrium price \( P_e \).
$$ P_c < P_e $$

主要後果 (Main Consequences):

  1. 短缺 (Shortage): 由於租金人為地壓低，需求量 \( Q_d \) 會大於供應量 \( Q_s \)。
    Because rent is artificially suppressed, quantity demanded \( Q_d \) will exceed quantity supplied \( Q_s \), leading to a shortage.
    $$ Q_d > Q_s $$
  2. 非價格競爭 (Non-price Competition): 由於不能以「價高者得」的方式分配，業主會採用其他準則（如租客背景、職業、甚至要求「黑市」枱底交易）來挑選租客。
    Since allocation cannot be based on "highest bidder wins", landlords will use other criteria (e.g., tenant's background, occupation, or demanding "black market" under-the-table payments) to select tenants.
  3. 質素下降 (Deterioration of Quality): 租金回報減少，業主缺乏誘因維修或翻新單位。
    With reduced rental returns, landlords lack the incentive to maintain or renovate the units.

✅The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): Option C

Option A: 錯誤 (因為這「極可能」發生 / Incorrect because this is HIGHLY likely)

有效的租金管制會壓低租金，導致需求量增加而供應量減少，必然產生短缺。

Effective rent control lowers the rent, increasing quantity demanded and decreasing quantity supplied, which inevitably creates a shortage.
Option B: 錯誤 (因為這「極可能」發生 / Incorrect because this is HIGHLY likely)

由於出現短缺，部分願意支付高於管制租金的租客會暗中與業主交易（例如支付「鞋金」或枱底費），從而衍生黑市。

Due to the shortage, some tenants willing to pay more than the controlled rent will secretly transact with landlords (e.g., paying "key money" or under-the-table fees), leading to the emergence of a black market.
Option C: 正確 (因為這「最不可能」發生 / Correct because this is LEAST likely to occur)

這正是本題的答案！雖然政府實施租管的原意是幫助窮人，但結果往往適得其反。在短缺情況下，業主有極大的選擇權。他們會偏好有穩定高收入、良好背景或願意支付黑市租金的租客。窮人在這些「非價格競爭」中處於劣勢，因此反而更難租到單位。

This is the answer! Although the government's intention is to help the poor, the result is often the opposite. Under a shortage, landlords have the power to choose. They will prefer tenants with stable high incomes, good backgrounds, or those willing to pay black market rates. Poor people are at a disadvantage in this "non-price competition" and will actually find it harder to rent a unit.
Option D: 錯誤 (因為這「極可能」發生 / Incorrect because this is HIGHLY likely)

由於租金被限制在低水平，業主無法透過翻新單位來收取更高的租金，因此他們會減少對單位的維修和投資。

Because rent is capped at a low level, landlords cannot charge higher rent by renovating the unit, so they will reduce maintenance and investment in the property.

🔍Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答這類題目的關鍵在於找出「樞紐字眼」(Pivot Point)：

  • 「有效」(Effective): 意味著管制發揮作用，必定會引發短缺等連鎖反應。
  • 「可能性最低」(LEAST likely): 考試中極易看錯！你需要找出一項錯誤的陳述（即與經濟學理論相悖的結果）。
The key to solving this type of question is identifying the Pivot Points:
  • "Effective": Means the control is binding, which will definitely trigger a chain reaction like shortages.
  • "LEAST likely": Very easy to misread in exams! You need to find the false statement (i.e., the outcome that contradicts economic theory).

⚠️Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：混淆「政策原意」與「實際經濟後果」

很多學生會直覺認為：「政府實施租金管制是為了幫窮人，所以窮人一定受惠。」這在經濟學上是大錯特錯的！經濟學分析的是客觀結果。價格管制破壞了價格分配資源的功能，導致窮人在非價格競爭（如靠關係、靠背景）中輸給富人。

Trap: Confusing "Policy Intention" with "Actual Economic Consequence"
Many students intuitively think: "The government imposes rent control to help the poor, so the poor must benefit." This is completely wrong in economics! Economics analyzes objective outcomes. Price controls destroy the price mechanism's ability to allocate resources, causing the poor to lose to the rich in non-price competition (e.g., relying on connections or background).

🙋Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 既然租金管制會令窮人更難租屋，為什麼現實中政府還要實施？

A: 好問題！因為租金管制能保障「現有」的租客（他們不用面對加租壓力），這群人往往有很大的政治選票力量。受害的是「未來/潛在」想租屋的人（包括很多窮人），因為他們面對短缺，根本租不到樓。經濟學告訴我們，政策往往有贏家和輸家。

Q: If rent control makes it harder for the poor to rent, why do governments still implement it in reality?
A: Great question! Because rent control protects "existing" tenants (they don't face rent hikes), and this group often has significant political voting power. The victims are the "future/prospective" renters (including many poor people) who face shortages and can't find a place at all. Economics tells us that policies always have winners and losers.

⚡Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧 (One-second Kill Trick):

在DSE經濟科中，只要見到「價格上限 (Price Ceiling) / 租金管制 (Rent Control) / 最高工時」等干預市場的政策，記住一個鐵律：「市場干預通常會帶來無效率 (Inefficiency)，並且往往會傷害它原本想幫助的弱勢群體。」 看到「窮人更容易...」這類過於美好的選項，99%是錯誤陳述，直接選它作為「最不可能」的答案！

Shortcut:
In DSE Economics, whenever you see market intervention policies like "Price Ceiling / Rent Control", remember this golden rule: "Market interventions usually bring inefficiency and often hurt the vulnerable groups they originally intended to help." If you see an overly optimistic option like "Poor people will find it easier...", it is 99% a false statement. Pick it immediately for "LEAST likely" questions!
