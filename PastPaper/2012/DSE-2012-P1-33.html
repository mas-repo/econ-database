主題 (Topic): 香港的稅制 (Tax System in Hong Kong)

難度 (Difficulty): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

核心概念 (Key Concepts): 物業稅 (Property Tax), 薪俸稅 (Salaries Tax), 豁免入息 (Exempt Income)

1. 題目 (The Question)

小玲的收入如下：
(1) 從出租物業獲得的租金
(2) 一本地上市公司所派發的股息
(3) 完成經理合約的酬金
(4) 作為經紀所收的佣金

根據香港的稅制，上列哪些收入可被徵稅？

A. 只有(1)、(3)
B. 只有(2)、(4)
C. 只有(1)、(3)、(4)
D. (1)、(2)、(3)及(4)

Serine has the following incomes:
(1) rental income from a leased flat
(2) dividends from a local listed company
(3) a gratuity after completion of contract as a manager
(4) commission received as an agent

According to the tax system of Hong Kong, which of the above incomes are subjected to taxation?

A. (1) and (3) only
B. (2) and (4) only
C. (1), (3) and (4) only
D. (1), (2), (3) and (4)

2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

香港的三大直接稅 (The Three Direct Taxes in HK):

  1. 物業稅 (Property Tax): 向香港非自住物業的租金收入徵收。
    Levied on rental income from non-owner-occupied properties in Hong Kong.
  2. 薪俸稅 (Salaries Tax): 向在香港產生或得自香港的職位、受僱工作及退休金入息徵收（包括底薪、佣金、花紅、酬金等）。
    Levied on income arising in or derived from Hong Kong from any office, employment, and pension (including basic salary, commissions, bonuses, gratuities, etc.).
  3. 利得稅 (Profits Tax): 向在香港經營行業、專業或業務而獲得的利潤徵收。
    Levied on assessable profits arising in or derived from Hong Kong from a trade, profession or business.

🚫 不須課稅的收入 (Non-Taxable / Exempt Income):

香港實行簡單低稅制，某些特定收入可獲豁免徵稅，最常見的例子包括：股息 (Dividends)、資本增值（如炒賣股票或樓宇的利潤）及遺產。

Hong Kong adopts a simple and low tax regime. Certain incomes are exempt from tax, most commonly: Dividends, capital gains (profits from trading stocks/properties), and inheritances.

📝 薪俸稅計算公式 (Salaries Tax Formula):

$$\text{Net Chargeable Income}=\text{Total Income}-\text{Deductions}-\text{Allowances}$$

其中總入息 \(Y\) 必須涵蓋所有應課稅項目，如上述的薪金、佣金及酬金。

The Total Income \(Y\) must encompass all taxable items, such as salaries, commissions, and gratuities mentioned above.

3. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): C

(1) 從出租物業獲得的租金 (Rental income from a leased flat) —— ✅ 須徵稅 (Taxable)

租金收入屬於物業的衍生利潤，須繳納物業稅。

Rental income is profit derived from a property and is subject to Property Tax.

(2) 一本地上市公司所派發的股息 (Dividends from a local listed company) —— ❌ 不須徵稅 (Not Taxable)

在香港，公司派發股息前已繳納了利得稅。為避免雙重徵稅，股東收到的股息不須再繳納任何稅款。

In Hong Kong, companies pay Profits Tax before distributing dividends. To avoid double taxation, dividends received by shareholders are exempt from tax.

(3) 完成經理合約的酬金 (A gratuity after completion of contract as a manager) —— ✅ 須徵稅 (Taxable)

約滿酬金是受僱工作收入的一部分，屬於勞力回報，須繳納薪俸稅。

A contract gratuity is considered part of the employment income (a reward for labor) and is subject to Salaries Tax.

(4) 作為經紀所收的佣金 (Commission received as an agent) —— ✅ 須徵稅 (Taxable)

佣金是因提供服務而獲得的收入。視乎經紀的受僱形式，須繳納薪俸稅（若是僱員）或利得稅（若是自僱人士）。

Commission is income earned for providing services. Depending on the agent's employment status, it is subject to either Salaries Tax (if an employee) or Profits Tax (if self-employed).

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

這道題目的「破題點」(Pivot Point) 在於識別香港稅制的豁免項目。題目列出了四種常見收入，考核學生是否知道香港的稅基相對狹窄。

The "Pivot Point" of this question lies in identifying the exemptions within the Hong Kong tax system. It tests whether students know that Hong Kong has a relatively narrow tax base.

  • 關鍵資訊： 項目 (2) 的「股息」是唯一的干擾項（Distractor）。只要你知道股息免稅，就可以立即排除所有包含 (2) 的選項。
    Crucial Info: Item (2) "dividends" is the main distractor. Knowing that dividends are tax-free allows you to immediately eliminate any option containing (2).

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

⚠️ 陷阱一：誤以為「酬金」等於「禮物」

有些同學以為「約滿酬金」(Gratuity) 是一種額外恩恤或禮物，因此不需要交稅。事實上，在合約中列明的酬金屬於「受僱工作入息」，必須繳納薪俸稅。

⚠️ Trap 1: Assuming "Gratuity" is a "Gift"
Some students think a contract gratuity is a compassionate gift and therefore tax-free. In reality, a gratuity stated in an employment contract is "income from employment" and is subject to Salaries Tax.

⚠️ 陷阱二：混淆香港與外國稅制

在美國或英國等國家，股息和資本增值通常需要繳納「股息稅」或「資本增值稅」。但香港實行簡單稅制，這兩項均免稅。考試時切勿將外國的經濟常識套用在香港稅制上。

⚠️ Trap 2: Confusing HK tax system with foreign systems
In countries like the US or UK, dividends and capital gains are usually subject to taxes. However, under Hong Kong's simple tax system, both are exempt. Do not apply foreign economic practices to DSE HK tax questions.

6. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

🙋‍♂️ 學生問：為什麼香港不對股息徵稅？這樣對富人不是很有利嗎？

Q: Why doesn't Hong Kong tax dividends? Isn't this very beneficial to the rich?

👨‍🏫 導師答： 這是為了解決「雙重徵稅」(Double Taxation) 的問題。公司在派發股息前，其利潤已經繳納了高達 16.5% 的「利得稅」(Profits Tax)。如果股東收到股息後再交一次稅，同一筆利潤就會被政府徵收兩次稅款。為維持營商環境的競爭力，香港選擇不向股東作二次徵稅。

A: This is to avoid "Double Taxation". Before a company distributes dividends, its profits have already been subjected to Profits Tax (up to 16.5%). If shareholders were taxed again on the dividends, the same pool of profit would be taxed twice. To maintain a competitive business environment, HK chooses not to tax dividends a second time.
