HKDSE Economics Tutorial (經濟學導修)

Topic (課題): Public Finance - Government Revenue Composition (公共財政 - 政府收入組成部分)

Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5 Stars)

Key Concepts (核心概念): Direct Tax (直接稅), Indirect Tax (間接稅), Percentages vs. Absolute Values (百分比與絕對值)

2. The Question (題目)

下圖顯示 2008-2009年度及 2009-2010年度香港政府一般收入帳目的組成部分。
The following charts show the composition of the General Revenue Account of Hong Kong in years 2008-2009 and 2009-2010.

2008-2009:
直接稅 (Direct tax): 46.20% | 間接稅 (Indirect tax): 22.80% | 其他收入 (Other revenues): 31.00%

2009-2010:
直接稅 (Direct tax): 38.70% | 間接稅 (Indirect tax): 26.60% | 其他收入 (Other revenues): 34.70%

根據上圖資料，在 2009-2010年度，
Based on the above data, in the year 2009-2010,

  • A. 香港出現了經濟衰退。
    there was an economic recession in Hong Kong.
  • B. 直接稅與間接稅的比率上升了。
    the ratio of direct taxes to indirect taxes increased.
  • C. 稅收總額減少了。
    the total amount of tax revenue decreased.
  • D. 稅收佔政府總收入的比例減少了。
    the proportion of tax revenue in total government revenue decreased.

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這道題目，我們必須釐清「百分比（比例）」與「絕對值（實際金額）」的分別。圓形圖（Pie chart）只能顯示各組成部分佔整體的比例，而不能反映整體的實際金額大小。
To answer this question, we must clarify the difference between "percentages (proportions)" and "absolute values (actual amounts)". A pie chart can only show the proportion of each component relative to the whole, but it cannot reflect the actual total amount.

  • 政府稅收 (Tax Revenue): 包括直接稅和間接稅。
    Includes direct tax and indirect tax.
$$Tax\ Proportion = \frac{Direct\ Tax + Indirect\ Tax}{Total\ Revenue} \times 100\%$$

只要我們不知道 $Total\ Revenue$（總收入）的實際金額，我們就無法計算出稅收的實際金額（絕對值）。
As long as we do not know the actual amount of $Total\ Revenue$, we cannot calculate the actual amount (absolute value) of tax revenue.

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

Option A: 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析： 經濟衰退的定義是實質本地生產總值（Real GDP）連續兩個季度下跌。圖表只顯示了政府收入的「百分比分佈」，完全沒有提供 GDP 或整體經濟表現的數據，因此無法推斷經濟是否衰退。
Analysis: An economic recession is defined as a decline in Real GDP for two consecutive quarters. The charts only show the "percentage distribution" of government revenue and provide absolutely no data on GDP or overall economic performance. Therefore, we cannot deduce if there was a recession.

Option B: 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析： 我們可以直接利用圖表中的百分比來計算兩者的比率（Ratio）。
2008-2009 比率 = $46.20\% / 22.80\% \approx 2.03$
2009-2010 比率 = $38.70\% / 26.60\% \approx 1.45$
比率明顯是下降了，而不是上升。
Analysis: We can calculate the ratio directly using the percentages from the charts. The ratio clearly decreased from approximately 2.03 to 1.45, it did not increase.

Option C: 錯誤 (Incorrect)

分析： 這是經典的「百分比 vs 絕對值」陷阱。雖然直接稅的百分比下降了，但我們不知道政府「總收入」的實際金額變化。如果總收入大幅增加，即使稅收所佔的百分比減少，稅收總額（絕對值）依然有可能上升。
Analysis: This is the classic "percentage vs absolute value" trap. Although the percentage of direct tax decreased, we do not know the change in the actual amount of the government's "total revenue". If the total revenue increased significantly, the total tax revenue (absolute value) could still have increased even if its percentage share decreased.

Option D: 正確 (Correct)

分析： 稅收佔政府總收入的比例 = 直接稅百分比 + 間接稅百分比。
2008-2009: $46.20\% + 22.80\% = 69.00\%$
2009-2010: $38.70\% + 26.60\% = 65.30\%$
65.30% 小於 69.00%，因此稅收佔總收入的比例的確減少了。這是一個可以直接從圖表數據（百分比）得出的客觀結論。
Analysis: Proportion of tax revenue = Direct tax % + Indirect tax %.
Since 65.30% is less than 69.00%, the proportion of tax revenue in total government revenue indeed decreased. This is an objective conclusion that can be drawn directly from the chart data (percentages).

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point):
這題的核心在於辨識「圖表類型所受的限制」。當你看到圓形圖（Pie Chart），你腦海中第一個彈出的警告應該是：「這只代表比例 (Proportions)，不代表實際數字 (Absolute Values)！」
The core of this question lies in recognizing the "limitations of the chart type". When you see a pie chart, the first warning that should pop into your mind is: "This only represents proportions, NOT absolute values!"

干擾資訊 (Distractors):
選項 A 和 C 就是利用了學生喜歡「腦補」的弱點。看到直接稅比例大幅下跌，學生容易聯想到「市民收入減少 ➔ 經濟差 ➔ 經濟衰退 / 總稅收減少」。在經濟學考試中，切忌過度推論 (Over-infer)。
Options A and C exploit students' tendency to overthink. Seeing a drop in the direct tax proportion, students might wrongly assume "citizens' income dropped ➔ bad economy ➔ recession / total tax dropped". In Econ exams, never over-infer without data.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：混淆「份額下降」與「總量下降」 (Confusing "Drop in Share" with "Drop in Total Amount")
很多同學誤以為直接稅由 46.2% 跌至 38.7%，就代表政府收少了稅。這絕對是錯誤的。
舉例：如果你有一塊薄餅，你分到 50%（半個）。後來換了一個大三倍的薄餅，你只分到 25%，雖然你的「百分比」少了，但你實際吃到的份量（絕對值）其實比以前更多！
Trap: Many students mistakenly believe that because direct tax fell from 46.2% to 38.7%, the government collected less tax. This is absolutely wrong.
Example: If you have a pizza and get 50% (half). Later, you get a pizza three times larger but only get a 25% share. Although your "percentage" decreased, the actual amount you eat (absolute value) is actually more than before!

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 為什麼其他收入 (Other revenues) 增加了，也不能代表經濟好轉？
Why doesn't the increase in "Other revenues" imply an economic recovery?

A1: 香港政府的「其他收入」通常包括賣地收入、投資回報等。有時候即使經濟不景氣，如果政府剛好賣出了一塊非常昂貴的土地，其他收入的比例也會飆升，從而壓低了稅收的百分比比例。因此，單看組成比例不能判斷整體經濟好壞。
Hong Kong government's "Other revenues" usually include land sales, investment returns, etc. Sometimes, even in an economic downturn, if the government happens to sell a very expensive piece of land, the proportion of other revenues will surge, thereby suppressing the percentage share of tax revenue. Therefore, composition ratios alone cannot determine the overall economic performance.

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧 (The "One-Second" Trick):
在選擇題中，一旦看到題目只提供圓形圖（或任何只顯示 % 的數據），你可以立即「秒刪」所有包含「總額 (Total amount)」、「實質 (Real)」、「增加/減少了多少元 (Amount increased/decreased)」等字眼的選項。在這題中，選項 A 和 C 都是關於絕對狀況的描述，可以直接剔除。剩下 B 和 D，只需按計數機簡單加減乘除即可分出勝負。
In MCQs, once you see the question only provides pie charts (or any data showing only %), you can immediately eliminate all options containing words like "Total amount", "Real", or "Amount increased/decreased". In this question, Options A and C are descriptions of absolute states and can be crossed out instantly. Leaving only B and D, a simple calculator check solves the rest.
