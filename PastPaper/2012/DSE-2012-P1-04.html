2. The Question (題目)

政府總部的某車位是預留給各政府官員使用的。這個車位屬_______。因為_______。

A. 私用品 … 它只作私人用途
B. 私用品 … 它不可能被同時共享
C. 共用品 … 政府擁有它
D. 共用品 … 沒有人享有其獨有使用權

A parking lot in the government headquarter is reserved for government officials. The parking lot is _______ because _______.

A. a private good … it is for private use only
B. a private good … concurrent consumption is not possible
C. a public good … the government owns it
D. a public good … no one has an exclusive right to use

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

經濟學中對物品的分類 (Classification of Goods in Economics)

在經濟學中，我們根據物品的消費性質（而非擁有權）來界定它是共用品還是私用品。這取決於兩個核心特徵：

In Economics, we classify goods based on their nature of consumption (not ownership) into public or private goods. This depends on two core characteristics:

  • 競用性 (Rivalry / Rival in consumption):
    一人消費會減少其他人的可消費數量。即不可能被同時共享 (Cannot be consumed concurrently without affecting others)。
    One person's consumption reduces the quantity available for others. i.e., Concurrent consumption is impossible.
  • 排他性 (Excludability):
    供應者能夠以合理的成本，阻止沒有付費的人享用該物品。
    The supplier can prevent non-payers from consuming the good at a reasonable cost.

數學表達 (Mathematical Expression)

對於純共用品 (Pure Public Good)，增加一名額外使用者的邊際成本為零：

For a pure public good, the marginal cost of providing the good to an additional user is zero:

$$MC_{\text{additional user}}=0$$

相反，對於私用品 (Private Good)，如果有額外的人想使用，就需要提供額外的數量，因此 \({MC>0}\)。

Conversely, for a private good, if an additional person wants to consume it, an extra unit must be provided, hence \({MC>0}\).

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): Option B

A. 錯誤 (Incorrect): 「私人用途」並不是區分私用品的經濟學定義。一件物品是否為私用品，取決於其消費過程是否具有競用性和排他性，與其最終用途無關。

"Private use" is not the economic definition of a private good. Whether a good is private depends on rivalry and excludability in consumption, regardless of its end purpose.

B. 正確 (Correct): 車位具有競用性。當一名官員把車停泊在該車位時，另一名官員就不可能在同一時間將車停泊在同一個車位上。也就是說，它不可能被同時共享 (concurrent consumption is not possible)。這符合了私用品 (Private Good) 的核心定義。

A parking lot is rivalrous. When an official parks their car in the space, another official cannot park in the exact same spot at the exact same time. It means concurrent consumption is not possible. This perfectly fits the definition of a Private Good.

C. 錯誤 (Incorrect): 這是學生最常中的陷阱！「政府擁有」並不等於「共用品」。政府可以提供私用品（如公屋、政府停車場、公立醫院床位），因為這些物品的消費依然具有競用性（一張病床只能睡一個人）。

This is the most common trap! "Government ownership" does not mean it is a "Public Good". The government can provide private goods (e.g., public housing, government parking lots, hospital beds) because their consumption remains rivalrous (one bed for one patient).

D. 錯誤 (Incorrect): 首先，車位是私用品。其次，政府官員實際上享有獨有使用權（透過預留車位，排除了非政府官員的使用，甚至在停泊時排除了其他官員的使用）。

First, a parking lot is a private good. Second, government officials actually do have an exclusive right to use it (the reservation excludes non-officials, and the physical act of parking excludes other officials).

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的破題關鍵 (Pivot Point) 在於分清物品的「提供者」與「物品的物理/消費屬性」。

The Pivot Point of such questions is to distinguish between the "provider" of the good and its "physical/consumption attributes".

  • 干擾資訊 (Distractors): 「政府總部 (Government headquarter)」和「政府官員 (Government officials)」。考評局故意放入這些字眼，誘使你聯想到「公共 (Public)」，從而錯誤選擇「共用品 (Public good)」。
  • The HKEAA deliberately includes words like "Government" to mislead you into thinking about "Public", tricking you into choosing "Public good".
  • 關鍵資訊 (Crucial Info): 「車位 (Parking lot)」。你只需要問自己一個問題：「兩部車可以同時停在同一個格內嗎？」 答案是不行。這就是「競用性 (Rivalry)」。
  • Just ask yourself: "Can two cars park in the exact same space at the same time?" No. This represents "Rivalry".

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

⚠️ 概念混淆 (Conceptual Confusion)：
很多學生會將 「政府提供的物品 (Goods provided by the government)」 與 「共用品 (Public goods)」 畫上等號。這是致命錯誤！

Many students equate "Goods provided by the government" with "Public goods". This is a fatal mistake!

緊記 (Remember): 共用品/私用品是根據物品的性質決定的。公廁 (Public toilet) 是政府提供的，但它是私用品（因為一個廁格同一時間只能供一人使用，具競用性）。

Public/Private goods are determined by their nature. A public toilet is provided by the government, but it is a private good (since a cubicle can only be used by one person at a time, it is rivalrous).

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

🤔 學生問 (Student): 既然政府用納稅人的錢建了這個車位，那它不是屬於公眾的嗎？為什麼叫「私用品」？

Student: Since the government built this parking lot with taxpayers' money, doesn't it belong to the public? Why call it a "private good"?

💡 導師答 (Tutor): 在日常用語中，我們可能會稱之為「公共設施」。但在經濟學中，「共用 (Public)」和「私用 (Private)」有著嚴格的數學和邏輯定義。只要增加一個人使用會妨礙其他人（即 \({MC>0}\) 或具競用性），我們在經濟學上就嚴格界定它為「私用品」。不要把日常用語帶入經濟學考試中！

Tutor: In daily language, we might call it a "public facility." But in Economics, "Public" and "Private" have strict definitions. As long as an additional user hinders others (i.e., \({MC>0}\) or it's rivalrous), it is strictly defined as a "private good." Don't mix everyday vocabulary with economic terminology!

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

「秒殺」技巧 (The 5-Second Rule):

在 DSE 選擇題中，當你看到題目問某物品是共用品還是私用品時，立即忽略「誰擁有它」或「誰付費」的資訊。直接在腦海中進行「物理測試」：

In DSE MCQs, when asked to classify a good, immediately ignore information about "who owns it" or "who pays for it". Run a physical test in your head:

  1. 這件物品可以被無數人同時享用，而不影響彼此嗎？ (例如：街燈、國防、電視廣播信號)。如果是，選「共用品」。
  2. Can it be consumed by infinite people at once without affecting each other? (e.g., streetlights, national defense, TV signals). If yes, it's a Public Good.
  3. 這件物品需要排隊嗎？佔用了就沒有了嗎？ (例如：座位、車位、圖書館的實體書、公立醫院服務)。如果是，選「私用品」。
  4. Does it require queuing? Once occupied, is it unavailable to others? (e.g., seats, parking spaces, physical library books, public hospital services). If yes, it's a Private Good.

車位明顯屬於第二種，直接鎖定 A 或 B。再看解釋，B 的「不能同時共享」完美擊中「競用性」定義，直接選 B。

A parking lot clearly falls into the second category. Lock onto A or B. B's explanation "concurrent consumption is not possible" perfectly describes rivalry. Choose B and move on!
