HKDSE Economics Problem Analysis
經濟學試題分析

Topic: Positive & Normative Economics (實證與規範經濟學) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Key Concepts: Positive Statement, Normative Statement, Testability

1. The Question (題目)

下列哪些是實證性陳述？

(1) 最低工資實施後，更多工人會以兼職的形式獲聘。
(2) 最低工資實施後，低技術工人會有較少人失業。
(3) 在計算最低工資時，膳食時間應被計入工作時間。
(4) 最低工資的實施擴大貧富的收入差距。

A. 只有(2)、(3)
B. 只有(1)、(4)
C. 只有(1)、(2)及(3)
D. 只有(1)、(2)及(4)

Which of the following statements are positive statements?

(1) More workers will be employed on a part-time basis after the imposition of a minimum wage.
(2) Less low skilled workers will be unemployed after the imposition of a minimum wage.
(3) Meal times should be counted as working time in the calculation of a minimum wage.
(4) The imposition of a minimum wage widens the income gap between the rich and the poor.

A. (2) and (3) only
B. (1) and (4) only
C. (1), (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (4) only

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

實證性陳述 (Positive Statement)

實證性陳述是關於「是甚麼」(what is)、「曾經是甚麼」或「將會是甚麼」的客觀描述。它的核心特徵是可被驗證 (Testable)。你可以透過收集數據和事實來證明它是對還是錯。請記住：錯誤的陳述也可以是實證性陳述！

A positive statement is an objective description about "what is", "what was", or "what will be". Its core characteristic is that it is testable. You can verify whether it is true or false by collecting data and facts. Remember: A statement can be factually incorrect but still be a positive statement!

規範性陳述 (Normative Statement)

規範性陳述是關於「應該是甚麼」(what ought to be) 的主觀看法。它牽涉價值觀判斷 (Value Judgements)，因此無法透過事實或數據來驗證真偽。常見關鍵字：應該 (should/ought to)、公平 (fair)、最好 (best)。

A normative statement expresses a subjective view about "what ought to be". It involves value judgements and therefore cannot be proven true or false using facts or data. Common keywords: should, ought to, fair, best.

在經濟學模型中，實證性陳述通常可以表示為變量之間的函數關係，例如檢驗最低工資 ($W_{min}$) 對失業率 ($U$) 的影響：

In economic models, positive statements can often be expressed as functional relationships between variables, such as testing the impact of minimum wage ($W_{min}$) on the unemployment rate ($U$):

$$ U = f(W_{min}) $$

If $\Delta W_{min} > 0$, we can measure the resulting $\Delta U$ to verify the statement.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 Correct Option: D. 只有(1)、(2)及(4) / (1), (2) and (4) only

分析 (1)：「更多工人會以兼職的形式獲聘。」這是一個對未來的預測。我們可以透過統計最低工資實施前後的兼職就業數據來驗證這句話的真偽。因此，它是實證性陳述。

Analysis of (1): "More workers will be employed on a part-time basis." This is a prediction about the future. We can verify its truthfulness by looking at part-time employment statistics before and after the minimum wage is implemented. Therefore, it is a positive statement. (✓)

分析 (2)：「低技術工人會有較少人失業。」根據經濟學理論，這句話很大機會是錯的（因為價格下限通常會導致盈餘/失業）。但這正是題目的陷阱！即使它是錯的，我們依然可以透過失業率數據來證明它錯。只要「可被驗證」，它就是實證性陳述。

Analysis of (2): "Less low skilled workers will be unemployed." According to economic theory, this statement is highly likely to be false (as a price floor usually causes surplus/unemployment). But this is exactly the trap! Even if it is factually incorrect, we can still use unemployment data to prove it is false. As long as it is "testable", it is a positive statement. (✓)

分析 (3)：「膳食時間應被計入工作時間。」這句話包含了「應被」(should) 一詞，純粹是個人或社會對「甚麼才算公平」的價值觀判斷，無法用數據驗證。因此，它是規範性陳述。

Analysis of (3): "Meal times should be counted..." This statement contains the word "should", which makes it purely a value judgement based on personal or societal views of fairness. It cannot be verified by data. Therefore, it is a normative statement. (✗)

分析 (4)：「擴大貧富的收入差距。」這是一個客觀現象的描述。我們可以計算堅尼系數 (Gini Coefficient) 或比較不同收入階層的數據來驗證差距是否真的擴大了。因此，它是實證性陳述。

Analysis of (4): "...widens the income gap..." This describes an objective phenomenon. We can calculate the Gini Coefficient or compare data across income percentiles to verify if the gap actually widened. Therefore, it is a positive statement. (✓)

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答這類題目的樞紐點 (Pivot Point) 是尋找「價值觀字眼」以及判斷「能否用數據驗證」。

The Pivot Point for solving this type of question is searching for "value judgement keywords" and asking "can this be verified with data?".

  • 干擾資訊 (Distractors): 題目中關於「兼職」、「低技術工人」等具體經濟細節其實都不重要。你不需要去分析這些經濟現象是否真的會發生，只需要判斷它們的性質。
  • Distractors: The specific economic details about "part-time workers" or "low-skilled workers" are actually irrelevant. You don't need to analyze if these economic phenomena will actually happen; you only need to classify the nature of the statements.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

致命陷阱：把「實證性」等同於「正確的經濟學理論」

很多學生看到選項(2)時，會立刻想到：「不對啊！最低工資高於均衡工資，會導致勞工供應量大於需求量，造成失業增加，所以失業人數應該是『較多』而不是『較少』。」然後就錯誤地把(2)剔除。
記住：實證性陳述只要求「可驗證」(testable)，不要求「必定正確」(correct)。「地球是平的」也是一句實證性陳述（因為你可以證明它是錯的）！

Fatal Trap: Equating "Positive" with "Correct Economic Theory"
Many students look at option (2) and immediately think: "Wait! A minimum wage above equilibrium causes labor supply to exceed demand, increasing unemployment. So there should be 'more' unemployed, not 'less'." They then wrongly eliminate (2).
Remember: A positive statement only needs to be "testable", not necessarily "correct". "The earth is flat" is also a positive statement (because you can prove it is false)!

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 老師，如果選項(4)改成「最低工資『不公平地』擴大貧富收入差距」，結果會不同嗎？

A: 絕對會不同！加上「不公平地」(unfairly) 這個詞後，句子就加入了主觀的價值觀判斷。到底怎樣才算「不公平」？每個人標準不同，無法用數據客觀驗證。這時它就會變成一條規範性陳述 (Normative Statement)。

Q: Teacher, if statement (4) was changed to "The minimum wage 'unfairly' widens the income gap", would the answer change?

A: Absolutely! By adding the word "unfairly", the sentence introduces a subjective value judgement. What constitutes "unfair"? Everyone has a different standard, and it cannot be objectively verified with data. It would then become a Normative Statement.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

「消除法」秒殺技巧 (The Elimination Method Shortcut)

在考場上，最快的解法是直接掃描四個句子，尋找強烈的規範性字眼（如：應、應該、必須、公平）。
1. 一眼看到選項(3)有「應」(should)。
2. 立刻判定(3)是規範性陳述，絕不是答案。
3. 劃掉所有包含(3)的選項：A 和 C 被淘汰。
4. 剩下 B (1, 4) 和 D (1, 2, 4)。兩者差別在於(2)。
5. 檢查(2)，發現它是可以用數據量度的預測（雖然理論上錯誤），所以是實證性。選擇 D。

The Elimination Method Shortcut
During the exam, the fastest way is to scan the sentences for strong normative keywords (e.g., should, must, fair).
1. You instantly spot the word "should" in option (3).
2. Immediately determine that (3) is normative and cannot be part of the answer.
3. Cross out any options containing (3): Options A and C are eliminated.
4. You are left with B (1, 4) and D (1, 2, 4). The only difference is (2).
5. Check (2). You realize it's a measurable prediction (even if theoretically false), making it positive. Choose D.
