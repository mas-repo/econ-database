HKDSE Economics Multiple-Choice Analysis

Topic: Macroeconomics - Exchange Rate (宏觀經濟學 - 匯率) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Key Concepts: Exchange Rate (匯率), Depreciation (貶值), Total Import Value (總進口價值), Linked Exchange Rate System (聯繫匯率制度)

2. The Question (題目)

日期：
2010年9月：10港元 = 1歐元
2011年7月：11.3港元 = 1歐元

上述港元兌歐元的匯率變動會引致：
A. 從歐洲來港的遊客人數減少。
B. 來自歐洲的進口貨物的價格（以港元計算）下降。
C. 來自歐洲的進口貨物的總值（以歐元計算）下降。
D. 美元兌歐元的匯率下跌。
Date | Exchange Rate:
September 2010: HK$10 = 1 Euro
July 2011: HK$11.3 = 1 Euro

The above change in the exchange rate between HK$ and the Euro will result in:
A. a drop in the number of tourists from Europe to Hong Kong.
B. a drop in the price (in terms of HK$) of imports from Europe.
C. a drop in the total value (in terms of the Euro) of imports from Europe.
D. a drop in the exchange rate of the US dollar against the Euro.

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

解答此題的核心在於理解匯率變動 (Exchange Rate Movements)及其對進出口和總值的影響。 從題目可見，購買1歐元所需的港元由10元增加至11.3元。這意味著港元貶值 (HKD depreciates)，而歐元升值 (Euro appreciates)。
The core to solving this question lies in understanding Exchange Rate Movements and their impact on imports, exports, and total value. From the data, the amount of HKD needed to buy 1 Euro increased from $10 to $11.3. This means the HKD depreciates and the Euro appreciates.
$$\text{Total Import Value (Euro)} = P_{Euro} \times Q_{Import}$$
定義 (Definitions):
  • 貶值 (Depreciation): 本國貨幣的價值相對於外國貨幣下降。
  • 進口總值 (Total Import Value): 以特定貨幣計算的進口商品總花費。公式中 \( P_{Euro} \) 是外幣定價，而 \( Q_{Import} \) 是進口數量。
Definitions:
  • Depreciation: A decrease in the value of a domestic currency relative to a foreign currency.
  • Total Import Value: The total expenditure on imported goods in a specific currency. In the formula, \( P_{Euro} \) is the foreign price, and \( Q_{Import} \) is the quantity imported.

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer: Option C

選項 A (錯誤): 港元貶值代表香港的物價對歐洲人來說變得更便宜。因此，從歐洲來港的遊客人數應該增加，而不是減少。
Option A (Incorrect): The depreciation of HKD means prices in Hong Kong become cheaper for Europeans. Therefore, the number of tourists from Europe to Hong Kong should increase, not drop.
選項 B (錯誤): 歐元升值代表歐洲貨品對香港人來說變貴了。購買相同標價1歐元的貨品，香港人以前只需付10港元，現在要付11.3港元。因此，進口貨物的港元價格會上升。
Option B (Incorrect): The appreciation of the Euro means European goods become more expensive for Hong Kongers. To buy a good priced at 1 Euro, HK people used to pay HK$10, but now must pay HK$11.3. Thus, the price of imports in HK$ will rise.
選項 C (正確): 因為歐洲貨品在香港的售價（以港元計）上升，根據需求定律 (Law of Demand)，進口數量 \( Q_{Import} \) 會下降。假設貨品在歐洲的售價 \( P_{Euro} \) 不變，以歐元計算的進口總值 \( (P_{Euro} \times Q_{Import}) \) 必然會下降。
Option C (Correct): Because the price of European goods in HK$ increases, according to the Law of Demand, the quantity imported \( Q_{Import} \) will drop. Assuming the price of the good in Europe \( P_{Euro} \) remains constant, the total value in terms of the Euro \( (P_{Euro} \times Q_{Import}) \) must drop.
選項 D (錯誤): 香港實行聯繫匯率制度 (Linked Exchange Rate System)，港元與美元掛鈎。如果港元兌歐元貶值，美元兌歐元亦會同步貶值。然而，「美元兌歐元的匯率」在香港的報價習慣是指「1歐元兌多少美元」。因為歐元升值，1歐元能兌換更多美元，所以該匯率數字實際上是上升的。
Option D (Incorrect): Hong Kong operates under a Linked Exchange Rate System, pegging the HKD to the USD. If the HKD depreciates against the Euro, the USD also depreciates against the Euro. However, "the exchange rate of the US dollar against the Euro" conventionally refers to "USD per 1 Euro" in HKDSE. Since the Euro appreciated, 1 Euro exchanges for more USD, so this exchange rate figure actually rises.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point): 看到數據的第一時間，必須立刻判斷誰升值、誰貶值。1歐元換到的港元變多（10 -> 11.3），這代表歐元變強（升值），港元變弱（貶值）。

干擾資訊 (Distractors): 題目中的具體日期（2010年9月、2011年7月）完全是干擾資訊，對解題沒有任何影響。
Pivot Point: The moment you see the data, immediately determine which currency appreciates and which depreciates. 1 Euro can exchange for more HKD (10 -> 11.3), meaning the Euro gets stronger (appreciates) and the HKD gets weaker (depreciates).

Distractors: The specific dates (September 2010, July 2011) are purely distractors and have no impact on solving the problem.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

最大陷阱：混淆「外幣總值」與「本幣總值」！
很多學生會猶豫選項 C，因為他們在想「進口需求彈性 (Elasticity of Demand for Imports)」。
注意：如果是計算港元進口總值 \( (P_{HKD} \times Q_{Import}) \)，因為 \( P_{HKD} \) 上升而 \( Q_{Import} \) 下降，總值變化確實取決於需求彈性。但題目問的是歐元進口總值！由於 \( P_{Euro} \) 不變，\( Q_{Import} \) 下跌，歐元總值必定下跌，完全不需要考慮彈性。

Biggest Trap: Confusing "Total Value in Foreign Currency" with "Total Value in Domestic Currency"!
Many students hesitate on Option C because they start thinking about the "Elasticity of Demand for Imports."
Note: If calculating the Total Import Value in HKD \( (P_{HKD} \times Q_{Import}) \), since \( P_{HKD} \) rises and \( Q_{Import} \) drops, the change in total value would indeed depend on price elasticity. But the question asks for the Total Import Value in Euro! Since \( P_{Euro} \) is constant and \( Q_{Import} \) drops, the total value in Euro MUST drop, regardless of elasticity.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

學生問： 為什麼選項 D 的匯率是「上升」？美元不是跟著港元一起貶值了嗎？
Student Q: Why does the exchange rate in Option D "rise"? Didn't the USD depreciate along with the HKD?

老師答： 沒錯，美元的購買力確實跟著港元一起相對歐元貶值了。但是，在香港經濟學的文字表達中，「A兌B的匯率」(Exchange rate of A against B) 通常是指「1個單位的B可以換多少A」。既然美元貶值、歐元升值，現在1歐元可以換到「更多」美元（例如從1.28美元變成1.45美元），所以這個匯率的「數字」是上升的，而不是下跌。
Teacher A: You are right that the purchasing power of the USD depreciated against the Euro alongside the HKD. However, in HKDSE terminology, "the exchange rate of A against B" usually means "the amount of A per 1 unit of B". Since the USD depreciated and the Euro appreciated, 1 Euro can now exchange for "more" USD (e.g., from US$1.28 to US$1.45). Therefore, the numerical figure of this exchange rate rises, it does not drop.

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

速解法 (The Arrow Method)：
在考場上，讀完題目立刻在試卷空白處畫箭頭：
HKD ↓ (貶值/變平) ， EUR ↑ (升值/變貴)
然後逐一套用：
A. 歐洲來港：來變平的地方 -> 人數 ↑ (A錯)
B. 買歐洲貨：買變貴的東西 -> 港元代價 ↑ (B錯)
C. 歐洲貨數量：變貴所以少買 -> 數量 ↓ -> 外幣總值必定 ↓ (C對，秒殺)

Shortcut (The Arrow Method):
In the exam, immediately draw arrows next to the question:
HKD ↓ (Cheaper) , EUR ↑ (More Expensive)
Then apply this to the options quickly:
A. Europe to HK: Traveling to a cheaper place -> Tourists ↑ (A is wrong)
B. Buying EU goods: Buying more expensive goods -> HKD cost ↑ (B is wrong)
C. Quantity of EU goods: More expensive so buy less -> Qty ↓ -> Value in foreign currency MUST ↓ (C is correct, instant kill!)
