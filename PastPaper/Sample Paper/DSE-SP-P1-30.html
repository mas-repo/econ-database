主題 (Topic): 貨幣政策與貨幣供應 (Monetary Policy and Money Supply)

難度 (Difficulty): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

核心概念 (Key Concepts): 擴張性貨幣政策 (Expansionary Monetary Policy)、貼現率 (Discount Rate)、法定儲備比率 (Required Reserve Ratio)、公開市場操作 (Open Market Operations)

2. 題目 | The Question

【繁體中文】

下列哪項可能會導致貨幣供應量增加？

(1) 中央銀行提高貼現率。
(2) 降低法定儲備比率。
(3) 中央銀行向一家商業銀行購買政府債券。

A. 只有(1)、(2)
B. 只有(1)、(3)
C. 只有(2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2)、(3)

【English】

Which of the following may cause an increase in money supply?

(1) The central bank raises the discount rate.
(2) The central bank lowers the required reserve ratio.
(3) The central bank purchases government bonds from a commercial bank.

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

3. 概念重溫 | Concept Review

要增加貨幣供應量，中央銀行必須採取擴張性貨幣政策 (Expansionary Monetary Policy)。這類政策旨在向銀行體系注入資金，或提高銀行的信貸創造能力（即提高銀行乘數）。

To increase the money supply, the central bank must implement an Expansionary Monetary Policy. These policies aim to inject funds into the banking system or enhance banks' ability to create credit (i.e., increase the banking multiplier).

  • 貼現率 (Discount Rate): 中央銀行向商業銀行提供短期貸款的利率。降低貼現率會鼓勵銀行借款，從而增加銀行體系的儲備。
  • The interest rate the central bank charges commercial banks for short-term loans. Lowering it encourages banks to borrow, increasing reserves in the banking system.
  • 法定儲備比率 (Required Reserve Ratio): 銀行必須將客戶存款的某個百分比作為儲備保留。降低此比率能讓銀行借出更多資金。
  • The minimum percentage of customer deposits that banks must hold as reserves. Lowering this ratio allows banks to lend out more money.
  • 公開市場操作 (Open Market Operations): 中央銀行在市場上買賣政府債券。當央行買入債券時，它會將資金支付給賣家（如商業銀行），從而增加市場上的貨幣儲備。
  • The buying and selling of government bonds by the central bank. When the central bank purchases bonds, it pays the sellers (e.g., commercial banks), thereby adding reserves to the market.
相關公式 | Relevant Formulas:
最高銀行乘數 (Maximum Banking Multiplier): $$m = \frac{1}{rrr}$$ 貨幣供應最大變動 (Maximum Change in Money Supply): $$\Delta M = \Delta R \times m$$ (其中 $rrr$ 為法定儲備比率，$\Delta R$ 為銀行體系儲備變動)

4. 答案與詳細分析 | The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案是 (Correct Option): 選項 C (Option C)

分析 (1) 中央銀行提高貼現率：錯誤 (Incorrect)

提高貼現率會增加商業銀行向央行借款的成本。銀行借款減少，導致銀行體系的儲備減少，信貸創造規模縮小，最終減少貨幣供應。這屬於緊縮性貨幣政策。

Raising the discount rate increases the cost of borrowing for commercial banks from the central bank. Banks borrow less, leading to a decrease in the banking system's reserves and credit creation, which ultimately decreases the money supply. This is a contractionary monetary policy.

分析 (2) 降低法定儲備比率：正確 (Correct)

降低法定儲備比率 ($rrr$) 意味著銀行需要保留的資金減少，可以借出的超額儲備增加。根據公式 $m = \frac{1}{rrr}$，銀行乘數會變大，信貸創造過程會擴張，從而增加貨幣供應量。

Lowering the required reserve ratio means banks need to hold fewer reserves, increasing their excess reserves available for lending. According to the formula, the banking multiplier increases, expanding the credit creation process and thereby increasing the money supply.

分析 (3) 中央銀行向一家商業銀行購買政府債券：正確 (Correct)

當中央銀行買入債券時，它會將資金存入該商業銀行的結算戶口中。這直接增加了銀行的實際儲備 ($\Delta R > 0$)。銀行有了更多的超額儲備，便能批出更多貸款，帶動貨幣供應增加。

When the central bank purchases bonds, it deposits funds into the commercial bank's clearing account. This directly increases the banks' actual reserves. With more excess reserves, banks can grant more loans, leading to an increase in the money supply.

5. 題目拆解 | Problem Decomposition

解題關鍵點 (Pivot Point): 題目的核心詞是「導致貨幣供應量增加」(cause an increase in money supply)。這代表你需要尋找能夠「放寬銀根」的政策（即增加儲備或提高乘數效應的操作）。

The core phrase is "cause an increase in money supply". This means you need to identify policies that "loosen monetary conditions" (i.e., actions that increase reserves or enhance the multiplier effect).

判斷準則 (Judgment Criteria):

  1. 資金流向：資金是流入市場（擴張），還是流回央行（緊縮）？
  2. 借貸能力：銀行的可借貸資金比例是上升（擴張），還是下降（緊縮）？

6. 學生常見疑問 | Student Q&A

Q: 為什麼央行買債券會增加貨幣供應，賣債券卻是減少呢？我經常把這兩個搞混。
Why does the central bank buying bonds increase the money supply, while selling bonds decreases it? I always mix these two up.

A: 想像一下實體現金的流動。當央行「買入」債券時，它必須付錢給賣家（銀行或公眾）。因此，央行把新創造的資金「推」進了市場，貨幣供應就增加了。相反，當央行「賣出」債券時，買家必須付錢給央行，資金就被央行從市場上「抽走」了，貨幣供應自然減少。

Imagine the flow of physical cash. When the central bank "buys" bonds, it must pay the sellers (banks or the public). Therefore, the central bank "pushes" newly created money into the market, increasing the money supply. Conversely, when it "sells" bonds, buyers must pay the central bank, meaning funds are "drained" from the market, decreasing the money supply.
