HKDSE Economics Concept Guide

Topic: Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Keywords: Scarcity, Opportunity Cost, Economic Goods

1. The Question (題目)

「稀少性」的存在意味着：
A. 人類慾望無窮。
B. 所有物品都是經濟物品。
C. 要獲得某些物品是有代價的。
D. 每一經濟都必定一個市價。
The existence of scarcity implies that:
A. human wants are unlimited.
B. all goods are economic goods.
C. there is a cost in obtaining some goods.
D. each and every economic good must have a market price.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

稀少性 (Scarcity)
Scarcity refers to the situation where limited resources are insufficient to satisfy unlimited human wants.
當資源的數量不足以滿足人類所有慾望時，就會出現稀少性。因為資源是有限的，我們不能擁有一切，所以我們必須作出選擇 (Choice)。而有選擇，就必然涉及機會成本 (Opportunity Cost)，即是必須放棄其他選項中價值最高的那一個。
When the quantity of resources is insufficient to satisfy all human wants, scarcity occurs. Because resources are limited, we cannot have everything, so we must make choices. Making choices inevitably involves an opportunity cost, which is the highest-valued option forgone.
$$ \text{Scarcity (稀少性)} \implies \text{Choice (選擇)} \implies \text{Opportunity Cost (機會成本)} $$
經濟物品 (Economic Goods)
Economic Goods are goods whose quantity supplied is less than the quantity demanded at zero price.
經濟物品是指在價格為零的情況下，其供應量小於需求量的物品。簡單來說，即是獲取該物品是需要付出代價的。
Economic goods are goods where the quantity supplied is less than the quantity demanded at zero price. Simply put, obtaining the good requires a cost or sacrifice.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是：選項 C (Option C)

The correct answer is: Option C

選項 A 分析 (錯誤)：人類慾望無窮是導致稀少性的原因 (Cause)，而不是稀少性存在的結果/意味着 (Implication)。題目問的是「意味着」(implies)，即稀少性會帶來甚麼後果。
Analysis of Option A (Incorrect): Unlimited human wants are a cause of scarcity, not an implication of it. The question asks what scarcity "implies" (its consequence).
選項 B 分析 (錯誤)：稀少性的存在代表社會上有資源不足的問題，但這不代表所有物品都是經濟物品。現實中仍然可能存在「免費物品」(Free goods)，例如沙漠中的沙或部分情境下的空氣，獲取它們無需付出代價。
Analysis of Option B (Incorrect): The existence of scarcity means there is a problem of insufficient resources in society, but it doesn't mean all goods are economic goods. "Free goods" can still exist in reality, such as sand in a desert or air in some contexts, which require no cost to obtain.
選項 C 分析 (正確)：正如概念重溫所述，稀少性 \( \rightarrow \) 選擇 \( \rightarrow \) 機會成本。因為資源稀少，我們要獲得某種經濟物品時，就必須放棄其他東西。因此，要獲得某些物品是有代價（成本）的。
Analysis of Option C (Correct): As stated in the concept review, Scarcity \( \rightarrow \) Choice \( \rightarrow \) Opportunity Cost. Because resources are scarce, when we want to obtain a certain economic good, we must give up something else. Therefore, there is a cost (sacrifice) in obtaining some goods.
選項 D 分析 (錯誤)：即使是經濟物品，也不一定要有「市價」(Market price)。代價/成本不一定是以金錢衡量。例如政府免費派發的醫療服務，其市價為 \( \$0 \)，但它仍是經濟物品，因為病人需要付出時間排隊（這就是代價）。
Analysis of Option D (Incorrect): Even for an economic good, it does not must have a "market price". Costs are not always measured in money. For example, public healthcare provided for free by the government has a market price of \( \$0 \), but it is still an economic good because patients must spend time queuing (which is the cost).

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的「破題點」(Pivot Point) 在於認清因果關係。關鍵字是「意味着」(implies)。
很多同學看到「稀少性」就立刻聯想起「慾望無窮」或「資源有限」，從而錯選 A。但考評局最喜歡考驗學生能否分辨「原因」和「結果」。
  • 原因 (Causes)： 慾望 \( > \) 資源
  • 結果 (Implications/Consequences)： 選擇、機會成本、競爭、歧視標準
The "Pivot Point" of this type of question is recognizing cause and effect. The keyword is "implies".
Many students see "scarcity" and immediately associate it with "unlimited wants" or "limited resources", mistakenly choosing A. However, the HKEAA loves to test if students can distinguish between "causes" and "effects".
  • Causes: Wants \( > \) Resources
  • Implications/Consequences: Choice, Opportunity cost, Competition, Allocation criteria

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱一：混淆「代價 (Cost)」與「市價 (Price)」
經濟學上的「代價/成本」是指機會成本，不一定涉及金錢。市價只是分配稀有資源的其中一種方式（價格機制）。排隊時間、抽籤運氣等也可以是獲取物品的代價。

陷阱二：忽略極端字眼
選項 B 中的「所有」(All) 和選項 D 中的「必定」(Must) 是常見的陷阱字眼。在經濟學選擇題中，除非是絕對的定論，否則帶有極端字眼的選項通常是錯的。
Trap 1: Confusing "Cost" with "Market Price"
"Cost" in economics refers to opportunity cost and doesn't necessarily involve money. Market price is just one way to allocate scarce resources (price mechanism). Queuing time, luck in a draw, etc., can also be the cost of obtaining a good.

Trap 2: Ignoring Extreme Words
Words like "All" in Option B and "Must" in Option D are common trap words. In Economics MCQs, unless it's an absolute theoretical certainty, options with extreme words are usually incorrect.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 如果題目問「稀少性的起因是甚麼？」，答案會是甚麼？
Q: If the question asked "What is the cause of scarcity?", what would the answer be?
A: 那麼選項 A（人類慾望無窮）就會是正確答案的一部份。完整的起因是「人類慾望無窮，但資源有限」。
A: Then Option A (human wants are unlimited) would be part of the correct answer. The complete cause is "human wants are unlimited, but resources are limited".
Q: 點解選項 C 寫「某些物品」而不是「所有物品」？
Q: Why does Option C say "some goods" instead of "all goods"?
A: 這正是考評局嚴謹的地方！因為世上仍有「免費物品」(Free goods) 存在，獲取它們不需要代價。所以只有獲取「經濟物品」（即某些物品）時才需要付出代價。
A: This shows how precise the examination board is! Because "Free goods" still exist in the world, and obtaining them requires no cost. Therefore, only obtaining "economic goods" (i.e., "some goods") requires a cost.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

速解法 (Shortcut)：聯想記憶法
在 DSE 經濟科，看到「稀少性」(Scarcity) 這個詞出現，你的大腦應該立刻自動彈出一個三角形關係：
稀少性 (Scarcity) ⇌ 選擇 (Choice) ⇌ 機會成本/代價 (Cost)
掃視四個選項，只有 C 包含了「代價」(Cost) 這個直接推論，再用極端字眼排除法剔除 B 和 D，30秒內即可選出 C。
Shortcut: Association Method
In DSE Economics, whenever you see the word "Scarcity", your brain should automatically pop up a triangular relationship:
Scarcity ⇌ Choice ⇌ Opportunity Cost/Sacrifice
Scanning the four options, only C contains the direct implication "cost". Then, use the elimination method for extreme words to cross out B and D. You can confidently select C within 30 seconds.
