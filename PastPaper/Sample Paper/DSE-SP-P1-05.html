HKDSE Economics Topic Analysis

Topic: Types of Goods: Public vs. Private Goods (物品的種類：共用品與私用品)

Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆

Key Concepts: Non-rivalry (共享性 / 非競爭性), Non-excludability (非排他性), Private Good (私用品)

2. The Question (題目)

下列哪項是共用品的例子？

A. 某慈善機構提供的一個自修室
B. 一個公眾足球場
C. 一首李白的詩
D. 一幢被棄置工廠大廈

Which of the following is an example of public good?

A. a study room provided by a charity organization
B. a public football court
C. a poem by Li Bai (李白)
D. an abandoned factory building

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

要判斷一件物品是否為共用品 (Public Good)，我們必須檢查它是否同時具備以下兩個特徵：

  1. 共享性 / 非競爭性 (Non-rivalry in consumption): 一個人享用該物品，不會減少其他人可享用的數量或質量。提供給額外一名消費者的邊際成本為零。
  2. 非排他性 (Non-excludability in consumption): 供應者無法（或成本極高以致不切實際）阻止沒有付費的人享用該物品。

To determine if a good is a Public Good, we must check if it possesses BOTH of the following characteristics:

  1. Non-rivalry in consumption: One person's consumption does not reduce the quantity or quality available to others. The marginal cost of serving an additional consumer is zero.
  2. Non-excludability in consumption: It is impossible (or prohibitively expensive) to prevent non-payers from consuming the good.

共用品的邊際成本條件 (Marginal Cost Condition for Public Goods):

$$ MC_{\text{additional user}} = 0 $$

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: C

選項 A: 某慈善機構提供的一個自修室 (a study room provided by a charity organization)

錯誤 (Incorrect)。 自修室的座位有限。當一個人佔用了座位，其他人就不能使用該座位，這具有競爭性 (Rivalrous)。管理員也可以檢查身分或鎖門來阻止某些人進入，這具有排他性 (Excludable)。即使它是免費提供的，它在經濟學上仍然是私用品 (Private Good)。

Seats in a study room are limited. If one person occupies a seat, others cannot use it, making it rivalrous. The administrator can also lock the door or check IDs, making it excludable. Even if provided for free by a charity, it is economically a Private Good.

選項 B: 一個公眾足球場 (a public football court)

錯誤 (Incorrect)。 「公眾」只是指由政府擁有，並不代表它是經濟學上的共用品。足球場的空間有限，同時有太多人使用會造成擠迫，具有競爭性 (Rivalrous)。政府亦可以鎖上球場大閘，具有排他性 (Excludable)。這是一件私用品。

"Public" simply means it is owned by the government, not that it is an economic public good. Space is limited, and congestion occurs if too many people use it (Rivalrous). The government can also lock the gates (Excludable). This is a Private Good.

選項 C: 一首李白的詩 (a poem by Li Bai)

正確 (Correct)。 詩詞是一種無形的知識 / 意念。你背誦或欣賞這首詩，完全不會減少其他人欣賞這首詩的機會，具有共享性 (Non-rivalrous)。同時，一旦詩詞流傳開來，你幾乎不可能阻止沒有付費的人去閱讀或背誦它，具有非排他性 (Non-excludable)。因此，它是共用品。

A poem is intangible knowledge/idea. Your recitation or appreciation of the poem does not reduce others' ability to do the same, making it non-rivalrous. Once the poem is published and known, it is almost impossible to stop non-payers from reading or remembering it, making it non-excludable. Therefore, it is a Public Good.

選項 D: 一幢被棄置工廠大廈 (an abandoned factory building)

錯誤 (Incorrect)。 實體的建築物具有競爭性（你佔用了某個空間，別人就不能用）及排他性（可以建圍欄或上鎖）。「被棄置」只代表暫時沒有人使用或需求低，並不改變物品本身的私用品性質。

Physical buildings are rivalrous (if you occupy a space, others cannot) and excludable (fences or locks can be installed). The fact that it is "abandoned" merely means it is currently unused or has low demand; it does not change its nature as a Private Good.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

關鍵突破點 (Pivot Point): 區分「提供者 (Provider)」與「物品的本質 (Nature of the good)」。

題目故意使用了「慈善機構」和「公眾」等字眼來混淆考生。在 DSE 經濟科中，我們絕對不能看見「免費」或「公眾/政府」就認定它是共用品。判斷的唯一標準是物品本身的物理或概念性質：它會不會被「用盡」？能不能「上鎖」？

Pivot Point: Distinguishing between the "Provider" and the "Nature of the good".

The question deliberately uses distractors like "charity" and "public" to confuse candidates. In DSE Economics, we must never assume something is a public good just because it is "free" or provided by the "public/government". The sole criteria are the physical or conceptual properties: can it be "used up"? Can it be "locked up"?

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：將「公眾提供的物品 (Publicly provided goods)」與「共用品 (Public goods)」混為一談。

政府提供的物品（如公立醫院、圖書館、公路、官立學校）絕大多數都是私用品 (Private Goods)，因為它們都有容量限制（競爭性）。「共用品」是一個嚴格的經濟學定義，通常只有國防、街燈、無形的知識（如詩詞、數學公式）才能完全符合。

Trap: Confusing "Publicly provided goods" with "Public goods".

Goods provided by the government (e.g., public hospitals, libraries, roads, government schools) are overwhelmingly Private Goods because they have capacity limits (rivalry). "Public Good" is a strict economic definition; usually, only national defense, streetlights, and intangible knowledge (like poems, math formulas) fully meet the criteria.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

問：如果我買了一本印有李白詩集的實體書，那本書是共用品嗎？

答：不是。 印有詩詞的「實體書」是私用品（你拿走了，別人就不能拿），但「詩詞的內容 / 意念」本身是共用品。考評局問「一首李白的詩」，指的是詩的意念，所以是共用品。

Q: If I buy a physical book containing Li Bai's poems, is that physical book a public good?

A: No. The "physical book" printed with the poem is a private good (if you take it, someone else cannot), but the "content/idea of the poem" itself is a public good. When the HKEAA asks about "a poem by Li Bai", they are referring to the intangible idea, making it a public good.

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧 (The 5-Second Rule)：

在 DSE 選擇題中尋找共用品時，直接尋找「無形 (Intangible)」的選項（如：知識、意念、電視訊號、燈光）。任何有實體形狀、佔用物理空間的物品（如建築物、蘋果、實體書），因為會產生擠擁 (Congestion)，幾乎 100% 是私用品。這能幫你快速刪除 A、B、D！

The 5-Second Rule:

When looking for a public good in DSE MCQs, immediately search for the "Intangible" option (e.g., knowledge, ideas, TV signals, light). Anything with a physical form that occupies space (like buildings, apples, physical books) will cause congestion and is almost 100% a private good. This helps you instantly eliminate A, B, and D!
