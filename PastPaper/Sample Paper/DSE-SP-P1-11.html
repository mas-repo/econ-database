2. 題目 | The Question

假設歐盟就出口到其成員國的中國製皮鞋徵收一項進口附加稅。在這些歐盟成員國內，

  1. 這些貨品的價格會上升。
  2. 這些貨品的價格會下降。
  3. 花費在這些貨品的總開支會上升。
  4. 花費在這些貨品的總開支會下降。

Suppose the European Union imposes an import surcharge on China-made leather shoes exported to its member countries. In the member countries of the European Union,

  1. the price of these goods will rise.
  2. the price of these goods will fall.
  3. the total expenditure on these goods will rise.
  4. the total expenditure on these goods will fall.

3. 概念重溫 | Concept Review

1. 進口附加稅 (Import Surcharge / Tax)
對進口貨品徵收的稅項會增加供應商的生產成本。這會導致供應減少，供應曲線向左方（或上方）移動。在需求不變的情況下，均衡價格 $P$ 會上升，而均衡交易量 $Q$ 會下降。

1. Import Surcharge / Tax
A tax imposed on imported goods increases the cost of production for suppliers. This leads to a decrease in supply, shifting the supply curve to the left (or upward). Assuming demand remains constant, the equilibrium price $P$ will rise, and the equilibrium quantity $Q$ will fall.

2. 總開支 (Total Expenditure, TE)
總開支等於消費者購買貨品時支付的價格乘以數量。

2. Total Expenditure (TE)
Total expenditure equals the price paid by consumers multiplied by the quantity purchased.

$$TE = P \times Q$$

3. 需求價格彈性 (Price Elasticity of Demand, $E_d$)
當價格 $P$ 上升而數量 $Q$ 下降時，總開支 $TE$ 的最終變化取決於需求彈性：
- 若需求屬低彈性 (Inelastic, $E_d < 1$)：價格上升的幅度大於數量下降的幅度，$TE$ 會上升。
- 若需求屬高彈性 (Elastic, $E_d > 1$)：數量下降的幅度大於價格上升的幅度，$TE$ 會下降。
- 若需求屬單一彈性 (Unitary Elastic, $E_d = 1$)：$TE$ 維持不變。

3. Price Elasticity of Demand ($E_d$)
When price $P$ increases and quantity $Q$ decreases, the ultimate change in total expenditure $TE$ depends on the price elasticity of demand:
- If demand is inelastic ($E_d < 1$): The % increase in price outweighs the % decrease in quantity, so $TE$ rises.
- If demand is elastic ($E_d > 1$): The % decrease in quantity outweighs the % increase in price, so $TE$ falls.
- If demand is unitary elastic ($E_d = 1$): $TE$ remains unchanged.

4. 答案與詳細分析 | The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案 Correct Option: A

選項 A (正確): 徵收進口附加稅會令中國製皮鞋在歐盟市場的供應減少（供應曲線左移）。因此，市場均衡價格必定會上升。

Option A (Correct): Imposing an import surcharge reduces the supply of China-made leather shoes in the EU market (supply curve shifts left). Therefore, the market equilibrium price must inevitably rise.

選項 B (錯誤): 如上所述，供應減少會導致價格上升，而非下降。

Option B (Incorrect): As explained above, a decrease in supply leads to an increase in price, not a decrease.

選項 C & D (錯誤): 雖然我們知道價格 $P$ 會上升，交易量 $Q$ 會下降，但題目並沒有提供關於中國製皮鞋在歐盟市場的「需求價格彈性」的任何資訊。既然我們不知道需求是高彈性還是低彈性，就無法確定總開支 $TE$ 到底是上升、下降還是不變。因此，C和D都不是必然發生的結果。

Option C & D (Incorrect): Although we know price $P$ will rise and quantity $Q$ will fall, the question does not provide any information regarding the "price elasticity of demand" for these shoes in the EU market. Without knowing whether demand is elastic or inelastic, it is impossible to determine whether total expenditure $TE$ will rise, fall, or remain unchanged. Thus, neither C nor D is a definitive outcome.

5. 題目拆解 | Problem Decomposition

關鍵突破點 (Pivot Point): 本題的破題關鍵在於識別出「資訊不足」。當題目要求判斷「總開支 / 總收入 (Total Expenditure / Total Revenue)」的變化時，你必須反射性地尋找「彈性 (Elasticity)」的線索。找不到彈性數據，就無法判斷 TE 的去向。

Pivot Point: The key to cracking this question is identifying "insufficient information". Whenever a question asks you to determine the change in "Total Expenditure / Total Revenue", you must instinctively look for clues about "Elasticity". Without data on elasticity, you cannot deduce the direction of TE.

干擾資訊 (Distractors): 題目中的「中國製」、「皮鞋」、「歐盟」都只是情境包裝，對於經濟學推論沒有實質影響。不要自行假設「皮鞋是必需品所以需求屬低彈性」，在經濟學選擇題中，未給出的條件絕不能自行假設。

Distractors: Terms like "China-made", "leather shoes", and "EU" are purely contextual dressing and have no material impact on the economic deduction. Do not make personal assumptions like "leather shoes are necessities, so demand is inelastic." In Economics MCQs, never assume conditions that are not explicitly stated.

6. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 | Common Mistakes & Traps

❌ 錯誤直覺： 很多學生會誤以為「物品變貴了，大家買這件物品的總開支就會增加」。

❌ Careless Intuition: Many students mistakenly believe that "if an item becomes more expensive, people's total expenditure on it will automatically increase."

💡 真相： 價格上升確實會增加每單位的開支，但同時會令購買數量減少（需求定律）。如果大家因為太貴而大幅減少購買（高彈性），總開支反而會下降！

💡 The Truth: While a price increase does raise the expenditure per unit, it also decreases the quantity purchased (Law of Demand). If consumers drastically cut back on buying because it's too expensive (elastic demand), total expenditure will actually fall!

7. 學生常見疑問 | Student Q&A

Q: 稅收不會影響需求曲線嗎？ (Doesn't a tax affect the demand curve?)

A: 不會。進口稅是向供應商徵收的（或者增加其成本），這會令供應曲線移動。對消費者而言，他們只是面對一個更高的價格，從而減少購買量。這是沿著同一條需求曲線的移動（需求量下降），而非需求曲線整條移動。
A: No. An import tax is levied on suppliers (or increases their costs), which shifts the supply curve. For consumers, they simply face a higher price and consequently buy less. This is a movement along the same demand curve (a decrease in quantity demanded), not a shift of the demand curve itself.

Q: 如果題目加上「對中國皮鞋的需求屬低彈性 (inelastic demand)」，答案會是甚麼？ (What if the question added that "demand for China leather shoes is inelastic"?)

A: 如果明確指出需求屬低彈性 ($E_d < 1$)，那麼當價格上升時，數量下降的百分比會小於價格上升的百分比。在這種情況下，總開支 ($TE$) 必然會上升。那麼選項 A 和 C 皆會是正確的（當然在實際考試中，只會有一個最準確的選項）。
A: If it explicitly stated that demand is inelastic ($E_d < 1$), then when the price rises, the percentage drop in quantity will be smaller than the percentage rise in price. In this scenario, total expenditure ($TE$) would definitely rise. Thus, both options A and C would technically occur (though in a real exam, there is only one best option).

8. 考試技巧與速解 | Exam Tricks & Shortcuts

「秒殺」技巧 (The Elimination Method):
在 DSE 經濟科卷一中，如果你看到選項包含「總收入 / 總開支上升或下降」，請立刻在題目中尋找「彈性 (Elasticity)」這個關鍵字。如果題目由頭到尾都沒有暗示彈性，你可以立即淘汰所有關於總開支/總收入確定變化的選項（直接劃掉 C 和 D）。剩下只需要判斷價格升跌，便能瞬間選出 A。

The "Instant Kill" Trick:
In DSE Economics Paper 1, if you see options involving "Total Revenue / Total Expenditure rising or falling", immediately scan the question text for the keyword "Elasticity". If there is no hint about elasticity whatsoever, you can instantly eliminate any options that make definitive claims about total expenditure/revenue (cross out C and D right away). You are then left to simply determine if the price goes up or down, allowing you to choose A in seconds.
