2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

A. 公營部門規模 (Size of the Public Sector)

公營部門的規模通常以政府開支佔本地生產總值的百分比來衡量。如果政府開支的增長率大於GDP的增長率，公營部門的規模就會擴大。

The size of the public sector is usually measured by the percentage of government expenditure to GDP. If the growth rate of government spending is greater than the growth rate of GDP, the size of the public sector increases.

$$ \text{Size of Public Sector} = \frac{\text{Government Expenditure (G)}}{\text{Nominal GDP}} \times 100\% $$

B. 變動百分率 vs 絕對值 (Percentage Change vs Absolute Value)

只要變動百分率是正數（\( > 0\% \)），其絕對值（名義數值）就在上升。變動百分率下跌（例如由 9% 跌至 5.5%）只代表「增長放緩」，並不代表數值下跌。

As long as the percentage change is positive (\( > 0\% \)), the absolute (nominal) value is increasing. A drop in the percentage change (e.g., from 9% to 5.5%) only means "slower growth", not a decrease in value.

C. 名義與實質的關係 (Nominal vs Real Values)

實質增長率大約等於名義增長率減去通貨膨脹率。

The real growth rate is approximately equal to the nominal growth rate minus the inflation rate.

$$ \text{Real Growth Rate} \approx \text{Nominal Growth Rate} - \text{Inflation Rate} $$

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Answer)：Option B

陳述 (1) - 正確 (Correct)

在2012年，名義政府開支的增長率是 6.0%，而名義GDP的增長率是 5.5%。因為分子的增長（6.0%）快於分母的增長（5.5%），所以政府開支佔GDP的比例上升了，即公營部門規模增大。

In 2012, the nominal government spending growth rate was 6.0%, while the nominal GDP growth rate was 5.5%. Because the numerator grew faster (6.0%) than the denominator (5.5%), the ratio of government spending to GDP increased, meaning the public sector size increased.

陳述 (2) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

在2012年，名義GDP和名義政府開支的增長率分別是 5.5% 和 6.0%。兩者都是正數（\( > 0 \)）。這意味著它們的絕對數值仍然在上升，只是上升的速度比2011年慢。因此，它們並沒有下跌。

In 2012, the growth rates of nominal GDP and nominal government spending were 5.5% and 6.0% respectively. Both are positive numbers (\( > 0 \)). This means their absolute values were still increasing, just at a slower pace than in 2011. Therefore, they did not drop.

陳述 (3) - 正確 (Correct)

實質增長率 \(\approx\) 名義增長率 \(-\) 通脹率。如果2012年的通脹率極高（例如 8.0%），那麼實質GDP增長率將是 \( 5.5\% - 8.0\% = -2.5\% \)，而實質政府開支增長率將是 \( 6.0\% - 8.0\% = -2.0\% \)。兩者均為負數，因此實質數值「可能」下跌。

Real growth rate \(\approx\) Nominal growth rate \(-\) Inflation rate. If the inflation rate in 2012 was very high (e.g., 8.0%), then the real GDP growth rate would be \( 5.5\% - 8.0\% = -2.5\% \), and the real government spending growth rate would be \( 6.0\% - 8.0\% = -2.0\% \). Both are negative, so a drop in real values is "possible".

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (破題關鍵): 區分「增長率下跌」與「數值下跌」。表格顯示的是「按年變動百分率」，不是絕對金額。

Distinguish between a "drop in growth rate" and a "drop in value". The table shows "percentage change", not absolute amounts.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

  • 陷阱 (Trap): 看到 2011 年的 9.0% 跌至 2012 年的 5.5%，學生直覺認為 GDP 「下跌」了。
  • Seeing 9.0% in 2011 drop to 5.5% in 2012, students intuitively think GDP "dropped".
  • 真相 (Truth): 只要增長率大於 0，數值就在增加。這叫「增長放緩」(Slowdown in growth)，不是「收縮」(Contraction)。

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼陳述(3)說「可能」下跌，而不是「一定」下跌？
Why does statement (3) say a drop is "possible" rather than "certain"?

A: 因為題目沒有提供物價指數（通脹率）的數據。如果通脹率是 2%，實質數值就會上升；如果通脹率是 10%，實質數值就會下跌。因為缺乏數據，所以兩種情況都有「可能」。
Because the question does not provide data on the price index (inflation rate). If inflation was 2%, real values would rise; if inflation was 10%, real values would drop. Due to the lack of data, both scenarios are "possible".
