課題 (Topic): 外部性與市場干預 - 私人得益與外部得益
Externalities and Market Intervention - Private vs. External Benefits
難度 (Difficulty): ★★☆☆☆
關鍵概念 (Key Concepts): 私人得益 (Private Benefit), 外部得益 (External Benefit), 第三者 (Third Party)

題目 (The Question)

韓國的流行歌手在香港越來越受歡迎。對這些歌手來說，下列哪些屬於他們的私人得益？

(1) 出售他們的唱片所得的版稅收入增加了。
(2) 韓國所設計的服裝其銷售收益增加了。
(3) 到訪韓國的遊客人數增加了。
(4) 他們在香港舉行演唱會所分成的收益增加了。

A. 只有(1)、(3)
B. 只有(1)、(4)
C. 只有(2)、(3)
D. 只有(2)、(4)

Korean pop singers are becoming more popular in Hong Kong. To these singers, which of the following are their private benefits?

(1) the rise in royalties from the sales of their albums
(2) the rise in sales revenue of Korean-designed fashion
(3) the rise in the number of tourists visiting Korea
(4) the rise in revenue shared from their concerts held in Hong Kong

A. (1) and (3) only
B. (1) and (4) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (2) and (4) only

概念重溫 (Concept Review)

私人得益 (Private Benefit): 指決策者（參與經濟活動的買方或賣方）直接獲得的得益。
外部得益 (External Benefit): 指沒有參與該項決策的第三者（Third Party）所獲得的得益，且他們無需為此支付補償。
Private Benefit: The benefit received directly by the decision-maker (buyer or seller) involved in an economic activity.
External Benefit: The benefit received by a third party not directly involved in the decision-making process, for which they do not have to pay.
$$ \text{Social Benefit (社會得益)} = \text{Private Benefit (私人得益)} + \text{External Benefit (外部得益)} $$
在本題中，決策者/主體是「韓國流行歌手」。只有直接落入他們口袋的利益，才算作他們的私人得益 \( \text{Private Benefit} \)。
In this question, the decision-makers/subjects are the "Korean pop singers". Only the benefits that go directly into their pockets are considered their private benefits \( \text{Private Benefit} \).

答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): B

正確 (1) 出售他們的唱片所得的版稅收入增加了。
版稅是直接支付給歌手的報酬。由於這筆收入直接歸歌手所有，因此屬於他們的私人得益。
Correct (1) the rise in royalties from the sales of their albums
Royalties are payments made directly to the singers. Since this income goes directly to them, it is their private benefit.
錯誤 (2) 韓國所設計的服裝其銷售收益增加了。
服裝銷售的收益歸服裝設計師或零售商（第三者）所有，而不是歌手。這對歌手來說屬於外部得益（如果我們從社會整體的角度來看），而非私人得益。
Incorrect (2) the rise in sales revenue of Korean-designed fashion
The revenue from fashion sales goes to fashion designers or retailers (third parties), not the singers. This is an external benefit generated by the singers' popularity, not a private benefit to them.
錯誤 (3) 到訪韓國的遊客人數增加了。
遊客增加帶來的收益歸航空公司、酒店和旅遊業（第三者）所有，歌手並不會直接從中獲得金錢回報。
Incorrect (3) the rise in the number of tourists visiting Korea
The revenue from increased tourism goes to airlines, hotels, and the tourism industry (third parties). The singers do not directly receive monetary returns from this.
正確 (4) 他們在香港舉行演唱會所分成的收益增加了。
演唱會的分成收益是直接支付給歌手的，因此這絕對是他們的私人得益。
Correct (4) the rise in revenue shared from their concerts held in Hong Kong
The revenue shared from concerts is paid directly to the singers, making it definitively their private benefit.

題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point): 題目的關鍵字是「對這些歌手來說 (To these singers)」及「私人得益 (private benefits)」。

你需要問自己一個簡單的問題：「這筆錢最後進了誰的口袋？」

  • 如果進了歌手的口袋 \(\rightarrow\) 私人得益。
  • 如果進了其他行業（服裝業、旅遊業）的口袋 \(\rightarrow\) 對歌手而言，這是他們創造的外部得益（惠及第三者）。

Pivot Point: The keywords in the question are "To these singers" and "private benefits".

You need to ask yourself one simple question: "Whose pocket does this money end up in?"

  • If it goes into the singers' pockets \(\rightarrow\) Private Benefit.
  • If it goes into other industries' pockets (fashion, tourism) \(\rightarrow\) It is an external benefit created by the singers (benefiting third parties).

常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

混淆「得益的創造者」與「得益的接收者」
學生常犯的錯誤是認為「因為是歌手帶動了韓國服裝和旅遊業，所以這些都是歌手的得益」。在經濟學中，我們必須嚴格區分誰是接收者。歌手創造了價值，但如果他們沒有從中收費，這就成為了正外部性（Positive Externality）。
Confusing the "Creator of the benefit" with the "Receiver of the benefit"
A common mistake is thinking, "Because the singers boosted Korean fashion and tourism, these must be the singers' benefits." In Economics, we must strictly distinguish who the receiver is. The singers created the value, but if they don't charge for it, it becomes a positive externality.

學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

問：如果題目說「歌手自己開設了服裝品牌」，那麼(2)會變成私人得益嗎？
答：會的！如果題目明確指出歌手是服裝品牌的擁有者，那麼服裝銷售的收益就會直接進入他們的口袋，這時就會變成他們的私人得益。但由於題目沒有這樣假設，我們只能按常理將服裝業視為第三者。
Q: If the question stated "the singers started their own fashion brand", would (2) become a private benefit?
A: Yes! If the question explicitly stated that the singers owned the fashion brands, the sales revenue would go directly into their pockets, making it a private benefit. However, since the question doesn't state this, we must assume the fashion industry is a third party.

考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

代名詞速算法 (Pronoun Shortcut):
在閱讀選項時，留意代名詞。選項(1)提到「他們的唱片 (their albums)」，選項(4)提到「他們...所分成 (shared from their concerts)」。這些字眼強烈暗示了利益直接歸屬於主體（歌手）。而(2)和(3)只提及廣泛的行業（韓國服裝、韓國旅遊），顯然是第三者。
Pronoun Shortcut:
Pay attention to pronouns when reading the options. Option (1) mentions "their albums", and Option (4) mentions "shared from their concerts". These words strongly imply the benefits belong directly to the subjects (the singers). Options (2) and (3) only mention broad industries (Korean fashion, Korean tourism), which are clearly third parties.
