HKDSE Economics Tutor Guide

📌 Topic (課題): 企業融資與商業擁有權 / Corporate Financing and Business Ownership

⭐ Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆

🔑 Key Concepts (核心概念): 債券 (Bonds), 股票 (Shares), 股東控制權 (Shareholders' Control)

The Question (題目)

某公眾有限公司計劃發行新的債券，以擴展業務。對現有的股東來說，下列哪項是發行債券而非股票的優點？

  1. 毋須向公眾人士披露公司的帳戶資料。
  2. 若公司清盤，他們會優先取回他們的資本。
  3. 付予債券持有人的利息比付予股東的股息低。
  4. 他們對公司的控制權不會被削弱。

A public limited company plans to issue new bonds for expansion. For the existing shareholders, which of the following is an advantage of issuing bonds instead of shares?

  1. The accounts of the company need not be disclosed to the public.
  2. They have priority in getting back their capital if the company liquidates.
  3. The interest paid to the bondholders is lower than the dividend paid to the shareholders.
  4. Their power of control over the company will not be diluted.

Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這道題目，我們必須清楚區分債務融資（發行債券）與股本融資（發行股票）的特質。

  • 股票 (Shares): 代表公司的擁有權。新股東會有投票權，這會攤薄現有股東的控制權。股息（Dividends）不是保證的。
  • 債券 (Bonds): 代表公司的債務。債券持有人是債權人，沒有投票權，因此不會影響現有股東的控制權。利息是固定的。

To answer this question, we must clearly distinguish between debt financing (issuing bonds) and equity financing (issuing shares).

  • Shares: Represent ownership. New shareholders gain voting rights, which dilutes the control of existing shareholders. Dividends are not guaranteed.
  • Bonds: Represent debt. Bondholders are creditors with no voting rights, so existing shareholders' control remains unaffected. Interest is fixed.

股東控制權比例 (Shareholders' Control Percentage):

$$ \text{Control Percentage} = \frac{\text{Shares Owned}}{\text{Total Shares Outstanding}} \times 100\% $$

發行新股票會增加分母 \( \text{Total Shares Outstanding} \)，從而降低現有股東的控制權比例。
Issuing new shares increases the denominator \( \text{Total Shares Outstanding} \), thereby reducing the control percentage of existing shareholders.

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): D

Option A (選項 A) - 錯誤 / Incorrect
公眾有限公司（Public limited company）無論發行股票還是債券，都必須向公眾披露財務狀況。
A public limited company must disclose its financial accounts to the public regardless of whether it issues shares or bonds.
Option B (選項 B) - 錯誤 / Incorrect
題目問的是對「現有股東」的優點。若公司清盤，債券持有人（債權人）有優先權，股東是最後取回資本的。因此這對股東並非優點。
The question asks for the advantage to "existing shareholders". In liquidation, bondholders (creditors) have priority, while shareholders are the last to claim assets. Thus, this is not an advantage for shareholders.
Option C (選項 C) - 錯誤 / Incorrect
債息與股息之間沒有絕對的高低之分。股息取決於公司的盈利和派息政策，而債息取決於市場利率和公司風險。
There is no absolute rule that interest is lower than dividends. Dividends depend on profits and dividend policy, while interest depends on market rates and company risk.
Option D (選項 D) - 正確 / Correct
債券持有人沒有投票權。因此，發行債券不會增加擁有投票權的人數，現有股東對公司的控制權不會被削弱（攤薄）。
Bondholders do not have voting rights. Therefore, issuing bonds does not increase the number of voters, meaning the existing shareholders' power of control will not be diluted.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題關鍵 (Pivot Point): 留意題目中的「對現有的股東來說 (For the existing shareholders)」。

很多學生會誤選 B，因為他們只記住了「債券有優先清盤權」，卻忘記了這個優先權是屬於「債券持有人」的，這反而對「股東」是不利的！

Pivot Point: Pay close attention to the phrase "For the existing shareholders".

Many students mistakenly choose B because they remember "bonds have priority in liquidation", but they forget this priority belongs to "bondholders", which is actually a disadvantage for "shareholders"!

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

視角錯誤 (Wrong Perspective): 學生經常混淆「公司視角」、「債權人視角」和「股東視角」。做這類題目時，必須先圈出題目要求代入的角色（本題為現有股東）。

Wrong Perspective: Students often confuse the "company's perspective", "creditor's perspective", and "shareholder's perspective". Always circle the specific role the question asks you to assume (in this case, existing shareholders).

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼發行股票會削弱控制權？

A: 假設公司原本有 100 股，你擁有 51 股（擁有 51% 控制權，絕對控股）。如果公司再發行 100 股新股票給其他人，總股數變成 200 股。你的 51 股現在只佔 \( \frac{51}{200} = 25.5\% \)，你失去了對公司的絕對控制權。這就是所謂的「攤薄效應 (Dilution effect)」。

Q: Why does issuing shares dilute control?

A: Suppose a company has 100 shares, and you own 51 (51% control, absolute majority). If the company issues 100 new shares to others, the total becomes 200 shares. Your 51 shares now only represent \( \frac{51}{200} = 25.5\% \) of the company. You have lost absolute control. This is known as the "Dilution effect".

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

關鍵字秒殺法: 在 DSE 經濟科中，看到「債券 (Bonds) VS 股票 (Shares)」的優缺點比較，立刻聯想兩大核心差異：

  1. 控制權 (Control): 股票 = 攤薄；債券 = 不變。
  2. 清盤優先權 (Liquidation Priority): 債券 > 股票。

題目問「對股東的優點」，直接找「控制權不變」即可。

Keyword Shortcut: In DSE Economics, when comparing "Bonds VS Shares", immediately recall two core differences:

  1. Control: Shares = Diluted; Bonds = Unchanged.
  2. Liquidation Priority: Bonds > Shares.

Since the question asks for the "advantage to shareholders", look directly for "control remains unchanged".
