題目 / Question (DSE 2014 P1 Q15)

參閱有關海底隧道的供需圖。政府建議提高海底隧道的收費，以減輕繁忙時段的交通擠塞情況。假設隧道最大行車流量是固定的。下列哪些陳述是正確的？

Refer to the supply-demand diagram for the Cross-Harbour Tunnel. The government proposed to raise the toll for the Cross-Harbour Tunnel so as to lessen the traffic congestion during rush hours. Suppose the maximum vehicular volume of the tunnel is fixed. Which of the following statements are correct?

(1) 對海底隧道服務的需求會減少。

The demand for the service of the Cross-Harbour Tunnel will decrease.

(2) 海底隧道的總收益會上升。

The total revenue of the Cross-Harbour Tunnel will increase.

(3) 由交通擠塞引起的效率損失會減少。

The deadweight loss caused by traffic congestion will decrease.

(4) 司機使用該隧道的機會成本會上升。

The opportunity cost of the drivers using the tunnel will increase.

A. 只有 (1)、(3) / (1) and (3) only
B. 只有 (1)、(4) / (1) and (4) only
C. 只有 (2)、(3) / (2) and (3) only
D. 只有 (2)、(4) / (2) and (4) only

📚 概念重溫 / Concept Review

1. 需求量改變 vs 需求改變 (Change in Quantity Demanded vs Change in Demand)

價格本身的改變只會導致「需求量」沿著同一條需求曲線移動，而不會移動整條需求曲線。

A change in price itself leads to a movement along the demand curve (change in quantity demanded), not a shift of the demand curve (change in demand).

2. 短缺與非價格競爭 (Shortage and Non-price Competition)

當價格設定在均衡點之下（如圖中的「現在收費」），需求量大於供應量 \(Q_d > Q_s\)，會出現短缺（即交通擠塞）。這部分未能滿足的需求會引發非價格競爭（例如排隊等候）。

When the price is set below equilibrium (like "Existing toll" in the diagram), quantity demanded exceeds quantity supplied \(Q_d > Q_s\), resulting in a shortage (traffic congestion). This leads to non-price competition (e.g., queuing).

3. 總社會成本 (Full Cost / Total Social Cost)

對於消費者而言，真正的代價（機會成本）是：
\[ \text{機會成本} = \text{金錢價格 (Toll)} + \text{非金錢成本 (Time cost)} \] 在短缺情況下，邊際消費者願意支付的最高價格（需求曲線上的點）等於這兩者的總和。

For consumers, the true cost (opportunity cost) is: Opportunity Cost = Money Price (Toll) + Non-money Cost (Time cost). Under shortage, the marginal consumer's willingness to pay equals the sum of these two.

✅ 答案與詳細分析 / The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確選項 / Correct Option: C (只有 (2)、(3) / (2) and (3) only)
1

錯誤 / Incorrect

分析： 這是典型的概念陷阱。題目說明是「提高收費」（價格改變）。根據需求定律，價格上升會導致需求量 (quantity demanded) 減少，是沿著需求曲線向上移動，而非整條需求 (demand) 曲線向左移動。

Analysis: This is a classic trap. The question states "raise the toll" (price change). According to the Law of Demand, an increase in price leads to a decrease in quantity demanded (movement along the curve), not a decrease in demand (shift of the curve).
2

正確 / Correct

分析： 留意圖中的供應曲線 \(S\) 是垂直的（完全無彈性），這代表行車流量（交易量）受限於隧道容量。目前的收費低於均衡點，存在短缺，交易量固定在 \(Q_s\)（供應量）。 當收費提高（但仍低於或等於均衡價格時），交易量仍然維持在 \(Q_s\)（因為仍有足夠甚至過多的車輛想使用）。 \[ \text{總收益} = P \times Q \] 既然 \(Q\)（交易量）保持不變，而 \(P\)（收費）上升，總收益必然上升。

Analysis: Note that the supply curve \(S\) is vertical (perfectly inelastic), meaning the traffic volume (quantity transacted) is limited by capacity. At the existing toll (below equilibrium), there is a shortage, so the quantity transacted is fixed at \(Q_s\). When the toll is raised (but stays at or below equilibrium), the quantity transacted remains at \(Q_s\). Since \( \text{Total Revenue} = P \times Q \), if \(Q\) is constant and \(P\) increases, Total Revenue must increase.
3

正確 / Correct

分析： 「交通擠塞」本身就是一種效率損失 (Deadweight Loss, DWL)，因為司機花費時間排隊（非金錢成本），這部分價值耗損了但沒有轉移給隧道公司。提高收費（金錢價格），會減少短缺量（擠塞程度）。這意味著司機支付的成本中，更多部分變成了金錢（轉移給隧道公司），而更少部分浪費在排隊上。因此，因排隊（尋租行為）造成的社會浪費減少，效率提升。

Analysis: "Traffic congestion" represents a Deadweight Loss (DWL) because drivers spend time queuing (non-money cost), which is a waste of resources not transferred to the tunnel operator. Raising the toll (money price) reduces the shortage (congestion). This means a larger portion of the cost paid by drivers becomes money (transferred revenue) and less is wasted on queuing. Thus, the social waste caused by queuing (rent-seeking) decreases.
4

錯誤 / Incorrect

分析： 這是一個高階概念。在短缺情況下，邊際司機的總機會成本（金錢 + 時間）其實是由需求曲線的高度決定的。只要交易量 \(Q\) 仍然固定在供應上限 \(S\) 處，邊際使用者的支付意願（Willingness to pay）就固定在需求曲線對應 \(S\) 的那一點上。 提高收費只是改變了成本的組合（金錢成本上升，時間成本下降），但對於邊際使用者來說，總代價（全價）維持不變。

Analysis: This is an advanced concept. Under shortage, the total opportunity cost (money + time) for the marginal driver is determined by the height of the demand curve at the quantity transacted. As long as the quantity remains fixed at the supply limit \(S\), the marginal user's willingness to pay remains fixed at that point on the demand curve. Raising the toll only changes the composition of the cost (higher money cost, lower time cost), but the total cost (full price) for the marginal user remains unchanged.

🔍 題目拆解 / Problem Decomposition

破題關鍵 (The Pivot Point): 垂直的供應曲線 \(S\)。
這意味著無論價格如何變動（只要低於均衡點），交易量 (Quantity Transacted) 都不會改變。

The Pivot Point: The vertical Supply curve \(S\). This implies that regardless of price changes (as long as it's below equilibrium), the Quantity Transacted remains constant.

邏輯鏈 (Logic Chain):
1. 交易量 \(Q\) 不變 + 價格 \(P\) 上升 \(\rightarrow\) 總收益 \(TR\) 上升 (選項 2 ✅)。
2. 價格上升 \(\rightarrow\) 短缺減少 \(\rightarrow\) 排隊時間減少 \(\rightarrow\) 浪費減少 (選項 3 ✅)。
3. 價格改變 \(\rightarrow\) 沿著曲線移動 \(\neq\) 曲線移動 (選項 1 ❌)。

1. Quantity \(Q\) constant + Price \(P\) up \(\rightarrow\) Total Revenue \(TR\) up (Option 2 ✅).
2. Price up \(\rightarrow\) Shortage down \(\rightarrow\) Queuing time down \(\rightarrow\) Waste down (Option 3 ✅).
3. Price change \(\rightarrow\) Movement along curve \(\neq\) Shift of curve (Option 1 ❌).

🙋 學生常見疑問 / Student Q&A

Q: 為什麼選項 (4) 是錯的？既然收費貴了，成本不是應該增加了嗎？

Q: Why is option (4) wrong? Since the toll is more expensive, shouldn't the cost increase?

A: 你只看到了「金錢成本」，忽略了「時間成本」。
想像一下：原本收費 $20，但要排隊 1 小時（假設時間價值 $100），總成本是 $120。
現在收費加到 $50，排隊的人少了，只需排隊 42 分鐘（價值 $70），總成本還是 $120。
只要供應量固定，競爭同一數量的車位，最終付出的「全價」（Full Price）是由需求強度決定的，不會因為政府收費多少而改變，只會改變「誰收到了這筆價值」（政府收錢 vs 時間浪費掉）。

A: You are only looking at "money cost" and ignoring "time cost". Imagine: Originally toll is $20, but you queue for 1 hour (value $100), Total Cost = $120. Now toll raises to $50, fewer people queue, so you only queue 42 mins (value $70), Total Cost is still $120. As long as supply is fixed, the "Full Price" is determined by demand intensity. It doesn't change with the toll; only the composition changes (government revenue vs. wasted time).
