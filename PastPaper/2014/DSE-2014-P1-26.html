2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

要拆解這道題目，我們需要清楚區分以下三個宏觀經濟概念：

To tackle this question, we must clearly distinguish between the following three macroeconomic concepts:

1. 名義本地生產總值 (Nominal GDP): 以當年價格計算的總產值。反映價格和產量的綜合變動。

1. Nominal GDP: The total value of output calculated at current year prices. It reflects changes in both prices and quantities.

$$\text{Nominal GDP} = \sum (P_{\text{current}} \times Q_{\text{current}})$$

2. 實質本地生產總值 (Real GDP): 以基年價格計算的總產值。剔除了物價變動的影響，純粹反映實際產量的變動。

2. Real GDP: The total value of output calculated at base year prices. It removes the effect of price changes and purely reflects changes in actual production volume.

$$\text{Real GDP} = \sum (P_{\text{base}} \times Q_{\text{current}})$$

3. 生活成本 (Cost of Living): 購買固定一籃子消費品所需的開支。通常以消費物價指數(CPI)或GDP平減物價指數來量度。重點是加權平均價格的變動。

3. Cost of Living: The expenditure required to purchase a fixed basket of consumer goods. It is usually measured by the CPI or GDP deflator. The focus is on the change in the weighted average price.

3. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案是：

The correct option is:

✅ Option A: 只有(1)、(2) / (1) and (2) only

🔍 逐項分析 (Step-by-Step Analysis)

陳述 (1): 第一年的名義本地生產總值高於第二年的。(正確)

Statement (1): The nominal GDP in Year 1 is higher than that in Year 2. (Correct)

我們需要計算兩年的名義GDP (當期價格 $\times$ 當期產量)：

We need to calculate the nominal GDP for both years (Current Price $\times$ Current Quantity):

$$\text{Nominal GDP}_{\text{Year 1}} = (44 \times \$5) + (25 \times \$10) = 220 + 250 = \$470$$ $$\text{Nominal GDP}_{\text{Year 2}} = (36 \times \$6) + (22 \times \$9) = 216 + 198 = \$414$$

因為 $\$470 > \$414$，所以第一年的名義GDP的確較高。

Since $\$470 > \$414$, the nominal GDP in Year 1 is indeed higher.

陳述 (2): 第一年的實質本地生產總值高於第二年的。(正確)

Statement (2): The real GDP in Year 1 is higher than that in Year 2. (Correct)

實質GDP反映實際產量。觀察數據：食物產量由44跌至36，成衣產量由25跌至22。由於所有物品的產量均下降，無論以哪一年作為基年，第二年的實質GDP必定低於第一年。

Real GDP reflects actual output. Observing the data: food output fell from 44 to 36, and clothing output fell from 25 to 22. Since the quantities of ALL goods decreased, Year 2's real GDP must be lower than Year 1's, regardless of which year is chosen as the base year.

陳述 (3): 第一年的生活成本高於第二年的。(錯誤)

Statement (3): The cost of living in Year 1 is higher than that in Year 2. (Incorrect)

生活成本反映物價總水平。我們可以比較購買同一籃子物品的成本。假設以第一年的產量作為「一籃子物品」(Laspeyres Index 概念)：

The cost of living reflects the general price level. We can compare the cost of buying the same basket of goods. Let's use Year 1's output as the "basket" (Laspeyres Index concept):

$$\text{Cost in Year 1} = (44 \times \$5) + (25 \times \$10) = \$470$$ $$\text{Cost in Year 2} = (44 \times \$6) + (25 \times \$9) = 264 + 225 = \$489$$

因為 $\$489 > \$470$，生活成本在第二年上升了，所以第一年的生活成本是較低的。

Since $\$489 > \$470$, the cost of living increased in Year 2, meaning the cost of living in Year 1 was lower.

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

做這類題目時，要有系統地處理數據：

When solving this type of question, process the data systematically:

  • 破題關鍵 (Pivot Point): 處理陳述(2)時，不需要盲目計算！只要發現所有數量的變動方向一致 (都在下跌)，就可以直接得出實質GDP的變動方向，這能為你節省大量考試時間。
    Pivot Point: When evaluating statement (2), blind calculation is unnecessary! If you notice that all quantities moved in the same direction (both decreased), you can immediately determine the direction of real GDP change, saving you valuable exam time.
  • 處理陷阱 (Handling Traps): 陳述(3)是最大的考驗。食物價格升了，成衣價格跌了，一升一跌之下，同學不能靠直覺猜測，必須利用「購買固定籃子」的成本來比較。
    Handling Traps: Statement (3) is the biggest challenge. Food price went up, clothing price went down. With mixed price signals, you cannot rely on intuition; you MUST compare the cost of purchasing a "fixed basket" of goods.

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

❌ 誤解： 「成衣的價格下跌了，由 $\$10$ 跌至 $\$9$，所以整體生活成本應該下跌了。」

❌ Misconception: "The price of clothing dropped from $\$10$ to $\$9$, so the overall cost of living should have decreased."

💡 真相： 生活成本是看加權平均 (Weighted Average)。食物的產量 (即在籃子中的權重) 遠大於成衣 (大約40多件對20多件)，因此食物價格上升 $20\%$ (由 $\$5$ 升至 $\$6$) 的影響，蓋過了成衣價格下跌 $10\%$ 的影響，導致整體生活成本上升。

💡 The Truth: The cost of living depends on the weighted average. The quantity of food (its weight in the basket) is much larger than clothing (40+ units vs 20+ units). Therefore, the $20\%$ increase in food price (from $\$5$ to $\$6$) overshadows the $10\%$ decrease in clothing price, driving the overall cost of living up.

6. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 老師，計算生活成本時，我可以隨便把兩件物品的價格加起來除以2，看看平均價格嗎？

Q: Tutor, when calculating the cost of living, can I just add the prices of the two goods and divide by 2 to see the average price?

A: 絕對不可以！這叫「簡單平均」(Simple Average)。但在現實和經濟學中，我們消費不同物品的比例不同。你每天吃三餐，但幾個月才買一件衣服，所以食物價格的升幅對你錢包的影響大得多。這就是為什麼我們必須用「數量 $Q$」作為權重，計算「加權平均」(即總開支)。

A: Absolutely not! That's called a "Simple Average". In reality and economics, we consume different goods in different proportions. You eat three meals a day but buy clothing only once every few months, so a rise in food prices impacts your wallet much more. This is why we must use "Quantity $Q$" as a weight and calculate the "Weighted Average" (i.e., total expenditure).

7. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

⏳ 「10秒速判實質GDP法」：

⏳ "10-Second Real GDP Judgment":

如果在選擇題中，表格顯示所有物品的數量 $Q$ 都上升了，那麼實質GDP一定上升；反之亦然。這時候你一秒鐘都不用花在按計算機上！直接判斷對錯，保留時間對付需要計算的名義GDP和物價指數。

If an MCQ table shows that the quantity $Q$ of ALL goods has increased, then Real GDP MUST have increased; and vice versa. You don't need to spend a single second on your calculator for this! Make the judgment directly and save your time for calculating Nominal GDP and Price Indices.
