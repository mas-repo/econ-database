HKDSE Economics Analysis

Topic: Functions of Price (價格的功能)
Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ (3/5)
Key Concepts: Rationing Function (配給功能), Allocative Function (分配功能), Scarcity (稀少性)

The Question (題目)

[Traditional Chinese]
在2021年，一個香港的YouTube頻道「試當真Trial & Error」舉辦首個現場表演。以下是門票價格的安排。
票價在每天上午10時調整。

日期票價
8月9日$10 000
8月10日$ 5 000
......
8月31日$20
9月1日及以後$10

上述個案說明了價格的 __________ 功能，因為 _______________。

  • A. 分配 …… 更多資源可以分配到該表演的製作
  • B. 分配 …… 這安排可以確保門票沽清
  • C. 配給 …… 這些門票售予最高價值的買家
  • D. 配給 …… 這些門票是以先到先得的準則分派

[English]
In 2021, Trial & Error, a Hong Kong YouTube channel, held its first live show. The following was the pricing arrangement of its tickets.
Ticket price adjusts daily at 10am.

DateTicket Price
9 August$10 000
10 August$ 5 000
......
31 August$20
1 Sept onwards$10

The above case illustrates the __________ function of price because _______________.

  • A. allocative …… more resources can be allocated to the production of the show
  • B. allocative …… the arrangement can guarantee a sell-out of the tickets
  • C. rationing …… the tickets are sold to the highest-valued buyers
  • D. rationing …… the tickets are distributed on a first-come, first-served basis

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 稀少性與競爭 (Scarcity & Competition)

由於資源稀少，我們必須決定誰能獲得物品。這就是競爭。我們需要一個準則來決定勝負。

Due to scarcity, we must decide who gets the goods. This implies competition. A criterion is needed to determine the winners.

2. 價格的配給功能 (Rationing Function of Price)

定義：價格作為一個準則，將有限的商品分配給對該商品估值最高（願意並有能力支付最高價格）的消費者。

Definition: Price serves as a criterion to distribute limited goods to consumers who have the highest valuation (willing and able to pay the highest price) for the good.
\( \text{Role: Distributing output (Who gets it?)} \)

3. 價格的分配功能 (Allocative Function of Price)

定義：價格引導資源流向需求最高的生產用途。當某產品價格上升，利潤增加，生產商會投入更多資源（如勞力、土地）去生產該產品。

Definition: Price guides resources to their most highly valued uses in production. When the price of a product rises, profits increase, signaling producers to allocate more resources (labor, land) to produce it.
\( \text{Role: Guiding resource allocation (What/How to produce?)} \)

Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer Option C / 選項 C

詳細拆解 (Step-by-Step Breakdown)

C. 配給 …… 這些門票售予最高價值的買家
(Rationing …… the tickets are sold to the highest-valued buyers)

正確原因： 題目中的機制是「荷蘭式拍賣」(Dutch Auction) 的變奏。價格由高位 ($10,000) 開始下降。誰會第一時間用 $10,000 買票？就是那些認為門票價值高於或等於 $10,000 的超級粉絲。價格在此充當了篩選工具，將有限的門票「配給」給那些願意支付最高價格的人。

Why Correct: The mechanism is a variation of a "Dutch Auction." Prices start high ($10,000) and drop. Who buys at $10,000 immediately? Superfans who value the ticket at or above $10,000. Price acts as a screening device, "rationing" the limited tickets to those willing to pay the highest price.
A. 分配 …… 更多資源可以分配到該表演的製作
(Allocative …… more resources can be allocated to the production of the show)

錯誤原因： 分配功能是指引導資源（如勞工、場地）去生產。題目討論的是門票（製成品）如何賣給觀眾，屬於消費層面的分配，即「配給」。而且，表演已經籌備好，門票正在發售，短期內很難因為票價高而突然增加「製作資源」。

Why Wrong: The allocative function refers to guiding resources (labor, venue) into production. The question discusses how existing tickets (finished goods) are sold to the audience, which is "rationing" at the consumption level. Also, the show is already organized; high ticket prices during the sale don't immediately pump more resources into the production.
B. 分配 …… 這安排可以確保門票沽清
(Allocative …… the arrangement can guarantee a sell-out of the tickets)

錯誤原因： 「沽清」並不是分配功能的定義。即使價格降到 $10，如果表演極不受歡迎，也未必能沽清。此外，這仍然是在描述產品的銷售（配給），而非資源的流向。

Why Wrong: "Selling out" is not the definition of the allocative function. Even if the price drops to $10, a sell-out isn't guaranteed if the show is unpopular. Furthermore, this still describes the sale of the product (rationing), not the flow of resources.
D. 配給 …… 這些門票是以先到先得的準則分派
(Rationing …… the tickets are distributed on a first-come, first-served basis)

錯誤原因： 這是一個巨大的陷阱！雖然買票有時間先後（8月9日先買），但決定你能不能在8月9日買到的關鍵是你是否願意支付 $10,000。如果是純粹的「先到先得」(非價格競爭)，票價應該是固定的（例如全部 $500），然後大家排隊，排前面的先得。這裡是以「願付價格」作為主要準則。

Why Wrong: This is a major trap! Although there is a time element (buying on Aug 9th), the key factor determining if you get a ticket on Aug 9th is your willingness to pay $10,000. If it were purely "first-come, first-served" (non-price competition), the price would likely be fixed (e.g., all at $500), and ownership would be determined by queue position. Here, "willingness to pay" is the primary criterion.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目通常包含兩個關鍵步驟：

  1. 識別功能 (Identify the Function):
    題目涉及的是「資源投入生產」(Input) 還是「製成品分發給消費者」(Output)？
    本題：門票賣給誰 $\rightarrow$ Output $\rightarrow$ 配給功能 (Rationing)。
  2. 識別準則 (Identify the Criterion):
    是用什麼方法來決定誰獲得物品？是排隊（時間）？抽籤（運氣）？還是出價（金錢）？
    本題：隨時間降價 $\rightarrow$ 高價者先買 $\rightarrow$ 價高者得 (Highest-valued)。
💡 關鍵點 (Pivot Point): 不要被「日期」迷惑。雖然價格隨日期變動，但該機制的核心是價格歧視 (Price Discrimination)，目的是榨取消費者的消費者盈餘 (Consumer Surplus)，讓願意出高價的人先買。
Pivot Point: Don't be misled by the "dates." Although price changes with dates, the core of the mechanism is Price Discrimination, aiming to extract Consumer Surplus by letting those willing to pay high prices buy first.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼不是「先到先得」？8月9日買的人確實比8月31日的人「先」得到門票啊？

A: 在經濟學上，「先到先得」通常指非價格競爭 (Non-price competition)。例如：免費派發口罩，大家排隊，排在前面的人拿到。
但在這個題目中，8月9日的人之所以能買到，是因為他願意支付 $10,000。如果他排第一但只願付 $500，他也不能在8月9日買到。所以，決定性的準則是價格，而非單純的時間。

Q: Why isn't it "first-come, first-served"? People buying on Aug 9th do get tickets "before" those on Aug 31st.
A: In Economics, "first-come, first-served" usually refers to non-price competition. E.g., Free masks distribution where people queue up. In this question, the person on Aug 9th gets the ticket because they are willing to pay $10,000. If they were first in line but only willing to pay $500, they couldn't buy it on Aug 9th. Thus, the decisive criterion is Price, not just Time.

Q: 什麼情況下才是「分配功能」？

A: 如果題目說：「由於門票價格高企，主辦單位決定加開三場表演，並租用更大的場館。」這就是分配功能。因為價格信號吸引了更多資源（場館、人力）進入這個市場。

Q: When would it be the "Allocative Function"?
A: If the question said: "Due to high ticket prices, the organizer decided to add three more shows and rent a larger venue." That would be the allocative function, as the price signal attracted more resources (venue, labor) into this market.
