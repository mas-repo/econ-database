📚 課題 (Topic): 國民收入會計 (National Income Accounting)
⭐ 難度 (Difficulty): ★★★☆☆
🔑 關鍵概念 (Key Concepts): 本地居民總收入 (GNI), 經濟居民 (Economic Resident), 要素收益 (Factor Income)

📝 題目 (The Question)

下列哪項不包括在香港的本地居民總收入中？

Which of the following is NOT included in the gross national income of Hong Kong?

  1. 一位日本居民從香港公司收取的股息
    the dividend received by a Japanese resident from a company in Hong Kong
  2. 一位英國顧問在香港工作18個月收取的薪酬
    the salary received by a British consultant working in Hong Kong for 18 months
  3. 一位香港居民從其持有的由香港政府發行的銀色債券收取的利息
    the interest received by a Hong Kong resident holding silver bonds which is issued by the Hong Kong Government
  4. 一位香港居民從投資內地物業收取的租金收入
    the rental income earned by a Hong Kong resident for investing in properties in Mainland

🧠 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

本地居民總收入 (Gross National Income, GNI) 衡量一個經濟體系的「居民」在特定時間內所賺取的總收入，無論這些收入是在本地還是海外賺取。

Gross National Income (GNI) measures the total income earned by the "residents" of an economy over a specified period, regardless of whether the income is earned domestically or abroad.

$$ \text{GNI} = \text{GDP} + \text{Net Factor Income from Abroad (NIA)} $$

經濟居民 (Economic Resident): 在香港中學文憑試 (HKDSE) 的定義中，任何個人在該經濟領域內逗留或打算逗留 12個月或以上，即被視為該地的常住居民。國籍並不重要。

Economic Resident: In the HKDSE syllabus, any individual who has stayed or intends to stay in the economic territory for 12 months or more is considered a resident. Citizenship does not matter.

✅ 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): A

選項 A (Option A) - 正確 (Correct)

日本居民從香港公司收取股息，屬於支付給非香港居民的要素收益。這筆款項會從香港的 GDP 中扣除，因此不包括在香港的 GNI 中。

The dividend received by a Japanese resident is factor income paid to a non-resident. This amount is deducted from Hong Kong's GDP as factor income paid abroad, and therefore is NOT included in Hong Kong's GNI.

選項 B (Option B) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

英國顧問在香港工作了 18 個月（超過 12 個月），因此在經濟學上他被視為香港居民。他賺取的薪酬屬於香港居民的收入，包括在 GNI 中。

The British consultant has worked in Hong Kong for 18 months (more than 12 months), making him an economic resident of Hong Kong. His salary is income earned by a resident and is included in the GNI.

選項 C (Option C) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

注意：政府債券利息通常被視為轉移支付而不計入 GDP，但此題目的核心考點在於「居民」定義。在多項選擇題中，選項 A 是最直接且必定不屬於 GNI 的要素收益流出。

Note: Interest on government bonds is generally treated as a transfer payment and excluded from GDP, but the core focus of this question is the "resident" definition. Option A is the most direct and definitive outflow of factor income excluded from GNI.

選項 D (Option D) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

香港居民從內地賺取的租金收入，屬於從外地賺取的要素收益（流入）。由於是由香港居民賺取，這會增加並包括在香港的 GNI 中。

Rental income earned by a Hong Kong resident from the Mainland is factor income from abroad (inflow). Since it is earned by a resident, it is added to and included in Hong Kong's GNI.

🔍 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解答這類題目的「關鍵點」(Pivot Point) 是辨別收款人的居民身分。只要是「非居民」賺取的收入，就不屬於 GNI。

The "Pivot Point" for solving this type of question is identifying the resident status of the earner. Any income earned by a "non-resident" is excluded from GNI.

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

學生常把「國籍」(Citizenship) 與「經濟居民」(Economic Resident) 混淆。例如選項 B 中的「英國顧問」，學生可能因為他具有英國國籍而誤以為他是非居民。記住：逗留滿12個月即屬居民。

Students often confuse "Citizenship" with "Economic Resident". For example, in Option B, students might mistakenly think the "British consultant" is a non-resident because of his nationality. Remember: staying for 12 months or more makes one a resident.

🙋 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 為什麼選項 A 的股息算作要素收益 (Factor Income)？
Why is the dividend in Option A considered factor income?

A: 股息是提供「資本」(Capital) 這種生產要素所獲得的回報。日本居民投資了香港公司（提供資本），因此獲得股息作為要素收益。

A: Dividends are the return for providing "Capital", which is a factor of production. The Japanese resident invested in a HK company (provided capital), thus earning dividends as factor income.

⚡ 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

找「外人」法則： 題目問「不包括在 GNI」，你只需要在選項中尋找「非香港居民」賺錢的選項。選項 A 明確指出是「日本居民」（且沒有說明在港逗留超過一年），直接選 A 即可秒殺此題！

The "Outsider" Rule: When asked what is "NOT included in GNI", simply look for the option where a "non-resident" is earning money. Option A explicitly states a "Japanese resident" (with no mention of staying >1 year). Pick A and save time!
