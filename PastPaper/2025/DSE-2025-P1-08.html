HKDSE Economics Tutorial

Topic: Wage Payment Systems (工資支付制度) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Time Rate, Commission, Risk Bearing

1. The Question (題目)

假設某健康產品公司的售貨員的付酬方式有以下改變：
舊制度：每月$30000
新制度：每月$15000加對營業額的貢獻的20%

下列哪項／些關於上述付酬方式改變的陳述是正確的？
(1) 該公司僱用售貨員的總成本會下降。
(2) 售貨員的工作意欲會下降。
(3) 部分經營風險被轉嫁到售貨員。

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (2)
C. 只有 (3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)
Suppose the wage payment system of the salespersons in a health product company changes as follows:
Old System: Salary: $30000 per month
New System: Salary: $15000 per month plus 20% of contribution to sales revenue

Which of the following statements about the above change in the wage payment system is/are correct?
(1) The company's total cost of employing salespersons will decrease.
(2) The working incentive of the salespersons will decrease.
(3) Part of the business risk is shifted to the salespersons.

A. (1) only
B. (2) only
C. (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

計時工資 (Time Rate) vs 佣金制 (Commission / Piece Rate)
題目涉及由「純計時工資」轉變為「基本薪金 + 佣金」的混合制度。
  • 計時工資： 員工收入固定，不論公司業績好壞。僱主承擔所有經營風險 (Business Risk)。
  • 佣金制： 員工收入與其產量或銷售額掛鈎。這能提供極大的工作誘因 (Working Incentive)，因為多勞多得。同時，如果生意慘淡，員工的收入也會減少，這意味著員工分擔了部分的經營風險。
Time Rate vs Commission / Piece Rate
The question involves a shift from a "pure time rate" to a mixed system of "basic salary + commission".
  • Time Rate: Employee income is fixed, regardless of company performance. The employer bears all the business risk.
  • Commission: Employee income is tied to output or sales. This provides a strong working incentive because "more work equals more pay". Simultaneously, if business is poor, the employee's income drops, meaning they share part of the business risk.
新制度收入公式 (New Income Formula): $$ \text{Income} = \$15,000 + (0.20 \times \text{Sales Revenue}) $$

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer: Option C (只有 3 / 3 only)

(1) 該公司僱用售貨員的總成本會下降。 (錯誤)
我們無法確定總成本會否下降。雖然基本薪金由 \( \$30,000 \) 減至 \( \$15,000 \)，但新制度包含了佣金。如果售貨員的營業額非常高（例如超過 \( \$75,000 \)），其總收入將會超過 \( \$30,000 \)，導致公司的總成本上升。
(1) The company's total cost of employing salespersons will decrease. (Incorrect)
We cannot be certain if the total cost will decrease. Although the basic salary drops from \( \$30,000 \) to \( \$15,000 \), the new system includes a commission. If the salesperson's sales revenue is very high (e.g., over \( \$75,000 \)), their total income will exceed \( \$30,000 \), causing the company's total cost to increase.
(2) 售貨員的工作意欲會下降。 (錯誤)
剛好相反！引入佣金制度將收入與表現掛鈎（多勞多得），這會增加售貨員的工作意欲，鼓勵他們推銷更多產品。
(2) The working incentive of the salespersons will decrease. (Incorrect)
Exactly the opposite! Introducing a commission system ties income to performance (more work, more pay), which will increase the working incentive of salespersons, encouraging them to sell more products.
(3) 部分經營風險被轉嫁到售貨員。 (正確)
在舊制度下，即使生意極差，售貨員仍穩賺 \( \$30,000 \)，老闆承擔所有虧損風險。在新制度下，如果生意慘淡（營業額為0），售貨員只能獲得 \( \$15,000 \)。由於售貨員的收入會隨公司業績波動，他們分擔了部分經營風險。
(3) Part of the business risk is shifted to the salespersons. (Correct)
Under the old system, even if business is terrible, the salesperson steadily earns \( \$30,000 \); the boss bears all the risk of loss. Under the new system, if business is poor (zero sales), the salesperson only gets \( \$15,000 \). Because the salesperson's income now fluctuates with the company's performance, they share part of the business risk.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point)：
這題的關鍵在於理解「風險」(Risk)和「誘因」(Incentive)在不同工資制度下的分配。固定薪金 = 零風險 + 低誘因；佣金 = 高風險 + 高誘因。

干擾資訊 (Distractors)：
題目中的具體數字（\( \$30,000 \), \( \$15,000 \), \( 20\% \)）其實是干擾。你不需要進行任何複雜的計算，只需要看出這是一個從「固定」轉向「浮動」的制度改變即可。
Pivot Point:
The key to this question is understanding the distribution of Risk and Incentive under different wage systems. Fixed salary = zero risk + low incentive; Commission = high risk + high incentive.

Distractors:
The specific numbers in the question (\( \$30,000 \), \( \$15,000 \), \( 20\% \)) are actually distractors. You don't need to do any complex calculations; you just need to recognize that this is a shift from a "fixed" to a "variable" payment system.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：只看底薪下降就以為成本減少
很多學生看到基本薪金由 \( \$30,000 \) 跌至 \( \$15,000 \)，就直覺認為陳述 (1) 是正確的。他們忘記了公式中還有 \( + 20\% \) 的佣金。在經濟學中，如果缺乏足夠數據（如實際銷售額），我們不能武斷地下結論說總額必定上升或下降。
Trap: Assuming costs decrease just because basic salary dropped
Many students see the basic salary drop from \( \$30,000 \) to \( \$15,000 \) and intuitively assume statement (1) is correct. They forget the \( + 20\% \) commission in the formula. In Economics, if we lack sufficient data (like actual sales volume), we cannot arbitrarily conclude that the total amount will definitely rise or fall.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 如果題目給了具體的銷售額，陳述 (1) 有可能正確嗎？
A: 是的！如果題目明確指出「預期每位售貨員每月的營業額只有 \( \$10,000 \)」，那麼新收入將是 \( \$15,000 + (\$10,000 \times 0.2) = \$17,000 \)。因為 \( \$17,000 < \$30,000 \)，這時總成本就會下降。但由於本題沒有提供銷售額，所以 (1) 是「不確定」，在多項選擇題中應視為錯誤。
Q: If the question provided a specific sales volume, could statement (1) be correct?
A: Yes! If the question explicitly stated "the expected monthly sales revenue per salesperson is only \( \$10,000 \)", then the new income would be \( \$15,000 + (\$10,000 \times 0.2) = \$17,000 \). Since \( \$17,000 < \$30,000 \), the total cost would decrease in this specific case. However, because this question provides no sales data, (1) is "uncertain" and should be treated as incorrect in an MCQ.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

消去法 (Elimination Method)：秒殺此題！
1. 看到「加佣金」，立刻聯想到「多勞多得」，所以工作意欲必定上升。陳述 (2) 說意欲下降，絕對錯誤！
2. 劃掉所有包含 (2) 的選項：淘汰 B 和 D。
3. 剩下 A (只有 1) 和 C (只有 3)。
4. 由於缺乏營業額數據，(1) 是未知的。而 (3) 是佣金制的標準定義（轉嫁風險）。因此，果斷選擇 C！
Elimination Method: Instant Kill!
1. Seeing "plus commission", immediately think "more work, more pay", so working incentive MUST go up. Statement (2) says incentive decreases, which is absolutely wrong!
2. Cross out all options containing (2): Eliminate B and D.
3. You are left with A ((1) only) and C ((3) only).
4. Since sales data is missing, (1) is unknown. Meanwhile, (3) is the standard definition of a commission system (shifting risk). Therefore, confidently choose C!
