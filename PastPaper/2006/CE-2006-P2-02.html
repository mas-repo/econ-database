HKDSE Economics Question Analysis

Topic (課題): Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念) Sub-topic: Opportunity Cost (機會成本) Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆
Key Concepts Opportunity Cost (機會成本), Time Cost (時間成本), Full Cost (全部成本)

The Question (題目)

每年農曆七月都有不少慈善團體派發免費米予市民。長時間輪候這些免費米的人多數是年長者。
Every July of the Lunar calendar, many charity organizations distribute packs of rice to people free of charge. Those who spend a lot of time queuing up for this rice are mostly elderly people.
在上述個案中，長者而非青年人會花時間輪候免費米是因為
In the above case, the elderly rather than the young people would spend time queuing up for these packs of charity rice because
  • A. 輪候這些米是一種傳統活動。
    it is a tradition to queue up for this rice.
  • B. 長者喜愛吃米多於其他食品。
    the elderly prefer rice to other kinds of food.
  • C. 長者較青年人貧窮。
    the elderly are poorer than the young people.
  • D. 對長者來說，他們在其他活動中最佳選擇的價值比青年人在其他活動中最佳選擇的價值為低。
    the elderly value their best choice of other activities less than that of the young people.

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 機會成本 (Opportunity Cost)
Opportunity cost is the highest-valued option forgone.
$$ \text{Opportunity Cost} = \text{Value of the highest-valued option forgone} $$
2. 全部成本 (Full Cost)
The full cost of obtaining a good includes both money cost (price) and non-money cost (e.g., time cost).
$$ \text{Full Cost} = \text{Money Price} + \text{Non-money Cost (Time)} $$
3. 時間成本 (Time Cost)
Time is a scarce resource. When we spend time queuing, we forgo the income or utility we could have gained from doing something else (e.g., working, leisure). This is the opportunity cost of time.

Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: D
Option A (Wrong)
分析：「傳統」並非經濟學解釋人類行為的主要因素。即使是傳統，如果成本極高，人們也不會去做。這選項無法解釋為何「長者」比「青年人」更多去排隊（除非假設青年人不重視傳統，但這不是經濟學的核心解釋）。
Analysis: "Tradition" is not a primary economic factor explaining behavior. Even if it is a tradition, people would not do it if the cost were prohibitively high. This option fails to explain why the *elderly* queue more than the *young* (unless assuming young people disregard tradition, which is not a core economic explanation).
Option B (Wrong)
分析：這涉及「品味」(Taste)。雖然長者可能較喜歡吃米，但這不足以解釋為何他們願意花費「大量時間」排隊。即使青年人也喜歡吃米，如果他們的時間成本太高，他們也不會排隊。重點在於成本，而非單純的喜好。
Analysis: This involves "Taste". While the elderly might prefer rice, this alone doesn't explain why they are willing to spend "a lot of time" queuing. Even if young people liked rice, they wouldn't queue if their time cost was too high. The key is cost, not just preference.
Option C (Wrong)
分析：這是一個強大的干擾項。「貧窮」通常意味著收入較低，雖然這暗示了時間成本可能較低，但「貧窮」本身是一個狀態描述，而非經濟學上解釋選擇行為的精確定義。經濟學關注的是「放棄了什麼」。
Analysis: This is a strong distractor. "Poorer" usually implies lower income, and while this hints at lower time cost, "poverty" itself is a description of a state, not the precise economic definition explaining choice behavior. Economics focuses on "what is forgone".
Option D (Correct)
分析：這是機會成本的精確定義。排隊需要時間。
• 對青年人來說，放棄的時間可能用來工作（高工資）或娛樂，價值很高。
• 對長者來說（多為退休人士），放棄的時間可能價值較低（如閒暇）。
因此，長者排隊的機會成本（放棄的價值）較低，所以他們更有可能去排隊。
Analysis: This is the precise definition of opportunity cost. Queuing takes time.
• For young people, the time forgone could be used for working (high wage) or leisure, which has high value.
• For the elderly (mostly retired), the time forgone likely has a lower value.
Therefore, the opportunity cost (value forgone) of queuing is lower for the elderly, making them more likely to queue.
Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)
關鍵點 (Pivot Point)：題目強調「花時間輪候」(spend time queuing)。這暗示了我們必須比較「時間成本」。
Pivot Point: The question emphasizes "spend time queuing". This implies we must compare "Time Cost".

邏輯鏈 (Logic Chain)：
1. 免費米 = 金錢價格為 0。
2. 全部成本 = 0 + 時間成本。
3. 誰的時間成本較低？長者 (放棄的替代品價值較低)。
Logic Chain:
1. Free rice = Money price is 0.
2. Full Cost = 0 + Time Cost.
3. Who has lower time cost? Elderly (Value of forgone alternatives is lower).
Common Mistakes (常犯錯誤)
誤選 C (貧窮)：學生常直覺認為窮人就會去排隊。雖然現實中相關，但在 DSE 經濟科中，必須選擇「定義最準確」的選項。選項 D 直接解釋了「為什麼」貧窮或退休會導致排隊（因為放棄的價值低），是更根本的原因。
Choosing C (Poorer): Students intuitively think poor people queue. While correlated in reality, in HKDSE Economics, you must choose the "most theoretically accurate" option. Option D explains *why* being poor or retired leads to queuing (because the value forgone is low), which is the fundamental reason.
