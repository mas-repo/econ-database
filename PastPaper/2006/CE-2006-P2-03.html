HKDSE Economics Question Analysis

Topic (課題): Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念) Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆
Key Concepts Scarcity (稀少性), Economic Goods (經濟物品), Free Goods (免費物品)

The Question (題目)

這些免費米是經濟物品，因為
These packs of charity rice are economic goods because
  • A. 這些米的數量有限。
    the quantity of this rice is limited.
  • B. 米能滿足人們的慾望。
    rice can satisfy people’s wants.
  • C. 人們對這些米的慾望無窮。
    the wants for this rice are unlimited.
  • D. 人們想獲得更多這些米。
    people want more of this rice.

Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 稀少性 (Scarcity)
Scarcity exists when resources are insufficient to satisfy unlimited human wants. It is a relative concept.
$$ \text{Quantity Demanded} > \text{Quantity Supplied (at Price = 0)} $$
2. 經濟物品 (Economic Good)
An economic good is any good that is scarce. Because it is scarce, people prefer having more of it.
  • 定義：其數量不足以滿足所有人的慾望（在價格為零時）。
    Definition: Its quantity is insufficient to satisfy all human wants (at zero price).
  • 特徵：人們願意付出代價或成本去獲取它。
    Feature: People are willing to pay a price or cost to obtain it.
3. 免費物品 (Free Good)
A free good is a good whose quantity is sufficient to satisfy all human wants even at zero price (e.g., sunshine, air in some contexts). One does not need to pay or forgo anything to get it.

Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: D
Option A (Wrong)
分析：「數量有限」(Limited quantity) 並不足以構成稀少性。例如，「變壞的雞蛋」數量也是有限的，但沒有人想要，所以它不是經濟物品。稀少性是「相對」概念，必須比較「擁有的數量」與「想要的數量」。
Analysis: "Limited quantity" alone does not constitute scarcity. For example, the quantity of "rotten eggs" is also limited, but no one wants them, so they are not economic goods. Scarcity is a "relative" concept; it requires comparing "quantity available" with "quantity desired".
Option B (Wrong)
分析：「能滿足慾望」只代表它是有用的物品 (Goods)，但不一定是「經濟物品」(Economic Goods)。免費物品（如陽光）也能滿足慾望，但它不是經濟物品。
Analysis: "Can satisfy wants" only indicates that it is a "Good", but not necessarily an "Economic Good". Free goods (like sunshine) can also satisfy wants, but they are not economic goods.
Option C (Wrong)
分析：這是一個常見的陷阱。人類的慾望「整體」是無窮的，但對「某一種特定物品」（如這包米）的慾望通常是有限的。此外，定義經濟物品的重點在於「現有數量不足以滿足慾望」，而不是慾望本身是否無窮。
Analysis: This is a common trap. Human wants *as a whole* are unlimited, but the want for a *specific good* (like this rice) is usually limited. Furthermore, the definition of an economic good focuses on "available quantity being insufficient to satisfy wants," not whether the want itself is infinite.
Option D (Correct)
分析：這是稀少性（Scarcity）最直接的驗證方法。如果人們「想獲得更多」(want more of it)，即表示在價格為零時，需求量大於供應量 (\(Q_d > Q_s\))。因為還未被滿足，所以人們願意付出代價（如排隊的時間成本）去獲取，這正是經濟物品的定義。
Analysis: This is the most direct verification of Scarcity. If people "want more of it", it implies that at zero price, the quantity demanded exceeds the quantity supplied (\(Q_d > Q_s\)). Because wants are not fully satisfied, people are willing to pay a cost (like time cost for queuing) to obtain it, which defines an economic good.
Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)
關鍵字 (Keywords)：「經濟物品」(Economic Goods)。
Keywords: "Economic Goods".

邏輯測試 (Logic Test)：
要證明某物是經濟物品，我們只需問一個問題：
「如果這東西免費放在這裡，人們是否想要拿走更多？」
如果是，它就是稀少的，就是經濟物品。
Logic Test:
To prove something is an economic good, we just ask one question:
"If this item is free, do people want to take more of it?"
If yes, it is scarce, and thus an economic good.
Common Mistakes (常犯錯誤)
混淆「有限」與「稀少」：
學生常誤選 A。請記住：有限 \(\neq\) 稀少。
稀少 = 想要 > 擁有。
有限 = 數量不是無限大 (物理概念)。
垃圾是有限的，但不是稀少的。
Confusing "Limited" with "Scarce":
Students often wrongly choose A. Remember: Limited \(\neq\) Scarce.
Scarce = Wanted > Available.
Limited = Quantity is not infinite (Physical concept).
Trash is limited, but not scarce.

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧：尋找「想要更多」(Want More)
在 DSE 選擇題中，如果題目問「為什麼 X 是經濟物品？」，正確答案通常包含以下字眼之一：
  1. 人們想獲得更多 (People want more of it)
  2. 其數量不足以滿足慾望 (Quantity is insufficient to satisfy wants)
  3. 獲取它涉及機會成本 (Obtaining it involves opportunity cost)
看到這些描述，幾乎可以確定是正確答案。
