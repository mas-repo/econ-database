Economic Analysis Guide

Topic: Money & Banking (貨幣與銀行) Sub-topic: Demand for Money (貨幣需求) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Keywords: Asset Demand, Liquidity Preference, Bond Prices

The Question (題目)

Question: Which of the following can explain the shift of the total demand for money curve from \(Md_0\) to \(Md_1\)?

題目：下列哪一項能解釋貨幣總需求曲線由 \(Md_0\) 移至 \(Md_1\)？

  • A. Expectations of a rise in bond prices (預期債券價格上升)
  • B. A rise in bond prices (債券價格上升)
  • C. An increase in inflationary expectations (預期通脹上升)
  • D. An increase in the real income of households (住戶的實質收入增加)

Concept Review (概念重溫)

Keynesian Liquidity Preference Theory (凱恩斯流動性偏好理論)

The demand for money (\(M_d\)) consists of two main components:

貨幣需求 (\(M_d\)) 主要由兩個部分組成：

  1. Transaction Demand (交易性需求): Money held to buy goods. It depends positively on Income (\(Y\)) and Price Level (\(P\)).
    持有貨幣是為了購買商品。它與收入 (\(Y\)) 和物價水平 (\(P\)) 成正比。
  2. Asset Demand (資產性需求) / Speculative Motive (投機動機): Money held as a store of value instead of bonds. It depends negatively on the Interest Rate (\(R\)).
    持有貨幣作為價值儲存，而非持有債券。它與利率 (\(R\)) 成反比。
$$ \text{Bond Price} (P_b) \propto \frac{1}{\text{Interest Rate} (R)} $$

Crucial Logic: If you expect bond prices to rise in the future, you will want to buy bonds now to earn capital gains later. To buy bonds, you reduce your money holdings.
關鍵邏輯：如果你預期債券價格將來會上升，你會想現在買入債券以賺取資本增值。為了買債券，你會減少手持的貨幣。

Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option: A

Option A (Correct) Expectations of a rise in bond prices (預期債券價格上升)

If people expect bond prices to rise, they anticipate a capital gain (profit) from holding bonds. Therefore, the attractiveness of bonds increases relative to money. People will convert their money into bonds now. This decreases the demand for money (Asset Demand), causing the \(M_d\) curve to shift to the left.
如果人們預期債券價格上升，他們預期持有債券會帶來資本增值（利潤）。因此，相對於貨幣，債券的吸引力增加。人們會現在將貨幣兌換成債券。這減少了對貨幣的需求（資產性需求），導致 \(M_d\) 曲線向左移動。

Option B (Trap) A rise in bond prices (債券價格上升)

The current bond price is inversely related to the current interest rate (the Y-axis). A change in the variable on the axis (Interest Rate) causes a movement along the curve, not a shift of the curve. A rise in bond prices means a fall in interest rates, which corresponds to a downward movement along the \(M_d\) curve.
當前的債券價格與當前的利率（縱軸）成反比。軸上變量（利率）的改變會導致沿著曲線的移動，而不是曲線的平移。債券價格上升意味著利率下降，這對應於沿著 \(M_d\) 曲線向下的移動。

Option C (Distractor) An increase in inflationary expectations (預期通脹上升)

While high inflation makes money a poor store of value, in the context of the Liquidity Preference Theory (Money vs. Bonds), Option A is the direct definition of the Speculative Motive shift. Furthermore, if expected inflation rises, nominal interest rates often rise (Fisher Effect), but the specific shift in the Asset Demand curve is most directly explained by the expected return on alternative assets (Bonds) via price changes.
雖然高通脹使貨幣成為糟糕的價值儲存工具，但在流動性偏好理論（貨幣對債券）的背景下，選項 A 是投機動機移動的直接定義。此外，如果預期通脹上升，名義利率通常會上升（費雪效應），但資產性需求曲線的具體移動最直接由替代資產（債券）的預期回報（價格變動）解釋。

Option D (Incorrect) An increase in real income of households (住戶的實質收入增加)

Higher income means people make more transactions. This increases the Transaction Demand for money. The \(M_d\) curve would shift to the right, not the left.
較高的收入意味著人們進行更多的交易。這增加了交易性貨幣需求。\(M_d\) 曲線會向右移動，而不是向左。

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

To solve this problem, follow these steps:

  1. Identify the Direction: The arrow points Left. \(M_d\) decreases. (確認方向：箭頭向左，貨幣需求減少。)
  2. Distinguish Movement vs. Shift: The Y-axis is Interest Rate. Anything related to the current interest rate or current bond price causes a movement along the curve. Eliminate Option B immediately. (區分移動與平移：縱軸是利率。任何與當前利率或當前債券價格有關的因素都會導致沿曲線移動。立即排除選項 B。)
  3. Analyze Determinants:
    • Income \(\uparrow\) \(\rightarrow\) \(M_d\) shifts Right (Eliminate D).
    • Expected Bond Price \(\uparrow\) \(\rightarrow\) Buy Bonds \(\rightarrow\) Hold less Money \(\rightarrow\) \(M_d\) shifts Left.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: Why isn't it Option C? Doesn't inflation make me want to get rid of money?
問：為什麼不是選項 C？通脹不是會讓我不想持有貨幣嗎？

A: You are partially right that inflation reduces the value of money. However, in HKDSE Economics (Keynesian framework), we focus on the choice between Money and Bonds.

The "Speculative Motive" is strictly defined by expectations of bond prices (interest rates). Option A hits this definition exactly. Option C is less precise because while it reduces money demand, it might also affect the demand for real assets (like houses), which is outside the simple Money-Bond model. In exams, always choose the answer that directly fits the theoretical definition (Asset Demand \(\leftrightarrow\) Bond Price Expectations).

答：你部分正確，通脹確實降低了貨幣的價值。然而，在 HKDSE 經濟學（凱恩斯框架）中，我們關注的是貨幣與債券之間的選擇。

「投機動機」嚴格由債券價格（利率）的預期定義。選項 A 完全符合這個定義。選項 C 不夠精確，因為雖然它減少了貨幣需求，但它也可能影響對實質資產（如房屋）的需求，這超出了簡單的貨幣-債券模型。在考試中，總是選擇最直接符合理論定義的答案（資產需求 \(\leftrightarrow\) 債券價格預期）。

Exam Tricks (考試技巧)

The "Inverse Rule" of Bonds and Money:

Memorize this chain of logic for Asset Demand:

Expected \(P_{\text{bond}}\) \(\uparrow\) \(\Rightarrow\) Buy Bonds NOW \(\Rightarrow\) Sell Money \(\Rightarrow\) \(M_d\) \(\downarrow\) (Left Shift)
Expected \(P_{\text{bond}}\) \(\downarrow\) \(\Rightarrow\) Sell Bonds NOW \(\Rightarrow\) Hold Money \(\Rightarrow\) \(M_d\) \(\uparrow\) (Right Shift)

記憶口訣：預期債價升，現在快買債，拋售手中錢，貨幣需求跌。
