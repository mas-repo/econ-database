Key Concepts (核心概念): Nominal GDP (名義GDP) Real GDP (實質GDP) Inflation (通脹)

📝 The Question (題目)

下圖顯示某經濟的名義本地生產總值增長率和通脹率。
根據上述資料，可得出的結論是 ：從2017年到2022年間，該經濟的 __________。

(1) 實質本地生產總值連續每年上升
(2) 物價水平連續每年上升
(3) 名義本地生產總值增長率一直高於實質本地生產總值增長率

A. 只有 (1)、(2)
B. 只有 (1)、(3)
C. 只有 (2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)

The following diagram shows the nominal GDP growth rates and the inflation rates of an economy.
Based on the above information, we can conclude that from 2017 to 2022, ____________ in the economy.

(1) the real GDP increased in all consecutive years
(2) the price level increased in all consecutive years
(3) the nominal GDP growth rate was always higher than the real GDP growth rate

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

💡 Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這條題目，我們必須掌握名義GDP、實質GDP與通脹率之間的數學關係：

To solve this question, we must understand the mathematical relationship between Nominal GDP, Real GDP, and Inflation Rate:
$$ \text{Real GDP Growth Rate} \approx \text{Nominal GDP Growth Rate} - \text{Inflation Rate} $$ $$ \text{實質GDP增長率} \approx \text{名義GDP增長率} - \text{通脹率} $$
  • Inflation Rate (通脹率): 反映一般物價水平的變動。只要通脹率大於 0 (\( \pi > 0 \))，物價水平就在上升。
    Reflects changes in the general price level. As long as the inflation rate is greater than 0 (\( \pi > 0 \)), the price level is increasing.
  • Real GDP (實質GDP): 反映實際產出。如果實質GDP增長率大於 0，實質GDP上升；如果小於 0，實質GDP下降。
    Reflects actual output. If the real GDP growth rate is > 0, real GDP increases; if < 0, real GDP decreases.

✅ The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是 / The correct answer is: C. 只有 (2)、(3) / (2) and (3) only

Step-by-Step Breakdown (逐項分析):

❌ 句子 (1) 分析 / Statement (1) Analysis:
根據圖表，在 2020 年和 2021 年，通脹率（虛線）高於名義GDP增長率（實線）。根據公式，這意味著這兩年的實質GDP增長率是負數（例如 2020年：約 2% - 4.3% = -2.3%）。因此，實質GDP在這兩年是下降的，並非連續每年上升。句子 (1) 錯誤。
According to the graph, in 2020 and 2021, the inflation rate (dotted line) is higher than the nominal GDP growth rate (solid line). Based on our formula, this means the real GDP growth rate was negative in these years (e.g., 2020: approx. 2% - 4.3% = -2.3%). Therefore, real GDP decreased in these years and did not increase consecutively. Statement (1) is incorrect.

✅ 句子 (2) 分析 / Statement (2) Analysis:
從 2017 年到 2022 年，通脹率一直處於 0% 以上（大約在 2% 到 4.5% 之間）。只要通脹率是正數，一般物價水平就會上升。句子 (2) 正確。
From 2017 to 2022, the inflation rate is always above 0% (ranging roughly between 2% and 4.5%). As long as the inflation rate is positive, the general price level is increasing. Statement (2) is correct.

✅ 句子 (3) 分析 / Statement (3) Analysis:
因為 \( \text{名義增長率} = \text{實質增長率} + \text{通脹率} \)，而圖中每年的通脹率都是正數（\( > 0 \)），所以名義GDP增長率必然大於實質GDP增長率。句子 (3) 正確。
Since \( \text{Nominal Growth} = \text{Real Growth} + \text{Inflation} \), and the inflation rate is positive (\( > 0 \)) for every year shown in the graph, the nominal GDP growth rate must mathematically be higher than the real GDP growth rate. Statement (3) is correct.

🚀 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

找尋「交叉點」(Find the crossover point):
在選擇題中，圖表的線條交叉點通常是破題關鍵。當你看到 2020 年通脹率的線「升穿」了名義增長率的線，你就可以立刻判斷出該年的實質增長率變成了負數。這只需 3 秒鐘就能排除選項 (1)！
In MCQs, the intersection of lines on a graph is usually the key. When you see the inflation line "cross above" the nominal growth line in 2020, you can immediately tell that real growth became negative. This takes 3 seconds and eliminates statement (1)!

⚠️ Common Mistakes (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

混淆「增長率下降」與「數值下降」 (Confusing a falling growth rate with falling absolute values):
圖中通脹率在 2017-2019 年間向下傾斜，很多學生會誤以為「物價下跌」(Deflation)。記住，只要線在 0% 橫軸之上，物價仍然在上升，只是上升得比較慢（這稱為反通脹 Disinflation）。
The inflation rate slopes downward from 2017-2019, leading many students to mistakenly think prices are falling (Deflation). Remember, as long as the line is above the 0% axis, prices are still increasing, just at a slower rate (this is called Disinflation).

🙋 Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼 2020 年實質 GDP 會下跌？(Why did Real GDP fall in 2020?)
A: 想像一下，如果你的名義收入（Nominal GDP）增加了 2%，但物價（Inflation）卻上升了 4.3%。你的購買力實際上是減少了的。同樣道理，國家的實際產出（Real GDP）在扣除物價升幅後，實際上是萎縮了。
Imagine your nominal income (Nominal GDP) increased by 2%, but prices (Inflation) rose by 4.3%. Your actual purchasing power has decreased. Similarly, the country's actual output (Real GDP), after adjusting for price increases, has shrunk.
