Topic: Money and Inflation 貨幣與通脹 Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Fisher Equation, Cost of Holding Money

1. 題目 (The Question)

債券的名義利率 6%
預期通脹率 4%
Nominal interest rate on bonds 6%
Expected inflation rate 4%
根據上述資料，持有貨幣的 _________ 是6%，而持有 __________ 的預期實質回報率是 -4%。
Based on the above information, the _________ of holding money is 6% and the expected real rate of return of holding __________ is –4%.
A. 成本 …… 貨幣
A. cost …… money

B. 成本 …… 債券
B. cost …… bonds

C. 名義回報率 …… 貨幣
C. nominal rate of return …… money

D. 名義回報率 …… 債券
D. nominal rate of return …… bonds

2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

A. 持有貨幣的成本 (Cost of Holding Money)

持有現金（貨幣）的機會成本，就是你放棄了的利息收入。因此，持有貨幣的成本 = 債券的名義利率。另外，由於現金不會產生利息，貨幣的「名義回報率」永遠是 0%。
The opportunity cost of holding cash (money) is the interest income you forfeit. Therefore, the cost of holding money = nominal interest rate on bonds. Additionally, since cash does not generate interest, the "nominal rate of return" of money is always 0%.

B. 費沙方程式 (Fisher Equation)

實質回報率是指扣除通脹影響後的購買力增長。我們可以使用費沙方程式來計算：
The real rate of return is the growth in purchasing power after adjusting for inflation. We calculate this using the Fisher Equation:
$$\text{Expected Real Rate of Return} = \text{Nominal Rate of Return} - \text{Expected Inflation Rate}$$ $$\text{預期實質回報率} = \text{名義回報率} - \text{預期通脹率}$$

3. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): A

Option A (正確 Correct)

第一部分 (First Blank): 債券的名義利率是 6%。這代表如果你選擇持有貨幣而不買債券，你放棄了 6% 的利息。所以，持有貨幣的「成本」是 6%。
第二部分 (Second Blank): 貨幣的名義回報率是 0%。根據費沙方程式，持有貨幣的預期實質回報率 = 0% - 4% = -4%。完全符合題目要求！
First Blank: The nominal interest rate on bonds is 6%. This means if you hold money instead of buying bonds, you give up 6% interest. Thus, the "cost" of holding money is 6%.
Second Blank: The nominal rate of return on money is 0%. By the Fisher Equation, the expected real rate of return of holding money = 0% - 4% = -4%. This perfectly matches the question!

Option B (錯誤 Wrong)

前半部分正確（成本是 6%），但後半部分錯誤。持有「債券」的預期實質回報率應為：6% (名義回報) - 4% (通脹) = +2%，而不是 -4%。
The first half is correct (cost is 6%), but the second half is wrong. The expected real rate of return of holding "bonds" would be: 6% (nominal return) - 4% (inflation) = +2%, not -4%.

Option C & D (錯誤 Wrong)

第一個空格錯誤。持有貨幣的「名義回報率」是 0%，絕對不是 6%。6% 是債券的回報，也是持有貨幣的成本。
The first blank is incorrect. The "nominal rate of return" of holding money is 0%, definitely not 6%. 6% is the return on bonds, which acts as the cost of holding money.

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

破題關鍵 (The Pivot Point):
解答這類題目的關鍵在於牢記一個隱藏數據：「貨幣的名義回報率恆等於 0%」。題目不會直接告訴你這個數字，你必須自己帶入這個知識點。
The Pivot Point:
The key to solving this type of question is remembering a hidden piece of data: "The nominal rate of return of money is always 0%". The question will never state this explicitly; you must supply this knowledge yourself.

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

  • 概念混淆： 學生經常把「持有貨幣的成本」(Opportunity Cost) 和「持有貨幣的名義回報率」(Nominal Return) 混為一談。記住，回報是你賺到的（現金是 0%），成本是你放棄的（債券的 6%）。
  • 正負號錯誤： 計算實質回報時，忘記考慮負號。現金不生息，但在通脹下購買力會縮水，所以實質回報必定是負數 (\(0\% - \pi^e\))。
  • Concept Confusion: Students often confuse the "Opportunity Cost" of holding money with the "Nominal Return" of holding money. Remember, return is what you earn (0% for cash), while cost is what you give up (6% from bonds).
  • Sign Errors: Forgetting the negative sign when calculating real returns. Cash pays no interest, but its purchasing power shrinks during inflation, so its real return is necessarily negative (\(0\% - \pi^e\)).

6. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q1: 點解持有貨幣的名義回報率係 0%？ (Why is the nominal return of holding money 0%?)
A: 因為放在你銀包裏的紙幣或硬幣不會自動衍生利息。100蚊放一年後，面值依然是100蚊。
A: Because the physical banknotes or coins in your wallet do not automatically generate interest. A $100 bill left for a year still has a face value of $100.
Q2: 如果題目問持有「債券」的實質回報，應該點計？ (What if the question asked for the real rate of return of holding "bonds"?)
A: 如果是持有債券，名義回報就是債券利率 (6%)。預期實質回報 = 6% - 4% = 2%。這代表你的購買力實際上增加了 2%。
A: For bonds, the nominal return is the bond interest rate (6%). Expected real return = 6% - 4% = 2%. This means your actual purchasing power increased by 2%.

7. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

秒殺技巧 (The 5-Second Shortcut):
一看題目第一個空格是「6%」。我們知道貨幣不可能有 6% 的名義回報率，所以選項 C 和 D 的「名義回報率」瞬間被淘汰（直接劃掉）。剩下 A 和 B，我們只需要看第二個空格：-4% 是個負數，只有不生息的「貨幣」在通脹下才會產生負實質回報，債券的實質回報是正的 (+2%)。答案秒選 A！
The 5-Second Shortcut:
Look at the first blank which equals "6%". We know money cannot have a 6% nominal rate of return, so "nominal rate of return" in options C and D are instantly eliminated (cross them out). Left with A and B, just look at the second blank: -4% is negative. Only non-interest-bearing "money" yields a negative real return during inflation, whereas bonds yield a positive real return (+2%). The answer is immediately A!
