Topic: 廠商擴張與結合 (Firm Expansion & Integration) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Key Concepts: Types of Integration, Economies of Scale, Stages of Production

題目 (The Question)

一間位於日本的連鎖超級市場收購了一間在香港的連鎖日本生活百貨店。下列哪項最不可能是上述收購的動機？

  1. 享有規模經濟
  2. 通過市場多元化分散風險
  3. 更有效運用資源
  4. 確保原料供應穩定

A supermarket chain located in Japan took over a Japanese grocery chain in Hong Kong. Which of the following is the LEAST likely motive of the above takeover?

  1. to enjoy economies of scale
  2. to spread its business risk through market diversification
  3. to utilise its resources more efficiently
  4. to ensure a steady supply of raw materials

概念重溫 (Concept Review)

要解答這類題目，我們需要清楚了解廠商擴張 (Expansion of Firms) 的類型及其背後的動機。這題涉及的是跨地域的擴張，同時兩者皆處於第三產業 (Tertiary Production) 的零售階段。

To answer this type of question, we need a clear understanding of the types of Expansion of Firms and their underlying motives. This question involves cross-regional expansion, and both firms operate in the retail stage of Tertiary Production.

主要結合類型 (Main Types of Integration):

  • 水平結合 (Horizontal Integration): 結合的兩間公司處於同一生產階段（例如：兩間超級市場合併）。主要目的是擴大市場佔有率及享有規模經濟。
    Integration of two firms at the same stage of production (e.g., two supermarkets merging). Main motives are expanding market share and enjoying economies of scale.
  • 垂直結合 (Vertical Integration): 結合的公司處於不同生產階段。
    - 後向垂直結合 (Backward Vertical Integration): 向原材料供應商方向結合（例如：超市收購農場），為確保原料供應穩定。
    - 前向垂直結合 (Forward Vertical Integration): 向市場銷售方向結合。
    Integration of firms at different stages of production. Backward integration secures raw materials (e.g., a supermarket buying a farm). Forward integration secures market outlets.

關鍵概念公式 (Key Concept Formula):

規模經濟 (Economies of Scale): 當廠商產量 \(Q\) 增加時，長期平均成本 \(AC\) 會下降。

When a firm's output \(Q\) increases, its long-run average cost \(AC\) falls.

$$AC = \frac{Total Cost (TC)}{Quantity (Q)}$$

答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

Distractor A: 享有規模經濟 (to enjoy economies of scale)

收購後，合併企業的整體規模擴大，可以在採購（例如大批量向供應商買貨以獲得折扣）、管理、融資等方面節省成本，這是一個非常合理的動機。

After the takeover, the combined firm operates at a larger scale. It can save costs in purchasing (e.g., bulk-buying discounts), management, and financing. This is a highly reasonable motive.

Distractor B: 通過市場多元化分散風險 (to spread its business risk through market diversification)

原本只在日本經營，現在將業務擴展至香港。如果日本本土市場經濟不景氣，香港市場的收益可以幫助平衡整體風險，這也是跨國擴張的常見動機。

Operating only in Japan initially, expanding to Hong Kong diversifies the market. If the Japanese economy faces a downturn, revenue from the Hong Kong market can help balance the overall risk. This is a common motive for multinational expansion.

Distractor C: 更有效運用資源 (to utilise its resources more efficiently)

兩間公司都屬於日本生活用品的零售商，它們可以共用資源，例如共享同一套存貨管理系統 (IT systems)、採購網絡或品牌管理團隊，從而減少重複開支。

Both companies are retailers of Japanese goods. They can share resources such as inventory management IT systems, procurement networks, or brand management teams, thereby reducing duplicated expenses.

Correct Option D: 確保原料供應穩定 (to ensure a steady supply of raw materials)

這是最不可能的！ 超級市場和生活百貨店都屬於「零售業」（第三產業），它們的作用是將製成品銷售給最終消費者。香港的生活百貨店並不生產或供應「原材料」（如農產品、木材等）給日本的超市。確保原料供應是「後向垂直結合」的動機。

This is the LEAST likely! Both supermarkets and grocery chains are "retailers" (tertiary sector), whose function is to sell finished goods to final consumers. A grocery chain in Hong Kong does not produce or supply "raw materials" (like agricultural products or timber) to a Japanese supermarket. Securing raw materials is a motive for "backward vertical integration".

題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

🎯 解題樞紐 (Pivot Point): 辨識生產階段 (Identify the Stages of Production).

當你看到題目中的公司名稱：「超級市場」(Supermarket) 和「生活百貨店」(Grocery chain)，你的大腦應該立即亮起紅燈——這兩者都是零售商 (Retailers)。

干擾資訊 (Distractors in question): 題目強調了「日本」和「香港」，這只是用來引導你思考「市場多元化」(選項 B)，而不會改變它們是同類零售商的事實。既然沒有涉及初級產業 (Primary sector) 的農場或製造商，就絕對不會是為了獲取「原料」(Raw materials)。

When you see the names "Supermarket" and "Grocery chain" in the question, your brain should immediately flag that both are Retailers. The mention of "Japan" and "Hong Kong" is just setting up the "market diversification" distractor (Option B). Since no primary sector (like farms) or manufacturers are involved, it cannot possibly be to secure "raw materials".

常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

  • 死背動機而不看情境 (Memorizing motives without context): 學生經常死背「確保原料供應」是擴張的優點之一，卻忘記了這僅適用於後向垂直結合 (Backward Vertical Integration)。
  • 忽略「最不可能是」(Ignoring "LEAST likely"): 考試時太緊張，看到 A 是正確的擴張好處就立刻圈選，忘記題目是在找錯誤/不合理的選項。

學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 如果這間日本超市收購的是香港的一間農場，答案會改變嗎？

A: 絕對會！如果收購的是農場（第一產業），這就變成了「後向垂直結合」。那時候，選項 D「確保原料供應穩定」就會變成非常合理的動機。但在本題中，收購的是百貨店，所以 D 不合理。

Q: If the Japanese supermarket acquired a farm in Hong Kong, would the answer change?
A: Absolutely! If it acquired a farm (primary sector), it would be "backward vertical integration". In that case, Option D "to ensure a steady supply of raw materials" would become a highly reasonable motive. But here, a grocery store is acquired, making D illogical.

考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

秒殺法 (The 5-Second Kill):

尋找「詞彙不匹配」(Vocabulary Mismatch)。
「超市」(Supermarket) /「百貨店」(Grocery store) = 賣完成品的地方。
「原料」(Raw materials) = 未加工的東西。
兩者邏輯衝突，直接選 D 作為「最不可能」的選項，連 A、B、C 都可以不用深入分析，節省大量考試時間！

Vocabulary Mismatch:
Supermarket/Grocery store = places selling finished goods.
Raw materials = unprocessed goods.
These concepts logically conflict. Choose D immediately as the "least likely" motive and save time without deeply analyzing A, B, and C!
