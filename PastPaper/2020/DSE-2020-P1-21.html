📊 經濟學題目分析 Economics Question Analysis

課題 Topic: 界外效應與市場干預 Externalities and Market Intervention

難度 Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5 Stars)

核心概念 Key Concepts: 負面界外影響 Negative Externality 邊際私人成本 Marginal Private Cost 實證與規範性陳述 Positive vs Normative

📝 題目 The Question

白麋鹿咖啡店 (The White Moose Café)
**小孩尖叫稅 (SCREAMING CHILD TAX)**
為員工和顧客雙方著想，我們決定向尖叫的小孩徵稅。若我們留意到你的小孩在尖叫，會自動在你的賬單加收 15% 附加費。你的同行者中，每多一位小孩尖叫，我們再收取額外的 10%。
深信你們會明白我們的立場。

參考以上資料，下列哪項陳述是正確的？

  1. 小孩在咖啡店尖叫引致負面界外影響，而所有咖啡店應該採取相似的政策以回復效率。
  2. 對小孩尖叫的額外收費增加有尖叫小孩的人士的邊際私人成本。
  3. 「小孩尖叫稅」是政府解決私人成本和社會成本分歧的一種方法。
  4. 「小孩尖叫稅」是一種歧視，故此不應實施。

The White Moose Café
**SCREAMING CHILD TAX**
For the benefit of both staff and customers alike, we have taken the decision to impose a tax on children who scream. If we notice that your child is screaming, an automatic 15% surcharge will be applied to your bill. For every further screaming child you have in your group, an additional 10% will be added.
We trust you understand our position.

With reference to the above information, which of the following statements is correct?

  1. Children screaming in the café results in negative externality and all cafés should introduce similar policy to restore efficiency.
  2. The extra charge on screaming children increases the marginal private cost of the groups with screaming children.
  3. The 'screaming child tax' is a government solution to the divergence between private and social costs.
  4. The 'screaming child tax' is a kind of discrimination and should not be imposed.

💡 概念重溫 Concept Review

要解答這道題目，我們需要理解以下幾個核心經濟學概念：

To answer this question, we need to understand the following core economic concepts:
  • 負面界外影響 (Negative Externality): 當一項經濟活動對沒有參與該活動的第三方造成未被補償的成本時發生。此時，邊際社會成本大於邊際私人成本。
    Occurs when an economic activity imposes uncompensated costs on third parties not involved in the activity. Here, Marginal Social Cost is greater than Marginal Private Cost.
    $$ MSC > MPC $$ $$ MSC = MPC + MEC $$
  • 內部化界外影響 (Internalizing an Externality): 透過收費或罰款，將第三方承受的成本轉嫁回決策者身上，使其邊際私人成本 \(MPC\) 增加。
    Transferring the cost borne by third parties back to the decision-maker through charges or fines, thereby increasing their Marginal Private Cost \(MPC\).
  • 實證性與規範性陳述 (Positive vs. Normative Statements): 實證性陳述是關於「是什麼」(客觀事實)，可以被驗證；規範性陳述是關於「應該是什麼」(價值觀判斷)，包含「應該 (should)」等字眼。
    Positive statements are about "what is" (objective facts) and can be tested; Normative statements are about "what ought to be" (value judgments) and often contain words like "should".

✅ 答案與詳細分析 The Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案 Correct Option: B

選項 A 分析 (Option A Analysis): ❌ 錯誤

雖然小孩尖叫的確引致負面界外影響，但後半句「所有咖啡店應該採取相似的政策」屬於規範性陳述 (Normative statement)，涉及價值觀判斷。經濟學不能客觀地證明所有咖啡店都「應該」這樣做，因為不同咖啡店的客源和成本結構不同。

Although screaming children do cause a negative externality, the second half "all cafés should introduce similar policy" is a normative statement involving value judgments. Economics cannot objectively prove that all cafes "should" do this, as different cafes have different customer bases and cost structures.

選項 B 分析 (Option B Analysis): ✅ 正確

咖啡店對尖叫小孩收取附加費，這筆額外費用直接由帶小孩的顧客支付。這將原本由其他顧客承受的滋擾成本「內部化」，直接增加了該顧客帶小孩來咖啡店並讓其尖叫的邊際私人成本 (Marginal Private Cost)。

The cafe charges a surcharge for screaming children, which is paid directly by the customers with the children. This "internalizes" the nuisance cost originally borne by other customers, directly increasing the Marginal Private Cost of bringing a child to the cafe and letting them scream.

選項 C 分析 (Option C Analysis): ❌ 錯誤

題目清楚指出這是「白麋鹿咖啡店」的私人商業決定，而不是「政府」的政策或稅收。雖然它被稱為「稅」，但實際上只是商戶的附加費。

The text clearly states this is a private business decision by "The White Moose Café", not a "government" policy or tax. Although called a "tax", it is actually just a commercial surcharge.

選項 D 分析 (Option D Analysis): ❌ 錯誤

這包含「不應實施 (should not be imposed)」的字眼，這是一個基於個人價值觀的規範性陳述 (Normative statement)，而不是客觀的經濟學事實。

This contains the phrase "should not be imposed", which is a normative statement based on personal values, not an objective economic fact.

🔍 題目拆解 Problem Decomposition

解題關鍵 (Pivot Point): 區分「實證性 (Positive)」與「規範性 (Normative)」陳述，以及認清政策的實施主體 (政府 vs. 私人市場)。

Pivot Point: Distinguishing between "Positive" and "Normative" statements, and identifying the entity implementing the policy (Government vs. Private Market).
  • 干擾資訊 (Distractors): 題目中提到的「15%」和「10%」具體數字其實是干擾資訊，解題時完全不需要用到這些數字進行計算。
    The specific numbers "15%" and "10%" are distractors. You don't need to use these numbers for any calculations to solve the problem.
  • 陷阱字眼 (Trap Words): 選項 A 和 D 中的「應該 (should)」。在 DSE 選擇題中，如果題目問「哪項陳述是正確的 (which statement is correct)」，通常是指客觀的實證性陳述。帶有「應該」的選項往往是錯誤的，因為它們無法被證明為絕對「正確」。
    The word "should" in Options A and D. In DSE MCQs, if asked "which statement is correct", it usually refers to objective positive statements. Options with "should" are often wrong because they cannot be proven absolutely "correct".

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 Common Mistakes & Traps

學生常犯的錯誤是看到選項 C 有「解決私人成本和社會成本分歧」就立刻選擇，因為這聽起來非常「經濟學」。但他們忽略了前方的「政府 (government)」一詞。這是一間私人咖啡店的行為，屬於市場自行將界外效應內部化的例子，而非政府干預。

A common mistake is choosing Option C immediately upon seeing "solution to the divergence between private and social costs" because it sounds very "economic". However, students overlook the word "government". This is an action by a private cafe, an example of the market internalizing an externality on its own, not government intervention.

🙋‍♂️ 學生常見疑問 Student Q&A

Q: 為什麼這項政策不是為了解決效率損失 (Efficiency Loss)?

Q: Why isn't this policy meant to solve efficiency loss?

A: 這項政策確實有助於減少因為負面界外影響而產生的效率損失。選項 A 的錯誤不在於「回復效率」這個概念，而在於「所有咖啡店應該採取」這部分。這是一個規範性判斷。有些咖啡店可能主打親子友善，對它們來說，實施這種政策反而會流失主要客源，得不償失。

A: This policy does help reduce the efficiency loss caused by the negative externality. The error in Option A is not the concept of "restoring efficiency", but the part stating "all cafés should introduce". This is a normative judgment. Some cafes might market themselves as family-friendly; for them, implementing such a policy would drive away their main customers, doing more harm than good.

⚡ 考試技巧與速解 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts

「秒殺」技巧 (Elimination Method):

Shortcut Technique:
  1. 掃視選項，尋找規範性字眼。選項 A 有「應該 (should)」，選項 D 有「不應 (should not)」。直接刪除 A 和 D。
  2. 剩下 B 和 C。看文章標題「白麋鹿咖啡店」，這是一間私人企業。選項 C 說是「政府 (government)」，明顯與事實不符。刪除 C。
  3. 得出答案 B。整個過程只需 15 秒。
  1. Scan options for normative words. Option A has "should", Option D has "should not". Eliminate A and D immediately.
  2. Left with B and C. Look at the title "The White Moose Café", a private business. Option C says "government", which contradicts the facts. Eliminate C.
  3. The answer is B. The whole process takes only 15 seconds.
