Topic: 企業擁有權 / Types of Business Ownership
Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Key Concepts: Public Limited Company Listing Liquidity

1. 題目 (The Question)

若香港一家公眾有限公司在股票交易所上市，

A. 它會面對更高的利得稅率。
B. 它不能再對其會計資料保密。
C. 它的股東會享有更高的資產流動性。
D. 它會享有規模經濟。

If a public limited company in Hong Kong gets listed on the stock exchange,

A. it will face a higher profits tax rate.
B. it can no longer keep its accounting information confidential.
C. its shareholders will enjoy higher liquidity of their assets.
D. it will enjoy economies of scale.

2. 概念重溫 (Concept Review)

公眾有限公司 vs 上市公司 (Public Limited Company vs Listed Company)

在香港，公眾有限公司（Public Limited Company）是指可以向公眾發行股票籌集資金的有限公司。然而，並非所有公眾有限公司都在股票交易所（如港交所）掛牌交易。當一家公眾有限公司上市（gets listed），它的股票便可以在二級市場（Secondary Market）上自由買賣。

In Hong Kong, a Public Limited Company is a limited company that can issue shares to the public to raise capital. However, not all public limited companies are traded on a stock exchange. When a public limited company gets listed, its shares can be freely traded on the secondary market.

資產流動性 (Liquidity of Assets)

流動性是指資產轉換為現金的速度和難易程度，且不會導致資產價值大幅損失。股票上市後，買賣變得極為方便，因此流動性大幅提高。

Liquidity refers to the speed and ease with which an asset can be converted into cash without a significant loss in value. Once shares are listed, trading becomes extremely convenient, drastically increasing their liquidity.

$$ \text{Liquidity} \propto \frac{1}{\text{Time and Cost to convert asset to cash}} $$

3. 答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案 (Correct Option): C

A. 它會面對更高的利得稅率。 (it will face a higher profits tax rate.) - 錯誤 (Wrong)

在香港，所有法團（包括私人有限公司、公眾有限公司及上市公司）的利得稅率 \( t \) 都是相同的（目前一般為 16.5%，受兩級制利得稅影響）。上市與否不會改變適用的稅率。

In Hong Kong, the profits tax rate \( t \) is the same for all corporations (including private, public, and listed companies), currently generally at 16.5% subject to the two-tiered regime. Listing status does not change the applicable tax rate.

B. 它不能再對其會計資料保密。 (it can no longer keep its accounting information confidential.) - 錯誤 (Wrong)

這是一個常見陷阱！題目指出該公司已經是一家「公眾有限公司」。根據香港公司法，公眾有限公司（即使未上市）已經需要向公司註冊處提交財務報表供公眾查閱，因此它本來就不能保密其會計資料。上市只是增加了披露的頻率和嚴格程度，而不是「從保密變成不保密」。

This is a common trap! The question states the firm is already a "public limited company". Under HK law, public limited companies (even unlisted ones) must file financial statements with the Companies Registry for public inspection. Therefore, it already could not keep its accounts confidential. Listing merely increases the strictness of disclosure, rather than changing it from confidential to non-confidential.

C. 它的股東會享有更高的資產流動性。 (its shareholders will enjoy higher liquidity of their assets.) - 正確 (Correct)

上市後，公司的股票可以在股票交易所（如香港交易所）自由且迅速地買賣。這為股東提供了一個現成的二手市場（Secondary Market），使他們能更容易地將股票（資產）轉換為現金，即流動性 \( L \) 增加。

After listing, the company's shares can be traded freely and quickly on the stock exchange. This provides a ready secondary market for shareholders, making it much easier for them to convert their shares (assets) into cash, meaning liquidity \( L \) increases.

D. 它會享有規模經濟。 (it will enjoy economies of scale.) - 錯誤 (Wrong)

雖然上市可以籌集大量資金，公司「可能」會利用這些資金進行擴張從而獲得規模經濟，但這並非上市的必然結果。公司也可能將資金用於償還債務或派發特別股息。因此「會享有」（will enjoy）這個絕對的說法是不正確的。

Although listing raises capital and the company *might* use it to expand and achieve economies of scale, this is not a guaranteed outcome. The company might use the funds to pay off debt or issue special dividends. Therefore, the absolute statement "will enjoy" is incorrect.

4. 題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解答這類題目的關鍵在於找出「轉捩點」（Pivot Point）：

  • 起點狀態： 已經是「公眾有限公司」（Public Limited Company）。這意味著它已經可以向公眾招股，且已經需要公開帳目。
  • 變化動作： 「上市」（Gets listed）。
  • 核心影響： 上市的唯一必然直接影響，是股票獲得了在交易所掛牌買賣的資格，從而創造了二手市場（Secondary Market）。這直接對應到資產流動性的提升。

The key to solving this is identifying the Pivot Point: The company is *already* a public limited company (so it already publishes accounts). The action is "getting listed". The only guaranteed direct impact of listing is gaining access to trade on the stock exchange (a secondary market), which directly boosts liquidity.

5. 常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

陷阱：混淆「私人有限公司」與「公眾有限公司」的特徵

很多學生誤選 B，是因為他們將題目看成了「私人有限公司上市」。如果是私人有限公司（Private Limited Company）轉為上市公司，那麼 B 就會是正確的，因為私人公司不需要公開帳目。但題目明確寫著是「公眾有限公司」。

Trap: Confusing Private and Public Limited Companies. Many students mistakenly choose B because they misread the question as a "private limited company" going public. If a private limited company goes public, B would be correct since private companies don't publish accounts. But the question explicitly states it's already a "public limited company".

6. 學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

Q: 難道上市不會讓公司規模變大嗎？為什麼 D 是錯的？

A: 經濟學選擇題中，要注意字眼。選項 D 使用了「會」（will）這個肯定詞。上市籌集資金後，公司規模「可能」（may/could）變大，但也可能因為管理不善反而出現規模不經濟（Diseconomies of scale）。只有 C 是上市帶來的「必然」結果。

Q: Doesn't listing make a company larger? Why is D wrong?
A: In Economics MCQs, pay attention to absolute wording. Option D uses "will". After raising capital, a company "may" grow, but it could also suffer from diseconomies of scale due to poor management. Only C is a guaranteed outcome of listing.

7. 考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

「絕對詞」排除法 (Elimination by Absolute Terms)：

在 DSE 經濟科中，帶有「會」（will）、「必定」（must）等絕對詞的選項，通常描述的必須是定義上的必然結果。規模經濟（Economies of scale）取決於長期平均成本 \( LRAC \) 的下降，這需要實際的生產擴張和效率提升，單靠「上市」這個金融動作是無法保證的，因此可迅速排除 D。

Elimination by Absolute Terms: In DSE Economics, options with absolute words like "will" or "must" require the outcome to be true by definition. Economies of scale depend on a decrease in long-run average cost \( LRAC \), which requires actual production expansion and efficiency gains. A financial action like "listing" cannot guarantee this, so D can be quickly eliminated.
