🔑 關鍵字 Key Concepts: 總需求 (Aggregate Demand), 價格水平 (Price Level), 緊縮性財政政策 (Contractionary Fiscal Policy)

題目 The Question

下列哪些政策會抵銷了投資意願上升對價格水平的影響？

(1) 利得稅率上升
(2) 轉移支付上升
(3) 免稅額下降

A. 只有 (1)、(2)
B. 只有 (1)、(3)
C. 只有 (2)、(3)
D. (1)、(2) 及 (3)
Which of the following policies would offset the effect of an increase in the willingness to invest on the price level?

(1) an increase in profits tax rate
(2) an increase in transfer payment
(3) a decrease in tax allowance

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (1) and (3) only
C. (2) and (3) only
D. (1), (2) and (3)

💡 概念重溫 Concept Review

1. 總需求 (Aggregate Demand, \(AD\)): 經濟體系內對本地生產總產出的總需求。公式如下：
1. Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)): The total demand for domestically produced output in an economy. The formula is:
$$AD = C + I + G + NX$$
2. 財政政策 (Fiscal Policy): 政府透過改變政府開支 (\(G\))、稅收 (\(T\)) 或轉移支付來影響經濟。
要「抵銷」價格水平的上升，政府需要實行緊縮性財政政策 (Contractionary Fiscal Policy)，即減少 \(AD\)，使 \(AD\) 曲線向左移。
2. Fiscal Policy: The use of government spending (\(G\)), taxation (\(T\)), or transfer payments to influence the economy.
To "offset" the increase in the price level, the government needs to implement Contractionary Fiscal Policy, which decreases \(AD\) and shifts the \(AD\) curve to the left.

✅ 答案與詳細分析 Answer & Detailed Analysis

正確答案 Correct Option: B

📌 前提分析 (Initial Situation): 投資意願上升會令投資 (\(I\)) 增加，導致總需求 (\(AD\)) 增加。\(AD\) 曲線向右移會令價格水平 (\(P\)) 上升。因此，我們需要找出能令價格水平下降（即 \(AD\) 減少）的政策。
📌 Initial Situation: An increase in willingness to invest increases Investment (\(I\)), leading to an increase in Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)). A rightward shift of the \(AD\) curve increases the price level (\(P\)). Therefore, we need to find policies that decrease the price level (i.e., decrease \(AD\)).
分析 (1) 利得稅率上升: 增加利得稅會減少企業的除稅後利潤。這會降低企業的投資誘因，令投資 (\(I\)) 減少，從而減少總需求 (\(AD\)) 及降低價格水平。這能抵銷初始的影響。(正確)
Analysis (1) An increase in profits tax rate: Higher profits tax decreases the after-tax profits of firms. This lowers the incentive to invest, decreasing Investment (\(I\)), which in turn decreases Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)) and lowers the price level. This offsets the initial effect. (Correct)
分析 (2) 轉移支付上升: 轉移支付（如綜援、消費券）增加，會增加市民的可支配收入 (Disposable Income)。這會刺激私人消費開支 (\(C\)) 上升，導致總需求 (\(AD\)) 進一步增加，價格水平會升得更高。這會加劇而非抵銷初始影響。(錯誤)
Analysis (2) An increase in transfer payment: An increase in transfer payments (e.g., social security, consumption vouchers) increases people's disposable income. This stimulates private consumption expenditure (\(C\)), leading to a further increase in Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)) and an even higher price level. This amplifies rather than offsets the initial effect. (Incorrect)
分析 (3) 免稅額下降: 免稅額（Tax Allowance）下降意味著市民需要繳交更多的薪俸稅。這會導致可支配收入下降，私人消費開支 (\(C\)) 減少，總需求 (\(AD\)) 隨之減少並降低價格水平。這能抵銷初始的影響。(正確)
Analysis (3) A decrease in tax allowance: A lower tax allowance means citizens have to pay more salaries tax. This leads to a decrease in disposable income, a reduction in private consumption expenditure (\(C\)), a decrease in Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)), and a lower price level. This offsets the initial effect. (Correct)

🔍 題目拆解 Problem Decomposition

解題樞紐 (Pivot Point): 本題的關鍵在於理解「抵銷」(Offset) 一詞的意思。你要先判斷事件一（投資上升）的結果是「通脹」（\(P \uparrow\)），然後尋找能帶來相反結果「通縮」（\(P \downarrow\)）的政策。換言之，題目其實是披著偽裝問你：「以下哪項是緊縮性財政政策？」
Pivot Point: The key to this question lies in understanding the word "offset". You must first determine that the outcome of Event 1 (investment increases) is "inflation" (\(P \uparrow\)), and then look for policies that bring the opposite result, "deflation" (\(P \downarrow\)). In other words, the question is a disguised way of asking: "Which of the following is a contractionary fiscal policy?"

⚠️ 常犯錯誤與陷阱 Common Mistakes & Traps

1. 混淆「免稅額」(Tax Allowance) 與「稅率」(Tax Rate): 很多學生看到「下降」就以為是減稅。注意，免稅額是不用交稅的收入部分。免稅額下降 = 要交稅的錢變多 = 稅務負擔加重 = 可支配收入減少。
2. 誤解「轉移支付」(Transfer Payment): 有些學生以為轉移支付是政府把錢轉移走，其實這是政府無償給予市民的錢（如津貼）。所以轉移支付上升會刺激消費 (\(AD \uparrow\))。
1. Confusing "Tax Allowance" with "Tax Rate": Many students see the word "decrease" and assume it means a tax cut. Note that tax allowance is the portion of income that is tax-free. Decrease in allowance = More taxable income = Heavier tax burden = Lower disposable income.
2. Misunderstanding "Transfer Payment": Some students think this means the government transfers money *away* from people. It is actually money given to citizens without expecting goods or services in return (e.g., subsidies). Thus, an increase in transfer payments stimulates consumption (\(AD \uparrow\)).

🙋‍♂️ 學生常見疑問 Student Q&A

Q: 利得稅率上升會增加商家的生產成本，這不是會令短期總供應 (\(SRAS\)) 減少（向左移）嗎？如果 \(SRAS\) 向左移，價格水平 (\(P\)) 不是會上升嗎？這樣怎能抵銷？
A: 這是個很好的問題！在 DSE 經濟學中，直接稅（如利得稅和薪俸稅）首要且最主要影響的是總需求 (\(AD\))。利得稅直接影響企業的稅後利潤和投資意欲 (\(I \downarrow \rightarrow AD \downarrow\))。雖然它可能對部分供應側有輕微影響，但考試局的標準設定是：直接稅變動主力改變 \(AD\)；只有間接稅（如銷售稅）或生產要素價格變動才會主力改變 \(AS\)。因此，這裡必定是 \(AD\) 向左移，導致價格水平 (\(P\)) 下降。
Q: Doesn't an increase in the profits tax rate increase the cost of production for firms, shifting Short-Run Aggregate Supply (\(SRAS\)) to the left? If \(SRAS\) shifts left, wouldn't the price level (\(P\)) increase? How does that offset the effect?
A: Excellent question! In DSE Economics, direct taxes (like profits tax and salaries tax) primarily and dominantly affect Aggregate Demand (\(AD\)). Profits tax directly impacts after-tax profits and investment incentives (\(I \downarrow \rightarrow AD \downarrow\)). While it might have a slight supply-side effect in reality, the examination authority's standard framework is: changes in direct taxes mainly shift \(AD\); only changes in indirect taxes (like sales tax) or input prices mainly shift \(AS\). Therefore, it's strictly a leftward shift of \(AD\) here, causing the price level (\(P\)) to fall.

🚀 考試技巧與速解 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts

方向箭嘴法 (Directional Arrows):
1. 寫下題目事件的方向：投資 \(\uparrow \Rightarrow P \uparrow\)。
2. 寫下「抵銷」的目標：我們需要 \(P \downarrow\)。
3. 快速掃視選項，找出「膨脹性」(Expansionary) 的政策並立即剔除。轉移支付上升是「派錢」，一定會令 \(P \uparrow\)。所以 (2) 是錯的。
4. 看看選擇：A有(2)、C有(2)、D有(2)。利用消去法 (Elimination Method)，秒殺出答案為 B。
Directional Arrows Method:
1. Jot down the direction of the given event: Investment \(\uparrow \Rightarrow P \uparrow\).
2. Jot down the "offset" target: We need \(P \downarrow\).
3. Quickly scan the options and cross out "Expansionary" policies. An increase in transfer payments is like "giving out free money", which definitely leads to \(P \uparrow\). So (2) is wrong.
4. Look at the choices: A has (2), C has (2), D has (2). Using the Elimination Method, you can instantly find that the answer is B.
