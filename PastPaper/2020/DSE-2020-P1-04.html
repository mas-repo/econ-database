HKDSE Economics: Question Analysis

Topic: Business Ownership & Financing (企業融資) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ (2/5) Key Concepts: Shares (股票), Debentures (債券), Risk & Return (風險與回報)

The Question (題目)

一些小投資者偏好購買債券多於股票。下列哪項是可能的原因？

  • A. 債券的回報普遍高於股票的回報。
  • B. 股票價格可能上升，而債券價格是固定的。
  • C. 股東需要花時間管理公司，而債券持有人則不需要。
  • D. 公司有贖回債券的義務，卻沒有贖回股票的義務。

Some small investors prefer debentures to shares. Which of the following is a possible reason for that?

  • A. The return of debentures is generally higher than that of shares.
  • B. The price of shares may rise while that of debentures is fixed.
  • C. Shareholders need to spend time managing the company but debenture holders do not.
  • D. Companies have obligation to redeem debentures but they have no such obligation for shares.

Concept Review (概念重溫)

要解答這道題目，我們必須清楚區分債權融資 (Debt Financing) 和 股權融資 (Equity Financing) 對投資者的分別。

To answer this question, we must clearly distinguish the differences between Debt Financing and Equity Financing from an investor's perspective.

Definitions (定義)

  • 股票 (Shares): 代表對公司的擁有權 (Ownership)。回報形式為股息 (Dividends) 及資本增值。風險較高，但潛在回報也較高。
    Represents ownership in a company. Returns come in the form of dividends and capital gains. Higher risk, but higher potential return.
  • 債券 (Debentures): 代表對公司的債權 (Debt/Creditorship)。回報形式為固定利息 (Interest)。風險較低，因為債權人有優先索償權，且公司有法律責任償還本金。
    Represents a debt owed by the company. Returns come in the form of fixed interest. Lower risk as creditors have priority in claims and the company is legally obligated to repay the principal.

Relevant Economic Theory (相關經濟理論)

在金融經濟學中，投資的回報率 (Rate of Return) 可以用以下公式表示：

In financial economics, the Rate of Return of an investment can be expressed using the following formula:

$$ \text{Rate of Return} = \frac{\text{Capital Gain/Loss} + \text{Income}}{\text{Initial Investment}} \times 100\% $$

對於債券而言，其主要收入為利息 \( I \)，而在到期日，投資者可取回本金 \( P \)。由於本金獲得法律保障，其風險溢價 (Risk Premium) 較低，因此預期回報率通常低於股票。

For debentures, the primary income is interest \( I \), and at maturity, investors get back the principal \( P \). Since the principal is legally protected, its risk premium is lower, meaning its expected return is generally lower than that of shares.

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer: Option D

A. 債券的回報普遍高於股票的回報。(錯誤)

根據「高風險、高回報」的原則，股票的風險較高，因此其預期潛在回報通常高於債券，而不是低於。

According to the "high risk, high return" principle, shares carry a higher risk, so their expected potential return is generally higher than that of debentures, not lower.
B. 股票價格可能上升，而債券價格是固定的。(錯誤)

首先，債券在二手市場的價格不是固定的，它會受市場利率 (Interest rates) 影響而波動。其次，如果股票價格會上升，這應該是投資者偏好股票的原因，而不是偏好債券的原因！

First, the price of debentures in the secondary market is not fixed; it fluctuates based on market interest rates. Second, if share prices may rise, this would be a reason to prefer shares, not debentures!
C. 股東需要花時間管理公司，而債券持有人則不需要。(錯誤)

雖然股東是公司的擁有者，但現代股份有限公司實行「擁有權與控制權分離」(Separation of ownership and control)。日常管理由董事局及管理層負責，普通小股東不需要花時間管理公司。

Although shareholders are the owners, modern limited companies practice the "separation of ownership and control." Daily management is handled by the board of directors and management; ordinary small shareholders do not need to spend time managing the company.
D. 公司有贖回債券的義務，卻沒有贖回股票的義務。(正確)

債券是一項借貸契約，具有明確的到期日 (Maturity date)，公司有法律責任 (Legal obligation) 在到期時向債券持有人償還本金。相反，股票是永久資本，公司沒有義務向股東退還本金。這種保障正是保守的小投資者偏好債券的原因。

A debenture is a loan contract with a specific maturity date, meaning the company has a legal obligation to repay the principal to debenture holders upon maturity. Shares, conversely, are permanent capital, and the company has no obligation to refund the principal. This protection is exactly why conservative small investors might prefer debentures.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point):
題目的關鍵字是「偏好購買債券」(prefer debentures)。這意味著你必須尋找一個描述「債券比股票有優勢」的選項。如果一個選項描述的是股票的優點（例如選項B），你可以立即將其剔除。

The Pivot Point: The keyword is "prefer debentures". This means you must look for an option that describes an "advantage of debentures over shares." If an option describes a benefit of shares (like Option B), you can eliminate it immediately.

干擾資訊 (Distractors):
選項C利用了學生對「擁有權」的誤解。學生容易將「獨資經營」(Sole proprietorship) 擁有者必須親力親為的概念，錯誤套用到「有限公司」(Limited company) 的小股東身上。

Distractors: Option C exploits students' misunderstanding of "ownership." Students often wrongly apply the concept of a "sole proprietorship" (where the owner must manage everything) to small shareholders in a "limited company."

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

  • 陷阱 1：混淆「固定利息」與「固定價格」
    學生常以為債券是「零風險」，價格永遠不變。實際上，債券的票面息率 (Coupon rate) 是固定的，但其市場價格 (Market price) 會隨利率波動而改變。這就是為什麼選項B的後半句是錯誤的。
    Trap 1: Confusing "fixed interest" with "fixed price". Students often think debentures are "zero risk" with prices that never change. In reality, while the coupon rate is fixed, the market price fluctuates with interest rates. This is why the second half of Option B is factually incorrect.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 既然債券必須償還本金，那發行公司破產了怎麼辦？投資者還能拿回錢嗎？
Q: Since debentures require principal repayment, what happens if the issuing company goes bankrupt? Do investors still get their money back?

A: 如果公司清盤 (Liquidation)，債券持有人（作為債權人）擁有比股東更高的優先索償權 (Priority of claim)。公司變賣資產後，必須先償還債務。雖然不保證能全額取回本金，但他們排在股東前面，風險依然比股東低得多。
A: In the event of liquidation, debenture holders (as creditors) have a higher priority of claim than shareholders. When the company sells its assets, it must pay off debts first. While full repayment isn't guaranteed, they are ahead of shareholders in line, making their risk much lower.

Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

邏輯篩選法 (Logical Elimination):

  1. 問自己：「為什麼我要買A而不買B？」答案必定是「A的某個優點」。
  2. 看選項 B：股票價格上升。這是股票的優點！立刻刪除。
  3. 看選項 A：債券回報高於股票。這違反基本經濟常識 (風險與回報成正比)，立刻刪除。
  4. 看選項 C：「股東要花時間管理」。這不符合現實中小股民買賣股票的經驗（你買騰訊股票不需要去上班），立刻刪除。
  5. 只剩下 D，直接選出正確答案！

Logical Elimination:
1. Ask yourself: "Why buy A instead of B?" The answer must be a "benefit of A".
2. Look at B: Share prices rise. That's a benefit of shares! Eliminate instantly.
3. Look at A: Debentures have higher returns. This violates basic economic logic (risk is proportional to return). Eliminate.
4. Look at C: Shareholders spend time managing. This defies real-world experience for retail investors (you don't go to work at Apple just because you bought their stock). Eliminate.
5. Only D remains. Choose it confidently!
