📊 HKDSE Economics Topic Analysis

Topic (課題): Price Mechanism - Functions of Price (價格機制 - 價格的功能)

Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

Key Concepts (關鍵概念):

Allocative Function (分配功能/引導功能) Rationing Function (配給功能) Resource Allocation (資源分配)

1. The Question (題目)

[中文] 近年，中國對私家車的需求上升，這吸引了褔士汽車(一家德國汽車製造商)在中國開設汽車製造工廠。

上述個案說明了價格的 ___________ 功能，因為 _________。

  1. 配給 …… 在中國，私家車賣給最高價值的用家
  2. 配給 …… 在德國，較少買家願意購買私家車
  3. 分配 …… 私家車的供應下降
  4. 分配 …… 更多的資源投放在私家車的生產上

[English] In recent years, the rise in demand for private cars in China has attracted Volkswagen, a German car manufacturer, to set up car manufacturing factories in China.

The above case illustrates the ___________ function of price because _________.

  1. rationing …… private cars are sold to the highest-valued users in China
  2. rationing …… fewer buyers are willing to buy private cars in Germany
  3. allocative …… the supply of private cars falls in Germany
  4. allocative …… more resources are allocated to the production of private cars in China

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

價格的兩大核心功能 (Two Core Functions of Price)

1. 配給功能 (Rationing Function): 指價格如何將現有（已生產）的物品分配給消費者。市場價格會將物品配給願意並有能力支付該價格的消費者（即最高價值的用家）。

Refers to how price distributes existing (already produced) goods to consumers. Market price rations goods to those who are willing and able to pay for them (i.e., the highest-valued users).

2. 分配功能 / 引導功能 (Allocative / Guiding Function): 指價格作為一個信號（Signal），引導資源（Resources）流向不同行業。當某物品的需求上升，市價 \(P\) 會上升，導致潛在利潤增加，從而吸引生產者將資源（如勞工、土地、資本）轉移到該物品的生產上。

Refers to how price acts as a signal to guide resources across different industries. When demand for a good increases, its market price \(P\) rises, leading to higher potential profits, which attracts producers to reallocate resources (like labor, land, capital) into producing that good.

The Profit Motive Mechanism (利潤動機機制): $$\text{Profit } (\pi) = \text{Total Revenue } (TR) - \text{Total Cost } (TC)$$ When Demand \(D \uparrow \implies\) Price \(P \uparrow \implies\) \(TR \uparrow \implies\) \(\pi \uparrow\).
Higher \(\pi\) signals producers to allocate more resources here.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer (正確答案): Option D

Option A (Incorrect / 錯誤):
題目重點在於「開設汽車製造工廠」（涉及生產與資源轉移），而非討論生產出來的汽車最終賣給誰。因此這不是配給功能。

The focus of the question is on "setting up car manufacturing factories" (which involves production and resource transfer), not on who ultimately buys the produced cars. Therefore, this is not the rationing function.

Option B (Incorrect / 錯誤):
除了功能判斷錯誤（同A）之外，德國買家的意願在題目中並無提及，屬無關資訊。

Aside from misidentifying the function (same as A), the willingness of buyers in Germany is not mentioned in the scenario and is irrelevant information.

Option C (Incorrect / 錯誤):
雖然正確指出了「分配功能」，但解釋錯誤。廠商到中國設廠，是因為中國的資源投放增加，我們無法單憑這點推斷德國的汽車供應必定下降（褔士可能只是擴充業務，而非將德國的生產線關閉）。

Although it correctly identifies the "allocative function", the explanation is wrong. The manufacturer sets up factories in China because resource allocation there is increasing; we cannot deduce that supply in Germany must fall (Volkswagen might just be expanding operations, not shutting down German lines).

Option D (Correct / 正確):
完全正確。價格上升作為信號，吸引了廠商（褔士）將資源（資本、技術）投入到中國市場的私家車生產中，這完美詮釋了價格的「分配功能」。

Perfectly correct. The higher price acts as a signal, attracting the firm (Volkswagen) to allocate resources (capital, technology) into the production of private cars in the Chinese market. This perfectly illustrates the "allocative function" of price.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (解題樞紐): 尋找題目中的「動作」。

Look for the "action" in the question.

  • 如果動作是 「開設工廠」、「增加生產」、「工人轉行」 (Setting up factories, increasing production, workers changing jobs) \(\implies\) 這是關於生產要素的移動 \(\implies\) 分配功能 (Allocative Function)。
  • 如果動作是 「價高者得」、「排隊」、「拍賣」 (Highest bidder gets it, queuing, auctioning) \(\implies\) 這是關於現成物品的分配 \(\implies\) 配給功能 (Rationing Function)。

Distractors (干擾資訊): 題目特意提及「一家德國汽車製造商」，這容易讓考生誤以為要去分析德國的市場狀況（如選項B和C），但實際上這只是背景資料，核心焦點應放在「資源流入中國生產汽車」。

The question deliberately mentions "a German car manufacturer", tempting students to analyze the German market (like Options B and C). In reality, this is just background info; the core focus should be on "resources flowing into China to produce cars".

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

Trap: The "Consumption vs. Production" Blur (消費與生產的混淆)

許多同學看到「需求上升」，就立刻聯想到消費者買東西，從而錯選「配給功能」。記住，需求上升的確是消費者的行為，但題目的結果是「吸引廠商設廠」。我們看價格功能，必須看最終受到影響的主體是誰：
影響消費者 \(\rightarrow\) 配給功能
影響生產者/資源 \(\rightarrow\) 分配功能

Many students see "rise in demand" and immediately think of consumers buying things, mistakenly choosing "Rationing". Remember, while rising demand is a consumer action, the result in the scenario is "attracting a manufacturer to set up a factory". To determine the price function, we must look at who is ultimately affected:
Affects Consumers \(\rightarrow\) Rationing Function
Affects Producers/Resources \(\rightarrow\) Allocative Function

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 老師，如果題目改成「由於晶片短缺，私家車價格大漲，經銷商決定只將現車賣給願意支付額外溢價的VIP客戶」，這會是甚麼功能？
(Teacher, if the question changed to "Due to chip shortages, car prices soar, and dealers decide to only sell existing stock to VIP customers willing to pay a premium," what function would this be?)

A: 這就是配給功能 (Rationing Function)！因為這裡討論的是如何把「現有已經造好的汽車」（現車）分派給買家（價高者得），而不涉及開設新工廠或投入新資源去生產。
(That would be the Rationing Function! Because it discusses how to distribute "existing, already manufactured cars" to buyers (highest bidder gets it), without involving setting up new factories or injecting new resources into production.)

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

The "Keyword Elimination" Method (關鍵字剔除法):

在DSE MC卷中，你可以使用「秒殺」技巧：
1. 掃描題目關鍵字：「開設工廠 (set up factories)」 = 資源投放 (resources allocated)。
2. 立即將腦海中的指標撥向「分配功能 (Allocative)」。
3. 直接刪去選項 A 和 B。
4. 比較 C 和 D：題目主體是中國市場，C 卻在講德國供應下降（無中生有），D 直接對應「中國資源投放」，秒選 D！

In the DSE MCQ paper, you can use the "instant kill" technique:
1. Scan for keywords: "set up factories" = resources allocated.
2. Immediately shift your mindset to "Allocative function".
3. Directly eliminate Options A and B.
4. Compare C and D: The subject of the prompt is the Chinese market, while C talks about falling supply in Germany (baseless assumption). D directly corresponds to "resource allocation in China". Instantly pick D!
