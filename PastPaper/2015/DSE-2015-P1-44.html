Q: Why does "Increase in reserves" mean a surplus? Isn't it recorded as a negative number in accounting?
A: Great question! In BoP Accounting, an increase in reserves is indeed recorded as a "Debit / Negative" to ensure the account sums to zero.
However, when we talk about a BoP Surplus, we refer to the sum of all items excluding the Official Reserves Account. If this sum is positive (money flowing in), the Central Bank buys foreign currency, increasing reserves. Thus, economically, "Increase in Reserves = Surplus".
