HKDSE Economics Tutor Guide
經濟學導師解析指南

Topic: Market & Price Mechanism 市場與價格機制 Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Keywords: Shortage, Non-price Competition, Black Market

1. The Question (題目)

[繁體中文]
很多人連日排隊買票觀看著名藝人子華的棟篤笑表演。有些人投訴即使他們願意支付主辦機構所訂的門票價格，他們仍買不到門票。上述情況意味著：

  • A. 價格是分配門票的唯一準則。
  • B. 生產商沒有爭取最高利潤。
  • C. 有可能出現黑市門票。
  • D. 門票市場存在有效的價格上限。

[English]
Many people queued up for days for the tickets to watch the show of Tsz-Wah, a famous artist performing stand-up comedy. Some people complained that they were unable to buy tickets even though they were willing to pay the ticket price set by the organizer. The above situation implies that:

  • A. price was the only criterion for allocating the tickets.
  • B. the producer was not maximizing its profit.
  • C. a black market for the tickets might emerge.
  • D. there was an effective price ceiling in the ticket market.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

Shortage (短缺)

當物品的價格低於均衡價格（Equilibrium Price）時，需求量（Quantity Demanded）會大於供應量（Quantity Supplied），導致短缺。

When the price of a good is below the equilibrium price, the quantity demanded exceeds the quantity supplied, resulting in a shortage.

$$ \text{Shortage} = Q_d - Q_s \quad \text{where} \quad Q_d > Q_s $$

Non-price Competition (非價格競爭)

在短缺的情況下，單靠價格無法分配所有物品，消費者必須透過其他方式（如排隊耗費時間、武力、關係等）來競爭物品。

In the presence of a shortage, price alone cannot allocate all goods. Consumers must compete through other means (e.g., queuing/spending time, physical force, relationships).

Black Market (黑市)

當部分消費者願意支付的最高價格（Marginal Benefit）高於官方/定價時，便存在圖利空間，黃牛黨（炒家）會以高價轉售圖利。

When the maximum price some consumers are willing to pay (Marginal Benefit) is higher than the official/set price, there is room for profit. Scalpers will resell the goods at a higher price.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer: Option C (正確答案：C)

Option A: Incorrect (錯誤)

題目提到人們「連日排隊」，這意味著除了金錢（價格）之外，消費者還需要付出「時間」來獲取門票。因此，價格不是唯一的分配準則，排隊（時間）也是準則之一。

The question mentions people "queued up for days". This implies that besides money (price), consumers also had to spend "time" to acquire tickets. Therefore, price was not the only criterion; queuing (time) was also a criterion.

Option B: Incorrect (錯誤)

雖然主辦方定價較低導致短缺，但我們不能單憑這一點斷定他們沒有爭取最高利潤。主辦方可能為了建立長遠的良好商譽（Goodwill）、避免公關災難，或者帶動周邊商品銷售而刻意定低價，這在長遠而言仍可能是利潤極大化的策略。

Although the organizer set a lower price causing a shortage, we cannot conclude they were not maximizing profit based solely on this. The organizer might intentionally set a lower price to build long-term goodwill, avoid PR disasters, or boost merchandise sales, which could still align with long-term profit maximization.

Option C: Correct (正確)

由於出現短缺（有人願意付原價但買不到），這代表部分向隅的消費者對門票的邊際利益（Marginal Benefit）高於主辦方的定價。這種差價創造了獲利機會，誘使「黃牛黨」排隊買票並以更高價格轉售，從而衍生黑市。

Because there is a shortage (people willing to pay the set price cannot get tickets), it means the marginal benefit of some unsatisfied consumers is higher than the set price. This price difference creates a profit opportunity, inducing scalpers to queue up and resell tickets at a higher price, leading to the emergence of a black market.

Option D: Incorrect (錯誤)

這是最常見的陷阱！「價格上限」（Price Ceiling）必須是由政府透過法例實施的市場干預。題目清楚指出價格是由「主辦機構」（私人企業）所訂，因此這只是企業的自願定價策略，而非價格上限。

This is the most common trap! A "Price Ceiling" MUST be a market intervention imposed by the government through legislation. The question clearly states the price was set by the "organizer" (a private firm). Thus, it is a voluntary pricing strategy, not a price ceiling.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答這類題目的關鍵在於找出題目的「關鍵字（Pivot Points）」：

  • 「連日排隊 (Queued up for days)」 \(\rightarrow\) 證明出現了非價格競爭（Non-price competition），直接排除 Option A。
  • 「願意支付...仍買不到 (Willing to pay... unable to buy)」 \(\rightarrow\) 證明市場存在短缺（Shortage），需求量 \(Q_d\) 大於供應量 \(Q_s\)。這為黑市（Option C）提供了誘因。
  • 「主辦機構所訂 (Set by the organizer)」 \(\rightarrow\) 證明這是私人商業決定，沒有政府干預，直接排除 Option D。

The key to solving this type of question is identifying the Pivot Points: "Queued up" shows non-price competition (eliminates A). "Willing to pay but unable to buy" shows a shortage, providing incentives for a black market (supports C). "Set by the organizer" means it's a private decision, not government intervention (eliminates D).

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱：混淆「人為低價」與「價格上限」

很多學生看到「價格低於均衡點導致短缺」，就立刻選擇「價格上限 (Price Ceiling)」。請記住，在經濟學定義中，價格上限/下限必須是由政府強制實施的。私人企業（如演唱會主辦方、蘋果公司賣iPhone）將價格定得低於均衡價格，純粹是商業決定。

Trap: Confusing "Artificially low price" with "Price Ceiling"
Many students see "price below equilibrium causing shortage" and immediately choose "Price Ceiling". Remember, in Economics, price ceilings/floors MUST be enforced by the government. A private firm setting a price below equilibrium is purely a business decision.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 既然主辦方可以定高一點的價格來賺更多錢，為什麼他們不這樣做？這不就代表他們沒有爭取最高利潤（Option B）嗎？

A1: 不一定。經濟學中的「利潤極大化」可以看長遠的。如果主辦方把票價定得極高（例如 $10,000 一張），雖然短期可能賺盡，但會被大眾批評為「吸血鬼」，損害藝人形象（Goodwill）。定價較低能製造「一票難求」的熱潮，有助於未來的宣傳和周邊產品銷售。因此，我們不能單憑「有短缺」就斷定他們放棄了利潤極大化。

Q1: If the organizer could set a higher price to make more money, why didn't they? Doesn't this mean they aren't maximizing profit (Option B)?
A1: Not necessarily. "Profit maximization" in economics can be long-term. Setting astronomically high prices might maximize short-term revenue but destroy the artist's goodwill and brand image. A lower price creates "hype" and can boost future sales and merchandise. Thus, a shortage alone doesn't prove they abandoned profit maximization.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

秒殺技巧 (Shortcut)：尋找「政府」字眼

在 HKDSE 經濟科中，只要選項出現「價格上限 (Price Ceiling)」、「價格下限 (Price Floor)」、「配額 (Quota)」或「稅/津貼 (Tax/Subsidy)」，請立刻回頭看題目：有沒有提到政府 (Government)？如果沒有，這些選項 99% 是錯的（陷阱選項）。

Shortcut: Look for the word "Government"
In HKDSE Economics, whenever you see options like Price Ceiling, Price Floor, Quota, Tax, or Subsidy, immediately check the question: Is the government mentioned? If not, these options are 99% wrong (they are traps).
