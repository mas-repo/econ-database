Topic (課題): Basic Economic Concepts - Positive & Normative Statements
基本經濟學概念 - 實證性與規範性陳述

Difficulty (難度): ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)

Key Concepts (關鍵概念): Positive Statement (實證性陳述), Normative Statement (規範性陳述), Value Judgment (價值判斷)

1. The Question (題目)

下列哪些是實證性陳述？

(1) 從內地輸入勞工會增加本港的職位空缺。
(2) 從廣東省輸入勞工會較佳，因為他們能操粵語。
(3) 來自中國的勞工的生產力比來自其他東南亞國家的勞工高。
(4) 從內地輸入30 000名勞工會令本港的失業率上升1%。

A. 只有(1)、(2)
B. 只有(3)、(4)
C. 只有(1)、(3)、(4)
D. 只有(2)、(3)、(4)

Which of the following are positive statements?

(1) Importation of labour from the mainland would result in a rise in job vacancies in Hong Kong.
(2) It would be better to import labour from the Guangdong province because they can speak Cantonese.
(3) Productivity of labour from China is higher than that from other Southeast Asian countries.
(4) Unemployment rate in Hong Kong would rise by 1% as a result of labour importation of 30 000 from the mainland.

A. (1) and (2) only
B. (3) and (4) only
C. (1), (3) and (4) only
D. (2), (3) and (4) only

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

實證性陳述 (Positive Statement)： 客觀、不涉及價值判斷的陳述。它主要解釋「是什麼」(what is)、「曾經是什麼」(what was) 或「將會是什麼」(what will be)。最核心的特徵是它可以被事實或數據驗證其真偽（Testable / Refutable）。請注意，即使一個陳述在現實中是完全錯誤的，只要它可以被事實推翻，它仍然是一個實證性陳述。 Positive Statement: An objective statement that does not involve value judgments. It explains "what is", "what was", or "what will be". The core characteristic is that it is testable or refutable by facts and data. Note that even if a statement is factually incorrect in reality, as long as it can be refuted by facts, it remains a positive statement.
規範性陳述 (Normative Statement)： 主觀、涉及價值判斷（Value Judgment）的陳述。它主要探討「應該如何」(what ought to be)。由於涉及個人觀點、道德或偏好，我們無法透過客觀數據來證明其對或錯。常見的關鍵字眼包括「應該 (should)」、「較好 (better)」、「太高 (too high)」、「不公平 (unfair)」等。 Normative Statement: A subjective statement involving value judgments. It discusses "what ought to be". Because it involves personal opinions, morals, or preferences, it cannot be proved right or wrong using objective data. Common keywords include "should", "better", "too high", "unfair", etc.
相關經濟指標定義 (Related Economic Indicators)： 題目中提到了生產力 (Productivity) 和失業率 (Unemployment Rate)。這些都是客觀的宏觀經濟指標，可以用數學公式計算出來。例如生產力，假設產出量為 \(Y\)，投入勞工時數為 \(L\)，其公式為： The question mentions Productivity and the Unemployment Rate. These are objective macroeconomic indicators that can be calculated using mathematical formulas. For example, for productivity, assuming output is \(Y\) and labor hours input is \(L\), the formula is:

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): C

分析陳述 (1)：「從內地輸入勞工會增加本港的職位空缺。」
這是一個實證性陳述。雖然根據經濟學邏輯，輸入勞工通常會填補空缺，令空缺數量「減少」而非「增加」，但這個陳述是可以透過收集就業數據來驗證其對錯的（即具備可推翻性 Refutability）。因此，它屬於實證性陳述。 Analysis of Statement (1): "Importation of labour... would result in a rise in job vacancies..."
This is a positive statement. Although according to economic logic, importing labor usually fills vacancies and "decreases" rather than "increases" them, this statement can be verified to be true or false by collecting employment data (i.e., it possesses refutability). Therefore, it is a positive statement.
分析陳述 (2)：「從廣東省輸入勞工會較佳，因為他們能操粵語。」
這是一個規範性陳述。句子中包含了「較佳 (better)」一詞，這屬於個人價值判斷。有些人可能認為語言溝通最重要，有些人可能認為技術水平更重要。我們無法用客觀數據去證明什麼才叫做「絕對的較佳」。 Analysis of Statement (2): "It would be better to import labour... because they can speak Cantonese."
This is a normative statement. The sentence contains the word "better", which involves a value judgment. Some may think language communication is the most important, while others may prioritize technical skills. We cannot use objective data to prove what constitutes "absolute better".
分析陳述 (3)：「來自中國的勞工的生產力比來自其他東南亞國家的勞工高。」
這是一個實證性陳述。生產力是一個具體的經濟概念，可以透過比較兩地勞工的平均產出（例如每小時產值）來分出高低。只要能用數據比較，就是實證性。 Analysis of Statement (3): "Productivity of labour from China is higher than that from other Southeast Asian countries."
This is a positive statement. Productivity is a specific economic concept that can be ranked by comparing the average output of labor from both regions (e.g., output value per hour). As long as it can be compared using data, it is positive.
分析陳述 (4)：「從內地輸入30 000名勞工會令本港的失業率上升1%。」
這是一個實證性陳述。句子作出了一個精確的預測（失業率上升1%）。政府統計處可以透過數據來證明這個預測最終是成真還是落空。因此，它可以被驗證。 Analysis of Statement (4): "Unemployment rate in Hong Kong would rise by 1% as a result of labour importation..."
This is a positive statement. The sentence makes a precise prediction (unemployment rate rises by 1%). The Census and Statistics Department can use data to prove whether this prediction eventually comes true or fails. Therefore, it is testable.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

面對這類題目，我們的核心判定點 (Pivot Point) 只有一個：「這句話能用客觀數據證明對或錯嗎？」。
  • 題目中的干擾資訊（Distractors）：陳述 (1) 故意寫出一個「違反常理」的經濟現象（輸入勞工反而增加空缺）。這是考評局最愛的陷阱，目的在於測試學生是否混淆了「錯的陳述」與「規範性陳述」。
  • 破題關鍵：找出所有包含價值判斷的主觀形容詞（如陳述2中的「較佳」）。只要排除這些，剩下的預測、因果關係描述，無論看起來多麼荒謬，都是實證性陳述。
When facing this type of question, our Pivot Point is singular: "Can this statement be proven true or false using objective data?".
  • Distractors in the question: Statement (1) deliberately describes a "counter-intuitive" economic phenomenon (importing labor increasing vacancies). This is a favorite trap of the HKEAA, aiming to test if students confuse "false statements" with "normative statements".
  • Key to solving: Identify all subjective adjectives that imply value judgments (like "better" in statement 2). Once these are eliminated, the remaining predictions and cause-and-effect descriptions, no matter how absurd they seem, are positive statements.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

致命誤解：實證性陳述 = 正確的陳述
很多同學會誤以為「客觀」就代表「正確」。在看陳述 (1) 時，同學心想：「輸入勞工怎麼可能增加空缺？這句話是錯的！所以它不是實證性陳述。」這是一個極嚴重的觀念錯誤。

正確觀念：實證性陳述的核心是「可被推翻」。正因為你能用邏輯和數據指出陳述 (1) 是錯的，這反而完美證明了它具備「可驗證性」，因此它絕對是實證性陳述。

Fatal Misconception: Positive Statement = True Statement
Many students mistakenly believe that "objective" means "correct". When reading Statement (1), a student might think: "How could importing labor increase vacancies? This statement is wrong! Therefore, it is not a positive statement." This is a very serious conceptual error.

Correct Concept: The core of a positive statement is "refutability". The very fact that you can use logic and data to point out that Statement (1) is wrong perfectly proves that it possesses "testability", and therefore it is absolutely a positive statement.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 陳述 (4) 預測失業率會上升1%。如果最後政府公佈的數據顯示失業率只上升了 0.5%，那陳述 (4) 還算不算是實證性陳述？
A: 算！這正是經濟學上實證性陳述的特點。它作出了一個假說 (Hypothesis)。當實際數據（0.5%）與預測（1%）不符時，我們就成功「推翻」了這個陳述。能被推翻的，就是實證性陳述。

Q: Statement (4) predicts the unemployment rate will rise by 1%. If the government eventually announces data showing the rate only rose by 0.5%, does Statement (4) still count as a positive statement?
A: Yes! This is exactly the characteristic of a positive statement in economics. It makes a hypothesis. When the actual data (0.5%) does not match the prediction (1%), we have successfully "refuted" the statement. Anything that can be refuted is a positive statement.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

消去法 (Elimination Method)：尋找「主觀炸彈字眼」
在DSE多項選擇題中，時間就是分數。做這類題目時，你不需要逐句深入分析。你的大腦應該啟動「關鍵字掃描模式」。
  1. 掃描所有句子，尋找主觀字眼：應該 (should/ought to)、更好/較佳 (better)、太 (too...)、必須 (must be)、公平 (fair)。
  2. 你立刻在陳述 (2) 看到「較佳 (better)」。判斷 (2) 為規範性 (Normative)。
  3. 看選項：A 和 D 都包含 (2)，立即劃掉。
  4. 現在只剩下 B 和 C。兩者的差異在於有沒有 (1)。
  5. 看陳述 (1)：這是一個客觀因果關係的句子，沒有主觀字眼，是實證性 (Positive)。
  6. 秒殺答案：C。
Elimination Method: Hunt for "Subjective Bomb Words"
In DSE MC questions, time is marks. When doing this type of question, you don't need to deeply analyze every sentence. Your brain should activate "keyword scanning mode".
  1. Scan all sentences for subjective words: should/ought to, better, too..., must be, fair.
  2. You immediately spot "better" in statement (2). Classify (2) as Normative.
  3. Look at the options: Both A and D contain (2). Cross them out immediately.
  4. Now only B and C remain. The difference lies in whether (1) is included.
  5. Look at statement (1): It is a sentence describing an objective cause-and-effect relationship without subjective words. It is Positive.
  6. Instantly determine the answer: C.
