2. The Question (題目)

[繁體中文]
若名義利率是5%，而預期通脹率是3% ，則持有貨幣的成本是_____________，持有貨幣的名義回報是_____________，而預期實質利率是_______。

A. 5% …… -3% …… 2%
B. 5% …… 0% …… 2%
C. 8% …… -3% …… 3%
D. 8% …… 0% …… 3%
[English]
If the nominal interest rate is 5% and the expected inflation rate is 3%, the cost of holding money is __________, the nominal return of holding money is __________ and the expected real interest rate is _________.

A. 5% …… -3% …… 2%
B. 5% …… 0% …… 2%
C. 8% …… -3% …… 3%
D. 8% …… 0% …… 3%

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

1. 費沙方程式 (The Fisher Equation)
實質利率 \(r\) 是名義利率 \(R\) 減去通脹率 \(\pi\)。如果我們看的是「預期」數字，公式如下：
The real interest rate \(r\) is the nominal interest rate \(R\) minus the inflation rate \(\pi\). When dealing with expectations, the formula is:
$$ r^e = R - \pi^e $$
2. 持有貨幣的成本 (Cost of Holding Money)
這是一個機會成本的概念。如果你把現金拿在手裡，你就放棄了把它存入銀行賺取利息的機會。因此，持有貨幣的成本等於名義利率 (Nominal Interest Rate)。
This is a concept of opportunity cost. If you hold cash, you give up the opportunity to deposit it in a bank and earn interest. Therefore, the cost of holding money is equal to the nominal interest rate.

3. 持有貨幣的名義回報 (Nominal Return of Holding Money)
現金本身不會產生任何利息。100元紙幣放在抽屜裡一年後仍然是面值100元。因此，它的「名義」回報永遠是 0%。
Cash itself does not generate any interest. A $100 banknote kept in a drawer for a year remains $100 in face value. Thus, its "nominal" return is always 0%.

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 Correct Option: B

分析 A (錯誤): 此選項錯誤地將持有貨幣的「名義回報」標示為 -3%。-3% 其實是持有貨幣的「預期實質回報」 \((0\% - 3\%)\)，因為通脹蠶食了購買力，但題目問的是「名義」回報。
Analysis A (Incorrect): This option incorrectly lists the "nominal return" of holding money as -3%. -3% is actually the "expected real return" of holding money \((0\% - 3\%)\) because inflation erodes purchasing power. However, the question specifically asks for the "nominal" return.
分析 B (正確):
1. 持有貨幣的成本 = 名義利率 \(R\) = 5%。
2. 持有貨幣的名義回報 = 永遠是 0% (因為現金不派息)。
3. 預期實質利率 = \(R - \pi^e = 5\% - 3\% =\) 2%。
Analysis B (Correct):
1. Cost of holding money = Nominal interest rate \(R\) = 5%.
2. Nominal return of holding money = Always 0% (cash pays no interest).
3. Expected real interest rate = \(R - \pi^e = 5\% - 3\% =\) 2%.
分析 C & D (錯誤): 這兩個選項錯誤地把名義利率和通脹率相加 \((5\% + 3\% = 8\%)\) 作為持有貨幣的成本，完全誤解了機會成本的概念及費沙方程式。
Analysis C & D (Incorrect): These options incorrectly add the nominal interest rate and inflation rate together \((5\% + 3\% = 8\%)\) to find the cost of holding money, demonstrating a fundamental misunderstanding of opportunity cost and the Fisher Equation.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這條題目測試你對三大相關但極易混淆的利率概念的清晰度。
  • 拆解點 1 (Pivot Point): 看到「持有貨幣的名義回報 (nominal return of holding money)」，立刻聯想答案必定是 0%。這一步已經可以直接排除選項 A 和 C。
  • 拆解點 2: 應用費沙方程式 \((r^e = R - \pi^e)\)。題目給定 \(R = 5\%\), \(\pi^e = 3\%\)，所以實質利率是 2%。這一步排除了 C 和 D。綜合兩點，答案呼之欲出。
This question tests your clarity on three related but easily confused interest rate concepts.
  • Pivot Point 1: When you see "nominal return of holding money", you should immediately know the answer is strictly 0%. This step alone eliminates options A and C.
  • Pivot Point 2: Apply the Fisher Equation \((r^e = R - \pi^e)\). Given \(R = 5\%\) and \(\pi^e = 3\%\), the real interest rate is 2%. This step eliminates C and D. Combining both points, the answer is obvious.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

⚠️ 死亡陷阱：混淆「名義」與「實質」回報
很多學生會選擇 A。他們心想：「通脹是3%，現金會貶值，所以回報是 -3%。」
糾正： 貶值的是「購買力」（實質價值），而不是鈔票上的數字（名義價值）。只要題目問「名義 (Nominal)」，現金的回報永遠是 0%。如果題目問「實質 (Real)」，答案才是 -3%。
⚠️ Death Trap: Confusing "Nominal" vs "Real" Return
Many students choose A. They think: "Inflation is 3%, cash loses value, so the return is -3%."
Correction: What depreciates is the "purchasing power" (real value), not the number printed on the banknote (nominal value). Whenever the question asks for "Nominal", the return on cash is always 0%. If it asked for "Real" return, then the answer would be -3%.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼持有貨幣的成本不是預期實質利率 (2%) 而是名義利率 (5%)？
A: 因為機會成本是指「放棄了的最高價值選擇」。如果你把錢存入銀行，銀行會支付你 5% 的「名義利息」。你持有現金，就是放棄了這 5% 的利息收入（即使這 5% 裡有 3% 會被通脹抵銷，但銀行派給你的數字確實是 5%）。因此，代價（成本）是名義利率。
Q: Why is the cost of holding money the nominal interest rate (5%) and not the real interest rate (2%)?
A: Because opportunity cost is the "highest-valued option forgone." If you deposit money in the bank, the bank pays you 5% in "nominal interest." By holding cash, you are giving up this exact 5% interest income (even if 3% of it gets offset by inflation, the number the bank pays you is exactly 5%). Thus, the sacrifice (cost) is the nominal rate.

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

⚡ 速解法 (10秒法則):
DSE 經濟科 MC 卷講求速度。遇到這種「三連空」的題目，先找最容易確定的一項。
最易確定的永遠是：「現金的名義回報 = 0%」。
一看中間的空格，只有 B 和 D 是 0%。
再看最後一格「實質利率」：用常識判斷 \(5\% - 3\% = 2\%\)。
B 選項符合 2%。不用看第一格已經可以圈出 B！
⚡ Shortcut (The 10-Second Rule):
DSE Economics MC papers demand speed. For these "triple-blank" questions, always target the easiest blank to confirm first.
The easiest is always: "Nominal return of cash = 0%".
Looking at the middle blank, only B and D have 0%.
Next, look at the last blank "real interest rate": Simple math gives \(5\% - 3\% = 2\%\).
Option B matches 2%. You can circle B without even checking the first blank!
