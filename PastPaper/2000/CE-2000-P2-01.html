HKDSE Economics Analysis

Topic: Basic Economic Concepts (基本經濟概念) Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ Keywords: Scarcity, Free Goods, Opportunity Cost
在下列哪一種情況中，物品X屬於「免費物品」? Good X is a ‘free good’ when
  • A. 使用它沒有任何機會成本。 / there is no opportunity cost involved in using it.
  • B. 它的供應量超過消費者願意在市場上購買的數量。 / its quantity supplied is greater than what consumers are willing to purchase in the market.
  • C. 沒有人想獲得更多數量的X。 / nobody wants more of it.
  • D. 它的供應十分充裕，因此消費者認為它沒有價值。 / its supply is so abundant that consumers place no value on it.
Concept Review (概念重溫)

稀少性 (Scarcity)： 當人類的慾望是無限的，而資源是有限的。 Scarcity: Exists when human wants are unlimited, but resources are limited.

經濟物品 (Economic Good)： 供應量不足以滿足所有人的慾望。即使價格為零，需求量仍大於供應量。 Economic Good: The quantity supplied is insufficient to satisfy all human wants. Even at zero price, Quantity Demanded > Quantity Supplied.
$$ \text{At } P=0, \quad Q_d > Q_s $$

免費物品 (Free Good)： 供應量足以滿足所有人的慾望。在價格為零時，供應量大於或等於需求量。 Free Good: The quantity supplied is sufficient to satisfy all human wants. At zero price, Quantity Supplied is greater than or equal to Quantity Demanded.
$$ \text{At } P=0, \quad Q_s \ge Q_d $$

Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)
Correct Option: C
Option A (Wrong): 使用它沒有任何機會成本。
這是一個常見的陷阱。「使用」物品通常涉及時間成本。即使陽光是免費物品，但你「曬太陽」這個行為需要花費時間，而時間是稀缺的（有機會成本）。因此，使用免費物品仍可能涉及機會成本。 There is no opportunity cost involved in using it.
This is a common trap. "Using" a good usually involves time cost. Even if sunlight is a free good, the act of "sunbathing" takes time, and time is scarce (has an opportunity cost). Therefore, using a free good may still involve opportunity cost.
Option B (Wrong): 它的供應量超過消費者願意在市場上購買的數量。
這描述的是市場出現盈餘 (Surplus) 的情況，通常發生在價格高於均衡價格時。這並不代表該物品本質上是免費物品。經濟物品在價格過高時也會出現供過於求。 Its quantity supplied is greater than what consumers are willing to purchase in the market.
This describes a market surplus, which usually happens when the price is set above the equilibrium level. This does not mean the good is inherently a free good. Even economic goods can have excess supply if the price is too high.
Option C (Correct): 沒有人想獲得更多數量的X。
這是免費物品的定義核心。如果「沒有人想獲得更多」，意味著所有人的慾望都已經被滿足了（飽和）。既然慾望已滿足，就沒有稀少性問題，因此它是免費物品。 Nobody wants more of it.
This is the core definition of a free good. If "nobody wants more," it means everyone's wants are fully satisfied (satiated). Since wants are satisfied, there is no scarcity, making it a free good.
Option D (Wrong): 它的供應十分充裕，因此消費者認為它沒有價值。
免費物品（如空氣）通常有極高的使用價值 (Use Value)，因為它對生存至關重要。它們只是沒有交換價值 (Exchange Value)（即市價為零）。說它「沒有價值」是錯誤的。 Its supply is so abundant that consumers place no value on it.
Free goods (like air) usually have very high use value because they are essential for survival. They simply have no exchange value (price is zero). Saying they have "no value" is incorrect.
Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

關鍵判斷點 (Pivot Point)： 區分「價格 (Price)」與「稀少性 (Scarcity)」。 Pivot Point: Distinguish between "Price" and "Scarcity".

這類題目在測試你是否明白「免費物品」的定義是基於供應與慾望的關係，而不僅僅是價格。 This question tests if you understand that a "free good" is defined by the relationship between supply and wants, not just the price.

  • 如果 \( \text{Wants} > \text{Supply} \) \(\rightarrow\) 稀少 \(\rightarrow\) 經濟物品。
  • 如果 \( \text{Supply} \ge \text{Wants} \) \(\rightarrow\) 不稀少 \(\rightarrow\) 免費物品 (即選項 C)。
Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

Trap 1: Confusing "Free of Charge" with "Free Good"
誤區：以為「不用錢」就是「免費物品」。
Example: A free sample at a supermarket. It is free of charge ($P=0$), but it is an Economic Good because it took resources to produce, and if everyone wanted one, there wouldn't be enough.

Trap 2: Ignoring Time Cost
誤區：忽略時間成本。
Students often choose Option A. Remember: "Using" something takes time. Time is limited. Therefore, using a free good has an opportunity cost (the value of the time spent).

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)
Q: 為什麼空氣是免費物品，但潛水氧氣樽裡的空氣是經濟物品？
A: 一般環境下的空氣極其充裕，滿足所有人慾望（\(Q_s > Q_d\)），所以是免費物品。但潛水氧氣樽裡的空氣經過壓縮、淨化和運輸，涉及了資源投入，且在深海中是稀缺的（你想要更多，但樽裡有限），所以變成了經濟物品。
A: Air in the general environment is abundant enough to satisfy everyone's wants (\(Q_s > Q_d\)), making it a free good. However, air in a scuba tank has been compressed, purified, and transported. This involves resources, and underwater, it is scarce (you want more, but the tank is limited), making it an economic good.
Q: 選項 B 說「供應量超過購買量」，這不就是供過於求嗎？為什麼不是免費物品？
A: 重點在於「在市場上購買」。如果價格定得很高（例如 $1000 一個麵包），供應量肯定超過購買量（會有賣剩的麵包），但这只是市場盈餘。只要價格降回 $0 時，大家想要的數量多於現有供應，它依然是經濟物品。免費物品必須是即使價格為 $0，供應量依然足夠。
A: The key is "purchase in the market". If the price is set very high (e.g., $1000 for a bread), quantity supplied will exceed quantity demanded (surplus). But this is just a market surplus. As long as people want more than what is available when the price drops to $0, it remains an economic good. A free good must have sufficient supply even at $0 price.
