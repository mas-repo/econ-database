HKDSE Economics Analysis Guide
經濟學試題分析指南

Topic (課題): Demand and Supply - Factors affecting Demand (需求與供應 - 影響需求的因素)

Difficulty (難度): ★★★☆☆ (3/5)

Key Concepts (核心概念): Expected Future Price (預期未來價格), Change in Demand (需求改變), First Registration Tax (首次登記稅)

1. The Question (題目)

下表顯示本港新註冊Tesla電動車的數量。

The following table shows the number of new Tesla electric vehicle registered in Hong Kong.

月份 (Month) 本港新註冊Tesla電動車的數量
(Number of new Tesla EV registered in HK)
2016年12月 (Dec 2016)168
2017年1月 (Jan 2017)168
2017年2月 (Feb 2017)590
2017年3月 (Mar 2017)2,939

政府在2017年二月中公布電動車首次登記稅的全額減免會在2017年3月31日完結。
下列哪項最能解釋上述資料？

The Government announced in mid-February 2017 that the full exemption from first registration tax on electric vehicles would end on March 31, 2017.
Which of the following best explains the above data?

  • A. 電動車的供應增加了，因為新技術減低電動車用電池的生產成本。
    The supply of electric vehicles increased because new technology reduced the production cost of battery used in electric vehicles.

  • B. 電動車的供應增加了，因為政府豁免首次登記稅。
    The supply of electric vehicles increased because of the exemption of first registration tax by the Government.

  • C. 電動車的需求增加了，因為政府在本港興建更多充電站。
    The demand for electric vehicles increased because the Government built more charging stations in the city.

  • D. 電動車的需求增加了，因為買家預期購買電動車的成本未來會上升。
    The demand for electric vehicles increased because the buyers expected the cost of buying electric vehicles to go up in the future.

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

Expected Future Price (預期未來價格)

當消費者預期某物品未來的價格將會上升時，他們會傾向「提早購買」以節省金錢。這會導致該物品在現在（Current period）的需求增加，需求曲線向右移。

When consumers expect the future price of a good to rise, they tend to "buy early" to save money. This leads to an increase in the current demand for the good, shifting the demand curve to the right.

$$ P_{t+1}^e \uparrow \implies D_t \uparrow $$

\( P_{t+1}^e \) = Expected Future Price (預期未來價格)
\( D_t \) = Current Demand (現時需求)

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Answer (正確答案): Option D
Option A: Incorrect (錯誤)

雖然新技術降低成本會增加供應，但這無法解釋為何銷量剛好在政府「二月中公布」後，於二月及三月出現爆發性增長。題目要求「最能解釋上述資料」的原因，必須與政府的公布有關。

Although new technology reducing costs would increase supply, it cannot explain why sales spiked explosively in February and March exactly after the Government's "mid-February announcement". The question asks what "best explains the above data", which must link to the specific announcement.

Option B: Incorrect (錯誤)

首先，首次登記稅一般向買家收取，主要影響需求而非供應。其次，政府是宣布「完結」豁免，而非「開始」豁免。如果豁免完結，未來的購買成本會上升。

First, the first registration tax is generally levied on buyers, affecting demand rather than supply. Second, the Government announced the "end" of the exemption, not the "start" of it. If the exemption ends, the future cost of buying will increase.

Option C: Incorrect (錯誤)

興建更多充電站（補充品）確實會增加電動車的需求。然而，與選項A相同，這是一般的經濟現象，完全忽略了題目中「二月中公布稅務豁免完結」這個關鍵資訊。

Building more charging stations (complements) would indeed increase the demand for EVs. However, similar to Option A, this is a general economic phenomenon that completely ignores the crucial information provided in the question: the "mid-February announcement of the tax exemption ending".

Option D: Correct (正確)

政府在二月中宣布三月底將取消免稅。這意味著在四月或之後購買電動車，買家需要繳交昂貴的首次登記稅。因此，買家預期未來的購買成本 \(C\) 會大幅上升。為了避稅，大量買家趕在三月底前買車，導致二月及三月的現時需求急增，成交量大幅上升。

The Government announced in mid-February that the tax exemption would end in late March. This means buying an EV in April or later would require paying an expensive first registration tax. Therefore, buyers expected the future cost of buying \(C\) to rise significantly. To avoid the tax, many buyers rushed to buy cars before the end of March, causing a sharp increase in current demand and transaction volume in February and March.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解答這類「資料回應題 (Data Response MC)」，關鍵在於尋找「轉捩點 (Pivot Point)」：

  • 數據特徵： 12月和1月數字平穩 (168部)，2月開始上升 (590部)，3月暴增 (2,939部)。
  • 事件時間線： 2月中公布消息 \(\rightarrow\) 3月31日死線 (Deadline)。
  • 邏輯推論： 數據的變化與時間線完美吻合。任何不能解釋這個「時間點」的選項（如A和C）都是干擾項 (Distractors)。

To solve this type of "Data Response MC", the key is to find the Pivot Point: The data was stable in Dec and Jan, started rising in Feb, and spiked in Mar. The timeline of the event (mid-Feb announcement, Mar 31 deadline) perfectly matches the data. Any option that cannot explain this specific "timing" (like A and C) is a distractor.

5. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼首次登記稅 (First Registration Tax) 是影響需求而不是供應？
Why does the First Registration Tax affect demand instead of supply?

A: 在香港，汽車的首次登記稅是由車主（買家）在運輸署登記車輛時繳交的。因為這筆稅款直接增加了買家獲得車輛的總成本 (Effective Price)，所以它改變了買家的購買意欲，即影響需求 (Demand)。

A: In Hong Kong, the first registration tax for vehicles is paid by the vehicle owner (buyer) upon registration with the Transport Department. Because this tax directly increases the total cost (effective price) for the buyer to acquire the vehicle, it alters the buyer's willingness to purchase, thus affecting Demand.

6. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

The "Match the Event" Rule (事件配對法則):

當題目提供了一段特定的背景資料（例如政府政策、天災、特定新聞），正確答案必須直接與該資料有因果關係。在考試中，你可以立即刪去所有「雖然在經濟學上正確，但與題目背景無關」的選項（秒殺選項 A 和 C）。

When a question provides specific background information (e.g., government policy, natural disaster, specific news), the correct answer MUST have a direct causal relationship with that information. In exams, you can immediately eliminate options that are "economically correct in general but irrelevant to the context" (instantly killing options A and C).
