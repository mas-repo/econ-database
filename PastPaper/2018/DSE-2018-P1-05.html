Topic: 基本經濟概念 (Basic Economic Concepts) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: 資本 (Capital), 經濟物品 (Economic Goods), 共用品 (Public Goods)

題目 (The Question)

下列哪項有關羅浮宮博物館展品的陳述是正確的？

A. 它們是共用品，因為羅浮宮博物館向公眾開放。
B. 它們是資本，因為它們是用於生產的人造資源。
C. 它們是消費物品，因為人們渴望更多的展品。
D. 它們是經濟物品，因為它們滿足人類的需要。

Which of the following statements about the exhibits in the Louvre Museum is correct?

A. They are public goods because the Louvre Museum is open to the public.
B. They are capital because they are man-made resources used in production.
C. They are consumer goods because more of them are preferred.
D. They are economic goods because they satisfy human wants.

概念重溫 (Concept Review)

1. 物品的性質 (Nature of Goods)

  • 經濟物品 (Economic Good): 數量不足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品。要獲得它，必須放棄其他物品（即機會成本大於零）。
    $$Q_d > Q_s \text{ at } P = 0$$
  • 免費物品 (Free Good): 數量足以滿足人類所有慾望的物品。獲得它不需要付出機會成本。
    $$Q_d \le Q_s \text{ at } P = 0$$

Economic Good: A good whose quantity demanded exceeds its quantity supplied at zero price (Opportunity cost > 0).
Free Good: A good whose quantity supplied is sufficient to satisfy all human wants at zero price (Opportunity cost = 0).

2. 共用品與私用品 (Public vs. Private Goods)

  • 共用品 (Public Good): 具備非競爭性 (Non-rivalrous) 及 非排他性 (Non-excludable) 的物品。
  • 私用品 (Private Good): 具備競爭性或排他性的物品。

Public Good: A good that is both non-rivalrous in consumption and non-excludable.
Private Good: A good that is either rivalrous or excludable (or both).

3. 資本與消費物品 (Capital vs. Consumer Goods)

  • 資本/生產者物品 (Capital/Producer Good): 用於協助生產其他物品或服務的人造資源。
  • 消費物品 (Consumer Good): 直接用於滿足目前慾望的物品。

Capital: Man-made resources used to produce other goods and services.
Consumer Good: Goods produced for direct, present consumption to satisfy human wants.

答案與詳細分析 (The Answer & Detailed Analysis)

正確答案是 (The correct option is): Option B

選項 A分析 (Analysis of A) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

即使羅浮宮向公眾開放，這並不代表展品是「共用品」。進入博物館通常需要購買門票，這具有排他性 (Excludable)；而且當博物館過度擁擠時，參觀者的享受會減少，這具有競爭性 (Rivalrous)。因此，這句話的因果關係是錯誤的。

Even though the Louvre is open to the public, it does not make the exhibits "public goods". Entering the museum usually requires an admission fee (meaning it is Excludable), and when the museum is crowded, the enjoyment of visitors decreases (meaning it is Rivalrous). Therefore, the causal relationship in this statement is wrong.

選項 B分析 (Analysis of B) - 正確 (Correct)

博物館展品（如畫作、雕塑）是人類創造的資源（人造資源）。博物館利用這些展品來向遊客提供展覽服務，從而賺取收入或實現其教育目的。因此，它們符合資本 (Capital) 的定義：用於生產的人造資源。

Museum exhibits (like paintings and sculptures) are resources created by humans (man-made). The museum uses these exhibits to provide exhibition services to tourists, thereby earning income or fulfilling educational purposes. Thus, they fit the exact definition of Capital: man-made resources used in production.

選項 C分析 (Analysis of C) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

「人們渴望更多」(more of them are preferred) 是指該物品帶來正邊際效用 (Positive Marginal Utility)，這只說明它是一件「物品」(a good) 而不是「壞品」(a bad)。這不能用來區分它是否為消費物品。展品的用途是生產展覽服務，所以它是資本。

"More of them are preferred" implies the item provides positive marginal utility, meaning it is a "good" rather than a "bad". This reasoning cannot be used to distinguish it as a consumer good. The actual use of the exhibits is to produce exhibition services, making them capital.

選項 D分析 (Analysis of D) - 錯誤 (Incorrect)

「滿足人類需要」(satisfy human wants) 只是「物品」(a good) 的基本特徵。免費物品（如陽光、空氣）同樣能滿足人類需要。要成為經濟物品 (Economic good)，其代價必須大於零。因此，「滿足人類需要」並不是成為經濟物品的正確原因。

"Satisfying human wants" is merely a basic characteristic of any "good". Free goods (like sunlight and air) also satisfy human wants. For something to be an Economic good, its opportunity cost must be greater than zero. Therefore, "satisfying human wants" is not the valid reason for classifying it as an economic good.

題目拆解 (Problem Decomposition)

解題關鍵 (Pivot Point)：

這類 DSE 題目通常採用「標籤 + 原因 (Label + Because)」的句式。要破解這類題目，你必須同時檢查兩部分：

  1. 前半部的「分類」是否正確？
  2. 後半部的「定義 / 原因」是否能準確解釋該經濟學名詞？

許多時候，選項的「標籤」可能是對的（例如展品確實是經濟物品），但其後跟隨的「原因」卻是錯的（選項 D 中，錯把「滿足需要」當成經濟物品的獨有定義）。這就是題目的干擾項 (Distractors)。

The approach: DSE questions of this type often use a "Label + Because" structure. To solve them, you must check two things: 1) Is the classification accurate? 2) Does the reason accurately define the economic term? Often, the label might be true (exhibits ARE economic goods), but the reason is a distractor (Option D wrongly defines economic goods as just satisfying wants). Catching the flawed reasoning is key.

常犯錯誤與陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Traps)

  • 陷阱一：將「向公眾開放」與「共用品」混淆。
    很多同學一看到「公共設施」或「公眾開放」，就直覺認為是 Public Good。記住，經濟學上的共用品有嚴格定義：必須是 $Non-rivalrous$ 及 $Non-excludable$。
  • 陷阱二：忘記「免費物品」的存在。
    看到「滿足慾望」(Satisfy human wants)，就馬上聯想到經濟物品。別忘了空氣和陽光也能滿足慾望，但它們是 Free goods！區分的重點在於有沒有「代價」(Cost)。
  • Trap 1: Confusing "open to the public" with "Public Goods". Many students assume public facilities are public goods. Remember, the strict economic definition requires the good to be non-rivalrous and non-excludable.
  • Trap 2: Forgetting about "Free Goods". Seeing "satisfy wants" makes students immediately think of economic goods. Don't forget that air and sunlight satisfy wants too, but they are free goods! The true differentiator is the presence of an opportunity cost.

學生常見疑問 (Student Q&A)

學生問 (Q)： 既然遊客去博物館是為了欣賞展品，展品難道不是直接被遊客「消費」了嗎？為什麼不能選 C (消費物品)？

導師答 (A)： 這是個很好的問題！判斷一件物品是資本還是消費物品，取決於誰在使用它以及它的用途。展品是屬於博物館的財產，博物館將這些展品組合起來，產生一種名為「展覽觀賞」的服務 (Service) 提供給遊客。因為展品被用來「生產」這項服務，所以對博物館而言，它們是資本。遊客真正消費的是「博物館的展覽服務」，而不是把蒙娜麗莎的畫作買回家掛在客廳。

Student (Q): Since tourists go to the museum to enjoy the exhibits, aren't the exhibits directly "consumed" by them? Why can't we choose C (Consumer goods)?

Tutor (A): Great question! Classifying a good as capital or consumer depends on who uses it and for what purpose. The exhibits belong to the museum. The museum uses these physical items to produce a "viewing service" for tourists. Because the exhibits are used to "produce" this service, they act as capital for the museum. Tourists are consuming the "museum's exhibition service", not the physical exhibits themselves (they don't take the Mona Lisa home to hang in their living room).

考試技巧與速解 (Exam Tricks & Shortcuts)

「因為」(Because) 排除法：

在考場上遇到這類題目，可以直接先看「因為」後面的解釋句，判斷它是不是該名詞的標準定義 (Standard Definition)：

  • A的後半：向公眾開放 $\neq$ 共用品定義 (直接排除)
  • C的後半：人們渴望更多 $\neq$ 消費物品定義 (直接排除)
  • D的後半：滿足人類需要 $\neq$ 經濟物品定義 (直接排除)
  • B的後半：用於生產的人造資源 = 資本的標準定義！(秒殺答案)

The "Because" Elimination Method: During the exam, look directly at the explanation after the word "because" and check if it is the precise textbook definition of the term:
- Latter part of A: "Open to public" is NOT the definition of public goods (Eliminate).
- Latter part of C: "More are preferred" is NOT the definition of consumer goods (Eliminate).
- Latter part of D: "Satisfy wants" is NOT the definition of economic goods (Eliminate).
- Latter part of B: "Man-made resources used in production" IS the exact definition of capital. (Instant match!)
