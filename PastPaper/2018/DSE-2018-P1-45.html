Topic: Exchange Rates (匯率) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Exchange Rate, Appreciation, Profit/Loss

1. The Question (題目)

黃先生以港幣10 000元在第一天購入人民幣，並在第二天出售該筆款項以獲取港幣，其兌換率如下表所示：

匯率：
第一天：1港元 = 0.83人民幣
第二天：1港元 = 0.77人民幣

在以上的交易，黃先生大約 ____________。

  1. 損失了港幣779元
  2. 損失了港幣600元
  3. 賺取了港幣600元
  4. 賺取了港幣779元

Mr Wong used $10 000 Hong Kong Dollars (HKD) to buy Renminbi (RMB) at Day 1 and sold the sum at Day 2 to obtain HKD at the exchange rate shown in the table below.

Exchange rates:
Day 1: 1 HKD = 0.83 RMB
Day 2: 1 HKD = 0.77 RMB

Mr Wong ____________ approximately in the above transactions.

  1. lost HK$779
  2. lost HK$600
  3. gained HK$600
  4. gained HK$779

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

匯率轉換 (Exchange Rate Conversion)

當匯率以「1 單位本國貨幣 = \(X\) 單位外國貨幣」標示時：

When the exchange rate is quoted as "1 unit of domestic currency = \(X\) units of foreign currency":
$$ \text{Foreign Currency} = \text{Domestic Currency} \times \text{Exchange Rate} $$ $$ \text{Domestic Currency} = \frac{\text{Foreign Currency}}{\text{Exchange Rate}} $$

升值與貶值 (Appreciation and Depreciation)

如果 1 港元能兌換的較少的人民幣（從 0.83 跌至 0.77），代表港元貶值，人民幣升值。持有升值的貨幣會帶來利潤。

If 1 HKD can exchange for fewer RMB (dropping from 0.83 to 0.77), it means HKD has depreciated and RMB has appreciated. Holding the appreciating currency yields a profit.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案 (Correct Option): D

Options A & B (Incorrect):

由於人民幣升值（1港元兌換更少人民幣），黃先生持有人民幣必定會獲利，因此不可能損失。

Since RMB appreciated (1 HKD exchanges for fewer RMB), Mr. Wong must have gained a profit by holding RMB. Therefore, he cannot have lost money.
Option C (Incorrect):

這個錯誤答案來自於錯誤的算式：\( 10000 \times (0.83 - 0.77) = 600 \)。這在數學邏輯上是錯誤的，因為匯率的基數是港元，不能直接相減後乘上本金。

This wrong answer comes from an incorrect calculation: \( 10000 \times (0.83 - 0.77) = 600 \). This is mathematically flawed because the base of the exchange rate is HKD, so you cannot simply subtract the rates and multiply by the principal.
Option D (Correct):

步驟 1 (Step 1): 第一天，將港幣換成人民幣。
Day 1, convert HKD to RMB.

$$ \text{RMB obtained} = 10000 \times 0.83 = 8300 \text{ RMB} $$

步驟 2 (Step 2): 第二天，將人民幣換回港幣。注意，這裡需要除以新的匯率。
Day 2, convert RMB back to HKD. Note that you need to divide by the new exchange rate.

$$ \text{HKD obtained} = \frac{8300}{0.77} \approx 10779.22 \text{ HKD} $$

步驟 3 (Step 3): 計算利潤。
Calculate the profit.

$$ \text{Profit} = 10779.22 - 10000 = 779.22 \text{ HKD} $$

因此，他大約賺取了港幣779元。

Therefore, he gained approximately HK$779.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的關鍵點 (Pivot Point)在於理解何時使用乘法，何時使用除法。

The Pivot Point of this type of question is understanding when to multiply and when to divide.
  • 當匯率標示為「1 HKD = \(X\) RMB」時，從 HKD 轉換為 RMB，我們乘 \(X\)。
  • When the rate is "1 HKD = \(X\) RMB", to convert from HKD to RMB, we multiply by \(X\).
  • 從 RMB 轉換回 HKD 時，我們必須除以 \(X\)。
  • To convert from RMB back to HKD, we must divide by \(X\).

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

學生最常犯的錯誤是在第二天將 8300 人民幣直接乘以 0.77。記住單位：\( 8300 \text{ RMB} \times 0.77 \frac{\text{RMB}}{\text{HKD}} \) 在數學上無法消去 RMB 單位！

The most common mistake students make is multiplying 8300 RMB by 0.77 on Day 2. Remember your units: \( 8300 \text{ RMB} \times 0.77 \frac{\text{RMB}}{\text{HKD}} \) does not cancel out the RMB unit mathematically!

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q: 為什麼不能直接用 \( 10000 \times (0.83 - 0.77) \) 來計算利潤？

Q: Why can't we just use \( 10000 \times (0.83 - 0.77) \) to calculate the profit?

A: 因為匯率的表達方式是「1 港元 = 多少人民幣」。當你計算 \( 0.83 - 0.77 = 0.06 \) 時，這代表「每 1 港元少換了 0.06 人民幣」。這並不能直接轉換為港元的利潤。你必須先算出實際持有的人民幣數量，再按新匯率折算回港元。

A: Because the exchange rate is expressed as "1 HKD = how many RMB". When you calculate \( 0.83 - 0.77 = 0.06 \), it means "each HKD buys 0.06 fewer RMB". This cannot be directly translated into a HKD profit. You must calculate the actual amount of RMB held, then convert it back to HKD using the new rate.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

邏輯消除法 (Logical Elimination):

在考場上，你可以先判斷是賺還是蝕。第一天 1 港元換 0.83 人民幣，第二天只能換 0.77 人民幣。這意味著人民幣變得更「貴」（升值）了。既然黃先生持有的是人民幣，他換回港幣時一定會變多，即賺錢 (Gain)。因此，你可以立刻刪去選項 A 和 B，將答對機率提升至 50%！

In the exam, you can first determine if it's a gain or loss. On Day 1, 1 HKD buys 0.83 RMB; on Day 2, it only buys 0.77 RMB. This means RMB has become more "expensive" (appreciated). Since Mr. Wong is holding RMB, he will get more HKD back, meaning a Gain. Therefore, you can immediately eliminate options A and B, increasing your chances of guessing correctly to 50%!
