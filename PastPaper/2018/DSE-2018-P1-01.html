HKDSE Economics: Question Analysis

Topic: Economic Systems (經濟制度) Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Key Concepts: Scarcity, Competition, Planned Economy

2. The Question (題目)

下列哪項有關計劃經濟的陳述是正確的？

Which of the following statements about a planned economy is correct?

A. 該經濟中共用品的數量比市場導向經濟的多。
The quantity of public goods in the economy is greater than that in a market-oriented economy.
B. 大部分生產活動是由政府官員進行。
Most production activities are carried out by government officials.
C. 所有資源由政府指揮分配。
All resources are allocated by government commands.
D. 在該經濟中，競爭仍然存在。
Competitions still exist in the economy.

3. Concept Review (概念重溫)

這條題目考驗你對「經濟制度」及「稀少性」這兩個核心概念的理解。我們需要釐清計劃經濟的特質，以及經濟學中最基礎的普遍真理。

This question tests your understanding of two core concepts: "Economic Systems" and "Scarcity". We need to clarify the characteristics of a planned economy and the most fundamental universal truth in economics.

核心定義 (Core Definitions)

  • 計劃經濟 (Planned Economy): 資源分配（生產什麼、如何生產、為誰生產）主要由政府或中央權力機構通過指令來決定。
    Resource allocation (what, how, and for whom to produce) is primarily determined by the government or central authority through commands.
  • 共用品 (Public Goods): 具有「非敵對性」(Non-rivalrous) 及「非排他性」(Non-excludable) 的物品，例如國防、街燈。這與「政府提供的物品」是兩個完全不同的概念。
    Goods that are non-rivalrous and non-excludable, such as national defense and streetlights. This is a completely different concept from "government-provided goods".

在任何社會中，只要存在稀少性（人類欲望大於可用資源），競爭就必然存在。經濟制度只是改變了競爭的形式，而不能消除競爭本身。

In any society, as long as scarcity exists (human wants exceed available resources), competition must exist. Economic systems only change the form of competition, not its existence.

$$ \text{Human Wants} > \text{Available Resources} \implies \text{Scarcity} \implies \text{Competition} $$

4. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是 / The correct option is: Option D

A. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

計劃經濟下，政府確實會提供很多物品（例如免費醫療、教育、房屋），但這些大多是「私用品」(Private goods)，只是由政府提供。計劃經濟並不保證「共用品」(Public goods) 的數量一定較多。
Under a planned economy, the government does provide many goods (e.g., free healthcare, education, housing), but these are mostly "private goods" provided by the government. A planned economy does not guarantee a greater quantity of "public goods".

B. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

政府官員負責「計劃」和「指揮」生產，但實際的「生產活動」是由工人、農民等勞動者進行的，官員通常不直接參與前線生產。
Government officials are responsible for "planning" and "commanding" production, but the actual "production activities" are carried out by workers, farmers, etc. Officials usually do not participate directly in frontline production.

C. 錯誤 (Incorrect)

即使在最極端的計劃經濟中，也無法做到「所有」(All) 資源都由政府分配。黑市交易、非正式交換或私人時間的分配（例如你決定花時間睡覺還是溫習）依然存在。
Even in the most extreme planned economies, it is impossible for "all" resources to be allocated by the government. Black markets, informal exchanges, or the allocation of private time (e.g., deciding whether to sleep or study) still exist.

D. 正確 (Correct)

只要有稀少性，就有競爭。在市場經濟中，我們主要進行「價格競爭」(Price competition)；在計劃經濟中，價格可能由政府鎖定，但人們會轉向「非價格競爭」(Non-price competition)，例如排隊、拉關係、爭取政治地位來獲取資源。
As long as there is scarcity, there is competition. In a market economy, we mainly engage in "price competition"; in a planned economy, prices may be fixed by the government, but people will turn to "non-price competition," such as queuing, pulling strings, or striving for political status to obtain resources.

5. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

解題樞紐 (The Pivot Point): 經濟學的「普世真理」。
The "universal truth" of economics.

做這類關於「經濟制度」的題目時，最重要的切入點是：制度不能改變稀少性的事實。當你看到「競爭仍然存在」、「稀少性依然存在」等選項時，通常就是答案所在。這題的干擾資訊在於試圖將「政府指揮」與「消除競爭」掛勾，這是不理性的推論。

When tackling questions about "economic systems," the most crucial angle is: systems cannot change the fact of scarcity. When you see options like "competition still exists" or "scarcity still exists," they are usually the correct answer. The distractor here attempts to link "government command" with "the elimination of competition," which is an irrational deduction.

6. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

  • 陷阱一：混淆「政府提供的物品」與「共用品」
    同學常以為政府派發的就是共用品。記住，免費公屋是「私用品」（因為有排他性，你住了別人就不能住），只是它由政府提供。
    Trap 1: Confusing "Government-provided goods" with "Public goods". Students often think anything distributed by the government is a public good. Remember, free public housing is a "private good" (it is excludable and rivalrous), it is merely provided by the government.
  • 陷阱二：忽略了「非價格競爭」
    同學以為沒有自由市場定價就沒有競爭。其實排隊拿配給票、走後門，全都是競爭的表現。
    Trap 2: Ignoring "Non-price competition". Students assume that without free-market pricing, there is no competition. In reality, queuing for ration coupons and using connections are all forms of competition.

7. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

問 (Q): 既然計劃經濟下物品是按政府指令平均分配的，為什麼還要競爭？
Since goods are distributed equally by government commands in a planned economy, why is there still a need to compete?

答 (A): 因為資源永遠不足夠滿足所有人的所有欲望（稀少性）。即使政府想「平均分配」，在資源極度匱乏時（例如只有10個蘋果卻有100人），政府也必須設定分配準則（例如按戶口、按年資或先到先得）。為了符合這些準則以獲取資源，人們就會展開競爭（例如提早去排隊）。
Because resources are never enough to satisfy everyone's wants (scarcity). Even if the government wants to distribute "equally," when resources are extremely scarce (e.g., 10 apples for 100 people), the government must set allocation criteria (e.g., by household registry, seniority, or first-come-first-served). To meet these criteria and obtain resources, people will compete (e.g., waking up early to queue).

8. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

「絕對字眼」排除法 (The "Absolute Word" Elimination) & 「普世真理」秒殺法 (The "Universal Truth" Shortcut)

在 DSE 經濟科的 MC 中，帶有「所有」(All)、「完全」(Completely) 等絕對字眼的選項（如選項 C 的「所有資源」）極大概率是錯誤的，因為現實世界總有例外。另一方面，只要選項描述的是經濟學的普世真理（如選項 D 提到競爭存在），這往往可以直接秒殺成為正確答案，無需浪費時間深究其他複雜選項。
In DSE Economics MCQs, options containing absolute words like "All" or "Completely" (like "All resources" in Option C) are highly likely to be incorrect because the real world always has exceptions. On the other hand, if an option describes a universal truth in economics (like Option D stating competition exists), you can often shortcut directly to this correct answer without wasting time overthinking the complex distractors.
