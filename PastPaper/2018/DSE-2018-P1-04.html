HKDSE Economics Mastery Guide

Topic (課題): Positive and Normative Statements (實證性與規範性陳述)

Difficulty (難度): ★☆☆☆☆ (1/5 - Fundamental)

Key Concepts (核心概念): Positive Statement (實證性) Normative Statement (規範性) Value Judgement (價值判斷)

1. The Question (題目)

下列陳述是取材自香港財政司司長的2017-18年度財政預算案演詞。
陳述(1)： 2016年環球經濟錄得自環球金融危機以來最慢的增長。
陳述(2)： 2016年全年經濟增長率為1.9%，符合去年預算案所估算的1%至2%。
陳述(3)： 在認同市場力量的同時，政府應積極發揮促進者的角色，善用公共資源，推出合宜的政策。

以上哪(些)項是規範性陳述？ (註：原題目的「規模性」為常見筆誤，正確學術名詞為「規範性」)

A. 只有 (1)
B. 只有 (3)
C. 只有 (1)、(2)
D. 只有 (2)、(3)

The following statements are adapted from the 2017-18 Budget Speech by the Financial Secretary of Hong Kong.
Statement (1): The global economy in 2016 recorded the slowest growth since the global financial crisis.
Statement (2): For 2016 as a whole, there was a growth of 1.9%, consistent with the 1% to 2% economic growth as forecast in last year’s Budget.
Statement (3): While recognising the effect of market forces, the Government should play an active role as a facilitator by taking forward appropriate policies with good use of public resources.

Which of the above is a normative statement / are normative statements?

A. (1) only
B. (3) only
C. (1) and (2) only
D. (2) and (3) only

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

實證性陳述 (Positive Statement): 處理「是什麼」(What is) 的問題。這類陳述建基於客觀事實，沒有涉及個人價值判斷。最重要的是，實證性陳述可以被事實推翻或證實。即使該陳述是錯誤的，只要它能被客觀數據驗證，它依然屬於實證性陳述。

Positive Statement: Deals with "What is" or "What will be". These statements are based on objective facts and do not involve personal value judgements. The key characteristic is that they can be refuted or verified by facts. Even if a statement is factually incorrect, it remains a positive statement as long as it can be tested against data.

規範性陳述 (Normative Statement): 處理「應該如何」(What ought to be) 的問題。這類陳述涉及個人的價值觀、道德標準或主觀偏好（例如覺得某件事好與壞、公平與不公平）。由於它是主觀的，我們無法單憑事實或數據來證明其對錯。

Normative Statement: Deals with "What ought to be". These statements involve personal values, moral standards, or subjective preferences (e.g., whether something is good/bad, fair/unfair). Because they are subjective, they cannot be proven true or false using facts or data alone.

Example of a Positive Economic Model (實證經濟模型例子):
經濟學家常使用客觀的公式來進行實證分析，例如計算本地生產總值 (GDP) 的開支計算法： $$Y = C + I + G + (X - M)$$ (Where $Y$ is GDP, $C$ is Consumption, $I$ is Investment, $G$ is Government Expenditure, $X$ is Exports, and $M$ is Imports)
這公式是客觀的定義，我們代入實際數字就能驗證經濟增長，因此屬於實證經濟學範疇。

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

Correct Option (正確答案): B

陳述 (3) 是唯一包含價值判斷的規範性陳述。選項 B 正確。

Statement (3) is the only normative statement containing value judgements. Option B is correct.

分析陳述 (1) - 實證性 (Positive)

「2016年環球經濟錄得自環球金融危機以來最慢的增長。」這是一句客觀的陳述。我們只需要翻查2016年以及過去幾年的全球經濟增長率數據，就可以驗證這句話是真還是假。因為它可以被事實驗證，所以它是實證性陳述。

"The global economy in 2016 recorded the slowest growth..." This is an objective statement. We only need to look up the global economic growth rates for 2016 and previous years to verify if this is true or false. Because it can be verified by facts, it is a positive statement.

分析陳述 (2) - 實證性 (Positive)

「2016年全年經濟增長率為1.9%...」這同樣是一個可以客觀驗證的陳述。政府的統計處有明確的數據顯示增長率是否正好為 1.9%，以及是否落在 1% 至 2% 的預算區間內。這裡沒有主觀的判斷，只有對數據的陳述。

"For 2016 as a whole, there was a growth of 1.9%..." This is also a statement that can be objectively verified. Government statistics clearly show whether the growth rate was exactly 1.9% and whether it fell within the 1% to 2% forecast range. There is no subjective judgement here, only a statement of data.

分析陳述 (3) - 規範性 (Normative)

「政府應積極發揮促進者的角色，善用公共資源，推出合宜的政策。」這裡包含了強烈的價值判斷詞彙：「應該」(should) 和「合宜的」(appropriate)。不同人對於政府「應該」做什麼、什麼政策才算「合宜」，都有不同的主觀標準，無法用單一的客觀數據去證明對錯。因此，這是規範性陳述。

"the Government should play an active role... taking forward appropriate policies..." This contains strong value-judgement words: "should" and "appropriate". Different people have different subjective standards regarding what the government "should" do and what policies are considered "appropriate". It cannot be proven right or wrong with objective data. Therefore, it is a normative statement.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

Pivot Point (解題樞紐): 尋找「主觀價值詞彙」(Subjective Value Words)。
在分辨這類題目時，不需要具備深厚的經濟數據知識。你不需要知道 2016 年的經濟增長是否真的是 1.9%。你的任務只是判斷「這句話能否被驗證」。

Pivot Point: Look for "Subjective Value Words".
When solving this type of question, you don't need deep knowledge of economic data. You don't need to know if the 2016 growth was actually 1.9%. Your only task is to determine "Can this statement be verified?".

Distractors (干擾資訊):
陳述 (1) 中的「最慢」(slowest) 和陳述 (2) 中的「符合」(consistent with) 很容易讓人誤以為是主觀評價。但實際上，在數學和統計上，「最慢」（即數值最小）和「符合預期」（落在區間內）都是絕對客觀的數學結論，不含價值判斷。

Distractors:
Words like "slowest" in (1) and "consistent with" in (2) might trick students into thinking they are subjective evaluations. However, mathematically and statistically, "slowest" (lowest numerical value) and "consistent with expectations" (falling within a range) are absolutely objective mathematical conclusions with no value judgement.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

⚠️ Traps to Avoid (避開陷阱)

  1. 錯誤的陳述 ≠ 規範性陳述 (False Statements ≠ Normative Statements)
    很多學生以為「講錯的話」就是規範性。例如：「香港目前的失業率是 99%。」這句話明顯是錯的，但因為它可以被數據證偽，所以它依然是實證性陳述。
    Many students think "incorrect statements" are normative. For example: "Hong Kong's current unemployment rate is 99%." This statement is obviously false, but because it can be falsified by data, it is still a positive statement.
  2. 混淆「預測」與「規範」(Confusing "Forecasts" with "Normative")
    陳述(2)提到「估算」(forecast)。預測未來（例如「明年通脹率將達到 3%」）仍然是實證性的，因為當明年到來時，我們可以用事實去驗證這個預測是否準確。
    Statement (2) mentions a "forecast". Predicting the future (e.g., "Inflation will hit 3% next year") is still positive, because when next year arrives, we can use facts to verify if the prediction was accurate.

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 題目原文是「規模性陳述」，是不是一種新的經濟學概念？

A: 不是的！這絕對是坊間練習或試卷掃描時常見的字詞錯誤 (Typo)。在 HKDSE 經濟學中，對應 Positive Statement 的是「實證性陳述」，對應 Normative Statement 的是「規範性陳述」。考試時請認準「規範」(Normative) 這個詞，這代表了社會或個人對事物的「規範或期望」。

Q1: The original Chinese text says "規模性" (Scale-related). Is this a new economic concept?
A: No! This is definitely a common typo found in unofficial exercises or scanned past papers. In HKDSE Economics, the correct term for Normative Statement is "規範性陳述". Always look for the word "規範" (Normative), which represents society's or an individual's "norms or expectations" of how things should be.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

⚡ Fast-Track Method (速解法)

在 MC 卷中，時間就是分數。遇到實證與規範的題目，請直接啟動「關鍵詞掃描」 (Keyword Scanning)：

  • 見到以下字眼 👉 99% 是規範性 (Normative)：
    • 應該 / 不應該 (Should / Ought to / Must)
    • 公平 / 不公平 (Fair / Unfair)
    • 最好 / 太差 (Best / Too bad - implying moral judgment)
    • 合宜 / 恰當 (Appropriate / Proper)
  • 見到以下字眼 👉 99% 是實證性 (Positive)：
    • 數字、百分比 (Numbers, Percentages like $1.9\%$)
    • 過去的年份、歷史紀錄 (Past years, Historical records)
    • 將會 (Will / Will be - because it can be verified in the future)

In MC papers, time is marks. When facing Positive vs. Normative questions, immediately activate Keyword Scanning. Words like "Should, Fair, Appropriate" almost always guarantee a Normative statement. Words involving hard data, historical years, or testable future predictions ("Will") almost always indicate a Positive statement.
