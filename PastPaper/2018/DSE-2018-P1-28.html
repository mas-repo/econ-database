📊 HKDSE Economics Question Analysis

Topic (課題): Macroeconomics: AS-AD Model (宏觀經濟學：總供應及總需求模型)

Difficulty (難度): ⭐⭐⭐

Key Concepts (核心概念): Aggregate Demand (總需求), Short-run vs Long-run Equilibrium (短期與長期均衡), Inflationary Gap (通脹裂口)

1. The Question (題目)

下圖顯示某經濟的總需求(AD)曲線、短期總供應(SRAS)曲線及長期總供應(LRAS)曲線。該經濟原初在 $E_0$ 運作。

The diagram below shows the aggregate demand (AD) curves, short run aggregate supply (SRAS) curves and the long run aggregate supply (LRAS) curve of an economy. The economy initially operates at $E_0$.

若政府每年向每位市民派發 $10,000 現金，短期均衡會在 _______，而長期均衡會在 _______。

If the government gives $10,000 cash to every citizen every year, the short-run equilibrium will be at ___________ and the long-run equilibrium will be at __________.

A. $E_2$ …… $E_0$
B. $E_2$ …… $E_1$
C. $E_3$ …… $E_0$
D. $E_3$ …… $E_4$

2. Concept Review (概念重溫)

總需求 (Aggregate Demand, AD)

總需求是經濟體系內對本地生產物品及服務的總需求量。政府派發現金（轉移支付）會增加市民的可支配收入 (Disposable Income)，從而刺激消費開支 (Consumption)。這會導致總需求曲線向右移。

Aggregate demand is the total demand for domestically produced goods and services in an economy. When the government gives out cash handouts (transfer payments), it increases citizens' disposable income, thereby stimulating consumption expenditure. This causes the AD curve to shift to the right.

$$AD = C + I + G + NX$$

短期與長期調整 (Short-run vs. Long-run Adjustments)

短期 (Short-run): 由於資源價格（如名義工資）具僵固性 (sticky)，產出可以偏離充分就業水平。$AD$ 增加會造成通脹裂口 (Inflationary gap)。

Short-run: Because resource prices (like nominal wages) are sticky, output can deviate from the full-employment level. An increase in $AD$ creates an inflationary gap.

長期 (Long-run): 當物價上升，工人會要求更高的名義工資。這會導致生產成本上升，短期總供應曲線 ($SRAS$) 會向左移，直到經濟回復至長期總供應 ($LRAS$) 的充分就業產出水平為止。

Long-run: As price levels rise, workers will demand higher nominal wages. This increases production costs, causing the SRAS curve to shift to the left until the economy returns to the full-employment output level on the LRAS curve.

3. The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

正確答案是 (Correct Option): Option B

❌ Option A ($E_2$ …… $E_0$)

短期均衡 $E_2$ 是正確的。但是，長期而言，由於生產成本上升，短期總供應 ($SRAS$) 會減少並向左移。經濟不會回到原來的 $E_0$，因為總需求 ($AD$) 已經永久增加。

The short-run equilibrium at $E_2$ is correct. However, in the long run, SRAS will decrease and shift to the left due to rising production costs. The economy will not return to the original $E_0$ because AD has permanently increased.

✅ Option B ($E_2$ …… $E_1$)

短期：政府派錢增加消費，$AD_0$ 向右移至 $AD_2$。新的 $AD_2$ 與原本的 $SRAS_0$ 相交於 $E_2$。
長期：在 $E_2$，實際產出高於潛在產出（出現通脹裂口）。資源價格（工資）最終會上升以追上物價，導致生產成本增加。$SRAS_0$ 向左移至 $SRAS_1$，與新的 $AD_2$ 相交於 $LRAS_0$ 上的 $E_1$。

Short-run: The cash handout increases consumption, shifting $AD_0$ rightwards to $AD_2$. The new $AD_2$ intersects with the original $SRAS_0$ at $E_2$.
Long-run: At $E_2$, actual output is higher than potential output (an inflationary gap exists). Resource prices (wages) will eventually rise to catch up with the price level, increasing production costs. $SRAS_0$ shifts leftwards to $SRAS_1$, intersecting the new $AD_2$ on the $LRAS_0$ at $E_1$.

❌ Options C & D ($E_3$ ……)

$E_3$ 代表總需求減少（向左移）。派發現金會刺激消費而非減少消費，因此一開始 $AD$ 絕不會向左移。可直接排除 C 和 D。

$E_3$ represents a decrease (leftward shift) in Aggregate Demand. A cash handout stimulates consumption rather than reducing it, so AD would never shift left initially. Options C and D can be eliminated immediately.

4. Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

  • 關鍵轉捩點 (Pivot Point): 釐清「政府派發現金」對模型的影響。現金轉移直接影響消費 ($C$)，屬於總需求 ($AD$) 的變數。
    Identify the impact of the "government cash handout". Cash transfers directly affect consumption ($C$), which is a variable of Aggregate Demand ($AD$).
  • 找出短期均衡: 移動一條曲線 ($AD \rightarrow AD_2$)，找尋與原有另一條曲線 ($SRAS_0$) 的交點。
    Finding SR Equilibrium: Shift one curve ($AD \rightarrow AD_2$) and find the intersection with the original opposing curve ($SRAS_0$).
  • 找出長期均衡: 由於長期的產出必定落在 $LRAS$ 垂直線上。我們只需找出新的 $AD_2$ 與 $LRAS_0$ 的交點。
    Finding LR Equilibrium: Since long-run output must fall on the vertical $LRAS$ line. We simply find the intersection of the new $AD_2$ and $LRAS_0$.

5. Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：混淆政策性質 (Confusing Policy Types)

有些同學會以為政府派錢是「生產津貼」，錯誤地將 $SRAS$ 向右移。緊記，派給市民的現金屬於轉移支付，影響的是需求側 (Demand-side)，而非供應側 (Supply-side)。

Some students might mistake a cash handout for a "production subsidy" and incorrectly shift the SRAS to the right. Remember, cash given to citizens is a transfer payment, which affects the Demand-side, not the Supply-side.

陷阱 2：忘記長期的自我修正機制 (Forgetting the Long-run Self-correction Mechanism)

同學可能只看到 $AD$ 增加至 $E_2$，卻忘記在長期，通脹裂口會推高工資，迫使 $SRAS$ 減少直至回到充分就業產出水平 ($LRAS$)。

Students might only see AD increasing to $E_2$ and forget that in the long run, an inflationary gap pushes up wages, forcing SRAS to decrease until it returns to the full-employment output level ($LRAS$).

6. Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 為什麼長期總供應 (LRAS) 曲線沒有移動？
Why didn't the Long-Run Aggregate Supply (LRAS) curve shift?

A: $LRAS$ 代表一個經濟體的潛在產出 (Potential Output)。它只會在生產要素（如勞動力、資本、土地）數量增加或技術進步時才會向右移。派發紙幣並不會奇蹟地增加社會的生產資源或提升科技水平，因此 $LRAS$ 保持不變。

A: The $LRAS$ represents an economy's potential output. It only shifts rightwards when there is an increase in the quantity/quality of factors of production (labor, capital, land) or technological advancement. Handing out paper money does not magically increase society's productive resources or improve technology, so the $LRAS$ remains unchanged.

7. Exam Tricks & Shortcuts (考試技巧與速解)

一秒破題法 (The 1-Second Shortcut)

長期均衡必定處於 $LRAS$ 之上！ (Long-run equilibrium MUST be on the LRAS curve!)

看著圖表，圖中位於 $LRAS_0$ 上的點只有 $E_1$, $E_0$, 和 $E_4$。因此，答案的「長期均衡」部分只可能是這三點之一。因為派錢必定增加 $AD$ ($AD$ 向上/右移)，新的均衡點一定比原本的 $E_0$ 高。在 $LRAS$ 上高於 $E_0$ 的點只有 $E_1$。一眼就能看出答案是 B，甚至不需要詳細分析 $SRAS$ 的移動軌跡！

Looking at the diagram, the only points on $LRAS_0$ are $E_1$, $E_0$, and $E_4$. Therefore, the "long-run equilibrium" part of the answer MUST be one of these three points. Since giving out money definitively increases $AD$ ($AD$ shifts up/right), the new equilibrium must be higher than the original $E_0$. The only point on $LRAS$ higher than $E_0$ is $E_1$. You can spot the answer is B at a glance, without even fully tracing the SRAS shift!
